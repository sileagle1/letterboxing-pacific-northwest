WA: Knot a great weekend.

From: hoopoe4me (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2003-05-26 23:20:13 UTC
What a frustration. I have 4 boxes ready to go. The long awaited
three for Pack Forest and one for Umptanum Falls.

I arrived at Pack early yesterday and could find no "take away"
maps. After studying the trailhead maps I realized that my original
plan was a bit stymied by distance and circumstance. Because this
area is a classroom, there may be road and trail closures at any time.

I hiked in my intended route but met with "road closed due to heavy
equipment" signs. I decided that I was not comfortable placeing at
this time. The director of the facility was most welcoming and
encouraged me to contact him, so I will get back with him and find
out what trail option is most likely to be available to visitors at
any time. A family I met as I was leaving says they often fail to
take down the closed signs on the weekend ( when there is no forestry
activity) so they tend to ignore them. I did not feel comfortable
with this so I will plan for placement after visiting with the
director.

I did give the family a flier on Letterboxing. :-D

Today I drove east and visited Fudricks new box in Ellensburg. (
nice stamp fun use of clues) I posted a while back that I would be
placing a box at Umptanum Falls, which is about 9 miles from Fudricks
box.

I got up to the falls area and thought it mighty interesting that on
Memorial Day, the parking lot was empty and very clean.

I hiked in about 15 minutes and found out why. The through trail to
the falls, which passes over private land, has been heavily fenced
and friendly No Trespass this means you signs posted everywhere.

Ah me, such a nice modest hike to a pretty venue. I could try making
an adventure hike using compass bearings, but did not feel this was a
good ploy in such rugged, isolated area.

So I planted the box in a nice area. It is not much of a hike, but a
nice spot for a picnic and nature study. It is not that far from the
freeway, but it feels miles away. It makes a nice 1 - 2 with
Fudricks box. A third box at Ginko makes for a solid day of boxing,
Eastern Washington style.
