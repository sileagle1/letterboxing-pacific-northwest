Many many thank yous to the fabulous Funhog and Amanda the Paisley Orca
for a wonderful weekend of letterboxing!
It was a great experience to meet all the fun and interesting folks
behind the names.
The park was beautiful and filled with great letterboxes to find.
We appreciate how nice everyone was to us not-very-experienced
letterboxers and our not-always-ruly boys. ;)

In one weekend we went from P1 F35 X0 to P2 F45 X29!
(plus the event stamp and the hitchhikers or whatever Molly, Oprah, and
K-Martha count as).

Our 9 year old Patrick "Lucky Charm" carved his very first stamp at the
carving lesson, and mom DblTall made her first log book at the
bookbinding class, so to celebrate this momentous occasion we added
another letterbox to a park already abounding with them. It's called
"First Time's the Charm" and I will post the clues soon.
Patrick was absolutely thrilled to plant his very own letterbox, and
have lots of people stamp in it right away.

We enjoyed meeting everyone, and had a wonderful time!

Thanks again to all the terrific people who made this possible.

From: charlietrailduster (letteryah@ckron.com) | Date: 2004-08-29 19:50:25 UTC
A major thank you to Camp Fire Lady, Pandora, Maiden and Bookworm for
putting together the wonderful Passport to Letterboxing gathering at
beautiful Battle Ground Lake. I really had a good time meeting new
people, attending two expertly presented and planned seminars,
collecting bunches of stamps and finding lots of letterboxes. It was
very fortunate that the excitement we had Saturday evening had a happy
ending and those who received the "walk" stamp certainly deserved it.
And what a joy to meet the famous "DER Mad Stamper". Thank you all
for helping Pandora celebrate her 28th and my 30th birthday, it was a
lot of fun! Oh, and while I always appreciate getting a new hat to
wear there are some designs I appreciate more than others. Well done
guys! Lets do it again!
