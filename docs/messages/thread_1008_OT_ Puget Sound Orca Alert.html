From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-12-11 01:01:05 UTC
For letterboxers who are also interested in seeing orcas in their
habitat while they're out 'boxing around Puget Sound, there have
been recent reports of Orca sightings in South Puget Sound. Not
good news for the resident seal population, but fun for the rest of
us who don't get to see the orcas all that often. I found a link to
a whale sighting map that plots reports you might enjoy.

http://www.orcanetwork.org/sightings/map.html#map

For letterbox treks that may improve your odds of spotting orcas in
the area because they may take you close to the Sound's shore, I
recommend these letterboxes:

Any of the San J*uan Islands letterboxes incl Whidbey Island
(assorted placers)
Manchester S*P* Series near Bremerton (Trial & Error's & E.G.'s)
Sunrise Beach Letterboxes (Gig Harbor--dvn2r ckr's)
P*t D*efiance Park Letterboxes (Tacoma--assorted placers)
Dash P*t Park Letterboxes (Fed Way--assorted placers)
S*altars Point LB (Steilacoom--dvn2r ckr)
Woodard Bay LB (Olympia area--assorted placers)
Mee Kwa M*ooks LBs (West Seattle--assorted placers)
Meadow P*oint LB (Seattle--dvn2r ckr)
Carkee*k Park LBs (Seattle--assorted placers)
B*rown's Point LB (North Tacoma--dvn2r ckr)
K*opachuck SP LBs (Gig Harbor--E.G.'s, I think)
Uninvited Guest LB (Bainbridge Island--dvn2r ckr)

I'm sure there are many others--these are just the ones I could
remember off the top of my head. If you happen to see an orca on
your outings, please let us know. We like to live vicariously!!! :)


Enjoy!

dvn2r ckr
