Please allow me to add a few things to Ryan's explanation.

Since I run both my own mail server and a website that sends out emails as
well as a Yahoo group and I do this kind of thing for a living, I can give
you the answer that I have found, although, please don't take this as an
admonishment of Ryan's answer or anything at all.

The bottom line as to why Yahoo group emails bounce is because of the spam
filters that every ISP provides now. Earthlink, AOL, etc, etc, etc, they
all have spam filters in place to weed out the spam before it reaches your
inbox and freeing you from the drudgery if having to wade through it
yourself.

So, what happens is this:

As shown by the headers in the email that I am responding to:

Delivered-To: rayvenhaus@lbworld.org
Received: from n7a.bulk.scd.yahoo.com (n7a.bulk.scd.yahoo.com [66.94.237.41])
by www.lbworld.org (Postfix) with SMTP id A82E5176888
for ; Sat, 8 Jan 2005 10:23:22 -0800 (PST)
Received: from [66.218.69.3] by n7.bulk.scd.yahoo.com with NNFMP; 08 Jan 2005
18:23:21 -0000
Received: from [66.218.67.193] by mailer3.bulk.scd.yahoo.com with NNFMP;
08 Jan 2005
18:23:20 -0000

Yahoo does things so very differently. Notice the "Received" lines above?
there are two of them, identifying that the message started at one
server, and transited through another server. That, along with the fact
that it appears to the receiving mail system that this letter went to many
different addresses, as it should, it is, after all, a mailing list
letter, some of the more stringent mail filters will identify it as spam
email and, in most cases, when it receives what it believes to be a spam
email, it responds with a "User Unknown" response to the originating mail
system, hoping that they will then remove the address in question (Yours
for example) from their spam mailing list and thereby reducing the amount
of spam mail you receive.

When I was still a part of the LBNA mailing list, along with about 12
other Yahoo groups, we would bounce regularly. After troubleshooting this
problem between my mailserver and Yahoo, with Yahoo's help, we finally
managed to get it working 95% of the time, lots better than the every
other letter bouncing back. At that time, I was told by yahoo Tech
Support, that the only email addresses they could guarantee would not have
any problems with yahoo groups would be Yahoo based email. (Go figure!
:-) )

My website also had a similar problem with emails it was sending out, much
like Ryan has experienced, though not with MSN, but with Earthlink, in
that Earthlink was identifying the emails as containing a virus because
the headers were malformed.... weeks of work between myself and Earthlink
to resolve that one, but not before I got accused of trying to infect
peoples computers. (chuckles softly)

There are also one other thing that can cause Yahoo to mark your account
as bouncing and that is a full mailbox, causing your mail system to refuse
the email from Yahoo, which causes Yahoo to mark your account as
undeliverable.

Unfortunately, there's not much you can do if the system is bouncing your
emails because they have incorrectly identified them as spam.
