From: rebeccalavelle (rebeccalavelle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-23 20:05:16 UTC
Is anyone else traveling for Christmas?
After Christmas I am heading from Portland to San Francisco.

I was thinking that I would get together a list of all the letterboxes
at rest stops on I-5. Not the ones near the highway, or in the towns
sourrounding the highway, but actually at real rest stops.

Here are lists of the rest areas along I-5:
California- http://www.dot.ca.gov/hq/maint/ra/
Oregon- http://www.tripcheck.com/Pages/RAentry.asp
Washington- http://www.wsdot.wa.gov/biz/RestAreas/restareamap.htm

It would be an interesting thing to someday have letterboxes at all
the rest stops along I-5. That way you could stop at any one, and know
that there was a box there! Think about how exciting family trips
would be! You would be stopping every hour or so, but it would be fun.

So far I have:
Applegate Rest Area

On The Road Again (at the Welcome Center in California.)

-Moon something.... I printed out the clues to this one, found it, and
then when I got back it had been taken off the website. I will see if
it is still there. I wonder if this one has been abandoned?

Any others to add to the list?

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-23 12:56:33 UTC-08:00
This isn't quite what you're looking for, and I hope you're not annoyed at my suggesting it, but here goes...

In case you are interested in an alternate route for either direction, I do have two boxes I hid in one location along Highway 101, north of the Bay area in California. They aren't at a rest stop, but it's equally as simple (a pull-off many use as a rest stop, I'm sure).

I have received one update about them a number of months ago and they figured they were missing because the spot is a little odd and they said there was poison oak nearby. (I was just looking to place them at the end of my road trip, had had a crappy couple of days, and just went for the nearest place I could find!)

I realize it's kind of a stretch, but rather along the lines you've mentioned (just a different highway) so I thought I'd mention it. It'd be nice if they had some visitors!! Here's the addresses:

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=14215&boxname=Rainbow_Brite_Series_#2_-_Red_Butler

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=14216&boxname=Rainbow_Brite_Series_#3_-_Lala_Orange

Have a great trip!!

ClioMouse
