Hey, this might interest some of you Washington area letterboxers out
there--just read an article on The Olympian website saying that they
have passed legislation that will end the $5 state park fee.

http://theolympian.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20060220/NEWS/60220001

It figures--just as we're moving away they lift the fee. I feel like
we've invested in the parks, though--we've spent at least $150 in park
fees just so we could letterbox a bit these past couple of years.
Compare that with out $40 Oregon State Park pass that was good for 2
years!

Re: WA: State Park fees

From: Nail Family (the7nails@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-21 03:33:59 UTC
We've got mixed emotions about the fees being lifted. On the one hand,
we are grateful that it will not cost $5 a day to access the parks..a
boon to the family budget. On the other hand, I kind of appreciate
the pay as you go system, less general tax dollars funding services
that, really, only a a small number of the population use. And of
course, we really have enjoyed the fact that the parks are less
crowded in recent years due to the discouraging fees. But that issue
is a real selfish one. I suppose that the parks belong to all the
people and all the people should have equal access to them regardless
of how often they use them or whether they can afford to pay a daily
user fee either (as long as they are Washington citizens who regularly
pay their sales tax. You Oregonians dont count). Still, no one can
access a movie theater anytime one wants at no charge. User fees make
some sense.

Perhaps a better alternative would be a smaller daily use fee. Say $1
instead of $5.

We really hope that this move will not result in more park closures.
It always seems to come back to that whenever there is any talk of
budget cuts of any kind.

I would be content to continue paying $5 per day if it keeps the parks
open.

Nailhead of the Nail Family
