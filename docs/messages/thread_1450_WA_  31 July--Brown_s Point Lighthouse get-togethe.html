WA: 31 July--Brown's Point Lighthouse get-together?

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-17 15:37:17 UTC
Anybody up for a mini-get together at Brown's Point Lighthouse
(Tacoma--North side of Commencement Bay) on the afternoon of
Saturday, 31 July 04 (2-4pm'ish)? The park is a small lighthouse
facility with lightkeeper quarters, rolling hillside with garden and
surrounded by small beaches (depending on the tide schedule for the
day) and a few picnic tables. This would be an opportunity to meet
with some letterboxers from Southern California (Spacy Holly &
family)! We could plan on meeting at the flag pole.

There's only one letterbox on/near the premises but probably another
couple dozen or so within 10 miles of the park.

Re: WA: 31 July--Brown's Point Lighthouse get-together?

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-26 17:59:32 UTC
This is just a chance to get-together to meet Spacy Holly & family
who are visiting from Southern California (I think she grew up in
this area & has placed a box or two in Renton?). The Brown's Point
Park venue doesn't really support a bunch of conventional boxes
(although alternative boxes might prove interesting) so it's really
just a meet & greet type thing not really a blow out LB gathering.
I'm learning that many of the regulars are unable to attend because
of other commitments. Unfortunately, I'm unable to adjust the time
with Spacy Holly as she & her family are enroute and don't/won't
have email access on the trip. I also don't have a phone number to
contact them before I see them in person on Saturday.

For folks who are unable to join us on Saturday, you could probably
still link up with Holly for an exchange at the Lighthouse Keeper's
Cottage on the park grounds sometime during the weekend/week
following 31 Jul. Just ask for her by name. She has plans to
letterbox around the Puget Sound region during the week so I can't
pass along any more 'concrete' times during which you can link up
with her there apart from Sat from 2-4pm.

It's a gorgeous venue so you won't be disappointed in spending a few
hours at the beach with an opportunity to 'dabble' a little bit in
letterboxing while you're there. Oh, dogs are allowed. Also, there
are a few other letterboxes just a few miles away from this park
(Dash Pt--think Trial N Error) so you can enjoy some other local
gems, too.

Hope to see you there,

dvn2r ckr
