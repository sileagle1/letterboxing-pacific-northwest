From: Danny McMillin (macdanny@mindspring.com) | Date: 2004-05-09 20:33:41 UTC-07:00
Hello fellow Letterboxers of the Great Pacific Northwest,

I have adapted my first letterbox, Mee-Kwa-Mooks Low Tide, in West Seattle,
Washington. Amanda from Seattle was looking after it, but has graciously
let me take it over. She adapted it from Jill-the-Reckless, the original
planter.

Jill made a great stamp, for those who haven't seen it. She also hand-made
a small journal that is quite beautiful. The cover is handmade paper and
the inside stamping paper is hand torn watercolor paper. It has finally
filled up with visiting stamps.

Rather than start a new journal, I was able to add more pages with
watercolor paper I already had. I hand-stitched them in by copying the
original pages and stitching. It was a much bigger chore than I expected as
I have never done any binding like this before. It's not nearly as pretty
as Jill's work, but it is functional and added 20 pages to the back of the
journal. Now folks visiting can still see the history of stamps since the
box's beginning.

Reading glasses, thimbles, strong thread, a needle and trial and error got
me through. A bit of work for sure, but it keeps a beautiful journal at the
ready for the next visitor.

See you on the trails.
