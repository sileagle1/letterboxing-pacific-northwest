availability during this visit to our neck of the woods.

I studied Des Moines' parks & rec site and there are quite a few
parks in the area. I'm only personally familiar with a few parks on
the list with the Beach Park jumping out at me (view, functioning
toilets & water fountains, playground, picnic tables, a couple
trails AND NARY A RESIDENT LETTERBOX). I'm really honing in on that
region because of our mystery guest's schedule, the location from
which he/she/they hail, and where he/she/they must be at 0dark30 the
following day (opposite side of the region).

Directions to Des Moines Beach Park from I-5 follow:

At exit 149B, turn RIGHT onto Ramp (WA-516 / Des Moines) 0.3m
Turn RIGHT (West) onto SR-516 [S Kent des Moines Rd] 2.1m
Bear RIGHT (North-West) onto SR-509 [Marine View Dr S] 0.3m
Turn LEFT (West) onto S 223rd St 0.2m
Road name changes to Cliff Ave S 0.1m
Road name changes to Nebo Blvd 0.1m
Road name changes to Local road(s) < 0.1m
End: Arrive Des Moines Beach Park

So, it isn't as far from I-5 as I thought (3.2 miles). It does,
however, feel like it's worlds away from the hustle & bustle of
traffic & daily life.

This park isn't the largest in the state but if folks are interested
in boosting the region's letterbox population, there are several
other neighboring parks scattered about the region that might
welcome a box or two. Plant the boxes within the next several weeks
and then post the clues a night or two before the gathering--then
folks could hunt for those boxes before the formal part of the meet
& greet. There is also a great 6-box letterbox series not terribly
far from this venue planted by Amanda from Seattle (Flight Path
Series)--so folks looking for some hunts & great stamps won't be
disappointed.

Any more ideas? Our other option is to gather at a restaurant
somewhere near the airport but that sort of event gets pretty
tedious requiring RSVPs/reservations, commitments from participants
and a case of heebies/jeebies for those of us with squirmy little
kids who subject them to interacting with the public in 'formal'
settings (i.e. involving utensils, food and physics experiments in
mass, force & projectiles). With the sun setting later in the
evening nowadays, we could start the formalities of the event around
2 or 3pm and then culminate everything with the appearance of the
guest(s) for exchanges? I'm not terribly certain, yet, about the
arrival time of our guest(s) but planning it this way would allow
those folks who can't stay very long to meet some area letterboxers
while the others who can stay for the duration will be able to meet
the mystery guest(s). Depending on the guest's' arrival time, we
could slide the start time forwards or backwards a bit so the most
folks in the region could participate...


Keep the ideas flowing. We'll get something sorted out,
