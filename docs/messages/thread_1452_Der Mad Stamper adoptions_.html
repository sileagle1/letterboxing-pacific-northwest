Der Mad Stamper adoptions?

From: kyledspinks (kylespinks@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-07-18 21:30:55 UTC
Hi all,
About a year ago I asked if there were any letterboxers out there that
would be willing to adopt Der Mad Stamper's letterboxes within
Tualatin Hills Park and Recreation District. One person stepped
forward, adopting "Cedar Hills Twins" and "Hyland Forest", and
retiring "Fanno Creek Greenway." Thanks Mudstumpers!

Unfortunately, there's a few left that need attention. I know that
Rock Creek #1 has been retired, but I was unable to find Rock Creek #3
or #4, one of the "Troll and the Dragon" letterboxes, or "Bronson
Creek Letterbox" ("The Dragon," was located in an area frequented by
drinkers, people doing drugs, etc., and should be moved to a more
appropriate location). NOTE: ROCK CREEK #2 HAS A WASP NEST NEXT TO IT
AND SHOULD NOT BE APPROACHED.

Another box, "The Lost Letterbox," is in good condition, but will need
adoption too.

Please remember that, at the Cornelius Pass Roadhouse Letterboxing
get-together about a year ago, I asked the letterboxing community to
be proactive in their letterboxing fun. All letterboxes must be
maintained by the person(s) that placed the box. When I monitor the
letterboxes, the first thing I think about is the safety of the
letterboxers, followed by attention to the impacts to the natural
areas. These last two things are very important for the continued fun
of letterboxing in District parks.

I also asked that letterboxers contact me ahead of time about new
boxes so that we can coordinate on placement. We have dozens of
restoration projects that go on in District parks each year and I will
try and contact letterbox owners prior to such projects to make sure
the boxes aren't disturbed as part of the restoration work.

Is there anybody else out there that would like to adopt Der Mad
Stamper's letterboxes? If I don't get any 'takers,' I'll be forced to
request that the unadopted boxes in District parks be removed from the
list of boxes.

Please feel free to contact me with any questions or comments. Thank
you for your support and cooperation.

Kyle Spinks
kspinks@thprd.com
503-644-8469 x32
