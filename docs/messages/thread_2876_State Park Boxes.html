Hi Wie Boxers,



Oddly enough WA state parks discourage the placement of letterboxes in their
parks. You may want to consider going somewhere other than a state park.
Or somewhere with a park nearby, perhaps.



It seems to be a misunderstanding on the part of the Parks & Rec Commission.
I'm not sure but it appears they think we tear up the terrain. As you
probably know, we're about NOT doing that.



So sorry if I threw a wet blanket on your plans. But I thought you'd want
to know.



Happy boxin'!

Kurious Jo

What I'd do, I'd start with a list of all the State Parks
(http://www.parks.wa.gov/parks/ gives you both a list and a search engine),
pick out the ones that sound the best for their other features and amenities,
and run them through AQ to see what might be around them. If nothing looks
good, pick out the next best and try again.

Oh, and don't forget to check promising points along your route, too.

I'd love to see somebody create an AQ/MapQuest fusion, where you could put in a
route and see what boxes lie along it (or better yet, put in the two ends and
get route possibilities listed by how many 'boxes lie along them). That
wouldn't be me, though; took me three weeks just to figure out how to set up my
modem....

Hope you find someplace good!

Re: [LbPNW] State Park Boxes

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-14 14:23:12 UTC-08:00
Have you tried using the trip planner on Atlasquest? I admit that I have not used it, but people seem quite pleased with it. You put in a start and end location, and it will give you boxes in the vicinity of that route, instead of just the vicinity of a point.

Also, as has been stated before, boxes are not actually allowed in state parks, a point of much discussion. Even if some boxes are listed on LbNA or AQ as being in a state park, park rangers often search for the name of the park and remove the box. For this reason, people disguise the name to not be found in a search.

Sometimes people plant near a state park, so as to honor the park but not break rules. Just something else to consider.

ClioMouse
