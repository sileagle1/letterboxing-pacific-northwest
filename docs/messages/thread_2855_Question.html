From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-03 12:17:58 UTC-07:00
You'd be surprised at how broad the definition of a letterbox can be!

There are series out there already much like what you're describing, so I'm sure yours would be a wonderful addition to the world of boxes. The one that comes to mind is SHH's Festival Letterbox, which changes throughout the year based on holidays. (You can see it here: http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/clue/index.html?gBoxId=9676 and I hope I'm not violating any rules by posting the address!)

Incidentally, there are also boxes that move locations, either because the previous location was not a good enough hiding spot or for other reasons. This can get tougher, since the clues have to change each time, instead of just the contents of the box. Traveling event boxes, however, don't exactly have clues, so they get passed from event to event and are mailed to people all over to share.

Good luck in all your 'boxing ventures!

ClioMouse

From: Sarah (directoroffun@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-05 00:51:31 UTC
Another example would be my 'Seeking Marmalade' box which I bring to
gatherings. I change the stamp each time, to reflect the occassion,
but it's always in the same container. It is a 'hidden in plain
sight' box, but people still need to find it.

I've been doing this for over a year (and I go to a lot of
gatherings). When I started, I worried that people would be bored or
disappointed since once they have found it the first time, they would
know what to look for next time. However, the feedback I receive has
been positive and folks seem disappointed if for some reason I didn't
do it. I guess the lure of PZKut is too great to resist.

Have fun!
Marmalade
