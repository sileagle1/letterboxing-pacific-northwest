Carving personal stamps. My first effort was my signature stamp
carved from "the pink stuff" that came in a kit I got at Michaels.
It held up well for those first 100 stamps. Personally I think it
turned out pretty OK. Wont comment on some of my other efforts.

I mostly use the trace and transfer technique. Using tracing paper
trace the image you want. The lettering too, don't forget this.

Flip the paper over place on carving medium. Rub with fingernail or
a coin.

Carefully peel off. The image should now be ready to carve.
Remember the letters are mirror image so when you stamp the will be
right way. I back some stamps with a bit of thin plywood from the
craft store.

I have used master carve ( too soft for my taste) and now use PZ
Kut. I like how PZ Kut holds the blade well but allows sharp
detained turns when carving. Holds enough to avoid stupid
heartbreaking OOOOps type mistakes.

Google is a great source for images. Type in a topic and have fun.
