Hi - bluerosegang here: We're also pretty new to LB {Nov 2004) but
we just jumped right in. We even tried carving our first stamp this
weekend and it turned out ok - nothing special but at least we know
we can do it and I'm sure we'll improve with practice.

We used a block of carving rubber from the craft store, a regular #2
pencil and an exacto knife as our only tools. We outlined the image
with pencil, turned the paper over and rubbed it onto the rubber
block - the image came out pretty clean. Then we just carved it
out. Does anyone else have a better technique? Our most asked
question is: What is the best way to get the image onto the rubber
block?

Happy LB'ing

Hi BRG,
There are several tutorials posted through out the web sites via
links. Ryan's Atlas Quest www.atlasquest.com would be a good
starting place. There is even a Carving Board (sounds like I'm
touting a cooking show)that is set up for conversations about stamp
carving. If you get any responses on this post from Funhog ,Pandora,
or Cadenza you should pay attention as they are all great stamp
carvers,as are many others in the PNW.
There are several ways to transfer images. One is the pencil, thumb
scaping method I still use, other carvers swear by acetone transfer
or warm iron transfer. I haven't tried the iron technique as I'm a
little afraid that any iron use by me might tilt the balance of
household duties. The acetone method can really work well, but it
takes some practice, and the right kind of printer.
Since you are fairly new to letterboxing here is an alternate site
for new letterboxers http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers/
