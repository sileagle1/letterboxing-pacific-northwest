I guess I need to clarify myself on a couple of points... I was not assuming that REI
would hire someone without experience just advising against it. I, too, would prefer
that an experienced letterboxer who understands the mystique of the hobby would be
the one to teach the class.

Then the logbook thing... I have nothing against 'cheap.' God knows, I am the bargain
queen and my handmade books cost way less than even the cheapest Dollar Store
memo pads. My complaint is really about lined pages. I just prefer to see the stamp
images on blank paper. Long before I learned how to stitch books (thank you
Heather) I was always on the quest for good quality unlined memo pads, cheap. The
good art paper books were out of my budget.

Amanda made some very good points about the tie-in of stamp with place. Those are
always my favorite stamps. Good luck with this endeavor... Funhog

From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-07-29 10:30:40 UTC-07:00
Okay I understand a bit more now -- and I totally agree with what is being
said here -- I don't think REI would go out and hire anyone to specifically
host these clinics -- but would pick the most qualified employee to do them.

As for the logbook thing I agree making logs books is cheaper and quite
honestly a lot of fun, however I think we also have to look from the other
perspective, not everyone has the time to hand make logbooks for all their
letterboxes. I agree that lined pages sort of suck for stamping images, but
given that it took me hours and hours to make the logbooks for Iron Bear's
Trail with Tears series I can understand why we don't see these as the norm
in most boxes. -- I'm with Amanda that there are a lot of things that add
to the lure of a letterbox - I personally find boxes more enjoyable that
have stories to them that teach me something, like Ryan's Walking Tour
taught me some wonderful history of Portland. -- I like when the stamp and
location mesh together, but overall, I think we need to realize that when
someone places a letterbox, it is for their enjoyment as well as ours, and
that it can't always be the way we think it should be. I don't think we
should be disappointed over things like log books, and whether or not the
image matches the surroundings any more that we should be disappointed that
we are not the first ones to find it.

Pandora{I`B}
