Nancy Drew and the Hog

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2003-09-14 00:54:22 UTC
We just returned from a three day jaunt over Mt. Hood to Central Oregon. The
original plan was to pitch a tent at Trillium Lake and enjoy the lovely late summer
weather. However, the rain deities changed our minds. We DID stroll in the mist and
find all six of the lovely little stamps that ring the lake. We also replaced the film
canister for the one that needed it and then proceeded on to the sunny side of the
state.

We stopped outside of Warm Springs and Amanda from Seattle got firsties at
Waterfowl Highway. It is a total mystery to me why no other letterboxers have found
this one before, being right off highway 26 and a short stroll. During the next two
days, we visited a slug o' boxes: the Cove, Crooked River Bridge, Crooked River Ranch
#1 & #4, Eagle Crest, Cline Falls, Sam Johnson Park, and both north and south
Redmond Dry Canyon letterboxes. All were just dandy.

After having the only boxes in the area for nearly a year and a half, it's totally exciting
to see the new goodies appearing in the area. It's on its way to becoming an official
letterboxing destination! Many thanks to Birder, Cyndie, Lennens 3, the Fleshmans
and 7Nails for upping the count in this part of the state. Just for good luck, we hid
two new boxes along the way. Clues will follow directly (although they themselves
may not be particularly direct.) Funhog
