Greetings,

I am a Girl Scout leader and would like to take my girls on a couple
of these. They seem like they would be fun for the kids and in
talking to the girls, they liked the idea as kind of like a treasure
hunt. We are in the Seattle area (Capitol Hill to be exact). So my
question to y'all would be what trips would you recommend and why? My
girls are 3rd - 5th grade kids. I would help them, although I want
them to really figure it out themselves. We would prefer to do ones
that cost us little to no money and I would prefer them to be
outdoors. We would be able to get our hands on some compasses. I've
been to both the Atlas and the Letterboxing websites, but don't know
which ones are too easy or which ones would be most fun.

From: mysticdreamer1111 (mysticdreamer1111@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-05 05:07:51 UTC
I don't know the boxes in Seatle, but I would suggest that you
research the hikes, where they are at, the length etc, and chose
along the guide lines of what the girls will enjoy. I have a 3 yr
old boy and a 9yr old girl and my daughter is very good at finding
most of the boxes. I also find that a 3 mile round trip is the max
that I can do with the kids.

Chose a box or series that will hold interest for them, like a
botanica garden where they will learn about plants as well, or a
hike to a waterfall, or something that will teach a little history.

It is all good, just have fun, and they wont even realize that they
are getting exercise and learning.

Mystic Dreamer
