Seattle, WA Lincoln and Mee Kwa Mooks Parks

From: Danny (rodneybender@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-03-14 17:35:54 UTC-08:00
Hi all.

A friend told me about letterboxing last week. Until then, I'd never heard
of this marvelous pastime. After reading about how it works, I became quite
excited about trying it out.

I stopped by my local rubber stamp store to buy supplies for making stamps.
And whom should I meet, but the moderator of this group, Ryan! He
graciously took me under his wing and gave me some great tips for getting
started. Thanks Ryan.

So my wife, our Airedale Terrier Keegan and I set out to find the Mee Kwa
Mooks Low Tide box and then on to Lincoln Park for Amanda's two boxes and
Seattle Mud Puddle Trotter's 3 box series.

As we got ready to go, I of course couldn't find my find my personal stamp
I'd carved. Twenty minutes later, I had another stamp carved and out the
door we went.

We successfully found the Mee Kwa Mooks Low Tide box. It's in good
condition and has a very nice handmade journal. It's down to its last few
pages though.

I mountain bike through this park as well as Lincoln Park and never even
knew letterboxes were hidden within. Cool!

Then it was off to Lincoln Park. Keegan got to chase lots of squirrels as
we searched for the five total boxes hidden about. The first up was
Amanda's box #1. We found the 4-in-1 clue. I assume the box is supposed to
be in the crotch of the 4-in-1 but I couldn't find it. This one could be
MIA.

We found box #2, the Vashon Island Ferry, alive and well. What a beautiful
place to see the ferries and the Puget Sound.

Then it was on to Mud Puddle's series of three boxes. #1 Clover box was
found with some difficulty, but worth the hunt. It's a two-sided stamp.
Nice.

#2 Stump box was a bit easier to find. It's on a trail I mountain bike
every time I ride the park. Amazing. Someone had written in the #2 journal
that they believed the #3 Slug box has gone missing.

I easily found the general area where the Slug box is supposed to be
hidden, but was unable to find it. I am not sure if it's MIA or I was just
not diligent enough in my searching. Anyone bagged this one lately?

So all in all not a bad day. Four out of six. I'll take it for being a newbie.

See you on the trail.
Danny
