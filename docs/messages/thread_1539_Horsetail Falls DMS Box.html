You will find Der Mad Stamper's clues for all of his boxes in the Gorge on the LbNA
site under the Columbia River Gorge Waterfalls series. Also look at Ryan Carpenter's
site for the clue to his replica of the Wahkeena Falls box. It has an entirely different
hiding spot from the original...

The DMS clues can be maddening but are always fun when the 'ah ha' moment hits.
The Multnomah Falls box is still considered a rite of passage box. It has stumped
many and taken multiple tries for others. Good Luck! Funhog

Horsetail was adopted, if I'm not mistaken, and the
was recarved to match the original....the clues were
modified slightly and you can find those on the LBNA
website.

As far as DMS boxes in general - ah, I've made my own
mind up that he left boxes behind for all types of
LBers.....those who enjoy hiking, those who enjoy
difficult puzzles, those who enjoy sightseeing, etc.
I wanted to attempt each DMS box in the area but I
can't - because all of his letterbox clues do not fit
with my ability to "solve" so I have to forego those
because you will find that LBers aren't generally open
to "divulging" precious info about where boxes are
located. Takes the fun out of the hunt....however, it
adds frustration to the person who searches for one
and it's NOT THERE and the placer won't confirm or
deny it. YIKES! LOL
