Re: Slick and High Tech

From: Amanda Arkebauer (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-06-08 08:41:29 UTC

Hey, I am sure that WE would look slick and high tech if Rubbermaid
would put out small waterproof boxes just for letterboxing AND if
Master-carve and Speedball would package "letterboxing" kits and if
Michaels and A.C. Moore would bankroll our website so that they could
sell the stuff.
Heck, I would buy it! :-) I ENVY the geocachers and their slick
stickers that they can put on their honking big boxes. (although I
think the whole decoding thing is a cop-out. What is the point of
putting clues in code if all you have to do is click on the mouse to
get the answer??)
And I am out there buying the regalia. I appreciate the effort put
into it by the Mohmers and all you guys. But I do prefer the folk
art aspect of the hobby. I prefer the homemade stamp as opposed to
the store-bought stamp. I prefer the homemade book to the lined
notepad. I prefer the intricate clever clues rather than the
hitchhiker that is handed to me at a gathering.
And this in not to say that I don't do all the easy things. I have
hidden a store-bought stamp. I consistently use lined notepads and I
am happy to stamp into a hitchhiker that is handed to me. :-)

And geocaching might look all slick with the cool website and the
gear and the stickers, but it's still a box of stuff from the dollar
store. Stuff that is likely waterlogged and soggy (especially up here
in the PNW). Yeah, yeah, I have heard about the boxes that have CD's
and gameboy games and "expensive" stuff. But it's still just stuff.

(but if you have any Letterboxing stuff to sell, I might be
interested...:-)
--Amanda
