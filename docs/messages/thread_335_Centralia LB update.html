Thanks for the nice verse. The message is obvious, but the presentation is

I did find my 'notes' for the 'easy' route to the chafing dish. I did err
(much to everyone's surprise, I am sure). I had writted 70 as the distance.
As I usually measure in paces (2 steps) that is what I assumed I had done.
No such luck, it was probably steps. Look for the upright roots on the
left, then look right just beyond them. I did update the info in the clues.

I would likely have taken 60 steps if I was trying to keep up with rnrB, but
without that, my step is likely shorter.

On the way UP the hill, I wasn't sure if I had gone up the steps or not, it
turns out I hadn't. I know, pay attention!!!!!!

Mummsy was not there. She is enthusiastic, but does not walk too well.
Went to Harry Fain Park. We made it all the way to the first box, and she
was done in. Stood in the playground and looked to see where the clues
went, then I went and got the box.

Took the grandkids to an Easter Egg Hunt. Alexis picked up a ball, when she
bit into it it was a paint ball. Was more worried about having done
something wrong than telling us what she had done. Called the consulting
nurse, who said that the paint was not hazardous but to wash her mouth out
real well.

The park is very nice; great trees and markers, many birds, not too far from
civilization, but a high degree of steepness (at least for some of us :-)

Saw your new stamps, they are great!

See you on the trials, Fudrick
