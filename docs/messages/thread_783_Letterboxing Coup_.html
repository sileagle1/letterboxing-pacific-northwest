Letterboxing Coup!

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2003-10-07 02:36:27 UTC
Last week I received an email from Jim Bader, Park Manager at Silver Falls State Park
telling me he had my letterbox from Frenchie Falls sitting on his desk. He told me I
could pick it up any weekday and gave me a phone number at which to contact him. I
had no idea what to expect.

Today, figuring it would be much better for me to visit him in person, I headed out
there to act as letterboxing diplomat extraordinaire. I took my logbook, my sig
stamp, a printed copy of a web page from the Connecticut State Forest Department
about their letterboxing program (They even give patches to you after you've found
five of them and a trekking pole if you get all thirty!) and a copy of my Frenchie Falls
clue. I was armed!

It turned out that he was TOTALLY receptive to having boxes in his park. He only
requested that he be notified about their existence and location. He suggested we
have Stamp Camp in the park next summer because they have miles and miles of
trails at Silver Falls that are rarely used. He figured we could seed some of those trails
with Letterboxing bait. I met Ranger Sarah, the education person at they park, who
now wants to incorporate letterboxing into the Junior Ranger program. She was so
excited that she exclaimed,"I think I've found a new hobby!"

Having spent an hour together, Jim returned my box to me and suggested I head out
and rehide it and was invited to participate in the camp program next summer. It was
a real thumbs up day for me and my Lb obsession.

That said, I checked on my Buddah box, Ryan's North Falls box and rehid my Frenchie
Falls box. The clue to the last needs a bit of revision since its old hiding place is now
exposed but the other two are just fine. I'm pretty sure that Ryan's Winter Falls box
is missing and I didn't have the time to check on his other plants. Amazingly enough,
almost all the visitors to these boxes were from out of the area. Here we have this
terrific park so close to Portland and the local boxers have pretty much ignored it.
The maples are beginning to change and the weather is still warm. It's the perfect
time to get on out there.
