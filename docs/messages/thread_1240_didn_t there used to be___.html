earlier mentioned stump. Hope I'm not saying too much, but as we

Re: didn't there used to be...

From: hooksbrandy (hooksbrandy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-05 06:14:46 UTC
We just got back from Watershed & these were great. Really nice
place to spend most of the day. We were the 2nd ones to stamp in the
new log books.
Both had been rehidden rather poorly. Trout was right in the open
behind the log, no piece of wood, so we grabbed one from the other
side of the trail & recovered it. We met up with Mini Bob's Wife HH
from OR there.

Sorry to say, but the Elementals have not fared so well.

Fire Elemental:
Was right where she should be & we were really excited to see this
MOST AWESOME!!! stamp.

Earth Elemental:
Couldn't wait to get to this next one however, the box, pad & stamp
were missing. The log book in an open bag was just sitting on top of
the stump it should have been behind. We looked ALL around for any
trace of the missing items, Mike even went over the edge & all the
way down to the bottom of the ravine. When he came across a log that
a bear or some other very large animal had ripped to shreds & had dug
a large den out below he decided to high tail it back up to the top.
We brought the soggy log book home with us & it's sitting here drying
now. My daughter wants me to photo copy the original stamp from
inside the cover so we can atleast have it in our books. After that
I would like to return it to Mischief. Contact me with how to do
that & I can mail it or... We live in Shoreline & since we've
already found many of your boxes & seen your stamp in others I'm
assuming you're from around here.

Air Elemental:
Here it gets real weird. Air stamp, pad & log book were in place,
however they were in Water Elemental box & slightly wet, we dried
everything off & put it back. Another great stamp. I must say these
were my favorite stamps ever, but I'm really into fairies.

Water Elemental:
Definately missing. We found the flat rock & the geocache right next
to the rock that had no box anywhere around, let alone under it. The
geocache log book had many references to the box , stamp & wondering
what it was. Have to say it didn't give us any warm & fuzzy feelings
about geocachers. How can they not know about us when we all know
about them?
Well all in all it was a great trail & total of 4 really awesome
stamps plus a HH. Also we found a Mystery Box at another stop
earlier. Another great day of letterboxing.


PS We found The Salmon Derby Mystery box last weekend & it was a
great find with fun clues.

Patty
