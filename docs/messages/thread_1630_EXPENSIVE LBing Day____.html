From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-09-20 23:31:12 UTC-07:00

Okay I realize before I say this it may cause some controversy, but hey,
that's what you count on me for right?

So here's the situation the way I see it. I really think that if a letter
boxer knew a certain area was prone to break-ins, burglaries, murders,
rapes, and (insert another nasty things that could happen here) - they
probably would not put a letterbox in that given place. I think it is very
important to remember however that most things vary on time and
circumstance. It is impossible for the placer to warn you about everything
that you may encounter when searching for a letterbox. There is no way they
could write a warning that long, that is the basic purpose of the disclaimer
at the bottom of all the clues, because the reality is that it is your
responsibility to take responsibility to make sure that you are acting and
behaving in a manner that is most effectively safe.

A couple years ago a new letter boxer set off on a letter box that said it
was an easy half mile hike of rock hopping and creek crossing. That new
letter boxer figured she could walk a mile in an hour so this box would take
all of 30 minutes. Set out for the box with an hour of daylight left. She
didn't take that rock hopping and creek crossing warning too seriously, wore
regular shoes instead of ones that might have been safer when wet. She also
misinterpreted the clues and had trouble finding the box. - That poor new
letter boxer got stuck on the trail after dark and had to hike out over the
rocks and creek with an ankle that was broken in three places. When she was
done, she went on the list and blamed the placer for not preparing her
better.

Two years later that same letter boxer is sitting here telling you all that
it was her own lack of responsibility that caused everything that happened
that day to happen.

I'm not saying that anyone did anything irresponsible here, but I am saying
that asking or expecting the placer to warn you about any problems you might
have in the area is an unreasonable expectation. Perhaps that box was
planted two years ago and crime in the area has tripled since then. Perhaps
the crime in the area goes up after 5pm and the box was planted at 7am and
the placer has no idea. Perhaps you were just unlucky and got caught in
random acts of someone committing crimes that day. Who knows? But the
placer surly didn't know or the box probably wouldn't be there to begin
with.

Just something to keep in mind :)
Pandora
