i went out with a friend to carkeek the other day to show him what
this letterboxing thing was all about. we started with this letterbox:
http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.asp?boxnum=3761&boxname=Carkeek
(not the salmon series or the fairy tale series). i see the site says
that it has been abandoned. well, the whole trail has been maintained
and so almost all of the clues are irrelevant. i did find the log
that's mentioned at the end but there are so many tree limbs and
other plant matter from them cleaning up the trail piled there that
finding the box was impossible. i'm sure it's gone. has anyone else
found it or been by? what's the best way of dealing with this? does
anyone know who originally created this box, as it no longer says on
the website? it should probably be removed, considering how much the
trail has been changed.

on a lighter note, the salmon series was great! we had a great time
and loved the clues. we even bumped into our first geocacher! and
tried to convert them! it was a group of 4 kids plus their dad with a
gps. i had seen them near the 2nd box and then they were near the
3rd. he asked me whether i had found "it" yet, although when i told
him we were letterboxing, he had never heard of it. i normally don't
mention what i'm doing to random strangers but i felt sorry for the
kids, trailing behind the dad. after we found the 3rd box (loaded
with geocacher's finding it by accident amd it was not at all hidden)
we wandered back towards the sounds of their voices. i've never seen
a cache - i was curious. as we got closer, all of the kids were on
the trail and they accosted me with questions. what is letterboxing?
do you have to tromp through the woods? are there spider webs? you
get the picture. they were all under 10 and were not at all having
fun geocaching. dad was tromping around in the woods off the trail,
obviously very irriated at not finding it with the quickly setting
sun. i showed the kids the stamps we had found and told them about
it - they were very excited about it, for obvious reasons. i hope
that they have a chance to get involved with it, rather than standing
around bored on the trail, asking dad every 2 minutes whether he had
found it yet...

can i say that i've been out letterboxing 5 times in the past 7 days
with 4 different people? i've only just begun but am very addicted!
the next challenge will be to hunt for the black rock desert mystery
box... wooboy. does THAT look challenging or what?!
http://www.mapsurfer.com/boxes/box22.html

- laura aka. starfish.
