Hi All!

I'm finally checking the list. I had gone no mail while we were in
Canada and have been so busy the past week that I didn't get back to
Daily Digests and reading past posts until today, finally.

I am thankful to Jane, Chris, Pamela and Wes for hosting the First
Canadian Thanksgiving! And though, we were brief in our visiting, we
were glad to see Team Garlic, Funhog, Iron Bear and Pandora, Ryan,
Bethany, Regan, and Brennan. I'm probably forgetting some folks. I did
see some new faces I didn't recognize and I'm sorry I didn't get to meet
you!!! We didn't even exchange with anyone. Sorry about that!

The gatheing was soggy, but very enjoyable! It was great to see you
all. We did get there ver late, but managed to get a couple boxes after
all. The rain was pouring so hard that I didn't really want to
letterbox too much and ruin Ryan's excellent event stamp in my logbook.
Instead of letterboxing we went into Ft. Langley to enjoy their
Cranberry Festival. It was obvious the rain stopped no one from coming
to that, as it was quite the well-attended affair.

And thanks to Iron Bear, Pandora, Funhog and Ryan for allowing us to get
together with them in the evening! It was wonderful to get a real
visit in under drier conditions!!!

And, yes, the kayaks did come off the car. Sunday we went into
Vancouver and put in on Granville Island and paddled all over False
Creek, dodging water taxis, and aqua busses and just having a giggly
good time paddling in an absolutely beautiful city!

We did go back to Derby Reach on Monday to place and get one more
letterbox before heading back to Oregon. The weather was MUCH kinder to
us on Monday and we had a very pleasant time! I tried to post Fraser
Valley Fall and Ft. Langely Visit letterboxes, but the database came
back with an error so I will have to try again when I get to a "real"
computer.

On Wednesday Carie of Lennens 3 and I went out to get the magnificent
"No Name Callng, Please". We make a pretty good team, as she figures
out the nasty mind-bending aspects of Der Mad Stamper's puzzling clues
and I manage to navigate and direct us where we need to go once the
clues are figured out. Friends are a GOOD THING!!!

It's so wonderful to see Der Mad Stamper back in action with his carving
talents! No more bogus foam stamps and once again we're all able to
take his glorious name in vain actively once again when "Der Mad
Stumper" places!!! Yowza!

Best regards,

Amanda Briles
