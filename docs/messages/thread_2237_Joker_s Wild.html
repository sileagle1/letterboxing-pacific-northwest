Joker's Wild

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2005-09-25 17:18:42 UTC-07:00
Well my friends, the stamps are carved, the log book made, the prizes
bought. It's time to gather! Next weekend is the Joker's Wild Letterboxing
Poker Run. As of right now there are about 50 people scheduled to be there.



We will meet at 9:30 in the first large picnic area to the left of the road
as you come in the gate. I'll have the LBNA banner up, so you can look for
it Be sure to wear long pants, Bookworm and I ran into plenty of stinging
nettles as we were planting boxes.



There will be a total of 87 boxes in the park that day! The 53 for the poker
run will be there for that day only. Check LBNA for the rest :-) There are
several picnic tables, but you may want to bring camp chairs if you have
them. The fun starts at 9:30 with the poker run portion of the day ending at
noon, with potluck lunch to follow. The afternoon will be open for
exchanging, chit chatting, and hunting the rest of the boxes in the park.



There's still time to RSVP!



Announcing the first ever
Joker's Wild Letteboxing Poker Run!

When?
~October 1st, 2005
Register and get your packet from 9:30 to 9:55 AM. The poker run begins at
10:00 exactly. You DON'T want to be late!

Where?
~Oxbow Regional Park in Gresham, Oregon. Exact location within the park to
follow as the date gets closer.

What is a Poker Run?
~Definition: A Poker Run is an organized motorcycle event where riders
travel over a prescribed course and at designated stopping points, select a
card. At the end of the run, the person with the best poker hand wins a
prize. But we decided to change it a bit and make it a letterboxing event.
53 cards will be hidden in the park. At 10:00 you will receive clues to the
cards. Without knowing which card is at which location, you have to find 7
cards and make the best hand you can in two hours.

Are there prizes?
~Of course!

Is it kid friendly?
~You bet.

What about lunch? I'm going to be hungry after finding all those boxes!
~We will have a potluck. Bring a main dish or salad to share along with
beverages for your family. We'll provide paper products and dessert!

Can I stay the night?
~Oxbow's campsites are open year-round and available on a "first-come,
first-served" basis. Maximum occupancy is six people and two cars per site.
Firewood is available for purchase at $4 per bundle. Camping fee is $15 per
night. There is also a $4 per vehicle entrance fee to the park.

Please RSVP to Maiden at maiden1974@verizon.net or at Atlasquest under
gatherings. We hope to see you there!

****You do not need a motorcycle to attend this event. All searching will be
done on foot!****
