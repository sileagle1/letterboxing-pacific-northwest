An Invitation to a Box in Honor of the Nail Patriarch

From: Nail Family (c.nail@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-04-02 01:52:59 UTC
Today, April 1 marks the anniversary of the departure of my late
father, Robert Nail.
Last summer the Nail Family placed the Titlow Beach Letterbox
in West Tacoma in memory of him the same day we spread his
ashes under the Narrows Bridge. Many of you local letterboxers
have visited the box. My thanks to Bethany, Fudrick, Hoopoe, Bill
and Evil Glenn (still waiting for the video of the float test by the
way), Sunseeker and DZ Dezi, Mischeif, Amanda, and His
Emminence- Ryan for paying tribute. Even a few travellers from
the other coast visited.
About a month ago it was reported that the log book was soaked
(probably by a high tide) and we were quickly able to replace it.

I would like to take this opportunity to invite those who have not
visited the box to please do so in honor of a very good man. He
is greatly missed. Here is a link to the clues for the Titlow Beach
Letterbox. http://www.letterboxing.org/wa/nail.htm#titlow

The Nail Family will be having a special toast to him with his
favorite beverage, chocolate milk, this evening. If you feel so
inclined, please have a glass as well

Chuck Nail
Nailhead of the Nail Family

When I was in Guatemala, my Spanish teacher and myself wandered down
to a cemetery to look around, and one of the interesting things she
told me was about a tradition that the indiginous people practice.
After a loved one passes away, they'll occasionally go back being the
favorite food of whoever it was that passed away and have a picnic
with it. So if the deceased loved chicken, the family members would
bring chicken. If the deceased love bread, they would bring bread.

I told my teacher that I didn't see any chicken bones or bread
crumbs, but I did see a lot of beer cans laying around!

I'll let you draw your own conclusions, though. =)
