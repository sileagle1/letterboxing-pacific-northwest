> Message: 10
> Date: Thu, 01 May 2003 01:40:20 -0000
> From: "dvn2rckr"
> Subject: WA adventures & mis-adventures today...
>
> Hi--another wild letterboxing day in WA!
>
> ***Adventures***
>
> We set out to find Mo's 5 new boxes today down in Lacey, WA. We
> found them all in great shape! What cute little stamps and boxes,
> too! The Carrot was a milestone for us as it was our 350th find!!!
> Thanks Mo for a great box--we'll have to celebrate with
> steamed/glazed carrots this evening! ;) Lois Lake Park was really
> neat--it's crazy, we've driven all around Lacey over the past
> several years but have never seen that particular park before.
> That's why letterboxing is so cool--it takes you to new and
> interesting places around your town that you never knew about!
>
> Just as we finished finding the 3 boxes there, an adult Bald Eagle
> swooped right down over us and then took off to the tree tops. What
> an incredible sight! Unfortunately, I couldn't get my camera out
> quick enough to get a good picture.
>
>
> ***Misadventures***
>
> We then headed out to Wonderwood Park (a park we've visited about 4
> times in the last 3 weeks prior to today). We soon find the
> Pencil. Then, we wander around to find the Robot. Just as we are
> looking under the log for the letterbox, an armed police officer
> calls us from about 20' away asking us to leave the park ASAP!
> Seems they had had a report of an armed hostage taker somewhere
> within the park's grounds. So, we didn't even get a chance to sign
> in (at first). We hightail it out of the park but have to hang out
> at the baby's playground because our car was on the other side of
> the park. We notice half a dozen police sweeping through the park
> with rifles in hands. Wow! We don't know if they found anything
> during their sweep--I figured if there was something, we'd hear
> about it in the news. About an hour later, we were given the all
> clear and headed back to the box to stamp in. Talk about an
> adventure! Pt Defiance Park, Priest Point Park, Wonderwood Park,
> etc... Are we just police activity magnets or what? I'm really
> thinking about changing our handle... By the way, Mo we really
> enjoyed your stamps there, too!
>
> dvn2r ckr, rnrB & trkr
