From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2003-06-11 16:15:31 UTC
...yeah, but.... Don't forget what Brett said. I would have sworn on a
hundred dollar bill that Mt Tabor was missing when I first looked for
it. Same with Blue Lake and Multnomah Falls. I know Hidden Dragon has
stymied many folks. Right now everyone seems to think Beneath the Blue
#2 is missing but I doubt it. I am one of the people who failed to
find Cedar Hills Twin #1 ages ago. I couldn't believe it when a recent
post reported finding it. I went back and there it was, not a single
entry for 2002! Who knows where that box was all that time??? All
these clues would have been pulled if searchers' reports were the
basis for removing clues.

Several of my boxes have been hidden in places that have changed
dramatically between the time hidden and the time a boxer went to look
for them. I have experienced several vanishing and appearing benches,
a grassy trail becoming a wetland, landmarks having been removed, the
entire tree that was the raison d'etre for a letterbox cut down. In
every one of these cases, the original clue was totally wrong, people
failed to find the boxes but the letterbox was still there.

I have the philosophy that any box I hunt may or may not even be
there. So be it. The only thing that I can imagine being an
appropriate addition to the clue page would be a warning that the
placer does not respond to emails regarding their box. Funhoh
