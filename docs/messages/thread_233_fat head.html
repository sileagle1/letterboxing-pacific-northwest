I'm gonna get all swelled up in the head from all the positive
feedback I've gotten on this list in the last couple of days about my
stamps. Many thanks for the kudos, you guys. Before I took up this
hobby/obsession I never knew I could carve a chunk of plastic into
something recognizeable, much less something people would actually
praise!

My first (not so hot...in fact, pretty darn crappy) stamps are still
out there. It's fun to see the progress. Anyone who thinks they can't
carve a stamp should see those. It just shows that anyone can do it.

For the benefit of any of those dreaded Least Coast spies that monitor
our sacred PNW List, I want to toot our horn out here. I think we are
really lucky that the originators of our local boxing scene,
Tom Cheney and Mitch Klink, were especially talented carvers and
clever clue writers. It raised the bar for the quality of our
letterboxes here. Most contain handmade stamps and many have
challenging clues. When I've gone boxing in New England, I have found
MANY store bought stamps, both as signatures and in the letterboxes.
All I can say is, "there's no place like home..." Funhog
