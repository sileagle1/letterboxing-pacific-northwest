Letterbox placements

From: buntraveler (AMRFZBNZ@CS.com) | Date: 2005-11-29 01:58:37 UTC
Thought I would start a topic. I live in Oregon and only started
boxing about 6 months ago. I have just a few boxes out which are
listed on AQ under Bun Traveler.
My first boxes were regular stamps but now I am placing my carved
stamps in all of them.

I would like to discuss placement of the letterboxes in the wild.

I have only found a few boxes. Have been out to several areas near my
home and only found a few because most of the listed boxes have been
stolen or damaged with nothing left in the containers.

Boxes have been placed on trails out in the open, in peoples yards out
in the open and along bike paths where others might come across it
easily. The owners of the boxes are not thinking about the total
picture here.
I am not sure if others realize how many people there are in this
world now that bike, hike and walk all over our states.

Unless you are planting in the deep woods your box is most likely
going to be found by just about anyone. Most of those people are
kids. Or young adults that might know better than to be distructive
but unfortunately they are.

Placement is very important as to someone visually watching you find
and replace as well. If you can see someone on a trail or in the
city---they can see you. Curiosity will make them go find out what
you just layed under some branches or set under a bridge.

Let's talk about how we can place our boxes out of site and in areas
where we can sit and take the time to stamp without having someone
walk right up on us or see us while we are doing the stamping.
How can we better hide these boxes?

Also at the this time I would like to mention containers. That can be
another discussion if you like. But it is a very good subject to talk
about as well.
What do you hide your stamp and journal in that won't get damp, moldy
or basically damaged by animals. Tupperware---plastic containers are
not the answer for the PNW. Let's put our heads together and start
thinking of a good way to make our boxes safer, last longer and be
long lasting while not littering up our environment at the same time.

Looking forward to the discussions if there are others that have been
thinking along the same lines.

Thanks

Bun Traveler






RE: [LbPNW] Letterbox placements

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2005-11-29 19:29:58 UTC-08:00
As a placer who has over 100 boxes hidden in this state and a few in other
states, I can say that I'm certain many people DO get the big picture and
that people DO think things through. Those same places that you are
"complaining" about, boxes placed on bike paths etc, are ones that were
placed there for a reason. There are thousands of letterboxers and
letterboxes in the United States and each has a different idea of what is
acceptable. It is true that some go missing sooner than others, but that's
the way of the world. Placers soon learn how to better camouflage boxes,
which trails will work and which ones will not. But, to open condemn
people's choices is not helpful.



I would rather see a discussion on what WORKS. How many boxes have you
placed, and what techniques have you used that you found worked? I
personally have boxes that have been out there for 3 years now, on a bike
trail, with no problem. I used small Tupperware type containers, hid them
directly along the edge of the trail where they could be retrieved without
to much fuss, and asked people to be discrete. So far so good.



As for the type of containers, it is much more about how the boxes get
sealed up than the type. I have boxes out there that are the cheap
disposable sandwich containers that have held up just fine for 3 years.
Books are nice and dry and cozy.



Thanks for the discussion,

Maiden
