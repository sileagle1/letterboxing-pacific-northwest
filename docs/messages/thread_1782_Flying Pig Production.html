From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-11-29 04:42:52 UTC

The sagebrush beckoned this holiday weekend. This morning,
Birder and I hit the road in search of the perfect spot for our
first collaboration. We left two new letterboxes lurking in the
desert in a wonderful remote location. Hopefully, this will only
be the first of our joint ventures. Mountain bluebirds flashed
their lovely colors for us, a good omen for sure.

Being a long weekend, I got to spend time three days in a row
tending to letterboxing pleasures in Central OR. I tracked down
the latest offering from the Fleshmans and hid another box at
Smith Rock on Saturday. While checking on my Mariposa
Lily box there I found that it has had only two groups of
letterboxers come for a visit in over two years. C'mon over
the mountains... the skies are brilliant blue, the scenery
spectacular and the letterboxes abound. Funhog

We recently went to Paradise Lodge (Agness, OR) for our precious
treasures and discovered only 3 groups of Letterboxers had stamped it
in 2 years! So its kind of like the one Funhog mentioned. It is
really kind of sad too because as far as LBing goes, getting the
paradise lodge LB was some of the best fun I have had and by far some
of the most beautiful landscape, unbelievably gorgeous! I am
dissapointed so few have attempted this trek. Unfortunately for some
(and good for others) the only way to reach this place is by private
plane (yeah like we all got those), jet boat, or 24 (total) mile
hike. Well, we cheated. We only had to hike maybe a total of 6 (easy)
miles. So I sent the directions to the placer for the shortcut and so
maybe now they will see more LB'ers out there. It is soooo worth it!
Just beware of cowpies...free range cattle roam quite happily out
there (until our dogs took chase, mine had never seen a cow before, i
think he was in heaven). Oh and watch out for bears :o)
