Hi erfellie,
To answer your question, a cootie was something that Iron Bear,
Pandora and I cooked up for the Passport to Letterboxing Stamp Camp
last August as an ice breaker of sorts and something that the kids
could occupy themselves with. A cootie is a very small furry creature
letterbox that can only be passed on by hiding it on an unsuspecting
victim be it in their bag, pocket, hood of their jacket, etc. When
you were given cooties, you were to stamp into the logbook with a
thumbprint figure and pass it on to another unsuspecting victim.
The cooties turned out to be a big hit and they brought many
letterboxers that didn't know each other together to mingle and talk.
I think the kids enjoyed them more than the adults did because they
got to sneak around and plant them on someone.
Cooties are not to be placed in letterboxes because they tend to
reek havoc on areas that are too confining. I do beleive that they
are still out there somewhere hiding in someone's bag just waiting to
be spread around. Keep your eyes peeled. There may be one lurking on
some letterboxers that you meet on the trail!
