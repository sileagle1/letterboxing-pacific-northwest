From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-06-08 15:01:14 UTC
> Why would there be boxes with no published clues?

Sometimes, the boxes are in locations that the authorities don't want
them to be, and the placer doesn't want to adversize that fact.
Sometimes, the placer just doesn't want a lot of people finding the
box--especially if they feel it may be hazardous to the health of the
box. Othertimes people just want to do something a bit differently.
And for many letterboxers, it harkens back to the Dartmoor history
where letterboxers didn't have the Internet to distribute clues and
it was very secrative and undercover.

Basically, there's a lot of reasons someone might choose not to
publish clues. I've been to events where there are 'temporary boxes'
just for those that go to the event--no reason to publish those clues
since they won't last for more than the afternoon. Other boxes are
strictly for certain type of people--those with F counts over 100 or
500 or whatever the case may be--and you have to join the clue before
getting the clue.

I'm sure I've missed some reasons, but those are the ones I can think
of off the top of my head. =)

-- Ryan
