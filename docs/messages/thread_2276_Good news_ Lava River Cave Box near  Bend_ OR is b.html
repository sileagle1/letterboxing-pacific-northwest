Good news, Lava River Cave Box near Bend, OR is back!

From: Nail Family (the7nails@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-16 23:38:00 UTC
The Nail Family just returned from a week long vacation in the Sun
River area of Central Oregon. We had the opportunity while there to
check up on our three boxes that were placed in October of 2002. Two
of the boxes, the Lava Lands box and the Benham Falls Picnic Area box
were both intact with log books almost completely full. We added new
log books for future finders. (Incidentaly, both of these boxes had
several log entries from first time boxers. This does not surprise us
since both boxes are quite easily accessible and close to the highway.
Also, boxes listed under the Nail Family seem to appear to be rather
family friendly and often invite new boxers. It makes us feel very
good to have boxes that give good introductions to the sport and we
are very glad to have been able to update these boxes on this trip.)

The other box we had placed in the area, the famous Lava River Cave
Letterbox was sadly missing. The good news is that we have replaced
the box with a new and different themed stamp and log book back into
the original location. For all of you who tried to find the original
and were dissapointed that it was missing, now you have a reason to go
back. For those few of you who actually found the original box, now
you have a reason to go back too, a new stamp.

Look for clues to appear soon on the lbna and Atlas Quest sites for
another letterbox we placed on this trip in the same area as the other
three.

Also while we were in the area we visited several boxes at Shevlin
Park, solved a Paulina Mystery, and made a trip to the Crooked River
Ranch. Seems there are many more letterboxes in the Central Oregon
area than when we last visited 3 years ago! Hats off to 4DToes,
Birder, the Lennens, and Funhog for some very nice boxes.
Unfortunately, we found it difficult to wrangle the entire Nail Family
for more than a few boxes per day. In fact, we honestly ran out of
daylight at Crooked River Ranch and only found one box. Not a good
thing to be down in a Central Oregon canyon after dark with a bunch of
rusted Nails (get it, Nails who live in Western Washington) Oh, but
wait until next time.

Again, all you Central Oregon boxers, watch for the clues to our
latest box in the Sun River area coming soon to a clue list near you.

Nailhead of the Nail Family
