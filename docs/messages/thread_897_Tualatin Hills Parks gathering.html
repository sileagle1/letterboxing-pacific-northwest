From: (ARoseLB@aol.com) | Date: 2003-11-06 02:16:31 UTC-05:00
Count me in! Either Saturday, December 6th, or Sunday, December 7th, works
for me as of now. I look forward to meeting (and re-meeting) y'all.

I had written to Kyle Spinks about my Burntwood letterbox and following is
the answer he sent me:

Hi Annette,
I have been checking the boxes in our parks at a rate of 1-5 daily and
haven't quite gotten to yours yet :-))

My main concern is for the safety of the Letterbox placers and seekers.
Typically, I don't encounter many problems with Geocaches and I don't expect to
find many with Letterboxes. There are those times when I need to alert a
Geocache or Letterbox hider of an issue with their 'treasure,' so it's nice to have
a current email account. Usually it's been a Geocache that has been found and
stolen, but I've also found caches in poison-oak bushes or near steep banks.

The general rule-of-thumb I use is the answer to the question "Would you
trust your 5-year-old to seek the [Geocache or Letterbox] ALONE while you stood
off to the side?" I usually think about things such as animals or insects that
may bite, terrain that is hard to cross, trip hazards, eye-level
branches....you get the picture.

Thanks for helping me out. I don't foresee any problems with your Letterbox
(or many others, for that matter), but I'll contact you via email if anything
seems amiss. I'll be using my work email address (kspinks@thprd.com) if I do,
but you can contact me at either address.

Have a great day...and stay warm!

Kyle Spinks
