Patches-the home stretch

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2006-01-25 17:40:14 UTC
It looks as though the initial rush of patch ideas has dwindled. I
will be creating a poll this weekend for everyone to select their
favorite entry. If you want to edit your post or add a new one, now's
the time to do it. Sunday, Jan 29 will be the last day for new or
altered pictures to be entered. The Poll will be posted on Monday and
will run for two weeks.

Some of the current suggestions are great drawings but may have too
much detail to work well as a patch. They might have to be altered
somewhat to be useable. Also, if your design can use a solid
background color with embroidery on top of it, it would keep the price
down. Up to seven colors with 50% embroidery is the least expensive.
The pricing then goes up at 75% and 100%.

Have a look at the links on Silent Doug's message #6310 on this list
to see what groups in other parts of the country have created. The
round ones are all 2.5 inches in diameter and the others are roughly
the same size. Although, I of course, think our area is by far the
best in the country, I think it would be a bit cheeky to make our
patches larger than everyone else's. Besides, Amanda has so many of
the dang things on her backpack, she'd probably have trouble finding
an extra square inch of space to put a large patch on.

Keep 'em coming. We need your contributions...

Re: Patches-the home stretch

From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2006-01-26 04:30:13 UTC
> Regarding price, I personally would prefer to pay more for a beautiful
> albeit detailed patch than try to cut costs by eliminating detail
> and/or color.

Besides the fact that simplier images are easier to turn into patches,
as a whole, they also tend to look better.

Consider some of the most 'powerful' symbols out there from the US
flag to the Nazi flag. Extremely simple, both of them, and when people
see them, it brings up strong emotions. Nobody ever looks at the
California flag and thinks, "Woah!" (I'd use Oregon or Washington as
an example, but those flags are so boring, I don't actually remember
what they look like. If it weren't for growing up in California, I
probably wouldn't even remember the California flag.)

But California's flag has a lot more details which, ultimately, takes
away from the 'symbol' it's supposed to represent. Heck, even the LbNA
logo is elegantly simple. If you try to fit in everything that's
representative of the Pacific Northwest--desert, salmon, Native
Americans, Oregon Trail, orcas, coffee, and whatever else you want to
throw in, it's going to be one ugly looking patch.

Simple is good. It's good for patches. It's good for us. =)

-- Ryan, getting off soapbox

After Ryan's butt chewing, I added another patch idea that I
worked on today. This one is more in line with the 'traditional' LBNA
patch concept as far as the lettering, colors (although my second hand

get it to look 'balanced'.

stick with a simple patch. We need bold, vivid colors with strong
images that send a powerful message to non-letterboxers the world
