From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-23 17:16:51 UTC
Like GG said, Cougar Mountain is teeming with boxes that require beyond
the typical effort hikes. Do your homework and you'll find even more
boxes there than meet the eye. Also, try for Rattlesnake Ledge, any of
the Mt Si boxes and even the Denny Creek Primitive series--these are
all east of the Issaquah area pushing into Snoqualmie Pass off of I-90.

For other interesting hikes in the region, try Mt St Helens--beautiful
vistas but it might be pretty hot this time of year. Again do your
homework and you might find some of those long lost treasures out there.

Otherwise, there are some great hikes & views around the Olympic
Peninsula--some on NPS grounds and many others that aren't. Again,
Cape Flattery is absolutely spectacular and very much worth the trip &
the $7 reservation hiking permit that's required to access the trails.
Another area that was neat was Salt Creek County Park off the beaten
path between Port Angeles and Cape Flattery. Not sure if the letterbox
is still there but the vistas along the hike and the tidepooling we did
at the beginning of the trail was so worth the effort!

Another neat area is Whidbey Island--especially the Deception Pass area
and Ft Ebey SP. Good hiking throughout and incredible water mixed with
rugged coastline views.

Hope this helps your trip planning. Enjoy your time in the area,
daelphinus

Yes, we left about 10 days ago--kicking & screaming all the way. We're
in Boise for a few more hours and then it's back on the road and beyond
the ethernet for a few more weeks. We're National Park & Monument
hopping our way across the US--camping most of the way. ;)

We're trying to letterbox a bit between here & there but our success
rate hasn't been so hot. For the last 11 attempts over 3 days in SE
Oregon & NE California, we found 2 letterboxes and about 15 geocaches
(mostly virtual/earthcache types as they were in 'hot' lands and many
of the LBs we searched for were status unknown as it was to begin
with). Otherwise the trip has been incredible so far!!!

Somehow we turned a 550 mile drive from Tacoma to Boise into a 1,300
mile, 10-day, 4-state adventure. Go figure! ;) I'd blog it but the
internet access as well as cell phone coverage is too sparse in the
locales we keep finding ourselves in. ;) We'll catch up on the flip
side, though. We also have our new baby (DSLR camera) with us so we're
having a blast learning how to use all its nifty features. Thank
goodness it doesn't need diaper changes. ;)

Have a great trip to the PNW!!! It'll be awesome!
