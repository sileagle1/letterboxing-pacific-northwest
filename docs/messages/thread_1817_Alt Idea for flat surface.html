Alt Idea for flat surface

From: Judy B (sowbiz@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-22 14:09:30 UTC

I like the 'elephant' idea for compactness and had made the following
for my Baker's Dozen trip to Dartmoor - thought it would be rainy
everyday.

At craft store get a wood 'cigar box' that they show for making a
purse -- forget the handle and other do dads.
I did paint it up (actually I printed all the LBNA site logos and
decoupage them on). In side I added a divided lengthwise on the
bottom half. A compartment holds 4x6 cards or log book, to the right
a compartment holds my stamp and prestamped labels for speed
stamping. The top half holds several color pens and two small ink
pads. For the inside top half I made a divider with an
acetate/plastic sheet across the front to hold the 4x6 cards after
they were stamped -- but the gorrilla glue didn't hold and I was too
rushed to get packed to do it up right. So scotch tape came into
play for a temp hold.

I can stamp either on the outside of the box, or if it is rainy, I
use one of the color pens to support the lid (like the bar for the
car hood?) and stamp inside the box, keeping everything dry. Of
course if it is a driving rain, adding some side vinyl panels to drop
down would be another feature.

If I get a chance I'll post a pic --- after the Xmas letters are
printed!!

Have a Happy Holiday, and may you all get your letterboxing wishes.

Judy B
