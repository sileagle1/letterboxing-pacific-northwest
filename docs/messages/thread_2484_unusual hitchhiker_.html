unusual hitchhiker?

From: troutehole (seatroute@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-11 20:36:40 UTC
I have a crazy idea and need input as to whether it will work, and
validation with ideas if everyone thinks it will work...

My daughter is graduating high school next month and will be off to
college. I want to make a letterbox in honor of this big life event
but with a twist.

Kristin wants little more than to be a world traveller -- during and
after her education. What do you think of a hitchhiker that, in
addition to a tradtional log book etc.., asks finders to send my
daughter a postcard from wherever the hitchhiker turns up? I think
she would get a big kick out of receiving cards from all over the
world while she's stuck studying for finals.

Would this work? Starting the hitchhiker on a path that increases
its chances of traveling long distances is important to its
successs. Anyone have any suggestions on how to do that?

Thanks in advance for any advice,

troutehole

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2006-04-12 19:49:28 UTC
I once received a PLB that the logbook was a bunch of postcards.
You could send out the PLB, have the participants add a postcard
from their hometown or maybe somewhere that they have traveled,
stamp the postcard with their signature stamp and write up a little
blurb about their card, and send the PLB on to the next recipient.
That way, she will get a box full of postcards all at once?
