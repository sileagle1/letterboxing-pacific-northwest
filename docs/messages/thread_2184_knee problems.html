Hi Everybody,
A word of warning - I'm having trouble going down hill too. Actually,
any time my knee is bent and bearing weight. Finally went to the Dr.
and found out I have what they call Femoral Patellar syndrome (pretty
common) - the knee cap is tilted and way off to the outside grinding on
cartilage instead of gliding nicely in the center. This is caused by
the big outer thigh muscles being much stronger/tighter and shorter
than the inner leg muscles so I am doing Physical Therapy
(strengthening of inner and stretching of outer) but it maybe too late
for me - the cartilage/meniscus eventually gets so roughed up and
stringy that it gets caught/pinches in the joint. I imagine massage
would also help too.
Anyway, don't be too tough or stubborn or procrastinate - get it looked
at before you need surgery!
