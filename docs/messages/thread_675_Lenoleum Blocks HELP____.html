Re: Lenoleum Blocks HELP!!!!

From: hoopoe4me (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2003-09-06 04:18:00 UTC
I 100% agree, this is the media for me. Economical. They even have
seconds, which have some flaws in it but is still carvable. It
carves well and you can get a lot of stamps from one sheet.

The Michaels in my old neighborhood never had any carving media,
which is what drew me to on line shopping. I have tried the pink
stuff ( nice but hard to find) Master Carve (too soft / crumbly ) and
then PZ Kut ( great for detail)

I have not tried Lino blocks for a long time. I am not sure how they
hold the ink that most people use.

I think you have to try them all before setting on our favorite.

Redmond WA

From: Twilight (twilight2001@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-09-06 16:52:47 UTC-07:00
i love the PZ Kut as well -- but even more economical is the White Stuff
I get at local art shops -- i get a 12 X 12 square of this stuff for
about 9 bucks -- about 3 sheets of the PZ Kut wide when I lay them on
each other.

It's a lot heavier and more durable -- great for detail, but harder to
carve than the PZ Kut. I use both -- but the White Stuff is cheaper.
Don't have any idea what it's real name is -- I just ask for carving
media at the local art store and this is what they give me...

Twilight
