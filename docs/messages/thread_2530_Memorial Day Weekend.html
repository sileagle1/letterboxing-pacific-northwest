Memorial Day Weekend

From: doubletreepfx (greenthummm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-05-29 02:19:05 UTC
Having heard on the radio last Wednesday that all reserved campsites
in the Oregon State Parks system were full for the Memorial Day
weekend, I proceded to call the reservation hotline anyway, and
managed to secure the prime dates of Thursday, Friday and Saturday
nights at Fort Stevens State Park with no problem. Exactly the
nights we wanted, exactly the park we wanted, and got a site with
electrical hookup too. (Compact space heater for the tent, griddle
and coffee pot, and we were happy).

Clamming was not the best, but not the worst either. Fortunately,
after getting near to our limits each morning, there was plenty of
time the rest of the day for boxing!

Amazingly, each time I found a box, the rain had stopped. Nary a
drop of water got on a logbook. Between this and getting the
reservations we wanted at the last minute, I really should have
bought a lottery ticket.

Here is the rundown on boxes that we found in the county:

Sunset -- dry, plenty of room in logbook, well-hidden.
Horsin Around -- also in excellent condition
Ecola -- logbook nearing full, is located with the geocache
Heritage Spruce -- well-hidden, dry, plenty of room in logbook
Northwest Oregon -- ditto
Dedicated -- dry, log book nearing full
Battery 245 -- very good hide. Dry, plenty of room in log.
Fort Clatsop -- dry, log nearing full
HIJMS Submarine I-25 -- ditto

I was VERY impressed with the quality of the stamps I found -- among
the best I've seen. The owners will be glad to know that every
single box I found was dry and sound. Thanks to all of them for
great carvings and thoughtful placements.

Double Tree

From: Lisa Harkins (lisah@whidbey.com) | Date: 2006-05-30 08:43:49 UTC-07:00
We also had a lovely weekend at the Oregon Coast, though much further south,
and planned much further in advance. We based out of Waldport KOA and had
an absolutely beautiful, though wet, set of days boxing. Wish I could have
gotten to more of them, but we'll definitely go back. It was an interesting
tally of rescues, replenishments, and odd circumstances. Seems good to list
them here, since it will be a while before I can get all the finds listed
and the owners contacted. Here's our tally.

Yahoo! Kernville - funny box in an interesting place with great birding
views into the adjacent protected area. Wish we'd had more duct tape, as
it's peeling off the box.

Beneath the Blue #3 by Funhog - AWESOME box and a lovely few hours spent at
Cape Meares

We also looked for the Cape Meares one by Birder, and it was listed as
possibly missing. We also were unable to find it.

D-Lake by Rat Pac - cutest little tiny park and a stamp that reminded Legend
of her favorite childhood story.

Boiler Bay by Hedgehog - log book was soaking wet, so we left a new one and
brought the old one home for drying out.

Cape Lookout by Birder/Calli K - We definitely need to come back here. This
was a fun box in a gorgeous area.

Also looked for the one on the Cape Lookout trail by the Rat Pac. Didn't
find it, but I'm sure it was because of us. We were so taken away by the
scenery that we couldn't find the right landmarks.

Darlingtonia Californica - I couldn't believe how interesting this little
park was. Thanks so much Pungent Bob for the suggestion to go there. The
log book was soaking wet, and there was a pink rubber ball in the container.
That didn't seem like a good sign, but the stamp was still there. We left a
new log book, took the wet one home to dry out, and buried the box a little
deeper in the duff in hopes the Geocachers don't find it again. The clue
says it's under the end of the log. We found it under a piece of bark on
bare ground next to the log.

Heceta Head by Green Tortuga - in great shape. Logbook was full. We left
another one, and also left the original there. So gorgeous.

Hobbit Trilogy by Heron A Foot- we had time to find 2 of the 3 before it got
dark. Such a gorgeous hike (is there an echo in here?)

Chris the Crab at Cape Foulweather - such a gorgeous (ous ous ous ooouus)
hike (ike ike ike iiiike), and we were glad to enjoy it on the one nice,
sunny day of the weekend.

Beneath the Blue #1 by Funhog - this (and the Hobbit Trail) were the two
non-negotiable hikes I had on my list the entire weekend. We were running
so late when we got there, but I insisted we go. We thoroughly enjoyed way
too much time on this hike, and the consequence was that we missed the last
ferry onto the island and had to drive around, so I got home at 4am this
morning, and dude, I am SO tired. But it was memorable, and thanks so much
Funhog for the trip. The log book was nearly full. I left a new one and
left the old one there because I know you like to leave the old logs in
place for people to read.

Drift"wood" Falls by WildHair - also a wonderful box in a great hiding spot
at a lovely rest in the trail. Also full log book (1 page left) so we left
another log book. Left the old one in place.

Strawberry Hill Wayside - I actually found this one last year, but just
checked on it to see how it was. Good thing I did. The box had a missing
lid, was wrapped in leaky bags, and the log book was completely soaked. We
gave it a new plastic box, new baggies, and a new log book, and brought the
old one home for drying.

Good thing we visited the very interesting Marine Science Center in Newport.
Such a great thing to do on a rainy day. They had these intersting little
"Boku" books for $1.25 each. We bought a handful and used nearly every one
replacing wet and full log books. Great find!

Thanks everybody for the wonderful weekend.

Thunderbird
