The evolution of a letterboxer's logbooks:

I started out with rather expensive, unlined logbooks. Looked very
good, except it started to become rather expensive with the number
of boxes I was hiding.

So then I degenerated to using lined memo pads for awhile. It
seemed like everyone else was using them anyhow, so no big deal.

As a bonus--maybe this was just my imagination--but it seemed that
in the lined books, people actually WROTE more. With the unlined
versions, people were more likely just to stamp their stamp and
continue along. With a lined book, people wrote about the weather,
who they were with, experiences along the way. It was really a lot
of fun reading those logbooks.

Then I started finding home-made logbooks. What an interesting
concept, I thought. Some were quite good. Some were--well--not. I
must give this a try sometime. Biggest problems I realized with
hand-made logbooks--they usually didn't have enough pages in them!

Now I'm generally making very cheap, very easy, unlined logbooks.
And I always put far more pages than I possibily think would ever
get used in the next couple of years. There's a certain lined pad
I've found at the Dollar Store a couple of times that I really,
really like, though, and will buy them out every chance I get. Six
for a buck and small enough to fit into all but the smallest
microboxes. I like those pads!

Then I find a box like Lemoney Snicket and HOLY CRAP!!!! Makes me a
little ashamed of my crappy little hand-made books. *walk of
shame* That S.O.B. is trying to make the rest of us look bad!

Who knows where the next few years will take me, but I've been
inspired to new heights yet again! =)

-- Ryan

From: Danny McMillin (macdanny@mindspring.com) | Date: 2004-07-29 20:07:53 UTC-07:00
Well now I'm in quite a quandry. I started out using unlined artbooks and
little journals for my letterboxes. Then for convinience sake, I started
getting lined memo pads when I was in a hurry and couldln't get to the art
store. I now have them in a couple of my letterboxes. Having seen lined
memo pads in other folks' letterboxes, I thought it was no big deal. I will
make an effort to get back into the unlined swing of things.

Late Saturday night, my letterboxing friends, my wife and I decided to
climb Little Mt. Si near Northbend, Washington. I got up at 5:30 am on
Sunday morning and whipped out a hand carved stamp. I dug around my
letterboxing supplies and all I had was a lined memo pad. I quickly put
together a letterbox with all its various supplies and markings. Then out
the door we went. The letterbox was placed with its less than perfect
contents. I am planning on a series of letterboxes on the way up Little Si.
Next time up, I'll replace the memo with a better journal. Whew. Who knew?!

On the plus side, I double bagged my logbook after reading Amanda from
Seattle's post. I also added the silica packets someone suggested to keep
moisture out as well. We have lots of them at my work.

I've seen some of the hand made logbooks from folks on this list and they
rock. Perhaps I too will strive for those lofty goals. But in a pinch, ya
gotta go with whacha got, eh?
