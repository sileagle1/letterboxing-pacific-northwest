I know I'm preaching to the choir here but I just got back from
checking on a letterbox that has survived a LONG, LONG time and found
its logbook destroyed because recent visitors failed to seal the
logbook's bag and they failed to double-bag the logbook (despite the
bags being clearly labeled). Please ensure you close the bags on
letterboxes you visit. If you introduce folks to this great hobby of
letterboxing please remind them that the plastic bags are in the boxes
for a reason--especially ones whose clues clearly indicate that the
letterbox is subject to fluctuating tides.
