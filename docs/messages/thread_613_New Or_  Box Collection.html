A new set has begun at Rood Bridge Park in Hillsboro Oregon, it has
been posted on the National site for those interested.

I plan to keep adding to these as time goes by.... Enjoy!




Rood Bridge Park is located in Hillsboro Oregon. From TV Hwy, take
Witch Hazel Road and follow out. Right on River Road, Left on Rood
Bridge Road. Take a hard left at the High School as the road turns
and follow the road closure signs. The park will be on your left
right before the road closure. Please be advised that the park gates
close electronically at dusk, so don't get caught inside!

I am calling this a collection because I thought, instead of putting
all the boxes out at once, I would make random new additions as I go.
I walk here frequently, so check back, the clues may change and more
boxes will be "released" as time goes by. I have been told there were
boxes here at one point and that they tend to go missing, so some of
these I plant could even become "Limited Editions"! I will however
keep adding more.....

Most of these are fairly easy paths to follow, being paved and
barked. I have placed all boxes pushing my stroller through them! The
whole thing is approx 2 miles at this time.

To the boxes:

Start out at the path through the rhododendron gardens. Stay on the
black top as you head North. When you come to a circle in front of
you, look for the "horse shoe wall". Standing with the wall in front
of you, to your left is a gravel road heading down hill (@350) Take
this path. At the Y, take the road less traveled (N) Walk a ways
enjoying the scenery. Lots of rabbits and wildlife can be seen here.
On a cool day, early morning and evenings you may even catch a
glimpse of the local deer grazing. Eventually you will come to a
bridge. Keeping it to your left continue on at the bend as the path
doubles back. A cluster of trees, a clearing on the right and a
cluster of trees going into the bend. You are close now, approx 15
steps in and you will see a small log on the right. Carefully lifting
the bark, catch the rabbit before it hops away!

Continuing on this trail, it will lead you back the way you came, up
the hill and back to the pavement.

Heading straight on the black top, immediately to the left is another
bark trail, take this, then left into the woods at the blue "S". Got
stump? Continue on, ahead on the right a younger tree with it's
walking stick, it stands in front of another. Between the two lies a
stump, inside you may very well find a shy chipmunk.

After stamping in, continue on the way you were going... back out to
the pavement. Take a left here, going up, resist the temptaion to act
as a child.... Many can be seen laughing and rolling downhill in the
grass on a hot Summers day. At the next junction, go forward, then
left at the bench. At this point enjoy your walk, a bridge to your
left, your on the right track, up a hill then heading down, At the Y
in the path, choose right. When the paving ends, go right again on to
bark. To your left is a lily pad pond. My daughter loves looking for
frogs here, their little heads, just above the water at the shore
line, all you can see is eyes! You will come to a log across the
path, sit for a while, take a look around, and peek deep inside the
stump at the end, A frog rests, waiting here for you.

Continue on, back up to the left to the black top path. Go back up
and left at the bench, Left at the Y takes you over a bridge and back
up to the parking lot.

I hope you have enjoyed your tour, please check back often, as you
never know what creature may pop up weekly in adition here! - - - - -
Wingnut
