Well we went out for a quick trip to nab some of Pupp's boxes in the Dog
park last week here in Vancouver. My oldest daughter, Daisy, just loves
bugs, NOT. She lifted the mossy covered rock but to her eyes did behold a
black snake all coiled around the box. As she proceeded to jump back about
10 feet away from the box, came mom to the rescue. Now I am not fond of
those critters either, but I grabbed a stick and prodded the little feller.
It did not move so I assumed that it was dead. I got it carefully under the
box and nabbed the box out just in case it was alive. As we thought we were
out just for a bit I had on my sturdy sandals but my girls had on those
lovely flip flops. So needless to say if that snake came toward any of us
the whole town would have seen and heard us running away. Daisy then
proclaimed that she was not coming near us to stamp in. I told her that she
had to or would not get the stamp, I am so mean. I kept looking into the
hole to make sure that the snake was still there as we stamped in. It was,
for the most part of the procedure. Then low and behold it was gone. Here
we go again. I kept hearing the song "I don't like spiders and snakes"
going over and over in my head. So here goes Daisy again in the 10 foot
range of the rock. So be forewarned about this box in the open field where
all can see. Willow, Daisey and Heartsey.
