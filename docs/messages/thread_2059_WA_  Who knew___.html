It's actually USFS land, which borders NPS land, and the boxes show
up on the regional website that introduces visitors to the area--
sort of like a Chamber of Commerce site. Can't quite figure out who
is responsible for planting the boxes--the email address goes to
a 'joint' effort Quinault Rainforest entity. I'll check the boxes
out in a few days and try to meet the folks who put this together.
A couple of years ago I planted a box at a regional interpretive
center out in Ocean Shores. At first there was a little 'ire' at
the box being there but then when they realized that we spent almost
$200 at the visitor center store there, they welcomed the box & the
hobby with open arms. I'm wondering if somebody active in the
interpretive center in Ocean Shores encouraged folks in the Quinault
Rainforest to follow suit--as it will attract more visitors to the
region (that's been hit hard with the closing of several area lumber
mills in the past several years). They even sell 'starter kits' at
the Visitor Center so people can get started right away during their
visits. This is so cool that it's 'taking off' in the area. I was
tiring of 'maintaining' my boxes in the area each trip--it's nice to
have something else to look for.

On a side note, the local library network has also sponsored
letterboxes within their libraries for almost 2 years now--so that
may be another way the word spread to them.
