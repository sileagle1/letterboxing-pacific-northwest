Re: [LbPNW] Poll Issues

From: Kathy Gilbert (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-02-03 08:54:28 UTC-08:00
To have a run-off, the current poll doesn't have to change. I would be
in favor of a runoff after this poll ends and would suggest posting now
when the runoff will end, such as "we will have a runoff between the top
(2, 3, however many) choices beginning Feb. 16, voting will end Feb.
26. Gives people ten days to vote, they know when the vote will be
underway, and they can voice objections to that time frame now rather
than later (if perhaps they would be unable to vote in that second poll
for some obscure reason). Funhog, if you aren't interested in taking on
the hassle of setting up that poll and monitoring it, I would be happy
to do it if you don't object to the runoff. Seems like you have been
doing a lot of things here lately. This is the first on-line poll I've
ever seen or participated in, and when I first checked out the "rules" I
thought it kind of weird you could change your vote at any given time up
until the end. I figured that was one way of doing a run-off albeit not
the best way IMHO. I mean, if my first choice is way behind on Feb. 14,
I could easily just change my vote to one of the front-runners. Again,
this is not a very good way to do a run-off, but this ability to change
your vote leaves you that option. I am not dismayed at all with the
current poll, I think it's a perfect way to start. I would like to see
a runoff though.

>I see your points and if lots of others are dismayed by the current
>format, I'm more than happy to change things. However, this seems like
>how voting generally works. What do the rest of you think? Funhog

When the LbMA voted on patches, we had a run-off with the finalists.
This was partly because there were some designs that were variants on
the same theme (such as the same general design in different colors),
so we ended up voting on four separate patches/themes that were the
leading contenders in the first round.

If one patch gets a majority in the current poll, then there's no
need for a run-off, of course. But if the results are close, then
maybe you could consider a run-off poll with the top candidates.
