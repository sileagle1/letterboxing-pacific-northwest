Ref Kent LBs,

We recently looked for many of the Kent area letterboxes and they're
all great! Not sure of any missing--all the ones we looked for, we
found with little/no difficulty. Recommend she locate a good map of
the area and study it well before coming because traffic &
navigating the area for the first time can be a bear. If she's
looking for boxes mid-week, try to avoid I-5, Hwy 18 or Hwy 167
during rush hour time (7-10am and 3-7pm) as it can be AWFUL!!!
Also, since she's coming from the East Coast there isn't much
daylight around here after 4:30pm--if she can plan her outings for
early morning to early afternoon that would give her great 'natural'
lighting--otherwise, she should bring a flashlight. She may want to
look into the area Parks & Rec websites for maps to the various
parks before coming (print them up, too!)--some of the surface
streets can be a bit confusing. Oh, definitely wrap her clues in
ziplock bags, bring a raincoat/parka and/or umbrella to ensure she
has a dry place to stamp in. You never know this time of the year.

Hopefully, this will help. As for narrowing down boxes--she should
try to focus on topics in which she's interested or whatever types
of venues she's looking for. Some are along waterways, others in
neighborhood parks. If she wants flora/fauna, she should look for
the 'longer' outing ones to increase her opportunities to view the
local wildlife.
