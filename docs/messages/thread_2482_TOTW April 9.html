TOTW: What is the most odd discovery you have made while
letterboxing?

Hmmm...we've come across a bunch of weird stuff. Here's the blurb
about it from our AQ profile answer with some elaboration on the
experiences:

SWAT teams forced us off the trails--twice(Wonderwood Park in Lacey,
WA), police line(s) (multiple locales), crime scene (stolen purse
debris scattered about an entire hillside in Golden Gate Park in San
Francisco which we returned to the police), whizzing bullets in the
distance and cops that later showed up to investigate it (Priest
Point Park in Olympia, WA)--dead orca calf (Hole in the Wall,
Olympic Peninsula), horse skull (Auburn, CA), rattlesnake
(Ellensburg, WA), tarantula (Alum Rock Park in San Jose), dead sea
lion (Moclips, WA), mating elephant seals (San Simeon, CA)...Good
friend 14,000 miles away from the high school we attended 14 yrs
later (we were on Oahu & attended high school in Germany) & some
medieval outdoor adventure game (can't remember the name of it but
they link up via the internet somehow) players in Spanaway, WA--they
threw this 'grenade' at me not knowing I was there and were shocked
when they went to retrieve it seeing that we were there stamping
into a letterbox (the grenade was made out of stiff foam wrapped in
duct tape and they were all dressed in these dark 'duster' jackets
and gear)--not sure which experience ranks as the 'oddest' yet,
though...

Oh, two weeks ago we came across some other 'odd' stuff at Blue Lake
Park near Troutdale, OR--it involved a studded belt and Ralph Lauren
boxers. Yeeeeeee-uuuuuucccccckkkk!


Thankfully, the great experiences we've enjoyed in letterboxing far
exceed the number of odd ones. daelphinus
