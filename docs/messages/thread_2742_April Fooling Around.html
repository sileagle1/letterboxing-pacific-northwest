Greetings NW Boxers!

I'll be in Portland visiting family for a long weekend of March 30 to
April 3rd. My brother doesn't 'box but is very tolerant of working
them in when we're out and about doing other stuff. I think he
should especially tolerant this trip since April 1st is my birthday -
no fooling! which is the main reason for my visit.

Last time I visited we did a road trip to the coast and went to the
Portland Zoo where I managed to get all the boxes there except for
that pesky Packy's Place which was hiding out in the lost and found
office about the time we went looking for it.

I know I'm going to spend time trying for Ace of Fun and most likely
the Musician series boxes which will probably wipe out my
limited 'boxing time.

So....Does anyone have any boxes they'd like to suggest besides these
that are relatively easy to get to and which are public transit
friendly? I'll only have the full day to myself to box on Tuesday
4/3 but w/o a car, the rest will be fit in around family time - in
which case we'd be using a vehicle.

I'm SOOOOO jealous how many boxes you have in the Portland area...way
more than my time for this trip will allow. Any input mucho
appreciated - on the list or by email.

If anyone thinks they might be up for an exchange and coffee that'd
be cool too!

Cheers!

Denver, CO

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-03-07 01:01:01 UTC
Rats! I will be gone the entire time you're in town. I would've loved
to have gone hiking with you. I am just glad we got to meet in the
Fall. I think the Musicians Series is a great choice and will probably
fill much of your day. I do hope you will be adding one of your own
crafting while you're at it. It's become quite a gallery of carvers'
work, with fourteen boxes by nine different letterboxers.

I always think folks should try for the Spirit of Dartmoor letterbox.
It's one of the earliest in the country and is a great piece of our
local history. Der Mad Stamper is first rate when it comes to clues as
well as carving. Then there's his renown as a karaoke singer but
that's another topic.

Have a ball while you're here and catch me on the next visit. Funhog
