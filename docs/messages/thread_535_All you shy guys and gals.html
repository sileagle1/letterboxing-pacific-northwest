Come on down!

I loved our first get together last Fall. There I got some ideas
for stamping and logbooks. Seeing so many stamps and images in
books really gave me some feeling for developing my "crafty" side.
Being able to see the different kind of carving media helped me
decide that PZ Kut was for me.

By the time of the event I had logged about 40 LB's, most of them
over the long 4th of july weekend.. It was really tough learning
how to find boxes once I had got to the spot. Meeting so many
people with a common bond and -------no hidden agenda----- ( that is
not a pun!), like other activities I belong to was refreshing.

It was a chance to learn that others had the same challenges I have
when sluthing harder than average clues. dvn2r skr has since given
me some common sense tips for we of the Short Memmory Clan.

The food certainly helped too!. I will be bringing my Deli Buster,
Bethaney ( and some Rice Krispy Treats because I have a hankering
for them and so this is a great excuse)

The Greater Oly area has some good boxing venues with multiple
stamps. It is about time to consider Kennedy Creek and all the
Salmon running. That is a small venue with a wide range of
box "difficulty " levels from basic to Glennish ( I never really got
through those clues with my faulty compass, just stumbled upon the
poorly hidden box.)

At heart I think many of us are rather private and shy, I know I
am. Unless work really interferes, I will be there with my log
book, my stamp show and tell, some give away carving media and a HH
if I can come up with an animal expression that fits my theme by
then.

Oh, and food.
