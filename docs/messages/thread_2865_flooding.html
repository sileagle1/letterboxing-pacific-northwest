From: Kurious Jo (KuriousJo@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-12-11 01:11:16 UTC-08:00
We are just fine - thank you for thinking of us, Pilgrim. Although were
on the flat prairie, we dont have water problems here. The ground percs
very well. We had snow all day Saturday (the 1st) then 2 of rain the next
2 days. 40 mph winds during the 1st wind storm on Sunday and even less on
the 2nd storm. Much of western WA got 15 of rain in 3 days (according to
one newscaster). What started out as freezing temperatures and snow in the
mountains melted as temperatures shot up to near 60 the following days.
Parts of the coast got 130-150(?) mph winds. Several wind gages broke so
wind speed are unknown in those locations.



As luck would have it Wood-aug & his sister, HumpTea, needed to move their
G-mas things out of her assisted-living apartment in Spokane that weekend.
Spokane is 7 hours from here on a good day. On the way W-a went up to
Montesano (between Olympia and the coast) to pick HumpTea up. Fortunately
they beat the snowstorm over the Cascade mtns. Monday, due to the weather
they decided to drive here (Vancouver, WA) first via the Tri-cities
(Richland/Pasco/Kennewick) then go up to Monte. That day I-5 closed but
it was a good decision, anyway. HumpTeas Hubby and son (USS Pizza) were
stuck at home without power or water for days and no generator. HT & W-a
were able to get one in Portland even though they were flying out of the
stores quickly. They drove it up north Wednesday normally 2 hrs one way -
turned into 5 hrs with the Morton detour. I was very thankful when W-a made
it home that night.



Soapbox alert: Portland TV stations appear to be geographically challenged
they dont cover WA state news! We had a terrible time figuring what route
to take north. On the internet the various agencies are giving conflicting
info concerning the detours and roads conditions. Now over a week later and
we still have no idea how areas in WA State are doing as all PDX news wants
to talk about is Vernonia. Grrrrr.



On the up-side, HumpTeas letter today said that there are some pretty neat
stories free food, water, Home Depot selling generators at cost (once they
got there), etc. She said that the PUD guys are gaining hero status after
5+ days of 24 hr work. Shes expecting PUD action figures complete with
trucks, cherry pickers, spools of cable & flaggers! Funny lady!



Kurious Jo & Wood-aug
