While the appeal of geocaching escapes me, I feel compelled to come to the defense
of those who enjoy that pastime. I have seen letterbox sites that were trashed in
much the same way as described in RayvenDad's missive (eg. PCC Sylvania before
Ryan replaced the stamp, Fallen Feather at B-V Trail) and I have happened upon
caches that were well hidden with no immediate damage.

In my opinion, the real problem is that geocaching has a much larger following. As a
result, caches get more traffic, both pinheads and eco-friendly folks. The numbers
alone end up causing more destruction. Frequently visited boxes in New England,
letterboxing central, sometimes have trails blazed right to them.

I have personally had two very positive interactions with geocachers. In both
instances, they have found my letterboxes out in the open, rehidden them and
contacted me to let me know about their status. One of my regular Lb IM buddies
lives in Nebraska where there are few boxes and fewer boxers. In self defense, he is a
geocacher and makes every effort to introduce his cohorts to the fine hoby of
letterboxing.

We are very lucky to have a small, respectful group of letterboxers here in the PNW
but I don't think we are without blame. I know that the geocaching.com advocates the
same treatment of the environment that we do. Food for thought... Funhog
