We got an ID card for Grace for a Canada visit two years ago. Mom
had to hold her on her shoulders in the picture. So Grace has a ID
card with a picture of her 13 months old that doesn't expire until
she's 9. I don't imagine that the picture/height/weight will be
particularly accurate by then. :)

For folks travelling with kids, the info I found then was pretty
vague as to whether picture ID was required or not for kids. The
guard we got said it made things much easier since with picture ID
he didn't have to verify names on the birth certificate. I think
there was something about kids going in with only one parent. If
you're planning to do this, you may want to find out what you need.

One note here -- while picture ID is *definitely* a plus for the kids
(makes transit MUCH easier) -- we ran into one that we never would have
thought of.

We went in 2 cars -- the girls in one, the boys in the other -- the
girls included my daughter -- the boys included my husband.

They pulled us *both* over and interviewed us *seperately* to make sure
the he was ok with my talking our daughter over the border. They were
*very* antsy about that-- and told us both that a passport for her would
have made ALL the difference...

From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-17 16:56:54 UTC
When Princess Lea and I went across (I think we took Hwy 539 up out
of Bellingham--not the busier I-5), I thought I had left my passport
at home, The Canadian Border guard when we went in gave me a lot of
grief about not having it...she said that they would let me into
Canada, but I would have trouble getting back into the US from the US
officials. Luckily, after much stress and searching of my luggage, I
found my passport. And I had no problem getting back into the US.
This is just beefed up security after 9/11. It used to be no big deal
to go back and forth and they hardly looked at your passport, if at
all. But Now they definitely pay more attention!

Lea and I were trying to think of all kinds of worse-case scenarios--
like she would have to drive back to my house and bring my passport
to me while I waited in Canada!! :-) Or we might have to go to the US
embassy in Vancouver and try to get them to issue me some sort of
emergency "hall pass" goodness knows what that would have entailed---
my mom might have had to fax my birth certificate or something.

--Amanda from Seattle
