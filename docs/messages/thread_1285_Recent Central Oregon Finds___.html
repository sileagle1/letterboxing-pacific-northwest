Greeting fellow letterboxers!!

Spent a day and a half with my hubbyman in Central Oregon and grabbed
the opportunity to hunt a few letterboxes!!

Chimney Rock: Wonderful hike and find, Funhog!! Camped at the
nearby campground and scooted up first thing in the morning and found
your box and was stamping by 7:10 a.m.!!! Have to let you know,
though, your "scattered pile of dead wood" has been replaced by
a "once burned campfire spot" (sniff, sniff). Alas, no more pile of
scattered dead wood. Saw two deer on the way back down and was
energized for a great day!

John Day Fossil Beds NB Series: Hey Ryan!! Wanted to make all three
hunts, but couldn't make the Blue Basin one. Will definitely go
back, though!!

Went to the Painted Hills and came up empty-handed on that one.
There were 3 trails to choose from. I chose the one that would give
me the "bird's-eye-view" (Carroll Rim), but when I got to the top and
found the bench, your compass readings weren't adding up. No parking
lot at 195 degrees, only vast desolation of land. Made the best of
what I could and spied a couple of possible spots for the box, but no
prize.

Finally made it to the Clarno Unit and really marveled at the
Palisades!! Found your box at 6:45 p.m. under partly cloudy and calm
skies. The view was great with the setting sun shining through the
light and dark clouds. Still wanted to make one more box before
heading home, so off we went!!!

Made it to the "45th Parallel at 97057" just as it was getting dark.
Stamped the book at 7:45 p.m., zipped up my coat against the early
spring chill and took a stroll of the immediate area!! Lovely.

Thanks for the fun, kids!!! Until the next time.

hill, etc. are all in view at the top, but the compass degrees would
