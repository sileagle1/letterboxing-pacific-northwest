You know, I don't live close enough to come, but I thought it was
interesting you mention it. I've been talking to the 4-H coordinator about
placing one-way boxes in each barn at the Island County Fair as a way to
educate the public as they get to know the barns and the animals there. In
the past, they've had this little passport that the kiddies get stamped in
each barn, and turn in for a prize at the end. But the problem is, they
rush into the barn, get the stamp from the person on duty, and then leave
for the next barn without stopping to see where they are. We thought
letterboxes with good clues would be a great educational tool, and fun on
top of it.

You could check down there and see if a similar situation would work fo you.
Because of the crowds, we were thinking we'd place them somewhere in the
barn where the person on duty knows where it is, but it wouldn't
accidentally be found by somebody or be in a place that could harm the
animals. We also thought of giving clues they would find the answers to in
the barn, and they'd have to have the answers to get the box from the person
on duty.

It could be fun, and I would certainly look for yours if I were close
enough.
