Well, actually, letterboxes and geocaches ARE allowed in Washington State
Parks. It's just that they require the planter to complete a permit form
and agree the location with the ranger. The box would have a time limit
(couple of years). I have actually spoken to a local park ranger about it,
and they were very friendly. They just want to protect sensitive areas and
prevent social trails and that sort of common damage. That being said, so
many WA State Parks have letterboxes in them. I would never approach a
ranger in a park that already had boxes, for fear of endangering the boxes
that are already there.

Here's a link to their policy on their Web site. Their rules are pretty
common sense, in my opinion.

http://www.parks.wa.gov/downloads/Directive%2006-01%20Geocaching%20%20letterboxing%20and%20related%20activities.doc
