From: Kathy Gilbert (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-01-10 19:10:07 UTC-08:00
On my way to Ashland last September I picked up Applegate Trail by
Erfellie, Applegate Rest Stop by Green Tortuga (I cheated, not too proud
to admit that, on this one), Myrtle the Turtle by Erfellie, and Bucky
Beaver, a geo/LB hybrid - all were not far from the freeway more than 10
minutes if I remember correctly, and they were at good places to take a
break and stretch your legs. I can't remember if Myrtle and Bucky were
south of Roseburg or not, but you could check them out. I think the
other two are way south of Roseburg but they were were nice images - I
know Applegate Rest Stop is down by Valley of the Rogue Campground,
because that's where we stayed. Of the four, if I had to choose one for
you it would be Myrtle for the short walk - if you are not lucky you
could spend a fair amount of time finding Bucky, as the park is bigger
than where Myrtle lives. Erfellie will give you very good driving
directions if you ask her. It's in a park I can't imagine anyone not
liking, and they have bathrooms :) Have fun on your trip!

There are several short hikes or drivebys on Hwy 138. All have
waterfalls. California Bear has 3 in his Thundering Water Series, and
we planted Watson Falls Fox and it is about as driveby as it gets.
Sunshine has one at Diamond lake it is a stroll, but flat. If you have
some time you could try for Dumbledore's Western Adventure a little
south of your destination. It is a driveby mystery box, but it takes
some figuring out prior to leaving your computer, and you must have
the weatherman on your side. Possibly the road may be closed?
