From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-10-21 18:47:57 UTC

Sorry to hear about your website, Bethany! =( I'm glad you have
copies of *almost* all of your clues, though. It would have been much
worse if you had lost ALL of your clues that, especially for
long-distance boxes, may have never seen the light of day again.

I'd also like to take this time to remind people to always back up
anything that's important that you have on the web. Someone,
somewhere is eventually going to hack into it. It's not a matter of
if, but rather a matter of when. And never rely on somebody else for
backups.

I mention this only because I know a lot of people post clues to a
website--doesn't matter if it's letterboxing.org, geocities, or
something else entirely--and assume that such websites will have
viable backups or never get hacked. And they might very well have it!
But nothing is more secure than having your *own* backups.

I actually lost my website on geocities one time--which was shocking
to me, because I expected them to have an extraordinarily reliable
site. It happened a long time ago, and I still had copies of all the
data on my local computer, so it wasn't a big deal.

I've also heard people say things like they're website wouldn't be
hacked--there's nothing of value to hack! It doesn't matter--people
will hack websites just for the pure challenge of doing it. They'll
do it because they like to cause trouble.

There is *always* someone, somewhere that's trying to hack into your
website. I log several attempts EVERY DAY of people trying to hack
into Atlas Quest, though so far none appear to be very serious
attempts and none of them have succeeded. But I know that eventually,
someday, it WILL happen. There's no such thing as a 100% secure
system, and it's important to be prepared to handle such an event.
Because if you think it can't happen to you, you're living in a dream
world.

This message is *not* directed at Bethany--while her website being
hacked might be a headache for her, she clearly had backups in place
to restore all but one of her clues, and she deserves credit for that.
A lot of us out there wouldn't have been so well prepared, however,
and this is a warning to them. Don't let your website get hacked, but
if it does, have a backup plan! And *don't* rely on others for backups.

-- Ryan
