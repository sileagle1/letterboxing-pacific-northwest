From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-19 05:58:59 UTC
I have a feeling that the 'contact the placer' feature on the LBNA
site is not working properly so I'll post updates on some WA
peninsula area letterboxes here since I have no other idea how to
contact some of these letterbox placers. We found a handful of new
letterboxes and repaired or replaced a few of our own letterboxes
yesterday & today. All the Poulsbo, Silverdale & Case Inlet
letterboxes were faring well. Also, all of the Bainbridge Isle
boxes are doing well, too (who knew that that isle in particular
would attract dozens upon dozens of boxers??? when the neighboring
environs' letterboxes barely greet a dozen new letterboxers each
year?). We replanted 'Uninvited Guest' as it appears
something 'demolished, decimated & utterly destroyed' its original
hiding spot. It's a new box, new stamp in a new location--same
scary clue.

We also found Harry the Hermit alive & well. What an awesome spot!
This is a place we never would have known about had it not been for
letterboxing. We now have a new place we hope to further explore
someday soon--the tide pools were amazing! Reminded us of North
Shore, Oahu--only with British Columbia's mountains off in the
distance. Unfortunately, no orca sightings this trip. :( A few
other awesome regional Olympic boxes were doing well, too--something
along the lines of a flattering crescent moon? Both just begging for
more visitors!

Well, we're back to our non-letterboxing ways again for a while...

dvn2r ckr, rnrB & trkr

appeared to be letterbox components but couldn't find a thing. The
guts either went home with the vandals or are floating somewhere in
the Strait of Juan de Fuca. I'm not sure if the planters of these
letterboxes are active on this message board or not. I'll attempt
to contact them individually somehow but wanted to shout out to
other letterboxers in the meantime.
