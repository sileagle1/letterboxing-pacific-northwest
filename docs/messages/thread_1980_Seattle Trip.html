From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-24 19:47:16 UTC

Mid-Seattle letterboxes?

There are only 2/possibly 3 relatively near to where you're staying
(by relative I mean a couple of miles). There's a new one near the
needle itself (I haven't yet visited it--I think it's Zen Fruit II
or something?) and then one near the REI flagship store and possibly
one still near Pioneer Square (although somebody said it's probably
missing). Otherwise, most of the letterboxes are in locations
further out and about the greater metro area. If you head to
Woodland Park Zoo, you'll be close to a few more. If you venture
over to West Seattle, you'll be near a bunch more. These locales,
though, require some sort of transportation, though--unless the kids
are up for some marathons. ;) If you head to the Ballard Locks,
you'll be near another one or two (not sure how they're doing these
days). One's definitely there--not sure about the other.

Some ideas--however, if I had a hotel near the Space Needle, I'd
probably just linger in the area and enjoy the sights around there--
since you'll be paying for the 'experience'. Must 'non-
letterboxing' dos with kids in Seattle--Pacific Science Center, Pike
Place Market, Odyssey Maritime Discovery Museum on the Waterfront
(Pier 66) http://www.ody.org/ If you can head over to West Seattle
during low tide--check out the tide pools at Mee Kwa Mooks (there
are a couple of nearby letterboxes, too). Alki Beach is a great
spot, too!

Have a fun trip!!!

dvn2r ckr

We were in Seattle two weekends ago, visited the Ballard Locks in the
pouring rain! Had a great time watching the locks but were unable to find
the "Ballard Locks" letterbox. Said a new log book was placed on 2/8/05 but

blast.
