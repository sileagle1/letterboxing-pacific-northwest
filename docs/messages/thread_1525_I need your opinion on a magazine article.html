I need your opinion on a magazine article

From: Susan Johnson (susan@kuku.org) | Date: 2004-08-15 22:33:26 UTC
I need your help on a matter and I want to ask you all first, before
I even consider asking the nation-wide list.

I am a rubber stamper. Once, many years ago, a RS magazine did an
article on Dartmouth moor & letterboxing.

A friend in my RS club thought it was great and wanted us to find
some way of getting it going in the US. (Course we didn't.)

Fast forward to now. A RS mag I just got had "The Letterboxer's
Companion" -- a great book by the way -- in their reviews of books.

I was wondering if I should write an article for the mag about LBing
in the US and the guidelines to know before starting this hobby. You
know the ones, always hide the box where you found it, make sure no
one can see it, etc.

The magazine, RubberStampMadness, employs my best friend since 7th
grade, so if the article would be heavily edited, I would know and
have a say.

What do you think? Should I write the article from a rubber
stamper's view? This is a limited audience, so it won't be like
Newsweek or Time.

Please let me know if you think I should write the article and what
you think should DEFINITELY be in the article if I did.

Thanks so much!

KuKu, Portland, OR

Re: I need your opinion on a magazine article

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2004-08-16 02:46:28 UTC
I was just recently interviewed by a reporter for a small town
newspaper in Camas, WA. and I made sure that I emphasized the need to
keep the surrounding areas as pristine as you found it and to use all
natural materials found in the immediate area to cover and hide your
letterbox. Also when placing and finding a box, make sure that you do
not trample through bushes, plants, trees or anything else for that
matter that would endanger or damage the surrounding areas.
Letterboxers are not just out there to find a letterbox but to also
enjoy the beauty of nature and to preserve it as well if it means to
bring along a garbage bag and pick up litter as you go. If you are
interested in more information to give to the article and you are in
the area, please stop by the Passport to Letterboxing Stamp Camp at
Battle Ground Lake on August 27-29 and listen to Iron Bear as he
gives you all the information you will ever need on letterboxing
etiquette. Hope to see you there!
Camp Fire Lady
