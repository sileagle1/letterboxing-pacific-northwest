Geo-Hybrids and Dogs

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-04-27 16:36:02 UTC
I'm here to third the opinion of geocaching hybrids. Those letterbox stamps have a
bad habit of taking a hike when combined with a geocache. As for the silicone wipes,
if they have a scent they may be an attractive nuisance for an animal with a good
sniffer. They are probably hermetically sealed but you never know. I'd hate for your
box to be ravaged by a wild beast.

Then there's dogs on the trail... being a birdwatcher, I'm not particularly fond of
meeting them on the trail. Dogs tend to frighten any wildlife anywhere nearby. It
totally drives me crazy when they are running loose in an area where leashes are
required. I also raise my eyebrows at the account of washing feces off in a local
stream. Can you say, "e. coli???" I've been trying to keep my mouth shut on this but
the Curmudgeon won out. When camping, you are always supposed to make your
latrine at least 300' from a water source. Why is a dog any different? Watersheds
receive so much negative pressure from human impact that this is just one more way
to pollute this precious resource.

That's my Eco-Rant for the day. I'm off to the Zoo. Funhog
