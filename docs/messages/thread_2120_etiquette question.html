etiquette question

From: Kathy (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-28 16:30:18 UTC
This board is a bit different from the one I use in my work - it has
each post threaded so that replies and further discussion all come
under one posted topic and can all be viewed at once or skipped
entirely by skipping the entire thread. One can mark a thread as a
favorite and be e-mailed any and all responses to it. It is
considered rude not to come back and thank a person for answering your
question or responding to your post even if their answer didn't really
help you. Here, on the other hand, it appears that one signs up for
e-mail of all posts or none, or a daily log of posts, receiving each
each and every post/reply, so I wonder if it is considered
"cluttering" to thank or otherwise make comments to those who respond
to any given post, seeings as that "thanking/responding" may not be
interesting at all to the group as a whole. If I respond to someone's
response via their e-mail and the group does not see that response,
will it look like I'm rude and not following up on my post, or will
they be grateful I'm not cluttering the board up with stuff nobody but
one person is interested in? Guidance please from those who have been
here a while.



Re: etiquette question

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-06-28 17:15:19 UTC
Gosh! I, for one, often respond to posts not
of general interest offline so as not to clutter
the board. I never knew that anyone would
see this as poor manners in some situations.
I wouldn't think you are rude if I don't see a
'thank you' onlist.

I also read this list online, in which case you can
set it up to be organized by thread. To do that
you click on Messages to the left and then at the
top of the list you have an option of organizing
by thread. I have no idea if this is possible if you
receive the list via email.

There is one netiquette issue that I sometimes see
here that does bother me. When quoting text from
a post, it does bug me when the entire post is
quoted in the response, not just the relevant sections.
Just my personal pet peeve...
