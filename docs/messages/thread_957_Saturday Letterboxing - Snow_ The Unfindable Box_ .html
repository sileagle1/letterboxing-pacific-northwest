Saturday Letterboxing - Snow, The Unfindable Box, Pandora gets MUGGED!!!

From: pandora{HIB} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2003-11-23 17:29:57 UTC
Well Iron`Bear and I have made it a new habit of going out boxing
with CampFire Lady and Cats Meow every Saturday, boy are we enjoying
ourselves too. We had SimKitty with us yesterday, which made for a
whole group of letterboxing fun

First we headed out to the Washington Park area. We nabbed Girl
Scout Cookies and Tribute and then we made a run at Wonderful Tour.
Unfortunately we didn't do so good with this series as we could never
find any of the first clues for a starting point couldn't find the
tour guide (Tree) or the gift to man (Fire) and sure could not find
Zeus's retarded lightening bolt. But we hiked a good way trying to
find it and pretty soon we found ourselves in the falling snow. It
was quite an experience being out hiking while the snow was falling.
It was cold too. The kids were complaining a lot so we called it
quits with the intention to go back and look another day.

Next I walked over to the Children's Museum to replace Puff the Magic
Dragon in the Fate of Childhood Series. Seems the tree that Puff was
hiding at had been pruned and they took Puff with him So I replanted
him in a different location, the clues have been updated and anyone
that wants to go get Puff, he is waiting for you.

Then we headed over to the location for Mary's Little Lamb. Several
people wrote and said they could not find this box, and I even asked
Funhog, who had found all 12 boxes to go have a look and confirm it's
status for me. She confirmed it was missing so I re-carved it and we
headed out to replant. Funny thing though, when Iron`Bear went to
stick Mary's Little Lamb in the appropriate hiding place; he could
not do it because something was already there! Ummm the original
letterbox you guys will just have to look a little harder I suppose.
But you are in the right place. I hold Funhog responsible for
confirming the box missing and causing me to re-carve it, silly Hog
but really I am only saying that so she might feel guilty and give me
a little bit more of a hint on Sleight which is driving me nuts!!

Next we headed up to Pitock Mansion, had a lunch that gave a whole
new meaning to the "Cold Lunch" your mom use to offer you for taking
to school and then had another go at Sleight. Even with 5 of us we
still didn't find it. That box must be hidden pretty well!

Then we headed out to Gabriel Park to have a go at The Murder
Mystery. We came up empty handed trying to find the location of the
body, and took a detour to go visit the Wetlands to the Woods
Letterbox. This is where my day started to turn and I started to get
a bit grumpy. You see I haven't found a hitchhiker hidden in a
letterbox since I moved from California, and I only found one there.
The rest of my Hitchhiker count are ones I have planted myself, or
ones that were mailed to me to get them moved to a different
location. So here we are going for Wetlands to the Woods, I might
mention that nobody in our letterboxing but I knew the box was there,
nor did anyone but I have the clues for it. So sweet pandora, always
eager to help out, I told CampFire Lady, Cats Meow and Iron`Bear
about this box and we decided to have a go at it. So we are walking
along the trail, and we come to the hiding place and I say "I would
check that log right there in that hole." And CampFire Lady asks
me "This one?" "Yeah that one." I say, sure enough she pulls the
letterbox out. Go Team!! Yeah well one little hitch, (pun intended)
There was a hitchhiker in the box! Probably the coolest one I have
ever seen too. And I squealed like a pig with excitement that I had
found my first hitchhiker! But you know what they would not let me
take it with me! They insisted they found it! The stole it from me I
tell you stole it!! And I whined for like 30 minutes about it and
they were not even sympathetic. I thought I would open a poll on the
group and see who the group thought the should have gotten the hitch
hiker, but alas, I would hate to ruin a good friendship by turning
everyone against the thieves in question, so I will just let it go
and smile knowing that they would never have gotten either the box or
the hitchhiker if it hadn't been for me. But it taught me a good
lesson never let someone else look where you think the letterbox is!
Look yourself just in case folks!

So a still pouting pandora and the rest of the group then headed back
to have another go at looking for the body funny thing though we
crossed this bridge taking a short cut that seemed just like the ones
in the clues, so we did some investigating and by goodness we found
that homeless man Art. He gave us some more clues during the
interrogation but alas it was dark and we had to start heading back.
We plan to hit Gabriel Park and continue our search again next
weekend.

Hope your weekend was as fun as mine was even if I did pout for the
larger portion of the end of the day!

pandora{HIB} "Pandy" of Team Chaos
