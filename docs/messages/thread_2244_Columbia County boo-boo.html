Maiden: Not at all! Just busy with lives. Sometimes you have to just
hang in there. I've got boxes out there that have seen only 2 or 3
visitors in a year.
Camp Fire lady: I have had a box that sat unfound for 6 months before
it was found and it isn't a tough one to find. Be patient. Someone
will get there.

Someone posted a while back that newbies tend to be self-centered, and
when I saw it I was a tad offended, but maybe it's true in a way. I'm
sure you all remember carving your first pieces of art, obsessing
about what to put in the box, what kind of box to use, all that etc.
etc., and then you place it and walk away and think about it lying
there hidden all night and the next day, and then maybe you go back to
look and see is it still there, and of course it IS, and then you
think about it all the time. When it rains you think about your
little log book and stamp all nice and dry inside the box. I've
planted quite a few in remote areas and will be surprised if they are
ever visited, but if they are, I will know I maybe had a hand in
bringing someone to place they might not have visited otherwise. I
know for a fact I would not have gone to many of the places I've been
this summer had it not been for certain boxes I was after. So
self-centered, maybe yes, but hopefully in a good way, and thanks
everyone for those boxes in the beautiful more remote spots as well as
the ones in the funky urban areas that are just plain fun to find.
That Wilson box is a hoot. I will just keep placing and try to be
patient waiting for the visitors that will come in time.
