FunHog -
I agree with you 100% - absolutely! Especially if I
know the placer personally or on the boards. However,
my post was "generally speaking" - again, rules that
aren't written and that CAN be broken "depending".
Really, this hobby is one big grey area.

I actually removed a large and OLD log from a box that
was left open, exposed and bagless. I knew I could
dry it out over a week and return it by weeks end
without inconveniencing myself. I did so. I placed
paper between each indvidual sheet of log (which
smelled like cat urine and was slimy!) and changed the
paper three times a day until it was dry enough to not
break apart when I touched it. I then used a hair
dryer to dry the sheets to the bone. The book still
reeked of urine but the book was dry and rebagged and
still "intact" for the most part.

However, we recently found TWO logs that were moldy
and one that was irrepairable. We contacted the
placer and have permission to "refurbish" the log as
they are unable. We also know they are local - big
difference.

If you trek out on an 8 mile hike and find a
decomposing log - yea, I think you do something about
it.
