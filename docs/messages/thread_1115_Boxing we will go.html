From: mudstumpers (mudstumpers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-02-02 11:30:39 UTC-08:00
We are having the same problem with Frenchie Falls. Even our French friend was a bit confused by one of the phrases. Linguistics is not an exact science nor is language ever interpreted the same by any 2 people, I know, it was my BA.

We are giving up on Pony Tail Falls, Saturday we must have checked every rock and boulder and fern up there. However, the Triple Falls box is is fine shape - still 3 pages left. We will replace the log book on our next visit. If you are planning to go up anytime soon, there are lots of new hazards on the trail. Several mudslides took out trees and rocks and the 3rd bridge (above the trail junction with TR400) was destroyed. There are also several major drifts still blocking the trails (about 6 feet high) and would not be appropriate for children - there were some dogs out but several were being carried over/across them. Most people found the trail impassible beyond Triple Falls. They said the drifts were too deep and unstable. We had hot soup and coffee near the bridge and didn't attempt going beyond the letterbox. We were content to sit and hear the water roar.

Anyway it was a glorious day!

We finally found a perfect day at the Zoo, Sunday, no crowds and nice weather. We were successful with all 3 boxes listed. Great hide for Packy! Winking Eagle was almost visible, we took care of that, and Wapiti was fine. My 3 year old nephew even gave me a ride in his wagon!

Friday we found the 2nd Annual Thanksgiving box. Nice stamp and great location!

Thank you everyone for all the fun locations!

P of SP
