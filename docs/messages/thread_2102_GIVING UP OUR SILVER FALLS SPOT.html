Re: GIVING UP OUR SILVER FALLS SPOT

From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2005-06-27 16:29:51 UTC
> Keep your eyes open for people with paper and/or scratching their
> heads!

I caught myself a couple of newbie letterboxers out in West Seattle
yesterday which was kind of fun. They were looking for the Birthplace
of Seattle letterbox and I had already found that box nearly a near
before but happened to be passing the area on one of my long, rambling
walks and saw two people crawling around looking suspicious with a
piece of paper in their hands.

They could have been geocachers, I suppose, so I checked them out for
a GPS and didn't see any. And when I got closer I saw the paper they
had with the block graphics of the curled parchment paper at the top
and knew the clue had to have come off of LbNA. I caught myself some
letterboxers!

They were having trouble finding the box--which didn't really surprise
me much because *I* had trouble finding the box the first time I was
out there, so I tried to help them out and contributed to the search.
I didn't remember which of those hundreds of nooks and crannies the
letterbox was located in, so they read me the clues and probably spent
a half hour trying to find that darned box. The other two--one was a
local and this was his first letterboxing outing. Didn't even have a
stamp to show for himself yet, while the other was from Boston and she
said she was pretty new to letterboxing as well, though this wasn't
her first box. They were just about to give up on the box before I
came to the rescue and found it. =)

So they stamped in and I did an exchange with the girl (Susan, I think
it was?) who actually had a signature stamp. Just so happened that I
had my letterboxing gear in my daypack--I had never taken it out from
my last letterboxing outing. =) Didn't think I'd be using yesterday!

But as the boy scouts say, always be prepared.....

Then I continued my walk and they went off to find more letterboxes
around West Seattle.

-- Ryan
