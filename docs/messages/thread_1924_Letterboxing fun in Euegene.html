Letterboxing fun in Euegene

From: erfellie (hemenekawan@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-02-18 18:19:53 UTC

Wow what a day! Sandi and I packed up the 2 mutts and drove to
euegene yesterday. Along the way we got Rambopups "Dig dog" LB at the
Metzhill I-5 rest area and was alive and well. We went to find
her "Cow creek" rest are box but realized too late that according to
the exit number, it was nowhere near cow creek (there are no rest
areas there) and realized that it was actually back near Glendale.
That was a bit disspointing, but we will get it on our next trip.
Then we traveled onto Roseburg for "easter joy". This one I
seriously reccomend, the view was amazing. Freezing cold, but hey, it
is february. What a great place though :o)
We then went to find the UofO LB and got to question #10 and
realized that I forgot to print up the 2nd part of the clues... darn
I felt stupid. Beutiful campus though. I felt really stupid though
when I had to ask someone how to use a parking meter.
So from there we went to CymruWitch's Rhododenron box at Hendricks
park, what a beautiful park, I loved all the ivy :o) and a
beautifully unique logbook. The LB is alive and well but quite
visible on the trail so we tried to tuck it a littler further under
the log into the ivy. At this point I started feeling really sick,
gee who knew the flu could come on so fast? which unfortunately this
shortened our trip...
So then we went to Skinner Butte to find, what else, but the
Skinner Butte LB and also the Sisters LB. At the bottom of the road I
was really excited to see columnar jointing, something I had studied
in geology classes but had never seen up close, that was really
awesome :o) So anyways we went up there and got the Sisters box and
fed and watered the dogs and tooks some pictures and walked to find
the Skinner butte box. At this point my dog ran down the hill and
rolled in something very dead and very rotten and when I called him
back to me I grabbed his collar to hook on his leash and put my hand
in something chunky and slimy and like an idiot, smelled my hand to
see what it was and immediatly started dry heaving violently for
about 10 minutes and Sandi, being the great friend she is, laughed so
hard she nearly wet herself (I wish she would have). Well when i
finally stopped dry heaving I sat down and stamped into Skinner butte
LB, another creative stamp and logbook by CymruWitch.
So then we were planning to go straight to Alton Baker Park for
that series, but had to stop at the first river I found to throw
mydog in it. He had the time of his life chasing ducks (he is a
retriever after all) and through this he got off most of the chunky
slimy stinky stuff on him. So then, feeling even sicker now, we went
to Alton Baker park, another really amazing park, and went to find
this series. Box number one was MIA so much to our dissapointment we
tried to locate box #2 which was also MIA and then it was getting
dark and I was getting sicker, not too mention the smell of my dog
was quite unpleasant, and we knew that #4 was MIA as listed on the
website, and so we figured in 1 and 2 were also MIA, #3 was probably
gone too so we turned around headed back to the truck.
From here we went to Mount Pisgah Park to locate those LB's but we
hadnt hiked very far into the woods when it got really dark really
fast and my lungs were hurting me reeeaally bad and then the flu hit
me like a bag of bricks so we turned around and headed back tot eh
truck for the long drive home.
I have never seen so much of Euegene before and was rather
impressed, I especially loved the beautiful parks and the people were
very polite and UofO had a lot of very very good looking guys. We got
a total of 5 LBs so it was a good trip, with the exception of me
getting sick, but at least when we go back there we will be more
familiar with the area and get more LBs. It was alot of fun, had a
great day (most of it). We are looking forward to going back up
there :o)
