We've stayed at the Travelodge in Troutdale rather often. It was
the least expensive place listed on Travelocity a while back--which
is how we learned about it initially. It's very centrally located
and perfect if you're interested in the Columbia River Gorge
waterfalls boxes. Those boxes alone are worth spending a couple of
weeks in the region. It probably costs about $45/night, though--
which is 'expensive' for us as our goal is usually a hotel room for
$35 or less (true roach motels). McMenamins in the Troutdale area
does have a 'luxury hostel' that runs about $35/night/person, so
that's an option, too. Just depends on your price range.

We usually camp along the way and look for campsites under $15/night-
-although we do splurge occasionally on an $18/night site near Mt
Rainier that's right along the river. ;) We usually stay in hotels
when the weather gets pretty unbearable for camping and often only
as a last resort. So, that gives you an idea on what
our 'cleaniless' standards are for a hotel room. I usually prefer
camping over staying at a hotel.

Have fun in Portland!
