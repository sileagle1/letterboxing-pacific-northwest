All the boxes of Trial n Error and Evil Glenn in Point Defiance Park have been visited today and found to be in good health.



Gwendolyn was so lonesome she almost didn't let me leave, she said she had heard of a new sheriff in the wood, but hadn't seen him yet, thou' excited at the prospect.



The Woodland Gnome was looking forward to Spring and time start chopping next winter's wood so it can dry.



The Forest Gnome wanted to know why he hears people going past wondering at the number of the last trail marker.



And Jarl, well you know Jarl, always with the basket and talking about new shoulder pads.



Point Defiance # 1 was still talking about the last visitor and how well hidden and snug he felt, while # 2 lamented at the solitude.



Camp Six hadn't been able hear anything since all the Santa Train activity, but was getting better and looking forward to Spring and some new fern fronds, said the roof leaked.



Pt. D. Bear was grumpy as ever, having been wakened from hibernation. He said "cover me well and natural please!"



The Sports Series was the hardest to catch, having had a recent visitor, the first two were running all over the place talkin' about it, except for number three who was still smiling at the confusion of the last sport to come by, he said "I had 'em on the hook, but couldn't reel 'em in!"



So, if it's on the Web Site, it's in the 'wood.

All in all not too bad a day. Didnt get rained on and added one
stamp to our book. Better one than none. Looking forward to a trip
back to the Point soon.

We found this one this past weekend. WE thought we had it found when
at the viewpoint..but it was the wrong first square..I will say that
there is a perfect hiding spot at this viewpoint that lines up with
the coordinates given in the clue when you count the wrong type
of "square" shapes!!

It was a bit tough since the final trail marker was recently broken
off at the ground. Some walker/jogger had leaned it against a tree.
We were informed by a runner that happened to run by us which trail
was the correct trail for the marker (I guess we looked lost) and
that someone had broken off the marker this past few weeks. When
checking the coordinates at where the marker now rests..it is way
off!! (The marker was broken off flush with the ground..we looked for
remnants for 20 minutes.) But..we rooted around and found the
box...MomNoTime actually stepped on the box when she was trying to be
subtle looking as a hiker walked past. You should of seen her face
when she did that!! Thank goodnes the ground is spongy!! The stamp
had some kind of "film/particles" on it and we had to rub that off so
that the ink would stay on the stamp.

Well..when I replanted the box...where we found it...I took a bearing
and there is a marker way, way, way yonder that lines up perfectly
with the box... one must be practically lying on the ground to see
the marker. THen when I hiked to the marker itself and looked towards
the box I noticed that you can't see the location at all until one
lies on the ground and even then...it's near impossible unless you
know what you are looking for! LOL!!
