What's truly driving my attempt to 'clean up' the WA map is that
some of the regions' lists of boxes have become quite unwieldy with
the appearance of many new letterboxes over time (i.e. Region 2).
As we attempt to modify this 'megalopolis' a bit, there may be some
things that will help you better manage the site as it currently
stands. Not sure if everybody is aware of some of the
www.letterboxing.org website's new features as they sort of appeared
without any fanfare over the past couple of months.

Folks who register as members (don't worry it's free--I think it's
mandatory if you plant new boxes and upload clues) can go into
Member Services and activate 'flags' for boxes they've either
planted or found/attempted. Then, you can change settings to 'hide'
these boxes you've marked as having already found or planted
yourself. That feature alone cleans up the lists fairly quickly.

Then, as you continue along your letterboxing adventure you can
annotate the statuses (stati?) of boxes you've found or planted
along the way--options such as 'found', 'attempted', 'ignore' (for
boxes you know you'll just never even attempt--like the ones that
require parachutes, crampons, EVA equipment or oxygen tanks). This
feature will keep the status of boxes fairly updated (as folks enter
their finds/plants) and will also help keep your lists much smaller
over time as you find new boxes in various regions.

Another thing that might be nice is to have a 'Spring Cleaning'
event for the entire state to encourage folks to update their box
statuses (alive, needs repair, missing, retired--that sort of thing).
