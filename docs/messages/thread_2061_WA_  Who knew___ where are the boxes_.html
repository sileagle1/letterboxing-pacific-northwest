Just got back from this series late this evening. It's pretty
cool! We found them all minus the one in the museum as it doesn't
open until the 'summer season' (which begins Memorial Day Weekend, I
presume). I kept asking folks along the way who was responsible for
the series and everybody pointed in one direction--to a very kind
lady who works at the library.

So, we stopped by the library to thank her for taking the time,
effort and resources to plant this neat series. She was so excited
about letterboxing and extremely enthusiastic about it. Turns out,
the library system offered a class on letterboxing a couple years
ago and this series came about from that. You'll notice that other
libraries around the region also have letterboxes on the premises.
The class was to encourage children to learn about the libraries and
discover something new. Well, this ensuing series was really cool.

Thanks to it, we visited several establishments that until
this 'adventure' we'd only raced past. We also hiked along some new
trails that we hadn't yet visited. Saw a glorious waterfall in the
process!!! We didn't complete the series in one day so we decided
to car-camp for an evening and finish it up the following day. Very
glad we did that as the weather went from intermittent downpour to
glorious blue skies with crisp, cool winds. Perfect letterboxing
weather!

I urge ya'll to check out this series and support the establishments
that are hosting the boxes. Pipe up when you're there so they know
you're a letterboxer. Enjoy the adventure. Hopefully, this series
will encourage other regions to support this neat hobby, too.

See you on the trail,
