From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-07-27 18:25:13 UTC
If it were, in fact, a fledgling, it very well COULDN'T fly
away. Birds like this are often brought in to rehabilitators
by kind souls who think they are helping. In fact, it's
usually better to leave the bird alone. The adults are
likely nearby, coming to feed it for a couple of days until
it figures out how to fly. The peregrines that nest on the
Fremont Bridge in Portland have had their young rescued
from the river below and the roadway by volunteers when
they failed to fly on their initial tries.

As for killing, as far as I know, these guys have to hit their
prey from the air. When they strike, their talons are built
to close on impact, piercing vital organs. Your dog could've
been 'footed' but not likely to have had a fatal injury from
a prone position. This is probably way more than you
wanted to know... but I am really curious to see those pics!
Funhog
