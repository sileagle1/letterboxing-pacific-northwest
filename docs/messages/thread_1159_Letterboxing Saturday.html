Letterboxing Saturday

From: (paisleyorca@webtv.net) | Date: 2004-02-23 04:26:36 UTC-08:00
Carie, of Lennens 3 and I broke free Saturday afternoon for some
letterboxing. Since we were late for any breakfast meet-up with others,
we chowed down on sushi and hit the road in Troutdale and Gresham. We
thought we'd see Brindle Babe, Melanie, and the others on the huntdown,
but it sounds like y'all were off to West Linn, instead, so no chance
meeting occurred.

Anyone who wants to get their "F" count up in a fast and furious way
ought to hit Troutdale and Gresham. All the boxes we got were pretty
much drive-bys. Not much hiking or walking involved. It was very windy
out there, but sunny and the stamps we got are lovely. I think we
mostly got Sleepy Whippet and Melanie boxes and one from a backwards
Pezman or something like that.

Also...Twinkletoes, whoever you are...I LOVE YOUR BIG SIGNATURE STAMP.
It's a beautiful carve!

Thanks to Carie for a fun afternoon of 'boxing in East County. It's
been a long, long time since I've done any letterboxing, and there were
no stamps in my book since the Roadhouse Gathering in December. How
pathetic is that? Life has just been too busy in other ways. Maybe
come spring and summer I will be able to get out and about more! I
don't think I've place anything new since last September, so I ought to
think of doing some new letterboxes.

Also..Thanks to Dave for his e-mail about my White-Fronted Bee Eater. I
will try to get over to this box when I have a chance. Thanks for the
heads-up on its status.

Best regards,

Amanda Briles
The Paisley Orca

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-02-23 06:37:08 UTC-08:00
Oh no! What a bummer, I'm so sorry we didn't get a chance to meet up with
the two of you. I've been looking forward to meeting you both. I hope I'll
still get the chance. We do have lots of boxes out here that are fairly
short hikes, but we have a few that are longer. I think the longest hike we
have in the area is the Beaver Creek Box, wonderful hike, but be prepared
for muddy paths and having to scale really high steps. The old stairs are
gone so there's several places you have to climb up and down make shift
ones. The Oxbow Series box and Riversbend are also good hikes. Even my
Springwater Trail #1 is a decent stretch of the legs.

Melanie
