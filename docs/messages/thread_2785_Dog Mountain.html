From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-05-23 10:36:18 UTC
I was along for the ride today at Dog Mountain. I have been doing this
hike for at least six years as an annual spring ritual and have to say
that I think this year has the best display of flowers I have seen up
there EVER! We hit it just right, with almost everything making an
appearance: balsamroot, paintbrush, orchids, phlox, larkspur,
chocolate lilies and lupine (in the lower elevations.)If you want to
see one of the most impressive wildflower displays in the area, lace
up those boots and hit the trail NOW. It's a hard one but the reward
is SO worth it.

There are at least seven letterboxes up there at this point but plenty
of room for more. Hamilton Mountain would be a wonderful hike too but
I'm saving that one for a different time. It's not known for its
springtime show of wildflowers but the views of the Gorge are stunning
along that trail. I'll make it my mission to visit that Wisconsin
Hiker letterbox this year. I promise! Funhog
