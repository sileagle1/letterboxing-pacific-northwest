Beginning to see the Minstrel's point...

From: (PNWEXPLR@aol.com) | Date: 2003-05-03 01:55:47 UTC-04:00
Heather & I spent two hours tramping about Milo McIver today, with kids in
tow, in search of Funhog's Royal Angler. We went this way, and tried that
way, and retraced our steps several times ("HEY!" said Heather's
kindergartener, "We already came this way!" Here, hush up and eat another
granola bar, kid). In desperation just before we finally did give up, H & I
searched around a "nurse" we thought might be promising, but encountered
stinging nettle rather than a box (and all this time I kinda thought I was
immune to the stuff. Well, you DO learn a lot while you're letterboxing).

Another thing I learned was that Milo McIver is not well-marked (at least not
the southern portion) and that their map is nothing to write home about
either. In the end, I am not sure we even STARTED in the right place--tho
there seemed no other choice.

So throw us a bone, Funhog, pluuu-ease! The parking area for "F" picnic area
wasn't listed on the map, so we could only guess it was the lot near the
southern boat dock. Is that right? (The hatchery is down in that area, so I
think we must have been right at least about that...) Then we walked past
the boat dock and back up the road, as instructed, and took the esquestrian
trail (from the road, with the river at our backs, it was on the left) marked
with three concrete posts. There was another trail a bit farther on, with
wooden posts...now I'm wondering if it's possible the concrete posts have
been added since the clues were written?? But wait...that trail would have
been even FARTHER away from the river, which we were supposed to follow at
some point, but never saw again after leaving the parking lot.

So what the heck?

On the plus side, it was a LOVELY day, we got some good exercise, and the
swelling & itching will go down in a few days....
