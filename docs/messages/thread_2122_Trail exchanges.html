From: Pungent Bob (PungentBob@HotPOP.com) | Date: 2005-06-29 16:37:51 UTC
I got my one and only trail exchange while going out to one of my
boxes to retrieve the Molly Looking for Ginger HH that was left there
for us to get. The people we exchanged with were looking for their
first box and got their first stamp, first HH, and first exchange all
at once!

For those who weren't around a few years back I created the Ginger
Looking For Molly HH and Chuck in Conn. created the Molly Looking for
Ginger HH. Ginger being our dog and Molly his. The goal of each HH was
to travel across the country to reach the other. Both managed to make
it across at approximately the same time, mine making it in time for
the Molly Day gathering in Conn. and his making it in time for the
Milo McIver Gathering in Oregon. Both were then sent back and retired.

The day I found Ryan's Crappy Stamp box, I noted that Funhog had
stamped in on the same day. I thought about roaming the trails
screaming "Funhog!" out at the tops of my lungs in case she was still
in the area but then I heard a Harley rev in the distance and knew the
opportunity had past.

My favorite trail exchange of the two that I've had,
happened in Arizona a couple of years ago. I go
to Tucson every February for business but somehow
always manage to squeeze in some letterboxing
while I'm there. Up until that time my boxing usually
consisted of hiding a letterbox since there were so
few in the area and I had long since exhausted the
supply. Well, some time in 2003 AZRoadie appeared
on the local scene and started hiding boxes right and
left.

Having a newbie on the scene, I was pretty darned
excited to meet up. We emailed and made plans to
have coffee together on Monday morning. On Sunday,
I decided to go birding at a wonderful place about an
hour east of Tucson, San Pedro House. There also
happened to be one of AZRoadie's boxes hidden there.
It was the perfect way to kill a box and a bird with the
proverbial one stone.

Once I figured out that this boxer's degrees given in
his clue weren't compass bearings but degrees on a
circle, I managed to track down the letterbox. As I was
walking away from the hiding place to stamp in, a
bearded fellow came up to me, looked at the letterbox
and said, "Are you a letterboxer?" I looked at him and
answered "Are you AZRoadie?" Sure nuff... we met on the
trail, a day early and far, far from town.

Incidentally, he and his wife made a trip to Oregon last
year. You may recognize his Route 66 sig stamp in the
local logbooks. They created an amazing scrapbook to
commemorate their trip complete with maps, photos and
all the stamps they collected, INCLUDING the ever-elusive
Oxbow #4, the box with no clue! Funhog
