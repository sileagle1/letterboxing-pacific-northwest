RE:[LbPNW] Replacement ediquet

From: (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-06-04 11:38:32 UTC-07:00
Contrary to popular belief, Der Mad Stamper did NOT give me permission to replace any of his boxes. But since he seemed MIA, I took it upon myself to recreate the original stamps to the best of my ability and replaced them mostly with the thought that he's a nice guy and probably appreciates the help.

In any case, before replacing someone else's box, I'd definitely try to get ahold of the owner first. Particularly if they aren't locals, they may greatly appreciate help in maintaining orphaned boxes!

If that fails--which it did in the case of MIA D.M. Stamper--I found copies of the original images and recarved them. I tried to replace them as close to their original locations as possible, although his Gorge boxes couldn't be rehid in their original locations.

The Bronson Creek box--if it is missing--I wouldn't have any qualms about replacing. It's a wonderful clue, and anyone that follows it to the end deserves the treat! I do have a copy of the original stamp back in California, although as Amanda said it wasn't especially impressive. Not only was it those lame foam stamps, but he glued them to the top of a peanut butter jar's lid for the backing--an awkward and clumsy stamp. I wouldn't mind seeing a new and improved stamp myself, but if you want to keep the original spirit of it, I can send a copy once I return to California. (It might be awhile.) There might be someone closer that can get a copy of the stamp quicker than myself, though.

-- Ryan
