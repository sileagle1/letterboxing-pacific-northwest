From: Laura B. (bedotcalm@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-21 20:33:11 UTC
Do you box throughout the winter? If so, how do you adapt?

Well now days...we do when it's conveniently located near an ice rink
in the greater Puget Sound Area. Letterboxing takes a backseat when
you have two kids that play ice hockey from August til March. We do
alot of boxing and geochaching in the spring when camping at
Millersylvania and near the ocean. Yes...I said the "g" word in a
letterboxing group and admit to participating in it.

I actually prefer letterboxing in the winter around here. Less people
on the trails and the dogs always enjoy a reason to play in the mud!
Half the time we don't get wet because we are on wooded trails.
MomNoTime and myself have a tradition of letterboxing on New Year's
Day too...it was spawned the year we had snow on New Years Day a few
years back.

I actually prefer to letterbox in poorer weather... drizzle, cold wind, light snow etc. Even as a youngster growing up back east in the country my favorite times to get out and explore were similar. I think part of it is feeling the power of nature. It makes you feel more alive and in tune with Mother Nature. The woods at dusk in a driving snow can't be beat (reminds me of a certain poem).

The thing that really helps is a Gor-tex jacket! (best purchase I ever made).
