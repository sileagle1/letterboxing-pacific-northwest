All,

I got the following note from Tryon Creek staff. Letterboxing is no
longer allowed and they have requested the boxes there be removed.
I'm not sure how I was elected the lucky recipent of the message. In
any case I've responded that the boxes aren't mine and that Der Mad
Stamper is MIA. I also said I would ask if someone can volunteer to
remove the boxes. Note that there are 5 boxes that I'm aware of, not
just the two she noted. I think this would be a good idea as a lack
of cooperation may lead to a more widespread ban in all State
Parks/Areas. It looks like from the bottom of the email that they
may be willing to work with us, a la THRPD. Perhaps the volunteer
could pursue that.

Message:
Hello, my name is Karen Trombley and I am the park manager at Tryon
Creek State
Natural Area in Portland, Oregon.
It has come to my attention that you currently have as many as two
letterboxes
located in the park (Iron Mountain & High Bridge).
We recently went through a process with geocachers and have decided
not to allow
caches in the park. The same would apply to letterboxes. While we
realize this is
a fun activity, we also realize the potential damage that can occur
to our natural
resource. This activity encourages off trail usage which can damage
our fragile
watershed and wipe out plants and animal habitat. The most
important point is
safety. Venturing off trail is dangerous to the person or persons
seeking a
letterbox. Unstable ground, stinging plants, and ground nesting
yellowjackets are
just a few of the many reasons it can be a health hazard.
Your cooperation in our effort to preserve our park and keep
visitors safe will be
welcome. Please remove letterboxes from all location in the park
asap and post on
your website.
Alternative solutions may be found if you are willing to explore
them. Please
contact me if you wish to explore other options for your group.
Again, thank you for your cooperation in this matter.

Sincerely,

Karen Trombley
Park Manager
karen.trombley@state.or.us

Yay! Go Funhog, go! I'm DEEELIGHTED that you volunteered to speak with the
staff at Tryon. This is the way to handle such situations, instead of LBers
getting all huffy, upset, or nasty about the ordered removal of our boxes.
Talking is good. You might not be able to convince them--but it's still the
best response! Maybe you can convince them to allow boxes subject to their
approval of each location.

Personally, I have always been uncomfortable with the location of Der Mad
Stamper's Red Fox Bridge--it requires seekers to go off the official trail, onto
a "user" trail. I don't see any way to successfully argue that this box
should remain in its current location. Hmm, Birdwatcher might also be considered
off trail? But (if memory serves) Wendy's Tryon Creek Orphan box, DMS's High
Bridge and the Iron Mt box--as well as, ahem, a certain mystery box--are all
within nabbing distance from an established/official trail.

Mudstumpers--consider leaving the remaining boxes in place for now--at least
until Funhog has a chance to speak with the Tryon folks. Maybe they would
want her to show them where a box or two are located so that they can see how
little these beside-the-trail boxes impact the environment. Maybe following the
clues and discovering the boxes (with the Hawg's kindly guidance) will spark a
recognition of how freaking fun our little hobby is???
