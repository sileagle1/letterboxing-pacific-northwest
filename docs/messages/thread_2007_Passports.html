Forgot the passport issue. When I turned 21 it was "the thing" to go
to the states. We had been legal for 2 years in Canada, but we still
had to do the "21" thing. That was 24 years ago... and things have
changed so much since then. In my early twenties, we sort of waved to
the U.S. border guards as we drove through. Then we waved to the
Canadian border guards as we drove home.

Zara and I drive down to Bellingham to a craft store where we buy
carving material that we can't get in Canada. (And I have spent a
small fortune making phone calls trying to find it up here). And now I
hear, that as of 2006, we will need passports to enter the U.S. I find
it all very sad. Our countries have always boasted the longest
unguarded borders in the world. And, I suppose it is still unguarded,
but it is becoming less so.
