The map (as we currently have it) is posted under the Files
Section. I'll add Vancouver as a city name to it and then wait for
any more guidance for additional breakouts (counties or regions).

I just studied Boxopolis Connecticut's map and saw that their wee
state is broken up into quadrants with about 400 letterboxes per
quadrant--PLUS another 100 or so mystery LBs. (I'm wondering--does
WA even have 400 boxes total?) ;)

I'm trying to balance a simple, streamlined map (in the traditional
feel of the 'classic' LBNA site) with one that is efficient and easy
to navigate. As we play around with different 'break outs' of
regions, it starts to get visually busy, pretty quickly.
Personally, I'm leaning more toward merging Thurston & Pierce and
standing King up individually. If anything, it's easier on the eyes.

Please let me know what you think about the modified map. I want to
get this right before we send it off to 'map' the hyperlinks to the
dbase (to avoid extra work on behalf of others on that end). Thanks,

dvn2r ckr

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-28 20:12:22 UTC-07:00

I'd always thought it was strange that one goes from a map view (the US, then the state) to a list view for all the actual boxes. I may have lived here all my life, but I still have trouble remembering where some of the smaller cities in the southern Puget Sound region are. It makes it tougher to do trip planning when you have to locate the Thomas Guide and match things up.

This involves a near overhaul of the whole site, but I think it would be neat if each of the areas linked to from the state map showed a region map. Cities with boxes would be highlighted, and hovering would make a tag pop up with the number of boxes in that area (and the name if it's a smaller city and there's no room for the name on the map). If you're looking for boxes on the border of two or three "regions," trip planning can become a biiig pain, since some cities are listed in several regions. Each city could be a list, though. Getting that particular could prove super difficult to update...

Just an idea, but I know it would take a lot of work!! But the new map is a nice improvement!!
