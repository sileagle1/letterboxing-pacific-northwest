Tree Removal/Thinning at Pt. Defiance Park

From: minibullymom (bedotcalm@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-02-01 01:21:37 UTC
Okay,
We were at Pt. Defiance Park today (got 2 of 4 boxes we were in
search of) and found out that they will be doing some
clearing/thinning/dangerous tree removal in the park and on the side
of Ft. Nisqually that the road follows to Camp Six. I've not been
able to clarify with Metro Parks if the thinning/brush removal
(supposedly to lower the fire hazard level) will include the Madrona
area nor how far around the immediate Fort area that it will include.
They have a sign posted at the Info Kiosk about it and one just
outside the front of the fort by the corner lookout tower too..but
they just mention the area in general.

Metro Parks has also done alot of marking of trees in the
entrance/front half of the park for removal. Apparently it has come
to their attention that alot of the old hardwoods have some serious
damage/rot/disease to them. I did notice some trees marked in the
Picnic/Playground area outside the Zoo and one or two in the Rustic
picnic area. THey have done extensive trail maintainence in the
Rhodedendron Garden and have removed alot of English Ivy and trimmed
the "brush" from that area.

SO...When you are out reconiteering box locations and hiding boxes
this next month keep this in mind! Even if we can't utilize that
small area on the far side of the Fort for box hiding as part of the
LeapDay Event we have plenty of other places to hide boxes around
there!!
