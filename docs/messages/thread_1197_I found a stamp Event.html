Notice how she says CrabbyDaddy's moniker doesn't fit... but says NOTHING

As we drove into the parking lot yesterday, I noticed the cachers right off
the bat. They're always the ones standing together, and then get a wild
hair and rush off w/o much rhyme or reason... and at least half of them
have a gps in hand.

However, I wasn't sure how I was going to figure out just WHO these
letterboxers were.

Interestingly enough, CrabbyDaddy and his crew were just getting out of the
car for a walk (toward the letterbox area) as I was getting my goods
together to head over to the cachers. My thought was to meet with them
first, make arrangements for the day, and then walk out the Dirt Works
trail to see if anyone was out there.

As I was making my way around for 'hello's', I noticed a woman I'd not yet
seen at any of our recent gatherings talking to a group of folks I did
know. I asked her who she was and she said she was a "letterboxer just
waiting... WAIT! You're CurmudgeonlyGal!"

It was perfect.

I SO enjoyed meeting with you Bethany and talking... and talking... and
talking... She gave me my first THREE exchanges and I gave her my LAME
'caching' stamp. (My excuse is that I've been THRUST into this a little
mo' quicklier than I'd intended so I haven't yet carved my own!) We found
out we have a LOT in common (box hunting aside) and I just enjoyed myself
thoroughly.

I am sure, had neither of us had other pressing engagements, we'd still be
standing in the park talking.

On my way out (my caching friends were getting impatient!) Bethany told me
the man/kids/dog I'd seen on my way in WAS another letterboxer and that I
just couldn't leave w/o getting more stamps!

Aaaaaagh!

I had to show everyone my stamps (including the missing Byrd Square) over
the course of the day, relate the story, and marvel at just how cool these
stamps are!

THANK YOU for such a wonderful intro to this hobby!



michelle
...curmudgeonlygal

caching travel bug (our geocaching hitchikers) with him to K would he?
