Re: TOTW 12/25/05

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-12-27 17:31:13 UTC
I received several letterbox oriented gifts during the last couple of
days. I got to go hiking along one of my favorite trails in Central
Oregon. Unlike the previous Sunday, when it was 20 degrees and
snowing, Christmas day was in the 50s and pretty much dry. The trail
was deserted except for me and hundreds of robins. They were
absolutely everywhere, chirping up a storm and zooming from tree to tree.

Then, on Boxing Day someone had success finding my Feathered Joy
letterbox in Iowa. This box has been in place since last March and
this is the first I'd heard of its being found. Only one other pair of
boxers had written me long ago when they tried and failed on the hunt.
I absolutely loved hearing that it's alive and well.

Finally, Santa brought me the Japanese rotary drill/punch that I had
been lusting after for months and months. It drills perfect round
holes, just the thing for bookbinding. Up 'til now, I had settled for
a hammer punch, since this slick drill was out of my budget but now I
have very own ultimate tool. All in all, I was gifted with a wonderful
Christmas. Funhog
