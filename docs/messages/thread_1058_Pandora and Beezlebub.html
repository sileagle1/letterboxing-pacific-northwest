Pandora and Beezlebub

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-01-06 04:48:01 UTC
Like any devoted letterboxers, Windairen and I were not to be dissuaded from
letterboxing by a mere wind chill factor of zero degrees! We had a letterboxing date
today and were gonna keep it, no matter what!

Figuring Pandora's Powell's letterbox to be a good choice for a day like today, we
began our quest in the nice warm confines of the largest privately owned bookstore
in America. We opened the doors to the box's hiding place, prodded and poked. No
letterbox! Highly disappointed, we decided to move on to Plan B. I think we now know
that B stands for Bad but we hadn't figured that out yet.

We headed down to Pioneer Courthouse Square, four pages of clues in our hands.
This was not for four letterboxes, mind you, but for the one measly box that Ryan so
thoughtfully hid for our boxing pleasure. After a good thirty minutes standing out in
the arctic blast deciphering the first part of this evil clue we ran into the conveniently
located Starbuck's and guzzled hot drinks.

Reinforced, we headed on to the next stopping point on this frigid tour. Conveniently
enough, we'd been sent to the wrong location by Mr. Carpenter. We figured out where
we were really supposed to be shortly. Snow dust skittered down the street as we
began to work on the next section of this hideous clue. About a third of the way
through, I noticed that my dripping nose had left a frozen trail on the front of my
coat. We looked at each other, looked at the remaining two and a half pages of code
and decided that this was entirely enough. Beezlebub and his nefarious clue would
have to await another day.

A much better plan was a stop at Dan and Louis Oyster Bar for chowder and blueberry
pie. Yum!!! Our grand finale was a bookbinding session in the warmth of my very
messy kitchen. There were no new stamps in our logbooks today but we each ended
the afternoon with a cool new book to put inside yet another letterbox somewhere,
someday.
