From: callik4lb (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-02-26 18:15:38 UTC
I didn't receive any suggestions for a TOTW from anyone, so I'm going
to post something that's of interest to me - be careful about spoilers
when you respond - I'm famous for sticking my foot in my mouth in that
way. Let's hear about the first letterbox you found and why it was
memorable (if it was). Mine was the Tetherow House, placed in Central
Oregon by funhog. We carved some stamps at my son's house and set off
for our first LB adventure. We had quite a time finding it, and when
we had just about given up this lady came down to the river with her
dog and seemed very interested in what we were doing. I thought for a
very long time afterwards that she must have been funhog and that she
was probably having a great chuckle at our comments and consternation.
I know now it wasn't funhog, but until you get to know a placer in
person, and all you have to go on is their trail name, the mental
image you form can be quite a ways from reality.

My first box (Old Blue was not into it until a couple of months later) was
"The Davis Box" in Davis, CA May 10, 2002. (The original placer is
who-knows-where and Ryan has adopted it) Since I live here and know the area the 2-page
clue took you through (The UCDavis campus) I jumped to the final couple of
paragraphs. Yes, I cheated on my very first box!! The last 3 words of the clue
(at that time) were, "raise the roof!" They wanted me to stand on a chair and
push on the ceiling tile to get this box!!! Can you imagine a Newbie in a
public place doing such a thing?? I was far too shy and waited until the next
day when I could bring my daughter to stand guard--and take a photo of me
finding the box!! (I pasted it in the first page of my first log book!)

Today, the box is no longer in such a precarious place (due to an
unfortunate 'security alert' post-9-11, when some unfortunate 'boxers got caught and
touched off a 'lock down'!) I'll tell you, it was a long time before I found a
box as exciting as that!!

From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-01 19:53:31 UTC
My first letterbox was Rattlesnake Ledge here in the Seattle area. It
was the only letterbox in the Seattle area at the time. The rest were
all down in Portland. I would drive down to Portland on day trips to
find Der Mad Stamper letterboxes! I planted 3 letterboxes here in
Seattle and now just look how the area has grown! It just goes to
prove that if you plant them, they will come. :-)

The tree where Rattlesnake Ledge LB was hidden fell down last year and
Ryan and I planted a new Rattlesnake Ledge Letterbox to replace the
original one. I was so sad to go up there and see that the original
was gone.


-Amanda from Seattle

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-04 09:36:57 UTC-08:00
After stumbling upon virtual letterboxes while looking for something else online, I was excited about the idea of regular letterboxing, but skeptical because I figured there would be none in this area. Lo and behold, there were TWO. My friend, Chrysotile, and I went to find these two and one further away. I tried explaining the concept to her, but she didn't really understand until we found our first box.

It was at Bradley Lake Park (where one of my earlier planted boxes is now, too) in the roots of a tree. It has since disappeared...does anybody know what happened to Go Fish? It seemed a pretty good spot to me, though bugs liked to gather on top of it. I'd never been bit on the toe by an ant before! After Bradley, we went to Wildwood Park to find the box there by Flowerlion. Our last stop for the day was Amanda's Flaming Geyser box. We went all the way up the hill and came back down without finding it! It turns out the flaming geyser wasn't actually flaming that day, and we mistook it for a random pit that had been used as a trash dump. Disappointing as that was, we did find the box and actually left the park before sundown.

Chrysotile called her mom as we were leaving to let her know she'd made it out alive (you know how mothers are...) and her mother asked, "Did you find that....thing you were looking for?" haha.

We actually had our own hand-carved stamps for that trip, and I used mine for quite a while before it desintegrated. It's hard to believe that was nearly two years ago now.

But seriously, if anybody knows what happened to Go Fish, please let me know. I kind of wanted to plant a box in the same place, but not as much if it got stolen or vandalized or something...

ClioMouse
