Hello there and welcome to the group.

Just to address a couple of your questions started out carrying a
notebook with me that was about 4x6 just about half the size of your
normal every day paper. One of the first things I noticed was that
most "journals" you would get would be just fine (like the kind you
buy at the book store or something) However for me I noticed that
with a wire bound notebook the wire would bend and my pages would
tear. So I then cut all my images out and moved into a notebook of
about the same size but hard bound instead of wire bound. My next
mistake was that the notebook I bought had blue pages, the ink colors
didn't look quite so good and I didn't always get the image I wanted.

Now granted I am pickier about images than the average person I
suppose because those upside down images REALLY drive me nuts.

What I ended up doing then was carrying around index cards with me
when letterboxing, it saved on space and I could make sure to get the
perfect image before adding it to my logbook.

Then at some events I saw some of the really neat scrap booking sort
of thing that some others were doing and I thought that was just
awesome. Adding photos of where they had been letterboxing and
such.

So this Christmas Iron`Bear bought me a very large leather bound
scrapbook. It is the size of a photo album and has some really fancy
sheer cover sheets for each individual page. I think it is
beautiful. What I am now planning to done of these days is
surgically remove my images from my old logbook and transplant them
into this new scrapbook along with all the recent images I have
collected on the index cards. I figure when it is finally done it
will make a beautiful coffee table book that invites tons of
discussion when we have company.

However I probably won't be bringing it to many events because I
won't want it to get damaged in travel. (I'm not sure though, I
suppose it depends on the schedule of events and what type of
gathering it was)

Just some thoughts from someone that has taken a long time to figure
out how they really wanted to do their letterbox logbook.

---

To address your other question, the size of your stamp totally
depends on you. Your average logbook is about the size of an index
card. One of those little mead notebooks seems to be the run of the
mill. However lots of people use different size logbooks in each
letterbox depending on the location. I for example rarely plant a
large letterbox and often do homemade logbooks that around about 2x3
my microboxes tend to have even smaller logbooks, and my hitchhikers
too, sometimes as small as 1x1. What I ended up doing was creating
my image the size and shape I wanted it to be and for a long time
when I would find a letterbox that has a logbook that my entire stamp
wouldn't fit in I just use a portion of the stamp, (the head for
example) After awhile I decided to just have two stamps one that I
carry around as a "Traveler" that is much smaller than my normal
signature stamp that I use to stamp in if the logbook is smaller and
then my normal signature stamp itself. I don't exchange the second
one in signature stamp exchanges however. But if someone knows to ask
me for "a little bit of chaos" then they get that image too, as a
Traveler in their logbook.
