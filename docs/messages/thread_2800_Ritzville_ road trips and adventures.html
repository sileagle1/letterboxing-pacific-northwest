Hi all,

Thunderbird, you didn't miss a thing. Wood-aug's placement in Ritzville is missing so Ritzville was added to the subject name. Hx6 is doing a road trip though Portland. So you lucky birds get to travel to Montana again, hey? We're hoping to make a trip to Spokane again this year. I'd like to do some boxing over the Idaho border there. If I'm not mistaken (no surprise if I am LOL) aren't there some new boxers in ID now? Or are they down south further?

Thank you for sharing your letterboxing story, Colls. Letterboxing brings adventure to life, hey?! . . . but then there are those moments in the dark and cold where you wonder how you got there!! Ha ha ha. Oh, the stories we can tell!! . . . and the fun we have along the way!

Poison oak is our latest adventure that makes us stop and ask ourselves, "Is this really smart?"
Wood-aug placed a box right in poison oak. I went to find it and didn't notice the PO until I was eye-to-leaf while replacing the box! Well, because of my health situation it became a pretty worrisome issue a week later while boxing in the Trout Lake area. Our adventure included midnight runs to the Hood River Safeway for remedies, calls to San Francisco to reach the doc-on-call while trying to find cell service on a phone with a dying battery and way too much angst . Thank God the PO didn't go systemic or do anything serious. My doc is telling me to avoid the woods. Oh man, that's like telling a fish to avoid the water!! Luckily W-a isn't allergic to PO so he wasn't effected.

So the moral of the story is . . . when searching for boxes, keep your head about you! MAN, IS THAT HARD TO DO WHEN YOU'RE looking for your next rubber fix!! . . . oh what these wonderful treasures put us through!

Yup, letterboxing brings adventure to life!

Kurous Jo
