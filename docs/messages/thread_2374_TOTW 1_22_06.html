From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2006-01-22 17:34:26 UTC
Good Topic! Okay, here are my useful and un-useful tools:

I actually have 4 useful tools that I pretty much always use:
1. A piece of black felt for those two sided stamps that Maiden and
Pupp are notorious for. It helps to keep my hands clean and for some
reason, the ink doesn't rub off of the felt and onto my hands.
(must be magic).
2. A flashlight. I have had to really dig some well-hidden boxes out
of deep dark places lately.
3. Lots and lots of extra baggies! Since the weather is very soggy
here now-a-days, so are many of the stamps and logbooks.
4. A piece of plastic to lay down when I want to sit on a log or put
my letterboxing gear down. Another great tool in soggy weather.

Here is what I spent good money on and always stays home:
My walking stick. I saw someone else with one and had to have one.
Now I just find a stick on the trail and use that. There are plenty
out there on the trails.

Camp Fire Lady

Most useful tool in your letterboxing gear?

A monocular, a digital camera, halogen flashlight &
single 'foxglove' glove. I never leave home without the first two
of these, never go LBing without all four.


Least useful item?

GPS. DH bought me one a few months after I started LBing, used it
twice and then it was stolen when our car was broken into. Never
found a need for it when letterboxing.


Example of the monocular can be seen here:

http://www.birdwatchersgarden.com/index.asp?
PageAction=VIEWPROD&ProdID=330

I purchased mine at a market in Seoul but this is close to what it
looks like. I love it! I use it all the time especially
birding/boat watching along the water--even when I'm stuck
in traffic/stoplights to investigate things further beyond
traffic/signs, etc. Comes in really handy!

From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-25 16:10:36 UTC
I find that I really like having a map of the area. Since I do so much
of my letterboxing away from home, it is really important for me to
have a good street map to navigate my way from Park to Park!
Especially when the letterboxing clues are nebulous or mysterious! A
map of the park also helps to interpret clues.

Duct Tape is a nice thing to have along too, A little bit can help to
mend a broken or cracked box!

What I don't need so much: my compass. It spends most of its time in
the bottom of my bag and it really doesn't work well anyway as it
always has a bubble in it. I always get a compass with a bubble in it!
aaaargh. Unless I am doing Compass Conundrum clues from Mapsurfer or
Green Torture, I mean Tortuga, then I find I can get along without it.

-amanda from seattle
