Central OR LB update

From: Jess (jmwestin@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-25 18:59:58 UTC
After a four-day camping trip in Central OR, I was able to explore a
bunch or letterboxes and introduce a friend (Kokopelli) to the lovely
hobby of letterboxing!

Here's an update of the boxes we found (and didn't find):

#1--Waterfowl Highway
Great box and great stamp!

#2--Crooked River Bridge
Kokopelli had great stories of bringing her godkids here. It was a
beautiful, warm windy day and the bridges are beautiful!

#3--Crooked River Ranch #1
On the way down to this box, I saw a Bandit camper that looked
suspiciously like the one Funhog was staying in at the McIver Campout
last summer! Hmm...could it be?

#4--Crooked River Ranch #2
Another good stamp! Time didn't permit us to get the other 2 however-
-maybe next time?

#5--Eagle Crest
This looks like a great little resort getaway! And a great stamp too!

We also attempted Funhog's box at Eagle Crest, but to no avail!

#6--Cline Falls
Beautiful falls!

#7--Tetherow House
Wow, all I could think of as I walked around this house was what it
must have been like to live there 100 years ago! Another great stamp
by Funhog!

#8--Blue Fisherman
It took us a while to find this stamp because there were a few old
trees lying across the path, but eventually Kokopelli figured it out!

#9--Dry River Canyon #1
We had no trouble finding the first stamp, but spent a considerable
amount of time searching for the second stamp--with no luck! After
about 45 minutes of searching where we thought it would be, we sadly
gave up.

#10--Redmond Redhawk
Very impressive stamp!

#11--My New Niece
Cute park, but there was some guy that was really interested in what
we were doing, so it took us a while to complete the operation
without him seeing.

#12--Sam Johnson Park
Great stamp--didn't see any rockchucks here, but saw them at many
other lb sites.

#13--Odin Falls
Another beautiful falls!

#14--Tetherow Crossing
I think this may have been the most impressive stamp I got on this
trip! It was the perfect end to letterboxing day #3.

#15--Sawyer Park
Because of construction on the street, we actually drove past this
park twice before we found it! Cute park though! There were
numerous picnics going on! I'm bummed that we missed the two new
boxes that were placed there the same day that we found this box!

#16--Drake Park
I absolutely loved this park! This was definitely a high-traffic
area though! It took us a while to get teh box replaced without
being seen.

#17--Lost Puppy
I was amazed by the huge houses that we saw on the way to this park!
To anyone else going for this box--although Red Tuff Gulch is not on
the trail map, it is out there! Great stamp--we couldn't believe
that it was hand-carved!

Thank you to everyone who has hidden stamps in this area! It was
actually the first time I have ever traveled to central Oregon! I
think the Hoppy Hunters were hitting the boxes about the same time we
were--on some occasions they had just stamped in before us on the
same day. It also looks like we just missed Amanda and Ryan by a
week on most boxes. Anyway, it was a great four days of boxing and a
much needed getaway!

Jess
