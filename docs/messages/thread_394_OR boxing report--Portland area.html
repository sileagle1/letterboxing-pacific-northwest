OR boxing report--Portland area

From: (PNWEXPLR@aol.com) | Date: 2003-07-08 23:42:52 UTC-04:00
Here's a report on some local boxes:

Bon Appetit was yummy! All 3 boxes alive & well. Clue #3 says "You will
come to a cross roads where gone are the days when you could sail off a boulder
on a rope swing, alas!" but there is indeed a swing there--newly placed, it
appears. I'm assuming the clues could now be rewritten (tho my boxing partner
reads the clue to simply suggest that we're too old to go swinging! Who,
us????).

I interrupted my Blues Festing on 4th of July to track down esp--found the
box with no trouble, but not the nearby geocache that others mentioned finding.
With this box I indoctrinated a nice teenager (who'd innocently come to
listen to the blues) into our curious hobby.

Tribute is alive & flaming away. But hey, Wingnut, the pages are coming out
of the log. (Glad to find this box--it was the only one we managed to find
at the Arboretum--looks like we need to do some homework before attempting to
take a wonderful tour again! Shoot, we never even met up with the flight
attendant!)

Lorikeet is quite discreet. A group of LBing kids had stamped in (ages were
listed with stamps)--we didn't recognize any of them. One side of the first
log page is filled up.

Winking Eagle rewarded Heather when she properly followed directions despite
her better judgement!


Gosh, these were all such clever/well-done stamps! Portland sets the bar
high. Thanks to Hedgehog, soggy2pair, Wingnut, Brett & Penny, and Windairen and
the Plundering Bunny for a lotta fun!!!!

--Wild Woman
