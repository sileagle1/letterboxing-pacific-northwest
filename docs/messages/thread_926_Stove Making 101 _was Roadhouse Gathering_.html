Alrighty, then, a MacGyver stove class it is. Anyone interested must
bring at least two soda cans (extra in case you think you might make
a mistake). Pepsi and Coke cans seems to work well.

If you REALLY want to make a MacGyver stove, there are directions for
it online at http://www.pcthiker.com/pages/gear/pepsistove.shtml
which is what I used to make my stove. They list a lot of other
optional tools (screwdriver, sand paper, drill bits, file, cardboard,
etc.). You don't really need any of that. Two soda cans at the very
least. Scissors, X-acto knife, and something thin and sharp (certain
nails work) are highly desireable. Those are essential for making a
stove, but you can use mine if you didn't bring it. (Which may
entail a wait if someone else is using them at the time.) The rest
really is optional and to experience the rugged simpleness of the
trail, we won't be using any of the rest. ;o)

Farewell!
