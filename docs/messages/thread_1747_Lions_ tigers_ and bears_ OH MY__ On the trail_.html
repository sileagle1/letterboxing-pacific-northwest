Lions, tigers, and bears, OH MY!! On the trail!

From: erfellie (hemenekawan@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-11-05 18:42:37 UTC

I went out in search of a wonderful LB yesterday, like we all do on
the days we skip work and call in sick. (cough-hack-achem!) *wink*
But this time was a new adventure, we encountered 2 black bears along
the way ON THE TRAIL. Ironically, the night before departure I was
reading "Letterboxing info safety articles" and it mentioned what to
do when you come along a bear on the trail. If its a grizzly, well
your screwed, but with black bears it said to yell and roar and
be "bigger" than the bear to scare it away, and what helpful
information that was! One of these furry teddy bears was so close to
me I could have hugged him and pinched his cheeks, and of course our
two trail dogs walked right past it and didnt even notice it!! (I'm
trading them in for new ones tomorrow)So I yelled and roared and
scared all the wild life away and waved my arms (and still the dogs
did nothing) but it scared away the bear, and a very short distance
down the trail there was another bear which the dogs did chase away
(finally!) but we called them back. So moral of the story, if you see
a black bear on your trails, be bigger than it is and dont run away
from it, you have to be the dominant one. (and once it is well gone,
then you can run away in fear). We were in southern oregon in the
Wild and Scenic Rogue Wilderness, prime bear country, but they are in
lots of other places too! So just keep it in mind when your out there
hiking, usually dogs (unless you have mine) will scare them away. Be
careful out on the trails, letterboxers are an endangered species
too, lets not get any killed. :o)
