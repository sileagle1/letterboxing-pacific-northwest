Intro & tips request

From: Shelli Wiebersch (ShellisJunk@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-12-05 10:11:58 UTC
Hi All,

I just joined your Yahoo group and thought I'd introduce myself.
I live in Puyallup with my hubby and 2 children, Elizabeth - 3 and
Thomas - 5 months. My little girl has really been into pretending to
be a pirate lately. She often drags me off to some corner of the
house to show me where she has found some "hidden treasure" (costume
jewelry I let her play with). So when I read a snippet in the most
recent issue of Family Fun magazine and went online to find out what
this "letterboxing" thing was, I instantly got hooked! Imagine
Elizabeth's excitement (and mine, too!) when I told her there was
real life treasure hidden in our favorite parks just waiting for us
to find!! We've found our first two boxes and can't wait to go find
more.

I'm so wildly grateful that this wonderful pastime/sport/hobby
exists. To be honest, I often find other things to do with our time
rather than going out on a walk and enjoying the beauty God gave us.
There is always housework, classes, work, friends and a host of
other things on my to-do list. Going for a walk rarely makes it into
our schedule. Letterboxing has now vaulted nature walks and "treasure
hunting" up in the top of our priorities and I pray that it stays
there for years to come. What a gift we've been given!

I would very much like to give back by planting some boxes for
other folks to find, and I'm wondering if you have any tips for a
newbie. The first 2 boxes we found had something written in them,
but we were so excited to stamp our books and replace the box
without being seen that we didn't take time to read it. Should I
have something written inside my box to explain what it is, incase a
non-Lb'er finds it? If so, what kind of things have you seen in or
written in letterboxes you've found/placed? Any other ideas would be
welcome! Thanks again and happy letterboxing!

~Shelli

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2006-12-05 15:09:18 UTC
Welcome Shelli and Family to the wonderful world of letterboxing!
First of all, my best advice would be to find some more boxes! This
is the best way to see how others have done a letterbox and how it
should be labeled, camoflauged, baggied, etc. Then I would check out
this site:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers,
and the FAQ's section of the letterboxing.org site.

If you still have questions, which you probably won't because this
should give you all the information that you need and then some, we
would be glad to help you out.
Welcome again to this wonderful hobby and I hope to meet you
sometime at a local gathering. Happy Hunting!
Camp Fire Lady
