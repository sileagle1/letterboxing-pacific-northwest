Between you PNW folk and I:
I have all the mechanisms for a box status set up already.
It just isn't active.
It fits into the whole scheme of tracking your PFX online.
You say you've been to a box, so it gets verified and you get a tally for your F
count.
And so on . . .

Right now, your best bet is to email the box's placer and ask for a clue or ask
them to put a note on their box that it might be gone.

But, if they're not an active 'boxer anymore and the webmasters have control of
their clue, should we put notes on boxes? Maybe just one person couldn't find
it. Do we put notes after two people say it's missing? Three?
You get the point.

Personally, I removed two boxes in the transition, because a nearby box's log
was full of entries saying that no one could find those boxes. I figured they
were long gone.

There probably isn't a right answer to please everyone, as usual.

Wes

Re: [LbPNW] Missing boxes [was Dead fly wings]

From: mudstumpers (mudstumpers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-06-11 08:00:19 UTC-07:00
I'm just thinking that if someone plants a box, they are responsible for it or at least call it an orphan.

If it is reported missing, the boxer and the placer should communicate privately to confirm locations. ie. I tried to reach Funhog but her email was full regarding the Metolius box. But she still responded to the talk page, well done, Funhog.

If the placer fails to respond and several others have had the same response, the box should be pulled from the clue list.

If the placer wants it back on the list, they can replace it and have it added back onto the clue list and maybe modify the clues.

If the box is so difficult to find that the boxer has to "bulldoze" the area to locate it, it fails the "leave no trace" idea of the game and it needs a change of location or better clues to pinpoint the location anyway.

The placer needs to stay active with their boxes, most seem to be well maintained simply from the responses of finders, but if they are not willing to verify and update the status when it goes missing, for example, like Ryan's webpage does, "Alive and well as of ddmmyy" , or "Last Checked: Never" ,then maybe a consensus or mass hunt should take place to put a box to bed (that could be fun in itself). If nobody finds it, it gets removed from the list, end of frustration.

Don't get me wrong, we are having a ball trying to find the last 2 of Ryan's "Murder in Gabriel Park", but we are stymied. We have been told that all 6 are alive and well, that's the way it should be, we know they are there, we just don't know where. But the ones that have had no response or acknowledgement from the placer should go byebye.
