Without a doubt, one of the tackiest themes I've encountered is Ryan's
Crappy Stamp Letterbox. Back before the Mighty Tortuga was the wizard
behind the curtain at AQ, he had quite a knack for stirring the pot on
the Big List. (In fact he wasnt' even the Green Tortuga yet, still
using his cheery, little purple spider sig stamp and calling himself
"Ryan" of all things!) One of his more memorable displays of faulty
tact was his less than favorable commentary on the letterbox stamps of
the time that he had seen in the Seattle area.

As he was bombarded with none-too-pleasant responses, he tried and
tried to dig himself out of the mire. With each post he seemed to dig
his hole deeper and deeper. I laughed harder at every subsequent
message. Fortunately, he is more than able to laugh at himself (and
his sense of tact) and as a result created this letterbox. Tacky it
is, but the quality of Seattle area stamp carving DID improve vastly
after The Green One made himself into Turtle Soup. BTW anyone gone for
the RKOT box? Funhog

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-08 22:11:18 UTC
You know I've been racking my brain trying to come up with an idea
for the tackiest or wackiest letterbox we've ever found and it's
been difficult to come up with one. Ryan's Crappy Stamp is up there
at the top.

I'm thinking that there was a box that we found in a cemetery in CA
that was up there, too. It was in one of these 'hippy' type areas
of a cemetery (not sure what you'd call it) but the area had all
these sorts of 'bizarre' burial plots--some were ornately detailed
with wrought iron, marble and stone, many many others seemed to have
plastic trinkets, fake grass, foam boulders and other weird stuff
you just wouldn't expect to see in a cemetery. There were also lots
of P-Patch types of gardens interspersed throughout the area with
picnic tables and BBQ grills!

I don't remember the box all that much except that I think it had a
goofy store bought stamp that had nothing to do with the area. At
the time I was thinking that the planter could have really gone hog-
wild with that one--using the 'interesting' plots as inspiration for
the clue and stamp or creating some mysterious story about a
fictitious individual who is buried there. It could have been a
really incredible letterbox. Now that I think about it, there was a
geocache there, too--as I seem to recall lots of non-letterboxers
having stamped into the log, too.
