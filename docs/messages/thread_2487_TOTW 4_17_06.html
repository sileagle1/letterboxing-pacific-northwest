TOTW -- Do you have a particular source for inspiration for letterbox
clues or placements?

My first and foremost favorite ideas for letterboxes stem from digging
into the local history of an area. Then, secondly I like to go for long
forgotten 'kitsch Americana'--just to keep some of the old 'mod'
history revived among the public consciousness a bit longer before it
fades away into oblivion. Then, finally I get inspired by locations--
just for their beauty or unique features & usually carve/hide something
on the spot.

I read incessantly so I often pore over all sorts of materials for
inspiration--newspapers, internet, old hiking books, travel
guides/brochures, maps--you name it. Sometimes I just find an image
that I like and think may work well for a letterbox and then eventually
I'll find the 'ideal' spot for it. I've got an entire photo/negative
album & another file folder that contains ideas for future stamps or
impressions of stamps I've already carved. I also have several computer
files that contain ideas for letterbox images/locations. In contrast, I
try to avoid 'commercial' type stuff for our letterboxes--TV shows,
tourist attractions (Disneyland?), copyrighted stuff but do have a
couple boxes that may fall into this arena because they bear special
meaning to us, etc.

daelphinus
