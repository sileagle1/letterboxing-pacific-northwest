Fright Fest '03

From: Steve S. (kerjin@myndworx.com) | Date: 2003-09-21 19:12:35 UTC-07:00


Come one, come all! Team Rayvenhaus is planning the Fright Fest '03 Gathering for Oct 25th, 2003, the Saturday before Halloween, from 10:00am till 2:00 pm (or later, the park doesn't close till 1/2 hour after sunset), at Wapato Park in Tacoma, Wa. We are carving the Event Stamp, the Monster Mash stamp, which is a rare picture of the Crypt Keeper 5 band of some fame. We are also setting a series of stamps for the event, and will not be publishing the clues before hand, with a bonus stamp for those that successfully find the others in the series. All of the stamps we are going to carve will be of a frightful nature, and I'm not referring to the carving ability of either of us when I say that. (Grin)

What I would like to also offer is for any one who can not make it to this gathering, to go ahead and carve up a stamp, make a box and ship it to us and we'll plant and maintain it, listing you as the creator of that box. If you'd like to take us up on this, contact RayvenMom at rayvenmom@myndworx.com and she'll give you the snail mail address to send it to. We're also planning the premier release of our newest Hitchhiker, the Voyager.

I invite everyone that can make it to come and join us, meet the people behind the posts. This is a pot luck dinner, bring a covered dish, a salad, something to eat, enough for your group and we'll provide the burgers once again, along with the chips. Along with the burger fixings. Buns, Mayo, Ketchup and Mustard. Please give us a simple RSVP to RayvenMom, Myself at rayvenhuas@myndworx.com or here on the mailing list so I know how many burgers I'm gonna burn, I mean cook!

Bring your own soda's or whatever, (minus alcohol of course, this is a dry park) to drink

Looking forward to seeing everyone there!

Steve of Rayvenhaus

We certainly hope that you can squeeze some time to be able to make it, we
missed you stamp at the last event (Grin). As far as costume, well, I don't
think anyone would even notice as there are 2 geocaches in that area
already, and, speaking of Dreaded Geocachers, let me share this story. When
we went there yesterday before announcing the gathering, as sot of a recon
of the area, we in fact did stumble across a group of dreaded geocachers in
their natural habitat.


This is not a Geocache Bash, this is an observation of what I saw. Had they
been Letterboxers, I'd still tell the story, good or bad.

There was a group of 6, a Man, a Woman and three kids of various ages, all
clustered around the bridge at Wapato Lake Park. They were literally
ripping through the underbrush looking for the cache, tossing the debris
into the lake. It was almost funny to watch, till one realized the damage
being done. I sauntered over and proceeded to speak to the man concerning
his children tossing the garbage into the lake, he said that his wife had
lost her contact and they were searching for it. I returned to where we
were sitting on the bench and watched they thrash around for 10 or so more
minutes when one child screamed I found it and they clustered around a spot
in the underbrush for 5 minutes then climbed back up and proceeded to
depart. Imagine my surprise when two of the children climbed on their bikes
and headed off in a different direction. Apparently one is supposed to grab
random strangers to help locate the cache.

Oh well.

Sorry to crosspost, just wanted to make sure I caught everyone..........

Just a reminder to let you know that 'The PNWLb Fright Fest '03' is gearing up to be a frightfully awesome event, if I may say so myself!!!

(We've included the PNWLb in the name as there is another Fright Fest in this area, at Wild Waves and we didn't want to overstep into copyright areas, so, 'The PNWLb Fright Fest '03' was born!)

We've got more boxes that you can shake a black cat at. We've received 1 from ScoutDogs and 1 from Chuck Straub, both incredible stamps. We've got three from Fudrick and we've got 6 of our own. We've got, of course, the Event Stamp, which I do believe is a fairly good stamp, themed of course. And last, but not least, we have at least one hitchhiker to be released on that day. You'll witness the inaugural flight of the USS Voyager as she starts off on her maiden voyage.

There's a rumor that, although the Crypt Kicker 5 has recently had a breakup and is now the Crypt Kicker 5(-2), we could still have a stamp by them to include, though, with the legalities surrounding the rumored breakup, one never knows.

Rayvenhaus is providing the Burgers and the burger fixings, along with a big bowl of Watergate Salad, provided by RayvenGranny! Everyone needs to bring a covered dish of some sort and drinks for themselves.

The park is Kid friendly and there is a play area, so don't worry about that, the RayvenKids will be there playing.

And let us NOT forget the raffle! Oh yes, the PNWLb raffle of The Official PNWLb Supplies Box. We've received enough supplies to create 5 boxes with, and we'll be raffling them off to everyone that attends. The contents are as follows: 1 4x6 sheet of 'Pink Stuff', 5 containers, 5 pens, 5 notebooks, 5 Ziploc bags (lg), 5 Ziploc bags (sm), First Finder Award sheets, 'About Letterboxing' sheets, 2 2x3 wooden backs and 3 2x2 wooden backs. All in all, everything needed to create and place 5 Letterboxes! And one lucky attendee will get it all!

We look forward to seeing everyone there!

Come one, come all to the Pacific NorthWest Letterbox Fright Fest at Wapato
