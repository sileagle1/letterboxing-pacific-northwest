Hitch hiker Protocol?

From: A Coast Guard Family (letterboxing2004@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-08 04:38:20 UTC
Okay, when one finds a hitch hiker, what is the protocol?
I see an email address of the starter....I emailed that person to tell
them the status and where it was found.
Now, do I stamp MY stamp in there? or the stamp of the place I found
the box? or the place that I drop the box???
Is the goal to keep the box moving? or to get it as far away from our
area as possible? or back to the owner?

Any and all advice is appreciated.

Smile.
ISignASLily


Re: Hitch hiker Protocol?

From: David (davidwcc@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-09 05:34:07 UTC
If the HH is not stamped into the box you found it in, you can stamp
it in. The box you found it in should be stamped in the HH. You can
exchange stamps with the HH box any time before you hide the HH again
in another box. When you do leave the HH in another box you should do
a stamp exchange with the new box, so the next finder doesn't have to.

David from Beaverton OR

see where it's been. I then try to send it on to a new location.

area. It found it's way out to the Oregon coast...
