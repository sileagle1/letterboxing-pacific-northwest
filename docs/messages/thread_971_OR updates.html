OR updates

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2003-11-27 06:08:06 UTC
I headed out this afternoon to stretch my legs before a day of overindulgence. In my travels, I found three
letterboxes, sent a couple of hitch hikers on their merry way and found another one that needs to find a home.
One of the hitchers set free today was created by ace carver Franzsolo from Ohio. This one is a real treat. I
also decided to take a look at my Sandy Delta box which I haven't visited for quite a while.

It seems that no one else has visited it for quite a while either, the last entry being made last February. The
box is just fine. The area has undergone amazing changes. Anyone who has been there before owes it to
themselves to go have a look. You won't recognize the place. In the past, acres and acres were swallowed up
by the dreaded Himalayan Blackberry. Now there are huge expanses of tilled earth with hundreds of baby
trees planted on some areas. There are no more stiles, fences or gates, just open land with lots of birds! This
location has undergone dramatic changes every time I've been there and today was no exception. Funhog



OR updates

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-06-26 23:11:25 UTC
The Ace of Fun is back from sabbatical. The clue is slightly different and has been
altered on the LbNA website. Go for it, Melanie!

My poor little Oaks Bottom box was most unhappy being crowded out of it's little
apartment by that big ol' letterbox that someone had put in the same hiding place. I
went to have a look at it yesterday and just as I had suspected, the box was so big
that it was plainly visible from the trail. Since there was no contact info in the box, I
took it upon myself to christen the usurper, "The Big Y" letterbox and evicted it. It
now is living happily in its own home along the same trail. The new clue can be found
inside the Oaks Bottom box.

One good thing I can say about this box is that it is downright HUGE and would make
a dandy Hitch Hiker Hostel. None of its contents are currently protected by plastic
bags and since I had none with me yesterday, I couldn't do the necessary first aid. If
you're headed that way please take a couple of extras and wrap up the contents.
Hopefully, I haven't spoiled some kids' fun but it really was necessary to move The Big
Y to protect it and it's roomate from accidental discovery. Anyone know who owns
this one? Funhog
