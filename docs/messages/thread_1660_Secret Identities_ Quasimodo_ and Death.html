>I don't know it's not feeling new and original anymore; you can only do it
>differently so many times before it feels like a copy cat.

As the poor, dead and misunderstoon Sir Isaac Newton once wrote me, "If I
have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants."

>But I would enjoy them just as much if the clues were done in the same
>fashion and
>the people doing them were not hiding behind masks.

My face, is (among other descriptive terms) horribily disfigured--a term
meaning something so ugly and horrifing that it makes small babies cry and
call for their poor, dead mothers--because of a fall into an acid pit while
escaping a herd of stampeding elephants during a lighting storm. Quasimodo
would be horrified to wake with my face, but to call it a mask?! Your words
have wounded me, and now I will go back to my dark, dreary cave to cry while
waiting for a clue to the whereabouts of those unlucky and unfortunate
Baudelaire orphans.

With all due respect,

Lemony Snicket
