Re: We're not letterboxing but...

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-02 22:57:56 UTC

Hi there--I think I 'hyperlinked' the campsite info website to the
blog. If the link isn't working, it was the Valley of the Rogue State
Park campsite--just off I-5. The reason I liked the site was because
it was quick on/off access from the interstate, inexpensive & had FREE
hot showers!!! Another nice thing is that, lately, I've been camping
alone with my kids--I try to find campsites with LOTS of other people
for security/safety reasons--the RVers seem to be nice, normal folks in
most cases.

Most other campgrounds we find (esp WA state) you must pay for
showers. If you're lucky at this particular campsite, they may even
have one of their 2 yurts available--so you can 'camp' indoors for
about $27/night. One drawback is that the interstate noise is not too
far off--but that seems to be a problem with many 'state park'
campsites we find (even ones relatively off the beaten path like
Larrabee SP up near Bellingham, WA)--if you're tent camping it's a
problem but in the winter we 'car' camp so it muffled the sound a bit.

Camping with kids can be a lot of fun but it can also be rather
chaotic! The trick is packing 'exactly' what you need and nothing
else. We've streamlined our packing list quite a bit--mostly down to
bare essentials and many items have multiple purposes--ie plates can be
used as bowls, lantern converts to a flashlight/British torch. My kids
have some rules for camping, too--like limited toys, we continue with
school and our typical 'bedtime' routine--reading, studying and such.
As we're homeschooling, I must bring along our 'school books', too--so
they add some bulk to our cargo a bit but I whittle those down to the
bare essentials we'll need for the trip. I also carry a water filter
pitcher--so lugging liters upon liters of water isn't always necessary--
if I have access to a spigot then I can fill up for the day whereever.
Hope this helps a bit. I suggest a couple of 'local' camping outings
to work through the 'glitches' that will always come up before you
venture out on some long distance trips. We still always manage to
forget one or two of those 'essential' items but after awhile--you
learn how to adapt and improvise i.e. 'MacGyver-ise' your way through
the trip. ;)

Enjoy! When we return back home from this trip, we plan to venture up
your way and explore quite a bit.

Blessings,

dvn2r ckr

PS The snake standoff in the Louisiana bayou was pretty interesting!
We'd seen lots of snakes on the trails before but we had never had
to 'fight' a snake(s) for a letterbox! ;) {{disclaimer--no snakes
were harmed in the hunting of this particular letterbox...}}
