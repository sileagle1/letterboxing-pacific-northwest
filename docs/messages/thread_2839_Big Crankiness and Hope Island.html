. . . Funhog, and Heyokahman, too! This box has been on my list to
find and as a Labor Day event we sought it out. We are fortunate to
own a canoe and launched from the Portland waterfront on the eastbank,
where Alder Creek also rents canoes. It was a fabulous day and a good
work-out for the guys. Greenmonkey and I got to sit back in style!
(It's a large canoe.) We had to transpose the clues as we were coming
from the north, but no worries mate, it was a breeze! Thanks for the
good time Kuku!!

If others of you are in the Puget Sound area and find this idea of
boat-boxing intriguing, please visit the box I planted last year
"Hundred Acre Island":
http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/showboxinfo.html?gBoxId=21362
I've updated the clues with a marine map. When we went there we came
from the west past Steamboat Island. Lots of jellyfish and seals in
the water. Super nice trip!!

This is a Washington State Park, and the GoNorthwest website refers to
it as the Puget Sound's best kept secret. You can pick apples, hike
the circular trail of the island and enjoy a nice afternoon on the
beach. Kayak rentals are available at Boston Harbor, just a few miles
north of Olympia.

Happy trails(on land and in water)!! Let me know if you go.
