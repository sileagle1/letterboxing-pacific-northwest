We just placed another letterbox in Puyallup at Wildwood Park:

No hiking necessary
Stroller Friendly
Easy

Butterflies don't have mouths with which to chew or eat. Instead,
they have a proboscis, which is like a straw, that they use to sip
nectar and juices. They don't taste with their proboscis, though.
Butterflies taste with their feet!

You'll find the third butterfly in the Butterfly Collection Series
sipping nectar if you'll follow the clues below.

Letterbox location: Wildwood Park
23rd Ave SE and 9th St SE,
Puyallup, Washington

Directions to letterbox:

There is no hike involved in this hunt. You'll be parking within just
a few feet of the letterboxes location. The challenge lies in
choosing a time when people will not be around because finding the box
discreetly will be impossible otherwise.

Choose a time of day, week, or year when the picnic shelters in the
park are not likely to be in use. (The middle of the week during the
day is pretty good.)

Upon entering the park take the first right turn following the arrow
for the play area.

Next follow the sign to Shelter #7.

Park right in front of Shelter #7.

Look for the tree with the mossy claw on the right side of the
shelter. The claw guards This butterfly while she sips her lunch.
She's well camouflaged, so look carefully.

~Millers 6 of Puyallup
