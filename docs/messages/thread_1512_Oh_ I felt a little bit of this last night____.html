Oh No!
First of all, I'm a Newbie and I treasure every box I
find and leave it in the same state I found it along
with my stamp. Every box I've found since I started
two weeks ago was in tact, hidden well, and I returned
the favor. And then yesterday I retrieved the Lacamas
Creek box, which was orphaned, to restore it and that
is complete and ready to be replanted with a few
surprises inside. So I know I don't fall into the
category below of "newbie's". Sigh.

However, I want to express my disdain when I went to
find the "Letterbox Where Are You" last night. I was
trucking through the dog park at 8:30 p.m. (which,
after the whole scenario at Hamburger Hamburger
Cheeseburger you think I wouldn't have done this
alone, but I did). Anyway, I got out of my Monday
night final exam REALLY early...rescued Lacamas Creek
then headed to the dog park in Vancouver. Was able to
stamp in at two books my children had already stamped
in at...however, when going to get the third one,
which was OUR FIRST ONE to find...it was GONE! I was
hurt. It was dark and I searched forever only to
realize it's gone. Got home, went online and sure
enough, the placer says the box had been vandalized.
Now WHO would do that? I thought only letterboxers
look at the sights and find the clues...then I
realized, with so much advertising lately that anyone
can go on the site and read the clues and be
malicious.

Very Sad. I left the Dog Park feeling empty because
I'd missed out on a VERY COOL stamp.

I agree with you Pandora...the lack of respect is
showing up in our hobby.

I do have to ask one question...what is GEOCACHING???
I read the article about the tube under the bridge and
the bomb squad but I don't understand what it is. Can
anyone of the PACNWLB answer this questions.

SMILE.
ISignASLily

Missing boxes are nothing new.... my poor departed Rock Creek #1 box was
stolen, returned, defaced and stolen again, all in the first year of its life.... This
was in 1999.

You certainly sound sincere in your letterboxing efforts. Don't let all of us old
schoolers get you down. In the beginning we were all trying to figure out how
to do this.

In regards to adopting letterboxes, I choose to consider them as an interactive
art piece. The artist may have carved a stamp, created clues and hidden the
box in a very artful manner. If I am in charge of someone else's art, my duty is
to change as little as possible. If the box and stamp are missing, then it is my
job to replace them in as similar a situation as I can. When that is not possible,
then IMHO, that box ought to be retired.

Box adoptions are a fairly new phenomenon. Some sort of accepted protocol is
needed so misunderstandings are less common.

G.Hornet
