Float Your Boat - What a trip!

From: SloMo (SloMo86@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-09-09 17:02:44 UTC-07:00
A big thank you to Home School Mommy for putting together the FYB gathering
in Spokane last weekend. HSM, indeed you are very ambitious - so many
boxes placed! No doubt this puts Liberty Lake on the map of destinations
for Lbers. And thank you to Colls who carved stamps (and not sure what all)
for the gathering. Also thanks to Thunderbird for the book binding class.

It was so nice to meet new people and have faces to go with trail names.
And of course see the T-bird family again.

We had a bit of a misadventure on the way home. While on Steptoe Butte
Wood-aug stepped with his toe and heard a pop - messed up his leg. The
Pullman doc thought it was a torn Achilles tendon but the orthopedic surgeon
here says it's a torn muscle. Poor guy. But he's a sport and hobbled
around placing and finding boxes anyway. We must have been quite a sight -
his strange halting gate and our dog limping along (Simon has a growth
between his pads that was quite raw from walking, poor thing). Funny,
usually I'm the one limiting our movement. What a change!

We learned a lot this trip - mainly that we suck at placing! {laugh} We
placed a record nine boxes this trip!! That is huge for us! But we learned
a lot from the experience. Wish we could have placed more in Spokane for
the Lb pioneers that are doing so much work there. Many of the boxes are in
far reaching places - some on the way to . . . well . . no where very
populated. {laugh} We hope to post the clues soon.

For the rest of you - There are lots of boxes already in Spokane proper (we
only had time to find 6). They are placed at various levels of physical
difficulty so there's something for everyone. If you go, don't miss Manito
Park!!! WAY cool place!! Who would have thought Spokane would have
something so grand! We can't wait to go back, see the rest of the park, and
find the boxes we missed there.

Colls, thanks for the great history you wrote up with your box in Rosalia.
Very neat location too. I won't say much just that it's an inspiring and
refreshing place for those from an urban environment. Nothing quite like
it here.

Colls, could you email me which Liberty Lk stamps you carved? I'd like to
note that in the log book. BTW, because of Todd's leg, we ended up camping
at Chief Timothy Park after all. It was neat to read the billboard which
connected the history together. Neat park.

Thanks again!
SloMo & Wood-aug
