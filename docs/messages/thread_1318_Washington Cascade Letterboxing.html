Washington Cascade Letterboxing

From: Danny McMillin (macdanny@mindspring.com) | Date: 2004-05-04 20:48:49 UTC-07:00
I took a trip to the Yakima River Canyon in Washington over the weekend. It
was a time for flyfishing, birdwatching, camping and a little letterboxing
on the side.

I field tested a new proto-type 6 person tent made by REI. It's huge, roomy
and lots of head room. Should be out in 2005.

I was able to find one of the two Yakima River Canyon boxes. The second box
in the series now appears to be on posted Private Property. Dang. The first
box, has a beautful stamp in a wonderful creek valley. It really is a must,
if you're in the canyon.

Then it was on to Umtanum Falls letterbox. Again it's in a beautiful,
peaceful spot. And the stamp is one of my favorites for sure.

I stopped by Irene Rhinehart Riverside Park for that letterbox. I was
unable to track it down.

Two out of four is not bad.

It was a great weekend in the Kittitas Valley.

And yes, the fishing was most excellent.
