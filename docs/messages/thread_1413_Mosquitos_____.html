Mosquitos!!!!!

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2004-06-29 17:56:26 UTC
Cat's Meow and I met up with Bookworm yesterday to look for a couple
of new boxes in the Vancouver area. We were able to locate two of
them and were "firsties" into the logbooks but had to turn back when
we went for the third because of the high concentration of mosquitos
in the area. After being eaten alive, the first thing I did when I
got home was find my bottle of mosquito repellent and put it in my
bag. I highly recommend that everyone do the same this time of the
year. I have heard on the news that a family in Southern California
has contracted the Nile Virus so it is sure to reach our region soon.
I wouldn't want to see any of you have to stay up all night itching
like we did. I have 21 bites and counting and Cat's Meow didn't fare
as well as I did since she wasn't as quick to swat them off of her
and she has some pretty interesting welts to prove it!
Take care all and pack that repellent in your letterboxing gear!

Camp Fire Lady (scratch, scratch, scratch, GGRRRRR!)

From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-06-29 22:16:39 UTC-07:00
Wow I didn't know someone got the Nile Virus in California - I do know that
the mosquitoes are in full force this summer. I hope people keep this in
mind when planting new boxes this season and try to keep us away from the
highly infested areas. I know that mosquito bites are not fun for anyone and
I am allergic to them, so I too have made repellant a staple in my bag this
season.



So far the only box that I have knowingly and willingly stomped through
mosquito infested areas for was Lemony Snicket. And I was very appreciative
of the warning Mr. Snicket gave us that there would be tons of little bugs
wanting to bit us and leave red sores on us because we were able to go
prepared.



I don't mind doing stuff like that if I know ahead of time -

Just my two cents - not worth much more than a couple penny's
