Hi all, just wanted to introduce myself. I am a new Letterboxer and
have been on the newboxers group for a couple of weeks or so.
Someone mentioned this group so I thought I'd check it out. I've
been reading the posts for maybe a week. I see a few of you go to
the newboxers group to help out. It is really appreciated.
Iron 'Bear and Pandora seem to visit frequently. You two are great.

I live just inside the north end of the Thurston County line. Just
above the Nisqually River. There seems to be many Letterboxes in
this area. I went out today and found a couple of more and meet
another Letterboxer along the way. Did my first Exchange.

My three children love it. Unfortunatly my wife does not share the
same enthusiasm as I. She does go along on the hikes and take
pictures though. She has her Scrapbooking and I have my
Letterboxing. It works out okay. At least I get great memmories of
the hunt and find.

I'm really bummed that I was not able to go to the meet at
BattleGround. I just learned about it too late and had other
plans. Can't wait until another.

Well, I've rambled enough so will bid you all well.

Happy Trails

CherokeeNewman

RE: [LbPNW] Introduction

From: IronBear (ironbear@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-09-05 22:48:38 UTC-07:00



CherokeeNewman - good evening.

Great to have you aboard - and welcome to the LbPNW list; I am sorry to hear
that you heard about the event to late - I assure you there will be others
come along. Hopefully one of our fellow boxers will give some mercy to
Charlie over there and have one here in Oregon. -chuckles-

I also wanted to make a comment or two -ok probably more than that I can
tend to ramble- about your wife not getting into letterboxing just yet. I
will be honest with you at the start I was not into Letterboxing as much as
pandora was. I have hiked all of my life - even walking to the school bus as
a kid was a 2 mile hike -in the snow both ways- [had to put that in there
you know] and had been to some amazing places during my hikes so I didn't
see the big deal of going out and finding some stamp out in the north 40
some where. However the more that we did it the more I was proven wrong -
and that was driven home to me when pandora my wife - sim`kitty my daughter
and I went on a six plus mile hike to the horsetail falls area in the Gorge
and we went for three boxes. I was so proud of my daughter for being able to
take every step right along with me even up through such a high elevation
gain - and equally happy and proud of being able to share the experience
with my family of visiting three amazing water falls. Then after reading
something that pandora had wrote in her logbook about her experiences with
letterboxing I was reminded yet again of just the sort of wonderful gifts
that this hobby can bring. She went on to write that this hobby had given
our family something to do together as just that - a family. That it was
something that we would always remember - and something that we would be
able to do for a very long time no matter what our age was. It was special
to her for being able to share something so simple with me.

That is when the preverbal brick fell from the sky and I began to seek out
things that I could enjoy within this hobby known as letterboxing on top of
hiking to find the box. I enjoy drawing - and at times I can draw more than
just a stick figure. So I began to figure out how to draw images that you
could carve onto a stamp. I also am not to bad at coming up with different
ways to make things work or to improve them - whether that is figuring out
how to get all of those different colored ink pads into one container so
that they do not bounce around in your bag all over - or have the caps come
off - to modifying speedball nibs so that they are smaller than store bought
ones therefore give the carver a better tool to create detailed stamps. And
I must say I really love it and now I wouldn't give up letterboxing for
anything in the world.

My point in rambling on like this - which I apologize for - is this. Keep
Letterboxing - and keep encouraging your wife to go with you. I have no
doubts that she will find her specialty in this hobby - perhaps she can
create a special family Letterboxing scrapbook in conjunction with her
pictures and your stamp images - or perhaps she would enjoy making some hand
made logbooks for the letterboxes - that I am hoping you will plant. But I
know that if you stick with this hobby long enough you are going to find
something that each one of you are really going to enjoy doing. It's a great
sport - game - hobby whatever you want to call it because it takes into
consideration so many other things - skills and other past times.

Once again welcome to the list - and I look forward to being able to meet
you someday.

I bid you well.

Iron`Bear

From: Pupp Naish (rambopupp@doglover.com) | Date: 2004-09-06 11:21:59 UTC-05:00
Iron Bear was able to convey quite well what I think and feel about letterboxing. I enjoy carving the most. My spouse loves to sit and chat and exchange personal stamps. My dogs love the walk outdoors.
I have two labradors. One is a therapy dog and one is in training. They need to be around people. I taught them to be that way and it's my responsibility to make sure they don't spend too much time alone. When planning activities, I often think about whether or not the dogs can come. For example, I considered getting a zoo membership but the dogs can't go. Rather than do something that takes a lot of time without the dogs, I decided to go only a few times rather than get a membership that would make me wanna go all the time. Not really a sacrifice, just a choice.
Letterboxing is something that the entire family can enjoy. If someone likes to hike, then the letterboxer can box at the same time. A bird watcher can go along with a letterboxer and both can be happy.
I love the dog park boxes cuz the girls can run their little lab hearts out and I can box. Of course, one of my boxes at a dog park in Vancouver has been vandalized TWICE. After the first time, I replaced the stamp with a similar one with the same theme. Less than a week later, the stamp was missing again. I had a hard time believing it.
Wow, why am I talking so much today? LOL
Happy boxing, Pupp (Team Nashoba)

Introduction

From: Mama Jade Cat (christie@anglinslegacy.com) | Date: 2004-09-06 16:49:44 UTC
Well, it looks like my introduction got lost, as it has been almost a
week and my other post did go through. So I better write it again.

We have been letterboxing for just over a year, but have taken a
rather leisurely pace compared to what I see on this list. I love to
letterbox, my two boys, 5 and 8 enjoy it and my husband comes along.
All along he has said that he would rather Geocache, he doesn't like
the clues.

A couple of weeks ago he picked up a Garmin GPS for a steal at a
garage sale and so this weekend instead of going Letterboxing we went
Geocaching. Let me tell you I would much rather go Letterboxing any
day. I was getting carsick trying to watch the GPS and check the
coordinates and you do a lot of driving around, sometimes in circles
trying to find your location or road (depending on the box). I
finally ended up doing the driving and let my husband do the
navigating. Hummmm.... The ones where you get out on a trail and
walked were better.

Afterward, he conceded that letterboxing is fun and we could keep
doing it. Like he had any say in the matter! So now we are a
crossover family. I saw CharlieTrailDusters name in one of the Geo
logs so I know he's a crossover. I just feel empty leaving a Geocache
box because I don't get a stamp in my log. I love the stamps! Of
course my kids love the toys!

I love all the new letterboxes that are popping up all over in SW
Washington. At our pace it will keep us busy for months.

Anyway, I have enjoyed the group so far and look forward to trying to
make it to a future gathering. I didn't find out about this group
until someone posted about how fun the Battleground gathering was in
lb-jr and when I asked how you find out about these events I was
referred to this group.

Thanks for being a great group of letterboxers,
Mama Jade Cat
