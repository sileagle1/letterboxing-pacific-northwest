My biggest issue with carving letters is remembering to be in
reverse! Wasted lots of pink stuff along the way. That resolved my
next tip is - make the lettering a segment of the stamp that can be
carved separately so you aren't wasting lots of pink stuff or the
design portion. Then glue the design and letter portions to one
backing. I also find that negative carving is by far the easiest,
but I have posted elsewhere about using a stencil cutter - just like
a regular xacto but narrower blade/slimmer holder. The narrow blade
allows for tighter turns in the rubber for those inside o's a's d's.
Good luck.

I have had some success by using Microsoft Word program, and using
either Arial Black or Blackadder ITC in Caps only and in either size
22 or 24. Anything smaller and I can't do it.
I also use Speedball nib #1 that has been squeezed thinner with vise
grips. Then I sharpen the nib very well. Sharpening during your
carving will help keep the image sharper. This seems to work with PZ
Kut, but I don't know about the pink stuff.

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-09 23:56:42 UTC

I find MS Word's Impact or Rockwell Extra Bold font's in 'bold' seem
to work well for lettering. The trick is using the biggest font
size you can get away with for your image. I either 'iron it on' or
transfer it using acetone and then outline the letters with a tiny
Xacto knife and then 'clear the debris' with the Speedball carving
tool's smallest tip (I think it's a #1 size). Also, I've found that
a tiny Exacto knife (I found a 'retractable' micro- Xacto knife for
sale with extra blades at Home Depot of all places--just hanging
around one of the ends of an aisle for a couple of bucks) is really
worth it in helping giving you the 'detail' around the letters--
without removing crucial bits of carving medium. I've also
experimented with other fonts over time (for mostly non-letterboxing
related stamps--including Old English, Gothic, Algerian and some of
the others. Those take a LOT more time & patience (something I
basically lack at the moment).
