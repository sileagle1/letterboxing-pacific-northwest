From: Der Mad Stamper (letterboxr@aol.com) | Date: 2005-04-01 00:01:43 UTC

I understand how frustrating such messages can be, but you've got to
admit that ANY correspondence is better than NO correspondence.

I would rather have someone tell me that they misplaced a stamp than
to have them just ignore the situation. At least then I have a
chance to go out and see if I can find it somewhere trampled
underfoot.

And I would rather have someone contact me and ask for help than get
discouraged and develop a resentment against letterboxing, in general.

Any time someone contacts you, it is an opportunity to show them that
you care about other letterboxers... unless you really DON'T care (in
which case I hope your letterboxes wash away into the ocean).

I hate to admit this publicly, but I almost always provide hints to
people who contact me and ask for help. Of course, in some cases,
my "hints" are more elusive than the original printed clues!

I do not place letterboxes out of spite and orneriness, although I
know many people who have hunted for my boxes will beg to differ. I
place them because I want to share a special place or experience with
other people.

In this modern age, people are so reluctant to communicate with each
other in a pleasant way. If someone takes the time to ask me a
question... and they are halfway nice about it... then I will take
the time to answer their question if it's at all humanly possible for
me to do so. Even if their question has convinced me that they have
the IQ of a moldy peanut, I think it deserves an answer.

Letterboxers are more important than letterboxes.

Peace and Love...
Der Mad Stamper
