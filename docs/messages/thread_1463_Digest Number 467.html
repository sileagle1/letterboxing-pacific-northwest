Subject: 31 July Mini Meet in North Tacoma, WA

Brown's Point Lighthouse Mini Meet

Hi--for anybody out there who is interested in joining us, a few
letterboxers from the region will get together from 2-4 pm (may last
longer if the kids want to play for a while) on 31 July 04 at
Brown's Point Park (Lighthouse), WAY-WAY North Tacoma (practically
Federal Way at this point) to meet with Spacy Holly and her
letterboxing family from Southern California for exchanges.

We plan on meeting at the flag pole in front of (West) of the
lighthouse keepers cottage. The park offers expansive and
incredible views of the Olympic Mountains, Puget Sound, Vashon &
Maury Islands, and depending on the tides lots of opportunity for
beachcombing and boulder scrambling. There's even
enough 'greenspace' to throw a football or frisbee. We plan on
bringing some picnic items, too.

Let us know if you plan on joining us then.

Perhaps we'll see you there?

dvn2r ckr, rnrB & trkr


*************************

Directions to park:

Brown's Point Park
intersection of Ton A Wan Da NE & Tok A Lou Av NE
Brown's Point (Tacoma), WA
Pierce County

>From I-5 North & South: Take I-5 to Tacoma. Turn onto I-705 North,
then head North on WA 509 North (also named Marine View Dr). Head
North for several miles as you wind your way around the Port of
Tacoma. As you approach the residential area of Brown's Point, turn
Left on Le Lou Wa Place NE. Continue on this road until you see the
park on your left. Start at the parking lot.

From: Smiley (dwharris@ipns.com) | Date: 2004-07-28 05:25:09 UTC
I love BC. We go there every winter to ski in Whistler.
You do not have to have a passport if you have your birth
certificate and a picture ID. This has worked for me the last three
times we have gone up there and I even called the border last time
before we left and they said that was all we needed. That was in
April. Just thought some people would like to know you don't have to
worry about getting a passport. I hope this gathering will be at a
time when I can attend.

"Smiley"
