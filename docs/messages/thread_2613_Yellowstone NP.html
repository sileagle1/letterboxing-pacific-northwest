Yellowstone NP

From: doubletreepfx (greenthummm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-19 23:10:18 UTC
I'm back from our trip to Yellowstone and can't say enough good things
about the experience. What an amazing place. (Only, Mammoth Hot
Springs was largely dry so not as spectacular as it usually is).
Canyon was absolutely awesome. Worth every minute spent there. Old
Faithful area is just loaded with people by late-morning, but get
there early (daybreak) and you'll have a few hours without them. And
a good parking space. The thermal features at Old Faithful are just
amazing. West Thumb thermal area was also incredible, just a bit
smaller than the other areas. We camped in 3 different areas and
attended the ranger-talks each night at the amphitheaters, where we
learned more history and details about Yellowstone. We saw deer and
elk (being from the PNW that's a bit routine); moose; bats; some sort
of wild ferret; the typical chipmunks and ground squirrels; numerous
birds including osprey, bald eagle, huge ravens, small colorful
sparrow-type birds, small killdeer-type shorebirds, herons, swans, a
couple cranes? I think; and we experienced the quintessential traffic
jam caused by a herd of buffalo, taking their sweet time wandering
among the cars. What massive animals.

I picked up several boxes on the way to and from Yellowstone, and
every one of them were exceptional carvings, with related themes that
made them just as much souvenirs of the trip as anything I bought in a
gift shop. If you are ever headed to Yellowstone, be sure to email me
and I'll be happy to point out some must-have boxes. My thanks to
everyone who suggested boxes to visit.
