We did the Walking Tour in about 6 hours with two young children in
tow--knowing the area really well (as it was smack dab in the middle
of my commute route) and even cheating on a few 'legs' along the
way. Even then we still needed a second trip to the area to actually
retrieve the box so I'd recommend doing something else during your
limited time in the area. Nothing's more frustrating than spending
hours 'solving' a box and then not having the time to actually
retrieve it!

Pacific Science Center is awesome, the Aquarium was unique but I'm
not sure if they've completed their total renovations by this time.
Another thing that few people know about but is really cool is the
Discovery Maritime Museum on the Waterfront--adjacent to the Aquarium
and well within walking distance of Pike Place Market. It offers
lots of hands on type activities for the kids--all related to
maritime activities ranging from Coast Guard, Navy, maritime
shipping, fishing, cruise ships, sea kayaks, etc--if it involves a
boat of any kind they probably talk about it there. It was really
interesting and in such a unique location because it offers great
views of Elliot Bay and you can listen in on the actual maritime
traffic as the ships come into port or enter the shipping lanes.
Very unique and educational--ideal for homeschoolers and a great
mental connection for Seattle especially tying that in with the route
that Amtrak follows along Puget Sound from Portland to Seattle!

As for letterboxes, not sure if there are any in the immediate
vicinity of those areas these days. There had been a couple at
Seattle Center for a while but if I'm not mistaken they either went
missing recently or were pulled not too long ago.

I'm not sure how different Pacific Science Center is from OMSI,
though. OMSI seemed to focus on a lot of biology and mechanical type
things whereas Pacific Science Center has a dinosaur display,
insects, hands on science experiments (quite similar to OMSI's--sound
waves, physics, weather forecasting, and a focus on Puget Sound tides
and the potential devastating effects of lahars/volcanic eruptions,
etc) and rotating displays (when we were last there they had a big
display on candy and how the body digests food). If you want
something unique to Seattle, go to the Maritime Museum instead. If
the aquarium is open, you could probably do both at the same time and
still make it back to your train.

Hope this helps.

daelphinus

The walking tour one is a little involved. I think that honestly you'd
have more fun, given your limited time, if you did the Aquarium /
Maritime Museum.

You might want to take a walk through Pike Place Market while you're
in the area - sometimes they have tours that tell you about the
history of the Market but I don't know what the schedule is for those.
The donut-making machine at the donut place near the newsstand (follow
your nose) is popular with my kids, and there's a cheese place on the
corner (Beecher's) that you can see how they make the cheese through a
big glass window.

The Alaska Gold Rush museum is also fun (and free!)- that's between
the train station and the Aquarium, if I remember right. Trolleys are
not running now I don't think, so don't count on it, but the busses
are free in that area. It's not that far of a walk, but I know little
feet get tired sometimes.

I'm actually planning to do a box in that area, as soon as I figure
out how to plant it - tupperware under a bush is not going to last
downtown ;)

From: Patch (2dragons@peak.org) | Date: 2007-02-24 00:04:56 UTC-08:00
Ditto the Underground Tour - it's just the kind of thing kids would like,
I think, as long as they don't have any touches of claustrophobia or
whatever.
http://www.seattletravel.com/seattleundergroundtour.html

Near the Underground Tour starting location is the Klondike Gold Rush
(Inter)National Historical Park Seattle unit museum - it's free, and has
GREAT history and info displays and short movies. http://www.nps.gov/klse/

Both are easy walking distance from the train station, the summer I
volunteered at Klondike I walked over to the train station a couple times
to replenish the station's brochure supply.

Downtown Seattle has the 'ride free' zone for the buses which is great for
getting around that area, going clear from the train station to the
Market, for example, is a long walk. :)
http://transit.metrokc.gov/tops/bus/ridefree.html
(there's also a link on that page to a downtown Seattle guide and map
that's pretty useful)
