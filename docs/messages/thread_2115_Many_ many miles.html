Many, many miles

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-06-27 06:36:02 UTC
Today was a day to do some long distance maintenance.
The primary goal was the Mill Creek 45th Parallel box.
The last couple of finders had let me know that the
logbook was full to overflowing and I knew that I needed
to get down there and replace it. However, if I was driving
all the dang way to Salem and beyond, I figured I may as
well pick up/check on a box or three along my route.

I found three of my boxes, Bluebird Trail, Bald Peak and
Mill Creek all faring fine. I tracked down Douglas Fir and Minto
Brown Island but failed miserably with Here Fishy, Fishy. With
the last, I never found the marked log, wasn't sure about which
sign was more baffling than another and never found a tree
with a rock at its base. There was one last tree that could've
been the correct one but there were five, count 'em five,
people enjoying the lovely Willamette near the base of that tree.
There was no way I could ever get close. Sigh...

Of the five boxes I visited today, three had standing water in
them. I am here to testify that ziplock baggies are the greatest!
Not one of the books was damaged by water. I poured out the
water, dried the insides, double bagged anything that wasn't
already, I want to thank all the letterboxers who have taken
such good care resealing the bags in my boxes and those
belonging to other folks. Funhog
