Letterboxing Seattle on Easter Sunday

From: Danny McMillin (macdanny@mindspring.com) | Date: 2004-04-11 19:40:37 UTC-07:00
Loud Eyes, Toast Boy, Scout the Barn Cat and yours truly, Keegan the
Airedale Terrier set out for a fun afternoon of letterboxing.

We started at the "Now and Then" series of boxes in Seward Park. Great
clues wound us through the park to the "Now" box. It was in a video tape
box that was open and wet on the inside. All was dry though in the bags. We
replaced several zip lock bags for insurance. Inside was the Lambton Dragon
Hitchhiker, my first! It is a beautiful stamp as some of you know.
Dvn2rckr's stamp in the "Now" box is fantastic! I'm glad we took along
different colored felt pens to really make it come alive.

It was on to the "Then" box. We found it in its cubby hole which holds
water. The box was quite wet but again the contents were dry in their bags.
Old bags were again replaced with new ones. Another fine stamp carved by
dvn2rckr. We put some sticks in the bottom of the cubby hole where it
resides to try to keep the box above the waterline. Hope that helps!

Then it was on to the Kubota Park letterbox placed by Amanda from Seattle.
It was a beautiful Easter Sunday in Seattle and a walk through Kubota Park
was just what the doctor ordered. Many plants and trees were flowering and
the smells were of deep springtime. This box was quite wet inside as well,
but the contents were dry in their zippy bags. We again dried out the
entire box and made colorful stamps with Amanda's fine carvings! We also
replaced ziplock bags in the hopes of keeping things dry.

I really wished we had some rubber bands to lock down these boxes. It will
be an item I take next time. Also a spare box or two, just in case!

I can't explain just how much fun it is to get out into the beautiful parks
with good friends and a trusty dog in search for letterboxes. Outstanding!

Keep looking, my friend.
