Proposed Oregon Summer Gathering!

From: bcostley (bobbyeubanks@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-05 20:40:11 UTC

I propose a summer gathering/campout at a group camping site at
Silver Falls this year sometime in July. I propose Silver Falls as
it is a beautiful park and thanks to our goodwill piggie ambassador,
letterboxing is allowed there.

I propose this gathering to work similar to the Milo gathering in
that it would be pretty informal and a Friday and Saturday night
(come for some or all). Just a date, location, planned time for a
potluck/stamp exchange and a consensus victim for carving the event
stamp. I'm not in charge, I'm basically just volunteering to secure
the group site and do some minor coordination like reminder posts to
the list.

Folks are more than welcome to take upon themselves planning stamp
carving or book binding how-to sessions or even discussions on the
finer nuances of John Candy movies.

So what say you - want to do this? If so, respond with any
preference/bad dates on the weekend in July, I'll try to figure out
what works best and make the reservation.

Thanks!

Re: Proposed Oregon Summer Gathering!

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-01-06 04:14:02 UTC

Camp at Silver Falls? At one of the most scenic
parks in the state? No need to ask twice. I'm
there! The weekend of the 9th sounds good
to me, too.

When I met with the staff at that park last year,
they actually suggested that we hold an event
there. The word was that there is an entire
network of trails away from the waterfalls that
are woefully underused. Their hopes were that
the presence of letterboxes would get hikers
into that part of the park, too.

Trails that rarely get used, staff that welcomes
our hobby, a part of the park unlikely to be
inundated with visitors. Sounds like a win-win
situation to me. Sign me up...

Funhog
