Re: PNW Spring Flinger April 9th!

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-14 04:47:00 UTC
What a nice gesture! Cool, cool, cool! We're always game for a get-
together!!! There might even be a guest appearance of a
certain 'mysterious' closet-letterboxer in our midst that day.

Other potential venues might be Wildwood Park in Puyallup (lots of
unexplored territory, covered pavillions near playgrounds,
interesting history & centrally located to the big city) or Pt
Defiance Park in Tacoma. Wildwood has never hosted a gathering yet
so that would make it a unique venue. My first visit there last
year I instantly thought "great spot for a gathering" but simply ran
out of time to coordinate one there this past year. It's funny--
when I last spoke to Amanda from Seattle about Wildwood she said she
had the same 'feeling' when she was there, too.

I can't remember much about Priest Point Park (expect where those
darn loons got lost 3-1/2 years ago!!!--thanks TnE for eventually
saving my marriage!!!) but I think there may have been a couple
small covered pavillions there, too, over by the water. By the way,
you can grab a great Thanksgiving meal there, too, if you're keen
(that is, if it's still available this time of year).

Pt D has great pavillions but it's dark in the winter/spring with so
many trees and I'm not sure how many letterboxes in the park are
still in the running. Many haven't seemed to last more than a few
years or so as the trail maintainers are quite fastidious about
patrolling the trails.

I'll keep thinking of potential locations around the area.

dvn2r ckr

One concern with Millersylvania in the Winter/early Spring is that
apart from the dry Summer season around here the trails there quickly
become a muddy, mucky mess. We've glopped along on some of the trails
in mud at least a foot deep--in April. It wouldn't be a problem if it
were just a couple of the trails but a couple of the more 'critical'
trails seem to be right around the level of the water table in these
parts and they turn into mush (the ground seems to liquefy sort of like
Boeing Field or Tolmie SP do during earthquakes).
