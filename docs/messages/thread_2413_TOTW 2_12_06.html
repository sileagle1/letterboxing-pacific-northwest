My letterboxing is somewhat limited to seasons. It isn't really just about
the cold, but more about how deep the snow is. I can do some local boxes
during some of the winter months, but most of the fun boxes are up on
mountain trails and I have a hard enough time finding the box when it isn't
covered in many, many feet of snow. Locally it isn't as deep and will
occasionally melt so I can get out (not that anyone else is out planting
them though).

I'm really looking forward to getting out and finding some letterboxes when
I come up in the middle of March. It will probably be my first serious
boxing for the year--that is if we don't have our 2nd annual Moab Box on the
Rocks before that. There has been discussion of heading down again to the
desert trails. Any of you are welcome if you want to get out of the rain!

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2006-02-16 19:26:27 UTC
The rain slows down my letterboxing because I hate to introduce any
trace of moisture into a box. Luckily, the rainy winter months are
prime time for viewing the visiting winter birds. So, I still grab the
Gore-Tex and get out on the trails. Hiking and birding in the rain are
good fun with the right gear.

When it's roasting hot, I avoid those hard uphill treks. Those, I
usually save for cooler days. The wildflower bloom in the Spring
always takes me up Dog Mountain. There are a couple of new boxes up
there that I'm just itchin' to get come a cool day in May or early June.

When it's icy cold, I avoid letterbox series with multiple boxes on a
short hike. I never have time to warm up my frigid fingers between
stops. Then, I head for one box on a longer hike. Funhog

From: lesliekusz (lesliekusz@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-21 00:48:11 UTC
This may get me lynched, but I *adore* rain, maybe because growing up in interior Alaska
we didn't see much of it. Sixty below actual temperature (not just wind chill)? You bet. A
lot of rain? No. I think it is my parents' fault. They used to take us out in the summer
rains to pick mushrooms. That was the best time to go, because the really fresh ones--
those the bugs hadn't been able to get to--popped out of the dirt when it rained. So I
have fond memories of walking around in boots and rain poncho with a pocket knife and a
small pail, hunting for mushrooms. Even if I can't be out in it, the rain makes me happy. I
think I like the lower barometric pressure, too--I always feel calm and collected when it's
raining.

So naturally, I *love* letterboxing when it rains. It's when it's hot that I get fainthearted. I
can't even *imagine* what it must be like to go letterboxing in Florida...I'd probably melt.

Hmm...just occurred to me that the old mushroom hunts may be what makes letterboxing
so attractive to me. That and the treasure hunts my dad used to set up for us. Just wish
I'd found out about this earlier--I guess I have a lot of time to make up on this one.
