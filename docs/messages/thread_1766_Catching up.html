Catching up

From: Mary Jo (maryjo@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-11-17 00:03:07 UTC-08:00
Hi All,
It's SloMo here and guess it's time to check in. Because of poor health we
haven't been doing much Lbing or going to the events. Der Mad Stamper -
Thank you for the cipher clue boxes. They have been a bright spot in this
cold, dark season. We caught the Turtle Soup LB and it is my favorite stamp
in the log book! So cool Pandora! Of course, being a turtle myself, and it
being Halloween that day, I was glad to make it out in one piece. ;-)

To Erfellie - I don't know which direction your traveling from but Vancouver
area has a number of boxes and most (well all, really) are without the big
city feel. I'm a country bumpkin, myself, so I understand where you're
coming from. Anyplace with street parking and meters is too much hassle for
me. :-)

Can anyone help with advice? -
We are planning a trip up the gorge (I-84) to the Tri-cities right after
T-day and would like to catch a box or two. Found Ryan's African Safari
series - would like to get what we can of those (love African wildlife) but
probably won't be able to get to them. There's only one box listed in the
Tri-cities (Corp of Discovery Sacajawea) We tried to find it last July but
it was not there and the site was disturbed. I'm still waiting to hear from
the placer on that one.
........ Anyway.... are there other websites with clues besides LbNA and
Ryan's site? Ideally there would be boxes in Richland, Pasco, or
Kennewick... or near there. We'd like to place a box too (Wood Splinters
grew up there). Maybe hiding Lbs in the desert is silly. *laugh*

Hope you're all are having fun!

SloMo

Re: [LbPNW] Catching up

From: Colleen (colleendurham@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-11-17 10:26:00 UTC-08:00
Hi Mary Jo--

We traveled through the Gorge in October, Spokane to Portland, to watch EWU play at Portland State. We found the same thing--not many letterboxes in the desert! We did find Sacajawea in the Tri Cities, it has a beautiful logbook and cool stamp. Our other finds were Multnomah Falls (Der Mad Stamper), Hidden Palms, Silver Eagle's Lewis & Clark Series: Lewis & Clark in Oregon, and Lower Latourell Falls (actually off I84, but well worth the detour. Another idea is to travel on the Washington side. I know there are boxes at the Maryhill Museum, that Stonehenge-like memorial, and several around Rooster Rock. I sure hope you plant something else in the Tri-Cities, I'd like to see some of the interesting sites there.
colls
