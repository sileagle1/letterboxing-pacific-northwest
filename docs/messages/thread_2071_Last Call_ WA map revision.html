the five 'unboxed' counties (those that are currently white on the
eastern part of the map--it's taken at least 3 years to get this map

opportunity) ;)

be as bad as having Pierce & King Counties combined--as that
combined LB list went on for days)

NOTE: I tried to add lakes to the map but it doesn't look good. As
it is, Puget Sound is over-exaggerated just so it appears as water
and not land. Any additional lakes/rivers would be too small to
even really show up.

1: Olympic & Kitsap Peninsulae (is that correct for plural?)
2: South Puget Sound
3: North Puget Sound
4: Northwest WA (this region's name is sort of funny as it's not
quite accurate--does something else fit better? perhaps Mt Baker
region or North Central WA?)
5: Cascades
6: Northeast WA
7: Southwest WA
8: South Central WA
9: Southeast WA
10: WA Mystery


Barring any more comments from folks over the next day or two, we'll
submit this revised image for the LBNA site soon.

Re: Last Call: WA map revision

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-30 05:13:20 UTC
Now for a follow up question: should Clark become its own region
entirely or should we merge Clark and Skamania together? Otherwise,
there will just be an 'island' of Clark smack dab in the middle of the
SW WA region. Combining the two counties would make it easier to look
at the map in toto. Not sure but as the hobby grows in popularity I
predict folks in Ilwaco would think it bizarre that they're regioned up
with Skamania (or vice versa) while it skips over much closer Clark
County.



On a side note to this entire map revision process, here's some food
for thought: Using Connecticut as an example, their entire state is
divided into four regions plus mystery boxes with about 2,500 boxes in
the entire state (in a really small landmass as compared to WA). Each
region has approx 500 boxes each. WA state has about 800 'listed' boxes
TOTAL--with soon to be 10 or 11 regions whenever this map revision is
officially adopted. Is splitting the state into this many separate
regions overkill in comparison to CT? Or is it just an attempt
to 'predict' an expansion in the hobby and be prepared for future
growth?

What's at stake here is trying to balance the 'original intent' behind
the simplicity of the original LBNA map (and the Old World feel of the
entire LBNA website for that matter) versus form and function for
letterboxing as it grows in popularity in our region. CT's state parks
and state forests system welcomes letterboxing with open arms, even
endorses them in many forms--so there are boxes in all sorts of nooks
and crannies there that wouldn't really happen (unless in my dreams)
here in WA. WA parks and forests (except for a few awesome but rather
limited instances) haven't been so 'welcoming' of letterboxing--
although that could potentially change in the future with some future
judicious letterboxing diplomatic activism.

Any thoughts on this?

Thanks,

dvn2r ckr

I, for one, think the two should be combined. I look
at Clark county as the gateway to the Gorge and
including the Skamania Gorge boxes seems like a
natural division to me. It's something like the Portland
listing for Multnomah county which merges into the
Gorge on the Oregon side. In fact, I also think that
Hood county in Oregon should be listed along with
the other Gorge boxes but then there's a whole other
can o' worms.

The overcrowding factor as seen in Connecticut
shouldn't really be an issue with the new features that
have been instituted at LbNA since you can set things
up to eliminate listings of boxes you've already found.
Then again, I guess a new letterboxer might be
overwhelmed. I just don't want the LbNA site to start
looking and acting like the geocaching site. I really
prefer simplicity. Funhog

dvn2r ckr

Great Job!!!

Region 5 advertises themsleves as 'North Central Washington' in their
advertising efforts and brochures. It should be called NCW to match
their 'official' travel destination efforts.

Region 3 should probably be King County or Seattle Area, Central Puget
Sound really has never shown up as a popular name for any area of the
state. For official planning purposes, the Puget Sound Regional
Council inclues King, Kitsap, Pierce and Snohomish Counties. It makes
sense to put Kitsap County on the other side of Puget Sound for
logistic reasons. It also makes sense to put Pierce and Thurston
togehter. Snohomish could go with eiter King or Northwest, Northwest
makes sense given the number of boxes in the two areas.

Region 4 is commonly known as Northwest Washington, being the area
North of Seattle and West of the Cascades, and does not include the
Olympic Penisula.

Region 9, South Central Washington is an appropriate name, but you may
want to include Benton County, making the Columbia River the Eastern
Boundary.

Sorry to be so late chiming in. This is based on having worked with
the counties and having been here over 50 years.

thinking it was a kayaking the Sound guide--that broke down the
entire Sound by public/private beaches, puts-in, etc. Of course,
when I need those guides, they're nowhere to be found. ;) Guess I
need a compass for my storage space these days...
