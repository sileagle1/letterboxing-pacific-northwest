Nature

Just got home from Corvallis where I planted two new boxes and was thrilled
to do the Camp Adair series. This is a fabulous series dedicated to the men
that perished defending our country. Very nice carving job by a newer
boxer. I also picked up a hitchhiker while I was there.

If you get the chance to get down to Corvallis/Philomath, there's definitely
some nice boxes down there. I believe we're up to a dozen, might be worth a
road trip!

Weekend Boxing

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-06-13 18:45:55 UTC-07:00
We were fortunate to spend the weekend at the coast. I came away with 7
stamps, all of which were fantastic. I don't think I've had a better
letterboxing trip. The crowning glory of the weekend was Lemony Snicket's
box, Bad Beginnings. The clues, the location, the book in it, and the stamp
were so far above anything I've seen so far that I can't even begin to
describe them. if you get the chance, it's well worth the effort!

Don't miss out on One Eyed Willie, Cannon Beach, Fort Canby Lighthouse
Series, or Lewis and Clark in Washington! Thank you to all the placers for
leading me to such awesome locations.

Maiden

Weekend Boxing

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2005-02-06 21:27:43 UTC-08:00
Saturday the Brat Pack dragged RavynMom out for a day of boxing in Gresham.
Most of the boxes were "checkups" for The Brat Pack, but it gave us a chance
to check the status of a few.

You Betcha was found, safe but in need of some minor help. The box lid is
falling apart and the nut on the log book is missing.
Swan Lake is missing. Tooth Fairy was very sad to see that her very first
hand carved box had gone missing but she plans to recarve it and replace it.
A Rose By Any Other Name was found safe and sound. It's had several
visitors, but none since last April.
Yellow Rose of Suffrage was found safe and sound. It is happily awaiting
visitors. The Brat Pack would like to thank all those that have left such
wonderful messages.

Our last attempt for the day was Multnomah Falls, but the rain and time ran
out on us before we could find it. That one will have to wait for another
day.

I hope others got out on Saturday to enjoy the weather and get some fresh
air. It's been a long time since we've heard about anyone's travels. Let's
hear what you've found lately.

Maiden
