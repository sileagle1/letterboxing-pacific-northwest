I'm on my way...

From: gamine32000 (gamine3@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-01-10 23:50:30 UTC
So, come June-ish my husband, daughter and I will be moving up to
the Seattle area from the dredges of the Silicon Valley. It will be
a good move, I think, I hope...

We are moving sight unseen, which is a bit scary to say the least,
and though we are dissatisfied with our present situation (do you
realize how RIDICULOUSLY EXPENSIVE houses are here?), it is familiar
and known. Comfortable cycles are so easy to stay in, whether they
are good for us or not, y'know? So move we will. I have faith, or
something, that the change will do us good.

So why am I writing all this here you ask? Well, my husband and I
(and our 9 month old daughter) just started letterboxing about 5
months ago. The three of us wander around on nice Saturday mornings
searching for clues and boxes and having a ball doing it. Having
Maggie with us has proven to be very useful. She's a great cover
when we stop to grab a box and greatly distacts attention away from
us with her winning smile and beguiling ways! :-)And now we are
moving right when we are starting to get the hang of this whole
Letterboxing thing. A new area. New lands. New sights. New smells.
How exciting and frightening.

I am a firm believer in having nets in place whenever doing
something new - just in case. A net in this case would be
information of all kinds. Anyone done anything like this before? Any
advice for us? How is moving with a babe? What is the Seattle area
like? Any areas to stay away from? To buy or to rent? Anyone
interested in starting up a convo. or a giving a tour (tee-hee)?
Oh, and of course, what boxes should we look for once we are settled?
What hikes will take us on magic journeys in the area?....and on and
on.... And so I turn to you, PNW Letterboxing community, to morph
yourselves into a net of sorts for my family and me.

Could you? Would you?

Our thanks!

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2004-01-11 05:39:31 UTC
Dear Up-rooted Family,
My husband, daughter and I made the same decision you have 4 years
ago and have not regretted a single moment. I lived in Southern
California for 38 years, born and raised there, and had a split
second opportunity to move away from there and live in Portland Or.
We left our entire families in So. CA. and packed everthing we owned
in a UHaul truck and took off and have never looked back. I believe
it was the best decision we have ever made. We too tried to buy a
home in CA. which was impossible and the area was not suited for
raising kids. I can honestly say that you could not pay me enough to
go back and live in CA. The Pacific Northwest is a totally different
world. The people are so much nicer, the traffic better, the air is
clean, mind you it does rain more here but the scenery is beautiful
when it stops raining, and the letterboxes are unending! We have been
to the most beautiful places that I have ever seen and met some of
the greatest people on the planet (of course I mean my fellow
letterboxers). We were able to buy a brand new home in Battle Ground,
WA. so I recommend buying instead of renting, and the schools are
some of the best in the country. I live about 3 hours South of
Seattle and I am not the one to ask about letterboxes in the area but
I am sure the wonderful letterbox community where you are will
contact you soon and welcome you to the area. In my opinion you have
made the right decision and you will not regret it in the least! My
daughter is 9 years old and was not a baby when we moved so the best
advice I can give you is to take your time, enjoy the scenery and
don't look back. Good luck on your journey and think of it as
starting your life over anew. Keep your mind open to new experiences
and your eyes open to all the beauty around you and you will be
amazed by what you find. After 4 years I still feel like everything
is brand new and I am loving every minute of it! I hope to meet you
at a gathering some day to see how you and your family are faring in
this beautiful part of the world. Happy Trails!
Camp Fire Lady
