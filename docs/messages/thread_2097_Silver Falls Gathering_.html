Silver Falls Gathering:
July 1 - 4, 2005 Silver Falls State Park

This is a reminder that the Silver Falls gathering will soon take
place at Silver Falls State Park near Silverton, Oregon.


Logistics:

Friday and Saturday nights we have group camp sites A and C reserved
under "Costley"
Additionally, on Sunday night we have group camp site C reserved
under "?" (Funhog - can you let us know the name for Sunday night?)

The reservations are from 4:00pm Friday to 1:00pm Sunday (site A) or
Monday (site C)

The group camp sites are paid for, we will get courtesy day use
passes for those in the group camp. If you are coming just for the
day, you'll need to get a day use pass for $3.00 (per car).

Note: Each group site can take 25 campers and 20 cars. As of now,
the spots are spoken for (and even a little beyond) so we can't take
any additional campers in the group camp. The files section has a
list of folks who have indicated when they want to camp
(http://groups.yahoo.com/group/LbPNW/files/). I believe the regular
sites are full too. You can always come for the day.

Please try to pick the camp site wisely - If you are staying Fri and
Sat but not Sun it probably makes sense to go to site A to minimize
the chance someone will have to move their tent from one site to
another.

Schedule:
------------

Friday:

4:00PM - earliest check in
7:00PM - Exchange and socializing *


Saturday:

6:00AM - Rise and Shine ***
6:00PM - Potluck dinner/exchange * **


Sunday:

6:15AM - Rise and Shine (you get to sleep in since it is Sunday) ***
Noon - BYO lunch/exchange *
1:00PM - Campsite A must be cleared


Monday:

6:00AM - Rise and Shine ***
1:00PM - Campsite A must be cleared


* We'll try to set up for the potluck and exchanges at site C since
that one is reserved the whole time. I request that hitchhikers and
other boxes are kept away from the exchange (exception only for the
official event stamp). With 50 people it can get quite hectic even
without extra boxes and hitchhikers going around. Logs and stamps
have a tendancy to separate in the chaos. You are welcome to pass
off hitchhikers in the normal manor via the boxes in the park.
Special event only boxes are welcome but please try to clue them
even if they are kept somewhere in the camping vicinity.

** I think potluck is pretty self explanatory. It would be a
logistical nightmare to try to organize a variety of dishes so I'm
not even going to try to do so. If we all wind up bringing a box of
Krispy Kreme doughnuts, we'll have to make do. I will make sure
there are plates/utensils/napkins. You are on your own for drinks.

*** That's a joke folks.

--------------------------

Hiding boxes:

If you are hiding boxes please do not hide them on the Canyon Trail -
there are already boxes on that trail and the State Park has
requested we use the other trails. The Canyon trail is by far the
most popular trail; the other trails get limited use and thus are
better suited for stealthy hiding and finding of boxes.

There is a file in the files section
(http://groups.yahoo.com/group/LbPNW/files/) that highlights which
trails the state park folks recommend we use.

Please bring copies of clues for boxes you plant in the park to
share at the gathering!

Information on the park (including directions) can be found at:
http://www.oregonstateparks.org/park_211.php

See you there!
