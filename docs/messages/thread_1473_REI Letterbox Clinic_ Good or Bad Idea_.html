This is both good and bad in my opinion. It was an aid to the success of the hobby in

respect the game as it has developed over the years.

run a clinic well are right at your doorstep. (What about the First Couple of West Coast
boxing???) If it has to happen I would hope that folks like this would be the ones in

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-07-29 00:21:49 UTC
> but this state also has a reputation for having many letterboxes
> hidden with store bought stamps and low quality memo pads in them.

I think part of that problem is just that 'back in the day', store-
bought stamps and low-quality memo pads were the norm. By the time
it reached up, hand-carved stamps were gaining more acceptance and
we had some great carvers out here to set an example for us--those
memo pads were still the most common logbooks found in letterboxes.

It's only in the last year or two that home-made logbooks have
started becoming popular.

The evolution of letterboxing.

Consequently, I don't think an REI clinic would cause a rash of
store-bought stamps and low-quality memo pads. With such a clinic,
we can 'indocrinate' them to using hand-carved stamps and high-
quality memo pads. (That's a joke--I'm not sure there is such a
thing as a high-quality memo pad!) Teach them how to carve.
They'll run around a bit finding a few boxes and discover that the
vast majority of them have hand-carved stamps, and they'll copy
everyone else's example.

At least in a setting like this, we can set their expectations
correctly. If they discover the hobby on their own through a random
google search or something, who knows what horrible things they
might think to do. ;o)

-- Ryan

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-07-29 06:14:20 UTC
> I think part of that problem is just that 'back in the day', store-
> bought stamps and low-quality memo pads were the norm.

Okay, I've been admonished by Funhog that store-bought stamps and
low-quality memo pads were not the norm until the unnamed-store
started getting invovled.

But, I'll still stand by the fact that I think the only reason they
could create 'the norm' like that was because one hadn't been
established and entrenched yet.

With Danny leading the charge, I doubt he'd be encouraging store-
bought stamps (since, so far as I know, all of his boxes are hand-
carved). And second, even if he did, once everyone started going
out there and finding all our wonderful hand-carved stamps, they'd
learn pretty quick that Danny left out an important part of the
story. ;o)

Let's face it: Most of us are imitators. After finding DMS's
Columbia River Gorge series, I was inspired try to carve as well as
that. And when I finally felt I reached that ability, I even
replaced two of the missing boxes of his! =)

Amanda says the reason she first carved a lot of negative-stamp
images (which, let's face it, usually aren't the best looking stamps
out there) was because for the longest time, that's all she ever
saw. It never occurred to her that there was any other way to do it!

So, 'back in the day' before there were standards and conventions,
it was a lot easier to set a bad example and let it grow.

Just my opinion, though. *shrug* I'm still for the REI clinic,
though. =) I'd rather try to sway them with a qualified person
explaining the ins and outs of letterboxing than have them discover
it on their own and figure it out the hard way. *shrug*

I agree with Ryan totally. And while I understand Funhog's concerns, I
think the issue is really quite different. REI already holds Geocaching

too much media and publicity for this hobby to ever stay the silent mystery
that some want and wish it would be. So at this point we have no option but
to educate the masses in a hope that they will pick up this hobby the way we
believe it should be played.

As a matter of fact, after taking Funhog's class last month I personally
suggested to her that she look into teaching a clinic at REI because I
thought she good reach a larger group of new boxers in an environment like
this. The trouble with that suggestion and Funhog's own suggestion of
recruiting Ryan or Amanda to teach this clinic is that REI clinics to my
understanding have to be taught by REI employees. And since Ryan didn't get
the job at REI he just can't do it ;) (Just giving you a hard time Ryan)

I think the assumption that an REI employee teaching the class would be
inexperienced is a little presumptuous. I have dealt with REI several times
and their employees always seem to have personal knowledge of what they are
selling and teaching. I know for example that Kermit, who teaches the
Geocaching clinic at the Tualatin Store, has been Geocaching for over a year
or so and has personally re-written the manuals for the most popular GPSs in
order to teach it with more user friendly and comprehendible material.

I personally suggest that Danny go ahead and hold the clinic - teach as many
people as you can the important aspects of this hobby. I've seen Danny's
stamps and had the opportunity to meet him personally and I can honestly say
that I know for a fact he would not tell people that store bought stamps
were acceptable. And if he did - just like Ryan said - they are going to
find out as soon as they hit the trails that we have a bit of higher
expectations in this area.

My intentions are not to offend anyone here, I am just throwing my opinion
and my two cents in like everyone else.
