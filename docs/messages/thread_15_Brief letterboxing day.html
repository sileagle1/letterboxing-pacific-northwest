Brief letterboxing day

From: Jill (jswindler0@msn.com) | Date: 2003-03-30 04:36:58 UTC
Today, hubby and I thought we'd hit Funhog's Discovery Trail and
Rookery boxes.

We found the Discovery Trail box easily (inside of box and plastic
bags were wet, but no real damage). We laid everything out in the
beautiful sunshine for a few minutes to try to dry it out. The walk
was beautiful, even with all the mosquitoes!


Unfortunately, we had no luck with the Rookery box. I know at one
time it had been missing, but I thought I saw on the main list that
it had been replaced. Were the clues changed? I'm sure we'll get out
there again once we know for sure we were looking at the right clues!

And just a side note: as we were driving to the boxes, we saw a
rubber stamp store on the side of the road and decided to hit it on
our way back out. We were so excited, just imaging all the great
stuff they'd have in there for us! We were very disappointed to see
it is, in fact, a rubber stamp store. They only sell pre-made rubber
stamp (what's the point?!). Oh well.

Jill aka Lillbit

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-03-30 06:56:34 UTC
> We were very disappointed to see
> it is, in fact, a rubber stamp store.

LOL!!!!! Well, you can't really accuse them of false advertising, I
suppose.... =)

Few rubber stamps stores I've come across seem to have really joined
the letterboxing bandwagon--which is kind of surprising since it
couldn't possibily do anything but help their buisiness. Even so,
most of them I've seen usually have a tiny (VERY tiny) area with
carving blocks and the Speedball carving tools. Hand-carved stamps
definitely aren't their specialty, though.

As a side note, if you're ever looking for the Mastercarve stuff (the
stuff that "cuts like butter"), most stamp stores I've been to
usually carry that instead of the pink stuff. If you don't know what
I'm talking about, for those that went to the South Sound
Letterboxing Quest Fest--the event stamp was made from the stuff.
The stamp in the Crappy Stamp Letterbox also uses the 'butter'.

And even if they don't have carving blocks or tools, they'll have
more colors and sizes of ink pads than you can shake a stick out--so
hopefully it wasn't a total waste! =)

-- Ryan
