Central OR weekend

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-07-23 16:19:15 UTC
Just got home last night from a very loooooong weekend in the high desert. While I
was basking in the sun there, I managed to track down some boxes that have been on
my list for some time.

The Fleshman's Haystack Resevoir box has a lovely stamp in it (did I expect anything
less?) and the view from the hiding place is stunning, especially with the late
afternoon light. Birder's Dry River Canyon boxes in Powell Butte presented a challenge
or two. The location of these two wonderful letterboxes is deep in the heart of posted
private property. I suspect that it was open range when these were planted since the
barbed wire and signage looks new an shiny. Since there was no one about but me
and one hereford with huge horns, I decided to put on my scofflaw hat and find the
boxes anyway. I made it out unscathed, not in legal custody and with two intricate
stamps in my book. The spot is beautiful but I can't recommend the legality of the
finds.

Another of my recent discoveries was a great trail on Crooked River Ranch. It is
naturally now home to some new letterboxes. Just think, you can come to Central
Oregon and collect my very first stamps (really bad) and my most recent ones (not so
bad) in the same day! Then there are the other treats from Amanda, the Fleshmans,
birder and the Nail Family awaiting you...
