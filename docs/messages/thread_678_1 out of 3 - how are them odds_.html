Leslie, I had that same experience when I started. At one point I had
not-found more boxes than I'd found, which it is dang discouraging when you're just
starting out. Here are some suggestions:

* E-mail the placer directly, when possible (this is not possible with Der
Mad Stamper) and tell them you couldn't find their box--describe where you
looked so they can tell if you were in the correct spot or not. If you can't
e-mail the placer, you could e-mail the list and let us know you couldn't find it.
When you e-mail the list, you want to be very careful not to mention any
"spoilers"--any specific info not already clarified in the clues that might spoil
the hunt for someone who hasn't searched for that box. Chances are that if
someone knows the box is really still there, they will be more than happy to
gloat about that knowledge ;o).

*Before you embark on a hunt, check when the box was placed; Der Mad
Stamper's boxes have been there awhile--some are missing. (A word to the wise: don't
ask why we don't just come up with a system to let people KNOW which boxes are
missing--this is a can of worms you do NOT want to open. Trust me on this.)
For now, you might choose to only go after relatively new boxes--happily,
there are a ton of them.

*Search the archives (main list and PNW) to see when was the last time
someone mentioned finding a particular box. The Blue Lake box, for example, was
recently mentioned as missing.

As for posting, most people list boxes on the LBNA site. While letterboxing
is not welcome in national parks, most other places haven't even heard of this
odd little hobby, so we're currently under the radar screen. And of course,
you could always approach the staff of a place and check that it's alright to
plant in that particular place. I've been pleasantly surprised to find that
some places welcome Lbing, once I've explained it to them. People often
announce/include the clues on this or the main list, but usually this is not the
only place where they are mentioned, since anyone who doesn't happen to read that
message (or someone who joins in the game later) would not know your box was
out there.

Don't worry--soon you'll actually find more boxes than you don't find!
