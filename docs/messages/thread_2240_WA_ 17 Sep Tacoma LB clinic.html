From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-16 00:26:38 UTC
For folks who are interested in learning more about letterboxing and
looking for help/tips on carving, the Tacoma Nature Center at Snake
Lake in Tacoma (in conjunction with the Metro Parks System) is
hosting a Letterboxing Clinic, Sat, 17 Sep 05 from 10am-11:30am.
Cost is $2 for children (5&up), $4 for adults. Register at 253-591-
6439. If you don't register in advance but decide early Saturday
morning that you'd like to attend I doubt they'd have any issue with
that--they have a large facility and plenty of workstations for the
event. There will be several letterboxes planted for this event.

Earlier today I volunteered to teach parts of the class, the staff
of the Nature Center will teach other parts.

Hope to see you there,

dvn2r ckr
(Adventure Seeker)

Directions to Tacoma Nature Center:

Take I-5 to Tacoma
Head West on SR-16 (WA-16 / Bremerton / Sprague Ave.)
Turn RIGHT onto Ramp (So. 19th St. East)
Bear RIGHT (East) onto S 19th St
Look for the brown sign to Nature Center

If you get lost due to the major construction going on in the area,
make your way to Cheney Stadium, then head East toward Foss High
School, the Tacoma Nature Center is due East of the high school
campus's Northeastern corner--hidden within a greenbelt area.

P.S. If you're interested in just meeting up for exchanges, come to
the area and look for us on the trails sometime after the clinic.
We'll be the ones with inky fingers! ;)

The 17 Sep Tacoma Nature Center Letterboxing Workshop went really
well. I can't remember the exact number of folks who attended but it
was over a dozen adults and a handful of children. Everybody left with
a signature stamp and a logbook and set out on the trail to find some
nature-themed letterboxes immediately after the class.

The center is interested in hosting future classes on the 'sub-topics'
of letterboxing--bookbinding, stamp carving, etc. So keep your eyes
posted for future events. Looks like they'll have a workshop in Dec
and Jan on one or two of these yet TBD topics. It was so nice to get
to meet some active letterboxers in the area, too--after exchanging
emails for, in some cases, a couple of years it was nice to finally get
to meet the letterboxers in person.

dvn2r ckr
