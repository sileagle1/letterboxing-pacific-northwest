From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-31 05:08:24 UTC

My question is, is there anywhere to get
> a stamp made for you...


My local rubberstamping store will custom make a rubberstamp for you
using your own artwork (this is in West Seattle) They charge as
little as $12 --it all depends on the size of the stamp-- You might
check out your local rubberstamp store or scrapbooking store and see
if they do this. Or you might check out the local rubberstamping
store and see if they have a 'storebought' stamp that means something
to you and your family. I have no problem with people using store-
bought stamps if the stamp has significance to you. Then after you
have gone to some gatherings and seen folks carving...perhaps carved
a little yourself, then you might carve your own stamp later. :-)

--Amanda from Seattle

From: Dave Whitcher (fudrick@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-01-31 08:43:40 UTC-08:00
I tried my first, it wasn't too good, so did it a second time, found out is
was me that was the problem. That didn't stop me from keeping at it, and am
now up to 'not totally hopeless'. Give it a try, it isn't that hard, and it
gets better.

If you have the artwork, you can also go to an engraving shop, probably
named a trophy store. They can turn your artwork into a stamp. They
engrave the image in plastic and use heat and pressure to make the rubber
stamp.

Enjoy this great pastime, and give stamp carving a try.
