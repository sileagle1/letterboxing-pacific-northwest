It's exciting to see the new activity in the area! Hopefully, we'll

From: Rayvenhaus (rayvenhaus@myndworx.com) | Date: 2004-04-10 14:17:44 UTC-07:00
Good point there dvn2ckr. We ourselves haven't been to busy in the physical
aspect of letterboxing recently, more so with the intangible side of it at
the website, making some major changes to the interface to the database and
ll.

I feel that articles such as this are a mixed blessing. On one hand we'll
get more people who will try it because it's new. Some of those will
undoubtedly try a few times and grow tired of it, some with run for a few
weeks, and then start to slip away as other things in their lives clamor for
attentions and some will become very, very enthusiastic about it! (grin)
We'll end up with some people that do things out of pure meanness which
means we'll end up with a few boxes damaged or destroyed, but, to me, that
means just recreate and rehide.

We've been, on our end here, other than working on the next 3 sets we're
putting out. The Curse of the Black Pearl starts next weekend, Ghosts of
Washington will follow that and A Secret Society Do Exist! (Quote from a
song on a movie).

We've started putting together supplies to donate to the Scouting
organizations, so, if you know of any in the area that are interested in
Letterboxing, have them get in touch with me.

Other than that, we're looking forward to another gather here in the near
future in the PNW, any takers on coordinating one? We were originally
planning on Boxaplooza to coincide with Earthday, but since that's not
happening and I don't know of anyone else planning on an event this month,
mayhap someone can pull one together? The weathers turning wonderful for
it.
