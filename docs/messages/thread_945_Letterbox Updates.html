From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-11-18 23:10:14 UTC
Not sure anyone noticed that I was gone, but I'm back after a fun
time in Portland. Thanks to those that came out to visit while I was
in the area! It was a lot of fun! =)

As for letterboxing updates....

I headed out to check up on the Murder at Gabriel Park series, and
learned that two of the boxes were missing. And a third box couldn't
be found since one of the missing boxes led to it. Anyhow, I
replaced the missing boxes and all six are now up for grabs! =)

Tualatin Hills Nature Park Series: Three of the boxes were still
there. Two of them I carved replacements for and rehid, so the first
five are now back in action. However, due to changes in topography,
I've altered the original hiding places and will have to see about
getting the clues updated to reflect the fact. Who's the official
owner of these clues? The last three boxes of the series are
definitely missing.

In case you can't wait--Box #1 is almost at the same place, but I put
the box in the stump on the right side of the trail instead of where
it used to be behind the stump on the left side of the trail.

Box #5--follow the given clues until you find yourself on the
Ponderosa Loop. Keep walking until you reach a bench. The box is in
the large stump behind the bench. Of all the boxes in this park,
this is the ONLY ONE left that has my VERY FIRST, original signature
stamp. Most of you probably know about the purple spider sig stamp
which I used for several months, but this signature stamp predates
even that one! =) As far as I know, it's the only box left with
this original signature stamp in it. =)

And, as was already reported, Pooch Place is definitely missing. I'd
love to carve a replacement since it was such a wonderful stamp. And
also replacements for the rest of the Tualatin Hills Nature Park
series.

On Sunday, I headed out to Silver Falls for a quick check on some of
the boxes. Two are definitely missing. One that was reported as
missing I didn't get a chance to check up on. And Frenchie Falls I
couldn't even look for since I didn't bring the clues in English.
*rolling eyes* But at least 7 of the Silver Falls Series are
definitely there as of Sunday.

I'll still need to update the clue page for that series, though, so
you'll have to wait a day or two to find out which ones are alive and
well and which ones are really missing.

Carpe diem!

-- Ryan

I am the Nature Park guy... they were originally hidden for a letterbox
class the Nature Park hired me to teach. From there, we were given a table
at their Earth Day party and the series was expanded. In anticipation of
this first event, Paisley Orca hid a great batch of boxes in the Park. It
was later that the Park Ranger took exception with these boxes and asked for
them to be removed. They thought the boxes were too far off the paths and
would encourage off-trail hiking. I later toured the park with a ranger and
she gave her opinions on the hiding spots for all of my boxes.
Interestingly, as we walked, she showed me several trails leading off the
established routes & told me that they were probably geocaches. She said
that they didn't mind either on the property, but would like to be contacted
by the placer so they could get a nod of approval. Their big concern is
people going off trail.

Letterbox Updates

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-01-16 18:24:02 UTC

Recently, I have managed to check on several of my letterboxes. Bishop's Close and
Discovery Trail are both nestled in their damp homes awaiting new visitors. The book
at Discovery Trail is full but I didn't have one to fit the box with me. Take paper! The
ever elusive Hidden Dragon is very lonely but still resting in his den on Sauvie Island.
The Carty box at Ridgefield NWR is back in action with a replacement.

The trail at Ridgefield has changed since my original hide. The revised clue is up and
running at LbNA. This is the best time of year to hunt for this box if you have even a
passing interest in wildlife. The tundra swans and sandhill cranes are on the refuge.
Since the trees have no leaves, you have a much better chance of seeing some of the
resident bald eagles. We even spotted one with a kill in its talons on Thursday!

Once this ice melts, get away from that computer and go find a letterbox! Maybe we'll
meet on the trail. Funhog
