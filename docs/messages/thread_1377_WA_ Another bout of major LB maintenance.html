WA: Another bout of major LB maintenance

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-02 05:48:43 UTC
Fluttered about the state today checking on various letterboxes.
Just wanted to note that the following letterboxes are alive & well
(or assistance rendered to) as of 1 Jun 04:

Tumuli (was M*ima Mounds), replanted, revised clues
Chamber of Secrets-alive & well
New Market Falls-alive & well
Olympia Brewhaus-alive & well
Olympia Beer-poor shape, I took the liberty to retire the old log &
deteriorating stamp, recarved a new one, created a new log & will
send the originals home to Silver Eagle--same clues, same hiding
spot, now contains a very similar stamp
It's Raining Animals-alive & well, slightly revised clues to #1
Mushroom & Fern-alive & well (despite frequent missing reports)
Spectacles-alive & well but totally out in the open--somebody
accidentally stumbled upon it today, signed in and said they were
going to look into letterboxing but left the entire box visible to
ALL passersby, we carefully tucked it back into its nook and it
continues to live on
Sisters' Pagoda-alive & well & heavily visited--who knew?
Lion of Lion's Park-alive & well

no luck with Memories
no luck with 2 others at Tum Hist Park


Also, forgot to mention yesterday that we put the Heath Farm LB back
into play--clues have changed slightly to it but it's back, better
than ever! As clues to Weepy Clown #4 are dependent upon this LB,
the clues to the original hiding spot are still required to find the
clown. Not sure how to deal with this.

dvn2r ckr

Not bad, found 14 letterboxes in one day & NARY a find added toward
my PFX count. :( Did get to add a geocache to my count, though,
and a nifty pencil sharpener, to boot. ;)
