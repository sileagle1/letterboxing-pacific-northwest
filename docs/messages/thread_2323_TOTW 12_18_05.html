From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-12-19 19:12:30 UTC
I'll have to say that a HUGE percentage of the boxes I've found are in
places I had never, or rarely, visited before. I couldn't begin to
list them. However, a very special trip was one I took to the midwest
in 2002.

For ages and ages, I had wanted to go there to see the sandhill cranes
during spring migration. I finally made the time to do it but instead
of doing the 'normal' thing and flying into Omaha or Lincoln, I flew
into Kansas City. A girlfriend from St. Louis met me there. In the
motel room that night, she carved a sig stamp and in the morning we
set out on our adventure.

We traveled across North Central Kansas with Der Mad Stamper as our
guide. This is home to many of his earliest letterboxes. He took us to
the geodetic center of the US, the log cabin where Home On the Range
was written, a fossil sea bed, a prairie dog colony. We traveled
hundred of miles of two lane blacktop and saw lovely rural Kansas, a
place I might never have visited if it hadn't been for letterboxing.

The cranes, incidentally, were everything I had hoped they would be.
Plus, the letterboxes I left on that trip are still alive and well.
One has even been adopted by a local Audubon Center. Some letterboxer
dropped a clue sheet on their grounds and staff found it. Amazingly
enough, they took it under their wing, even removing it when they did
a controlled burn this fall! Funhog

Where has letterboxing led you that you otherwise would never have
known about?

--Ft Pike Louisiana at the mouth of Lake Pontchartrain's 'delta'
--New Orlean's gater-infested bayou
--hiking the Appalachian Trail along the east bluff overlooking the
Hudson River (I was born about a mile on the 'other side' of the
River but had never explored it the 6 years I lived there)
--Battle of Monmouth battlefield in New Jersey
--South Point Hawaii
--a beautiful pioneer cemetery in Cincinnati
--all around the greater Boise, Idaho area (Gem County especially!)
--a couple dozen unique and breathtakingly gorgeous spots around San
Diego
--many of the California missions (thank you Sierra Sally)

Seattle area:
Rattlesnake Ledge
Carkeek Park
Ravenna Park
Cougar Mountain
Ft Worden

--a bunch of places in Korea & China where we planted LBs
--a breathtaking 'rope' bridge across a waterfall chasm in British
Columbia

--searching for unique treasure up and down the
California/Oregon/Washington Coast and along the Columbia River Gorge

--and all those new (to us), unique, interesting and bizarre places
Funhog, Green Tortuga, Amanda from Seattle, Der Mad Stamper,
legerdemain, franzsolo, ABear, Fudrick, 7Nails, BOXDN, ModernGypsy,
Paisley Orca, Trial N Error, Evil Glenn, more recently
SoHillHendersons and myriad others have led us to over the years

It's been a fun & interesting journey!!!
