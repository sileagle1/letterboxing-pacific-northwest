Passport to Letterboxing Stamp Camp Event

From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-08-29 16:51:15 UTC-07:00
Hi Everyone,

I just wanted to take the time to say a few words about the Passport to
Letterboxing Stamp Camp Event.

This event was by far the most fun I've had in an extremely long time. I am
so proud of the turn out we had and I think Iron`Bear, Camp Fire Lady,
Maiden and Bookworm deserve a huge pat on the back.

I wanted to send a special thank you to Der Mad Stamper -- Mitch, whether
you realize it or not, meeting you had to be hands down, one of the most
memorable experiences I have ever had in letterboxing. Meeting you was an
honor I will never forget. Though I heard you had an encounter with Bigfoot
and had something to show for it that I forgot to ask for - so I hope to see
you again soon!

Team Garlic -- You are such a unique family and your kids are all so very
special. I cannot thank you enough for the kind birthday present you gave
me, the wonderful gift of your bagpipe playing and the opportunity to get to
know you guys.

Bethany - As usual you had something to do with yet another memorable moment
in letterboxing for me. When I saw your son passed out on the ground, sound
asleep, using a letterboxing bag as a pillow Saturday night I could not help
but think -- wow -- this is one of the moments you love the fact that
letterboxing is a family hobby, and a Kodak moment you don't want to miss!

Team Nashoba -- I am so grateful for getting the opportunity to meet you and
have gained such and understanding of your group. Pupp said something this
weekend that just tickled me pink -- that if she carved a stamp you could
get for cleaning her house our group would come get it! I think your right
-- so you let me know when to come do the dishes and I'll be there! And
thank you for the birthday card! It was just perfect!

Slo Mo, Wood Splinters & Ladybug -- I cannot tell you how special the three
of you made me feel. You were all so excited, so eager and so full of
compliments that you made me feel like a star when all I was really doing
was giving to you the gifts of knowledge other letterboxers and some
extraordinary carvers had given to me.

ISignasLily -- It was a pleasure to meet you and to be a part of introducing
you and your family to the wonderful experience of letterboxing events! You
are truly a delight and the carving you did during our carving workshop was
just awesome! I can't wait to see the stamps you will be putting out in the
future!

KeliKana - Thank you so much for attending and for all the gracious
compliments you gave our little group of hosts. I hope to see a lot more of
you and your family in the letterboxing community and I'm excited to see
where I run across your signature stamp in the future.

Cathy -- It was awesome to watch you as you experienced your first
letterboxing event - I can't wait to see what you end up selecting as a
trail name and the signature stamp you come up with to go along with it.

Goofy & Critter -- Watching the two of you work together in the workshops
was so fun! It is always touching to me to see a mother and daughter have so
much fun together. With my mom being in California it always makes me smile
to see mothers and daughters that have such good relationships. And Goofy,
that book you did came out better than mind did the first 10 times I did it!
Way to go! You're the bookbinding queen!!

Willow, Heartsy & Daisy -- Thanks for coming and thanks for attending my
birthday party, nice to see you again.

Maiden & Tarnished Knight - Thank you for helping to host one of the most
fun events I have ever attended. You bookbinding workshop was awesome and
defiantly the only way I will ever made log books again! And thank you for
bringing my Birthday Cake!

Bookworm & Seahunter - Thank you for all your help and assistance making
this event the best it could be -- and Bookworm, a special thank you to you
for rescuing my Leaf of Lorien Hitchhiker from a less that desirable fate! I
just adore you sometimes!

Team Rayvenhaus - Having you at this event was by far one of the high
points. I was so glad to actually get to participate in boxing with you
again. I know the last several months have been really hard on you guys and
it was so good to see you and the kids out having fun again! And Steve --
you and Iron`Bear mystify me with your ability to orchestrate such perfectly
synchronized snoring symphonies. Next time I'll make sure your camp site is
just a little bit farther away! Or I have earplugs -- either way I can't
wait to camp with you again!

Old Vet - every time I see you I am amazed at your enthusiasm, your sense of
humor and your ability to make all of us smile and awe -- I wish my dad
would get into letterboxing and participate in such a cool hobby with me.
Bethany is sure a lucky daughter!

Charlie Trail Duster -- as always it was an absolute pleasure to be around
you, and an honor to share the same birthday with you. Thank you again for
the cool coffee mug - I'm a big coffee drinker and now I finally have a
coffee mug I really like and all my own so I don't have to steal Iron`Bear's
Harley Davidson ones anymore! You are such an amazing person Charlie -- even
if you lie about your age and complain about the hat they made me wear too!

Last but not least -- Camp Fire Lady - I don't think this event would have
been half of what it was without you and it was yet again a reminder to me
how lucky I am to have such a wonderful friend. Iron`Bear and I love you
dearly and we cannot thank you enough for putting together this wonderful
event. The present you gave me was so absolutely perfect and beautiful that
I cannot tell you how special it will always be to me. It will find its
permanent spot on my desk here real soon and make me think of my friend and
smile every time I get a tool out of it.

If I missed anyone it is completely unintentional and I do apologize, with
such a busy weekend and so much going on it's hard for me to remember
everything. Everyone that attended Passport to Letterboxing helped make the
event the spectacular event it was and I thank each and every one of you!

Pandora

We had so much fun at the event that we only NOW got home!!! Of
course, we took the 'road less travelled' on the return trip and hit
a couple of rarely visited gems of letterboxes along the way.

Gigantic kudos to the folks who planned Stamp Camp!!! My kids and I
had a wonderful time and learned a bunch along the way. Honestly,
any event that gets my father (Ole Vet) doing something artistic
(carving stamps!!!) is a resounding success just by itself! I can
honestly say that I've NEVER seen him do anything artistic in my
entire 30+ something years!!! I presume the last time he carved
anything would have been the 'surgery' phase of med school sometime
in the 70s. My kids absolutely loved the event--as evidenced by the
previous comments about them falling asleep on the ground at the
birthday party! They keep asking when the next one will be. It was
so neat meeting all the letterboxers from the area, too. It's great
to be able to put names and faces with stamps & trail names.

Thank you for planning such a neat event! Can't wait for the next
one. I felt like it was a giant Scout Camp for adults--with every T
crossed and every I dotted! We had so much fun!!!
