The Concorde is IN Seattle! =)

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-11-06 02:37:44 UTC
Man, it was sooooo cool watching the Concorde come in! First it flew
over Boeing Field for everyone check out, then looped around over
SeaTac and landed.

Turns out it made a new record for a flight from New York to Seattle--
appropriate given the plane involved, I suppose of 3 hours, 55
minutes, and 12 seconds. Something to think about the next time any
of your board a plane between New York and Seattle and wonder why it
has to take so long. ;o)

Canada even opened a flight path over their northern territories
where the Concorde could break the speed of sound (usually a no-no
over land!) to make the record. I just love international
cooperation. *sniffle*

Sorry none of you guys were able to witness the event with me, though!

However, I'd heard rumors that the plane may yet fly once more....
Supposedly they have to fly it to Renton to do something to it (don't
look at me, I don't know!) and fly it back to Boeing Field tomorrow
before it becomes a permanent museum piece. So if you're driving
around the area, keep your eyes open for that piece of history. =)

Farewell!

-- Ryan
