Greetings from a new member

From: callik4lb (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-28 00:22:22 UTC
Hello all, I just want to introduce myself, a relatively new
letterboxer. I'm 55, married to someone who can't walk much, but we
run a medical word processing business together, so LBing is perfect
for me when I want to get out and about on the weekends or after work,
even when we are camping or otherwise away from home. Being together
is great, but neither of us minds when we go off alone since we are
together so much during the day. I'm pretty involved in my work and a
message board that is associated with that, but I want to spend some
time looking for boxes and watching this board for the feel of the
community. My interests are gardening, bicycling, RV camping,
calligraphy, and Kewpie dolls. You'll see a calligraphy K as my stamp
when I visit your books. We will be first-time grandparents in August
to a grandson from our son and his wife, who live in Terrebonne,
Oregon, near Redmond. I have only found a few boxes but had the fun
of looking one up in New York in 2004 and a couple in Eastern Oregon
as well as a few here nearer my home. I hope to have my first box,
The Lonely Juniper, placed in the next week or so, am working on a few
details. I need to spend some time reading the FAQs and such; I
already missed out on taking a hitchhiker stamp with me because I
didn't realize what it was but will know what to do next time. I'm
sure there are some etiquette type things I should be aware of as
well. We will be at Cape Lookout State Park over the coming holiday
and hope to check out some boxes near there, any suggestions will be
welcome. I know that many of you will be at Silver Falls a
beautiful place to spend some time with friends. I bought stamps for
my first box, but I carved my personal stamp and am looking forward to
fixing up more boxes to place as I have time and would like to carve
the stamps for them as well. You seem to be a nice bunch of people,
and perhaps I will meet one or more of you someday. I look forward to
at least enjoying the outdoors in this new and unique way as much as I
can. I actually thought I saw some letterboxers on a trail at Oxbow
I was seated a ways off fooling with the stamps and box I had just
found when I saw these people at a crucial intersection looking at a
paper with their heads together. Turns out they were just looking at
a map, but they were quite interested in what I was doing. I think I
told them enough but not too much, certainly not where I found what I
was looking at! I've learned to take gloves along for sticker bushes
and a stick for poking about in the leaves and dirt so far it has
been nothing but fun. Thank-you funhog and maiden for the kind
communications I've had with you, most likely will be more of that
considering how active you both are in this hobby. Enjoy your
weekend, wherever you may find yourself. Cheers from Kathy.
