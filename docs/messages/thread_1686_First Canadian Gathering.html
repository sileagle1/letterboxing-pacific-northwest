First Canadian Gathering

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-10-11 19:47:19 UTC

The weekend get together in beautiful (and very WET) BC was good fun. Many thanks
to the Secret Gardeners for introducing us to Canada's Thanksgiving, a quaint historic
town and a wonderful park. It was especially fun to meet the bearers of the
letterboxing torch in this province, Zara and CSB.

These boxers seem to be the only serious hobbyists in the area. If anyone visits their
stomping grounds, it'd be a great gift to leave a box or two behind for them to find.
As always, meeting with old friends, as well as making new ones, added to the
enjoyment of this intimate event. Funhog

Iron`Bear and I made it back just fine :) We stopped on our way out to pick

Re: First Canadian Gathering

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-12 16:20:30 UTC

Wow! What a fun time--in spite of the liquid sunshine! Many kudos
to the Secret Gardeners and Canada Goose for planning & organizing
this neat event. We really enjoyed seeing our 'friends in
letterboxing' and meeting new friends, too. What delightful
letterboxes in the area, too! My children enjoyed the festivities
and already can't wait to return.

After the gathering, we braved the 'tarp-draped tent' one more
night, found a few more Ft Langley LB (BTW 'Wood' was damaged--we
only found the lid & the stamp and tucked them into a ziploc bag and
hid it in the dust at the base of the hollow tree) and then worked
our way over to N. Vancouver. We sought out the Lynn Canyon
Letterboxes (did you know the suspension bridge is closed for a
while?) and they were very much alive & well although a bit more
challenging without access to the 'bridge' this month.

Then we visited the 'tourist trap' of Capilano Suspension Bridge--
really to explore the Tree Top Adventure. The kids loved the 'Swiss
Family Robinson' style tree house and crossing the incredibly wobbly
suspension bridge--a bit scary when you're one adult with two little
ones and only two hands available. Mom's still not sure if
she 'enjoyed' that part of it. ;)

Anyways, thanks so much for planning & participating in this event!
It truly was the 'jump start' we needed to get our ducks in a row to
cross the border (passports-wise). Now, we'll be able to come & go
anytime!

See you at the next Canadian BC Gathering!!!

dvn2r ckr, rnrB & trkr (hopefully, trekee can attend the next event!)
