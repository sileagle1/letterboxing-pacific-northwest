WA: Wright Letterbox...finally!

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-07 03:21:55 UTC
Sort of like a prodigal letterbox story, but not quite...

After a couple of years, a long hiatus, and numerous unsuccessful
attempts, somebody has FINALLY found this letterbox. What a comfort
to know it's still there and that somebody has actually stamped into
it.

Fudrick attempted it back in Winter 2002/2003 right after it was
first planted AND he was really close--but didn't 'connect'. Then,
it took a hiatus for a long while. Then, it was replanted and at
least three groups attempted it since--but no luck. BUT one recent
seeker was fortunate, the stars/planets aligned and all that jazz
and they actually found it--so if you're looking for a neat place to
take Mom for Mum's Day and a nice place for a stroll in the park,
check out this Wright Letterbox in Tacoma. It's definitely been
waiting for visitors!!!

And, if you can venture a few miles further West of there, the
rhodies in Pt Defiance Park were planted 50 years ago with the full
intention that they will PEAK this May (ie ASAP) for the 100th
Anniversary of Pt Defiance Park! So, it's truly a once in a
lifetime opportunity to see some gorgeous plants at their peak of
beauty. Unfortunately, I'm some 7,000 miles away and won't be able
to enjoy them, yet. :(

Not sure about the letterboxes (if any) in the vicinity of these
blooms, but the natural treasures alone should be reason enough to
head out that way. If you can find a letterbox or two along the way,
bonus!!!

Enjoy!
