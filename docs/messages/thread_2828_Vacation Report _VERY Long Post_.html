Vacation Report (VERY Long Post)

From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-08-18 16:16:47 UTC
For those that dislike long posts listing of "Found" letterboxes
please jump over this one. Gwen may go back and send individual notes
to the placers, but here is a post that covers most of our finds. Now
should you choose to jump over this post then of course you should
realize you will miss the story of Sahallie Falls.

*Don't Know Jack, nice carving and great location.
*Dee Wright Observatory, we got both boxes, and it was interesting
that each box had different finders stamping in.
*Gazing the Night Sky, quite a collection of stamps and interesting
location.
*All Girls are Princesses.
*Calliope Crossing, Early Birder plant.
*Gooblers Knob, The dogs and Gwen stayed on top while I did the
climbing down.Yikes!
*Sweet Fragrance& A River Hood, both quick nice finds
*Fruit Loop Series, Lost Lake Series were shared with a brand new
letterboxer that lives in Hood River.
*Trillium Series, early Wingnut
*Pioneer Conestoga, Mark & Sue plant
*Cloud Cap Inn, I talked with some of the volunteer recovery searchers
*Columbia River Gorge Wine Tour, and WOW!!!!! the bonus stamp at
Cathedral Winery. That stamp is the most inticate carving we have
ever seen. Double Tree blew us away.
*Cheyennes Summer Vacation.
*Hood River Marina series, We were but a few hours behind Double Tree
and were with Kuriou Jo and Wood Aug
*Spidey and Gentle Touch was shared with a new letterboxer
*The HOG'S Hurricane Llamas...Oh that trail?
*Rest Day, alive and well.
*Fish Kiss, some of Pirates of the Carribian
*Wood Aug's Elk, Gwen took the ride, Don watched the dogs.
*Jr. Asparagus and Larry the Cucumber
*Eagle Lake
Of course we left a few in our wake, so anyone finding that our boxes
need some work please help us out.

We didn't print out clues to some of the places we went and this
could be the case of Sahallie Falls. We saw this sign for Sahalie
Falls,and took the turn off for about a mile to an old bridge and a
beautiful falls. I had carved the stamp (see Endangered Bull Trout LB)
that I was just itching to plant. While Gwen was checking out the
bridge and falls I went looking for a likely hiding spot. I found
this long log next to the road running parallel to the road and went
down to the far end. I went around to the down hill side at the far
end and started poking around under the log. I figured that this
would be easy to identify for clues. All of a sudden my feet slipped
out from under me and I went down on my left hand and ended up on my
butt. I sat there for a while kind of taking inventory of any
damages.
I startered to get up, looked at my left hand that was covered in
something and realized with the accompanied smell that I landed in
crap. Now I've been in my share of doodoo in my life so I quickly
understood that this wasn't dog doodoo or bear doodoo but stinky
human doodoo. YUK! Oh well it was just a bit on my left hand and a
small bit on my pants left side, I can handle that. I got up got out
some water containers and was able to wash the stuff off. Gwen
maintaining her composure and not giggling turned to me and asked" Do
you have any extra pants in the truck?" Knowing that I had completely
washed everything off asked why as I was getting some shorts out of
the truck. She just said don't bother to ask, just change, but leave
the pants in the back of the truck. It seems I hit the pile of doodoo
smack dab in the middle of my butt.

There is no Don & Gwen letterbox at Sahallie Falls as I didn't want
to leave the previously carved stamp there and didn't want to carve a
new one. Had I left one you know what the carving would have looked
like and we all have seen enough crap in our lives.

Next time I see a prone log I'm not going on the downhill side to
poke around.

All in all, we had a great time in the PNW finding all of the boxes,
spending time fishing and Kayaking on your beautiful lakes and rivers.
Thanks for planting!

Don
