about 8 hours (including driving time).

hours. The length of the hike is not a problem, but our lack of time
is.

I would love to find your letterbox, and I hope I have the opportunity
to hunt for it some day. Too bad it's not in my neighbourhood!

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-14 09:16:49 UTC-07:00
The only reason a lot of my new boxes get visitors is because South Puget Sound is hungry for new ones. After the regulars in the area stamp in, there's very few visitors. I noticed some time ago that Bellingham's pretty skimpy on the Letterboxers, so I tried to do my part, but ended up moving and was only able to place a few in convenient places! The Faery Tryst isn't THAT short of a hike...the map on the city parks site even lists some of the segments as being "difficult." Tsk tsk, you and your high expectations... ^_-

My friend, "Vida," and I got in a lot of our Letterboxing by traveling to Canada and finding CSB and Zara's boxes, as well as others. If you REALLY want to plant a tough box, you can steal the Easton Glacier hike, which I will probably never manage to do. That's a few miles uphill. But then that IS a National Park... Still, you can plant tough ones, or get a lot of visitors. Rarely will you get both!

Would you happen to know the state of my Whatcom Falls box? Last I knew it had been pulled apart and left in shambles, but a 'boxer put back together what was left of it. Is there a new log book? I can't get an update from anybody, and I can't get back there to check it out for myself!

Happy Trails,
ClioMouse
