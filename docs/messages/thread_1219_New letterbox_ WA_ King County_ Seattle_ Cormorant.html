Cormorant Cove Park Letterbox
Seattle, Washington
King County
Directions to Cormorant Cove Park can be found at:
http://www.cityofseattle.net/parks/parkspaces/cormorantcove.htm
3701 Beach Drive Southwest
Hours of the park are 4:30 AM to 11:30 PM.
Clue Difficulty: Easy
Terrain: Easy, with some scrambling over boulders and driftwood. Kid friendly!
Bring a camera, binoculars and a lunch.

Cormorant Cove Park is located at the T-intersection of 61st Ave. SW and
Beach Drive SW. As a kid, I grew up in this neighborhood. At that time, the
Beach Drive Market was located here. It was a nice Mom and Pop store where
we'd grab a cold drink or some candy on the way home. After the Market was
torn down, the lot was left empty. The City of Seattle purchased it and
installed the present Cormorant Cove Park. It's a great place to bird watch.

Clues:
Find the metal handrail that has images of cormorants woven in it. Walk
down the steps. At the bottom of the steps, head 120 degrees (all readings
are magnetic) for 38 paces. Face 240 degrees and take 19 paces. Here you
will find a circular patio with a mosaic pattern of a Cormorant at its
center. Take time to read about the cormorant, the park and other birds you
may see from this location.

Retrace your steps to the base of the metal cormorant handrail. With the
handrail to your back, bear 260 degrees for 15 paces. Did you find a bench?
