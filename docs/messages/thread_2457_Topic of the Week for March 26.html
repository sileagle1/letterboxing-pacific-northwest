From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2006-03-26 20:47:48 UTC-08:00
Ive seen lots of different types of log books since Im an active postal
letterboxer. My favorite ones have been the most unique. Some are very
simple, some are more intricate, but the ones that reflect the theme of the
box are always my favorite.



One of my favorite ones had shiny scales on it for a dragon box. Ive
always loved a certain Portland letterboxers Coptic stitch boxes, Ive
personally found stiff craft foam to make good covers.



Another hint: You can get book boards in several thicknesses at Art Media.




Maiden

From: SloMo (SloMo86@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-03-27 00:21:27 UTC-08:00
I like log books with thick enough paper so the ink doesn't bleed through so
we got a ream of cover stock on sale. Using one of his never-ending wood
shop tools, Wood-aug cut it into various size rectangles. They're crude
books compared to Camp Fire Lady's and other hand made books.

We ran across a log book that has the center cardboard from a roll of toilet
paper for a cover. It fit the theme of the box too! (No, it wasn't potty
humor) I still smile when I think of that one! Very funny.

SloMo
