The Generousity of Strangers

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2006-12-04 15:43:50 UTC
Since this is the holiday season and a time of giving and doing good
unto others, I thought I would share a wonderful story with you all:

I had planted A Box of Chocolates in a small park in Pleasant
Valley, WA near Vancouver. I had gotten numerous reports that it was
missing and that there was another box in it's place which of course
wasn't one of mine. In this park is a small creek approximately 30
feet away from where my box was hidden and that posed no threat
whatsoever as to the box being swept away. I am assuming that my box
had been tossed into the creek. Anyway, I got an email the other
night from a fisherman who had been doing some fishing in Salmon
Creek. He told me that he had found my box stuck in a hard to reach
log jam. He fished out my letterbox and was kind enough to research
the letterboxing.org site so he could return my box to me. I met up
with him at a Park and Ride parking lot where he returned my
letterbox in very good condition I might add and told me that this
hobby sounded very interesting to him and that it might be something
he and his kids might be interested in doing. We chatted for a bit
and then went our seperate ways. It is the generousity of strangers
like this fisherman that make the holidays and letterboxing for that
matter, all the more meaningful to me.
Needless to say, I have pulled the clues for A Box of Chocolates so
whoever has their box planted in this spot might want to post their
own clues to their box. I will relocate my box somewhere else as
soon as I find a spot. Happy Holidays and Happy Trails to you all!
