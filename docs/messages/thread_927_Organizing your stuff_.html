I'm wondering how all of you store your letterboxing stuff. I just
cleaned out our big..."box". It was delapitated ripped down two of
the corners and shoved up against two walls to hold it together. It
was full of wrappers from opening new boxes, erasers and other
letterboxing item packages.

After sorting through it all we've finally got all the clue pages in
a file folder, carving medium in another container and boxes upon
boxes waiting to be filled stacked in, of all things, a rubbermaid
type storage container.

I'm just about ready to invest in a little plastic roll cart with
drawers! LOL

How do you store your stuff?

I am lucky to work at home and have a small studio in the house. It has
built in cabinets and a handy sink along one wall. I also have a very large
oak drawing board that I bartered for on one of my first paying art jobs lo
so many years ago. Most of my art/computer equipment is stuffed, stacked
and jammed in this room. I also have built in storage in the garage. What
can live out there, does. If it gets too neat, I freak out. I thrive in
clutter. There are all sorts of little plastic containers, airbrush
equipment and rolls of sign vinyl that come tumbling out every time I open
the cupboard under the sink.

Most of the rest I try to keep in my backpack. Since it flies with me on
the too numerous business trips, I can't always take my carving stuff, but
the signature stamp, an ink pad and my letterbox log goes on each and every
trip. Clues are downloaded and printed as needed.
