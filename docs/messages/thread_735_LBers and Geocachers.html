LBers and Geocachers

From: Glenn (Glenn.Hansen@usa.net) | Date: 2003-09-23 08:26:12 UTC-07:00
The main difference between LBers and Geocachers is LBer's clues are usually
more precise causing us to search an area less. The bottom line is both
groups are known to not always leave the area the same way they found it.
Many a box can be found by following the path to the pile of sticks
signaling it's hiding place. If we want this hobby to continue we need to be
selective and creative in choosing our hiding places AND follow Rayvenhaus's
example and clean up behind ourselves and sometimes others. We already have
a problem with boxes in some State and Federal Parks and it isn't because we
are a positive impact on the parks. To change this, if it's not too late, we
had better take a close look at how and where we are hiding our boxes.

All this ties into something that has been on my mind for quite some time.
The first box I found was on Rattlesnake Ledge. A moderate hike, HA! A
killer for this old man but well worth the effort. A beautiful place. It
made me think that a big part of Letterboxing was finding a special place to
lead people to. What I have seen since then is a shift from quality to
quantity (Sorry Ryan). To some it seems to have turned into a race of how
many boxes can they get or place and little else.

Just my opinion and maybe a tibit for thought.



Evil Glenn

My intention was not to bad mouth any individual or any individuals stamps. Buried in my blather was the point I feel we need to be very careful where we place our boxes and my personal preference is placing them someplace really worth visiting. Quality over quantity, instead of many stamps in one area, visit the same locations in said area while looking for fewer boxes. The more we plant the more likely we are to be found out by the wrong folks. Rather than more boxes I would like to see more time on clues and hiding places. Not only is finding a new "neat" spot fun but being challenged by well thought out clues is a kick.

But it is just my opinion so please don't be offended. OTOH the do call me Evil.

From: wingnut_lb (wingnut_lb@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-09-24 05:39:07 UTC
I do agree that there are quite a few boxes being placed lately and
quite a few of lesser quality in all aspects, hikes, carvings, clues,
origonality..... I suppose being a newbie myself I am guilty of this
even, and no, no offense was taken, I think as we "mature" we figure
out better ways to box.... As a newbie and a mother of a 2 year old
in tow, I also see the value for some easy boxes... it keeps it
interesting and provides some access to the sport that others
wouldn't get if they had to always do a 6 mile hike.

ALTHOUGH..... I have become very dissapointed lately with bad
rehides, and even bad placement of new boxes.... (that go missing
within days of placement) and 30 second carvings..... or even worse,
store bought stamps. I blame it partially on the recent articles in
local papers. I certainly hope when the publicity dies down, things
can go back to being much more descreet! New people mean new
boxes..... I think that's a good thing.... and I am sure many people
will fade given a bit of time for the newness to wear off.

I find as I get more into it, I am writing better clues, placing
better, and carving much better...... I have definately adopted a
quality over quantity rule for myself, and a sense of respect for
everyone, by checking up frequently on my placed boxes... and
maintaing clues. I try to only place what I can keep up with.

I see the value in the longer hikes for just one box... And really
enjoy the hiking and all the nature I am seeing. I am even finding
ways to sneak out for a day with out my 2 year old to do the more
dificult boxes....

All in all I would have to say.... there is something for everyone.
And I hope that the new people take the time to research things a bit
and think throught thier carvings and placings, and maintence, it
makes it that much more enjoyable for all!

And Mr Evil (he he he) We are all entitled to an opinion.... speak
away, I love to hear it! Wingnut

I too am a newbie and have a family in tow, and I also see the value of some easier boxes. If
the hikes get too long then I start hearing, "are we there yet?" they lose interest and then the
outting loses its purpose, having FUN. So maybe the solution is that if you don't like the drop
by or easy boxes then don't get them. The same for us with small children, we may not want to
go get those boxes that are longer hikes. I think the variety is great! As for the quality, I agree,
we need to make a valiant effort to make those stamps, clues and hiding places special. there is
no greater thrill than figuring out great clues and being rewarded with a great stamp! A good
example of some great clues is "Quail Ale" in Central Oregon, placed by Funhog. Check it out.
Happy letterboxing,
