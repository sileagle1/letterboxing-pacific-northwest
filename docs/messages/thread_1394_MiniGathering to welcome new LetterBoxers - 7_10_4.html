MiniGathering to welcome new LetterBoxers - 7/10/4

From: Dave (fudrick@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-06-17 05:57:41 UTC
You may recall that Funhog is now a BLT!! Bona-fide LetterBoxing
Trainer! If your recall is as good as your assuredness of being at
the exact right spot for a box, see below :-)

Her LetterBoxing class is going to be in Longview. The final exam
is going to a local park to find some LetterBoxes. She has invited
others to join in welcoming the Newbies. This fun event will be
Saturday, July 10th (see the Longview Parks and Rec website for
details).

She has some very specific rules for this gathering. They are:

This is a paid class, for the paying students. Funhog obtained
permission for other LBers to join in to welcome the Newbies, but
the Newbies get first chance at the boxes. They get the clues at
1:00 (start of class), we can show up at the park at 2:30 to get
clues and find boxes. We can meet somewhere nearby and carpool to
the park, arriving just before 2:30, or show up individually,
arriving just before 2:30. The main point here is to arrive no
earlier than just before 2:30. A prefunction, postfunction, or
hunting of other area boxes is not out of the question, it just
can't be at the park the class is being held at.

She has permission to keep the gate open later than 4:00 for this
event. We will all (Oldbies and Newbies) gather back at the
beginning point at 4ish to meet and greet the Newbies, swap tales
and exploits, convince them that LetterBoxing is the greatest sport
there is, congratulate Funhog on her fine class, get the award-
winning event stamp, and perform the exchange ritual.

Wake Robin is a fairly small park used for environmental learning by
kids and parks and rec groups and others in the Kelso-Longview
area. We will need to be on our best behavior, proving that
LetterBoxing is eco-friendly, by being good stewards of the
environment and the park. So, everyone should be especially careful
about going off trail, disrupting plants, rocks, logs, etc. She
worked hard to get permission to leave these letterboxes on the
property indefinitely, not just for this one day. However, bad
behavior will get us 86ed, and possibly create a bad reputation that
will spread to other Parks and Rec Departments.

She will publish the directions to Wake Robin shortly before July
7th, when she gets home from yet another road trip (the sun came
out, a letterboxing we will go). The clues will be available at 2:30
at the park. This is to avoid the temptation to go early and claim
firsties. Firsties have been reserved for the students, so they can
have the thrill of finding new boxes. Show up just before 2:30 and
you will have your chance.

How much fun could a fun hog hog if a funhog could hog fun? Even
with these requrirements, Funhog would still have a great time.

Come join the fun and welcome the Newbies. See you there!!!

Fudrick

PS: Funhog may have more information and/or requirements, they will
be included with the directions or other informative communications.
