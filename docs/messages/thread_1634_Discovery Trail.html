This letterbox has been in its current location since March 8, 2002. To my knowledge,
this is the first letterboxer to have a break-in. I have been there as recently as July. I
agree that like any trailhead this area has its share of problems and is no worse than
any other and would never hesitate to go there. Please do not avoid this box.

I recently read that in Montreal they issue tickets for leaving desirable objects in plain
view in your car! While I don't believe this is a good idea, I, personally, never leave
anything in of interest in sight when I leave my car. In fact, I do not put anything in
the trunk and then walk away... I always plan ahead. Be safe! Funhog

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-09-21 19:34:52 UTC
> but break ins in parking lots/trail heads are pretty well known
> issues...

That reminds me.... When Amanda and I went to the Ft. Steilacoom
event to hide our boxes in the park before the South Sound
Letterboxing Stamp Fest (or whatever the event was called--I'm
getting foggy on it now!), we had driven up within minutes after a
car had been broken into. I parked next to them then Amanda and I
went off to hide our boxes.

She was kind of concerned about OUR car--but I told her not to
worry. The break-in had been reported to authorities and there was a
large crowd forming around the broken-in car. They wouldn't be THAT
stupid to break into another car right next to them with such an
audience nearby! =)

Just glad we didn't park ten minutes earlier and left to hide the
boxes....

As a side note, the victims of the break in said that the woman's
purse was stolen out from the front seat--it had been left in plain
view. *shaking head*

Break-ins can and do happen everywhere. The best thing you can do is
make sure to hide those valuables!

I had a friend who, in his younger, wilder days used to break into
cars to steal CDs he saw that he wanted. (Actually, he finally quit
that habit when he was caught!) Wasn't even to steal money, but just
a CD that he wanted. Would crack a baseball bat against the
windshield which he said was VERY cool to watch it shatter.

But after he was caught, he was required to pay for all the damage he
caused. Decided a $10 CD wasn't worth paying hundreds of dollars
for.... =)

Anyhow, the point of my story is this: Even a $10 CD was
considered 'valuable' by this person. Best bet: Leave nothing in
plain view--even if you don't think it has any great value.

-- Ryan
