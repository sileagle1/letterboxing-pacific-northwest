Beach Box Update

From: Kris Leibrand (bookwormlb@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-03 16:22:59 UTC
It was a terrific weekend on the Oregon Coast, even if it did rain
on my every time I pulled out my log book. While I went down there
to scrapbook, I managed to sneak in a couple of letterboxing
moments. I thought I'd give you all an update on the boxes I went
looking for this weekend.

Ona At Beaver Creek did not find

Tank's First Camp Out did not find, pretty sure it's missing

Jett's Victory Tour did not find, pretty sure it's missing

Viking Victories found (box was destroyed, though. I contacted
the placer and, on her request, removed the box for maintenance)

Chicken of the Sea did not find, pretty sure it's missing

Yahoo Kernville! alive and well (clever hiding spot, too)

Chris the Crab at Cape Foulweather alive and well (I renamed this
one Kris the Krab at Kape Foulweather because I twisted my knee here
and was most grouchy. Great stamp though, and such a beautiful
spot. Also, if any of you collect smashed pennies, there is a
machine here)

Beverly Beach Duo the river/creek was too high to cross, so I
couldn't look for box #1, and did not look for #2 (see Kris the Krab
note)

Canteen did not find, pretty sure it's missing

Bookworm Box alive and well. AWESOME box. Thank you for planting
this box just for me :-)

Howard Street Charter School Alive and well. Bring nuts for the
squirrels!

MITCH Charter School alive and well.

Bush's Pasture Park alive and well.

Road Trip Series Salem to the Coast Did this one backwards.
#7 alive and well
#6 alive and well
#5 did not find, pretty sure it's missing
#4 wasn't paying attention and passed the place. Dang it!!!
#3 alive and well
#2 did not find, pretty sure it's missing
#1 alive and well

I guess I got more letterboxes than I did scrapbooking pages
completed, but it was fun. I noted the ones that I found the spot
that seemed to be the right place but did not find the box. Please
remember that no box is truly considered missing until the placer
says it is. I have been known to miss a box that is right under my
nose!

Bookworm
