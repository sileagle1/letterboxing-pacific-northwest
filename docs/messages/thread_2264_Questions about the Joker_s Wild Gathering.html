From: melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2005-10-02 19:36:25 UTC-07:00
Hi all!

Here's the low down.

On October 1st, 2005 31 people gathered at 9:30 to receive their "info
booklet". Included in that booklet was the rules of the day, a map of the
park, a poker cheat sheet, and the official score card. Also included were
the clues to 52 boxes hidden in the park. Each box contained a stamp
representing one card from a deck of cards. The rules pointed out that there
was in fact a 53rd box, the Joker, if you could find it. The clues were
folded in half and sealed so that no one could look at them until 10:00. At
10:00 AM, all guests were welcomed and instructed to open their clues. They
then had 2 hours to search out as many boxes as they could. No where on the
clue sheet did it indicate which card was at which location. Once you found
a box, you could stamp it into your log book and then you had to decide
whether you wanted to stamp it on to your official score sheet. The official
score sheet had room for only 7 cards. With 15 minutes left we blew a
whistle to give everyone a heads up. At 12:00 exactly, those that had
returned had their had scored by the pit boss. Ryan had the winning hand
with a full house. (Including the Joker). He received a beautiful wooden box
that says "Poker Grand Champion" on it. Double Tree and Braidy got the prize
for most boxes found (13). They received a hand made fleece poker blanket.
The second place person got a set of ceramic poker coasters, third place got
a set of poker candles, and the highest pair got a set of poker chips.
Others got poker sets, including the last person in, half an hour past the
deadline, Wanderlust and Tooth Fairy :) Everyone went home with either a
deck of cards or one of our personalized votive/shot glass. The
personalization said "Joker's Wild Letterbox Gathering, October 1st, 2005".
After prizes had been given out, the potluck commenced with a poker cake and
lots of good food. (I want the recipe for those potstickers!).

I've posted some pictures at Atlasquest and hope that others that took
pictures will do so as well.

Maiden
