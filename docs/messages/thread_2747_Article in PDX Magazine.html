Hi there - not sure if this has already been noted and discussed, but I
was just looking through the latest PDX Magazine and saw that they
devoted 2 pages to an article on letterboxing. It covered the "basics,"
but unfortunately left out some really important aspects that make
letterboxing what it is...just one example, it says that you should
bring a rubber stamp, but doesn't say anything about carving your own,
nor does it give credit to all of the beautiful, creative hand-carved
stamps out there.

Although the author gives the usual disclosure about discretion, I
personally was disappointed by the overall article. PDX Magazine is a
freebie, and while it's very nicely done compared to a lot of other
freebies, it's basically a glossy hipster's guide to the ultratendy
hotspots in Portland. They give it away at local hotels, bars, shops,
etc. I just hope it attracts some new boxers who take the sport
seriously, and that we don't see an increase in missing boxes.
- Monkeygirl
