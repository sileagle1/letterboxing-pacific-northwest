From: Kathy (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-08-01 15:56:58 UTC
Went to Lost Lake this past weekend to place some boxes, found one
placed by Kristi called "The Lost Lake," but it was empty save for a
crayon, a bandaid, a little piece of wood and two pieces of paper with
various names (no stamps) on them and a comment "yeah, quit littering"
written in child-like handwriting. Was also sitting in plain site
from 20 feet away right off the trail where it was supposed to be,
only with no "concave rock" as a covering. It was closed and dry, a
nice tupperware-type box with a good seal. I was tempted to bring it
home with me but decided to put a small notebook in there for people
to use stamping in and try again to contact the placer. Had tried to
contact her before I went to get her OK for me to place boxes there -
receiving no reply I decided to go ahead and place them anyway. Do
people often plant boxes with no stamp and/or log book? There was a
recipe card in there saying something to the effect of "congratulation
on finding me, please write any comments you may have" but not much
more information than that, especially not her name or e-mail address.
Is a box without a stamp and/or log book considered trash even if
hidden? This one is hidden again now, as I put it back in it's hole
and covered it with the rock as described in the clues, but I would
like to know what to do if I run into that situation again. What do I
do if I don't get any reply at all from her? I don't intend to go up
there for at least a year unless my boxes need attention. I plan to
include a short blurb about her box in my own clues - is that a good
idea? Seems the only way to alert people that the box is there but is
stamp-less. Also, her clues don't give directions to the lake, says
"what would be the fun in that?", but I would like to give driving
directions because there is one way up there that is much shorter than
any other and not apparent on maps. I'm thinking of waiting a few
days and then posting my clue with driving directions if I don't hear
from her - any opinions?



Re: lost lake letterboxes

From: wingnut_lb (wingnut_lb@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-01 16:27:53 UTC
I am sure you will get quite a few responses to this post. It sounds
like someone has placed a letterbox in the vacinity of a Goecache and
the Cachers have found it and begun to us it as such. Unfortunately,
they take something and leave something, this is why you found items
in the box. They probably took the stamp not knowing what a
letterbox was. The best thing you can do is try to contact the
placer, and leave it alone really. It is always nice to see people
helping to maintain our boxes, but if the placer has abandoned it,
there's not a whole lot you can do.

As far as your clues, I would highly suggest that you make them a
seperate entity from this box. Write them how you like including
directions and publish them. I try to never give information in my
clues for other's boxes, I have had people freak out on me about it.
It seems that many others don't like any references to their boxes,
especially if they went to great lengths to not add directions or
make them not easy to find. I suppose it comes down to a "live and
let live attitude" I have tried to help ppl only to get screamed at
for it, so I have found it best to take care of my boxes and leave
others to their owners. We all have different ideas of how to place
and write clues for our boxes, which gives us infintie choices to
hunt for, so I guess, I just choose to respect the way someone else
does it and not go changing anything.

Please don't think I am telling you what to do, or that this is
written harshly, it is so hard to tell in emails! I am just bouncing
some ideas off for you, 'casue you asked..... There are a whole
bunch of new boxers out there now, we love to see them, it means more
boxes! But there is a huge trend happening that I see; people trying
to take on caring for everything. It really does suck when you go
out and find a box that is not maintained, missing, or Geocached.
But I guess in the end, my opinion is it's not yours to fix or
change. I know it's exciting to box and we want all boxes to be
found by everyone it seems. It's just all part of the game, to not
find some and not figure some clues and locations out, and to have
some go missing. If we all take responsibility for our own, I think
we would be on the right track.

I hope my 2 cents helped! Keep on boxing! Wingnut

Hey callik4lb! I see nothing wrong with carrying an extra note/log
book and a few extra baggies and paper towels along with my kit. I

there, the logbooks are a lot of fun to look at. You'll be a seasoned
letterboxer in no time! Wingnut

He he, a "thin line" remember, "there are no rules"..... none except
the thousands of unwritten ones I have found. Some people care, some
people don't I have found. I suppose it just comes down to talking
to some seasoned vetrans and figuring it all out as it happens.....
Respect for people and their boxes and the way they do it is always
nice. I figure, I can do my boxes my way, and everyone else's aren't
my responsibility now..... That sounds harsh, but I suppose if
people want help they will ask?

After the "newness" wears off, you start to worry less, and enjoy
boxing more. I have met several boxers now, I know their styles,
their likes and dislikes for their own boxes, clues, etc. If I don't
know someone, I just leave it alone and resist the urge to "help"
until I contact them about it. Seems to work the best for me at
least. You may have, or have not offended someone already, trust me,
they will get over it! If you don't tred on someone's toes around
here, you aren't boxing enough! :) Just keep doing what you are
doing! Wingnut
