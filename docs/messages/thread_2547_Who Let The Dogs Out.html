Let me tell you the story about the Little Shoes.

Some of the Forest People (and a Wild Rowan) went looking for Who Let
The Dogs Out this past Sunday. It started out poorly with us stopping
at the wrong trailhead. We ultimately discovered that we had driven
past the correct parking spot to the next point at which the Wildwood
Trail comes close to the road.
Sitting on a sign post at this trailhead we noticed one lonely toddler's
sandal.

After vainly attempting to follow the clues we ultimately worked our way
around to the trailhead we should have started at, and realizing our
mistake, started out again to find "Skip". Now the clues lead us right
to him.
While stamping in at a nearby log making a convienent seat, we
discovered a second lonely sandal, the mate to the first! Using our
best Sherlock Holmes deductive reasoning, we determined that these shoes
belonged to a young letterboxer, under 1 year old (no wear on the soles,
but scuff marks on the toes from crawling) who had been riding in a
backpack and also looking for Skip, Fido and Spot.. This young
letterboxer, wanting to leave a trail back to his/her point of origin
had kicked off first one, then later the other shoe.

Our path took us back to the first trailhead where we reunited the two
little shoes. After searching successfully for "Fido" and
unsuccessfully for "Spot" (where is that witness post anyway?) we
returned and retrieved the little shoes, thinking that a letterboxer
would be missing them, and might be reading this list.
We would be happy to return them to their rightful owner if possible,
but will donate them to Goodwill if we don't hear from anyone.

So there you have the story of the Little Shoes.

David (of the Forest People)
