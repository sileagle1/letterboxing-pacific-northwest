Sometimes you don't find the box, but you still have a great visit

From: (PNWEXPLR@aol.com) | Date: 2003-10-05 02:11:57 UTC-04:00
I went in search of the Spruce Goose today (McMinnville, OR), with
nine-year-olds Button & Wild Child. I'm sorry to report that I think the box is
missing...but we found other very cool things instead in this little airport park.
The Mushroom House (wow, with cave paintings!) & shell fountains mentioned in
the clues were wonderful--and some other visitors to the park showed us the
madrona home of a little Pacific treefrog.

Now, I've seen many a treefrog in my day, but despite the name, I have never
found one in a tree. According to the folks who showed it to us, they've
found a treefrog living in that tree for several years now. It's gone during
mating time--otherwise, someone's usually home--and sometimes more than one. So
if you go to this park, watch for a lovely, large, several-trunked madrone tree
growing right beside the trail. Its trunks spread out low enough to the
ground that it is obviously quite inviting for people to climb into--you can see
how the bark has been worn smooth by lots of feet. There's a little hole in
one of the trunks, facing toward the trail. It's maybe about 3 feet above the
ground. If it's been raining, you may see a frog looking out the hole
(presumably it's on the porch because the house is flooded). If it's been dry, you
can gently stick your finger into the hole, and "tickle" the frog out--sorta
crook your finger & wiggle it, it'll hitch a ride and come out for a visit. The
young man who showed us this trick has tickled 3 or 4 frogs out at a time!
Okay, so maybe you hafta be kind of a naturalist/tree-hugger/Mark Trail/Annie
Dillard type to do this--but hey, you are, aren't you???!!

A frog in the hand in an interesting replacement for a stamp in the book.

--Wild Woman

P.S. I've already e-mailed A. Rose, placer of the Goose--hopefully she'll
report that we are inept and the box is in place...
