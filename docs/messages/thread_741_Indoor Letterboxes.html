These are the ones I know about:

The Spirit of Dartmoor (Portland)--It's a mystery box, so I can't tell you where in Portland, but saying it's indoors probably won't spoil it.

Aces of Fun (Portland)--Same story, a mystery box, so I can't tell you what type of place it's hidden in.

Walking Tour (San Luis Obispo, CA)--I guess you could say it's a mystery box, but it's my box so I feel no guilt in saying that I hid it in a bookstore. You still won't find the box without figuring out the clue first. =)

Bookstore Box (San Luis Obispo, CA)--Another one of my boxes, but as you can probably tell from the name, it's hidden in a bookstore.

There's another box in Frederick, Maryland, that's cleverly hidden in a library. (Sorry, I don't remember the name of the box off the top of my head.)

I started working on a box that I was going to hide in a stamp store, and even have a clue ready for it, but I've heard rumors that somebody else beat me to it. =( I don't know what the box is called, though, since I haven't looked on the letterboxing website for new clues in several months. (It's in San Luis, though.)

And those are all the ones that I know about personally--either because I've found them or hid them. There are plenty of others out there, though! (Libraries and bookstores really seem to be a hotbed of letterboxing activities, though.)

Carpe diem!

-- Ryan
