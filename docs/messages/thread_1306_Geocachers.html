From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-28 22:31:29 UTC
I'd say the majority of the geocaching and letterboxing communities
is extremely nice & respectful of what they find. They all seem to
participate in these hobbies for very similar reasons--see something
new, experience the excitement of solving puzzles, get some exercise
that doesn't involve LCDs and opposable thumbs (meaning TVs not GPS
screens), learn a bit about the history of the region and
occasionally meet some other folks who enjoy doing the same hobby.

Most folks who accidentally stumble upon letterboxes, though, just
have no idea what they've found and either remove it thinking it's
trash or leave it uncovered thinking that whoever dropped it will
surely return to retrieve it (at least that's been my experience to
date). What I wonder is if occasionally folks are finding
letterboxes that have been 'nudged' out of their hiding spots by
animals/kids/weather/tsunamis/space aliens, who knows? Most
recently I got a report that some letterboxers found one of our
letterboxes in the only trash can in the park. Why they even
thought to look in there, I haven't a clue--but I'm thankful they
did. That move saved a letterbox in this region!!! So, who knows
what's going on?

Despite all the bad press geocachers get on the letterboxing boards,

letterboxers) are decent people that don't deliberately try to ruin
each other's fun. But it's like the news: If something good and

We had a wonderful geocacher email us about a vandalized box. They opted to check out the clues for the other boxes and go find them. They then emailed us to tell us which box they had put the stamp in so that we could pick it up and relocate it. They liked the letterbox series.

The hobbies are different but similar and the people who are involved in one and/or both seem to be after similar goals.

It is the distructive individuals who "know not what they have found", see that it says "do not destroy" and destroy it for that very reason alone, are the ones we should be pointing at (pick a finger). These are the people who see a "don't pick the flowers" sign and do it anyway :-( They have little or no respect for anything other than their own entertainment and what they think of as fun. Unfortunately, that is one side of human nature. We just need to ensure that each find is treated as if it is our own, and rehide them very carefully. ;-)

On that note, we will be finding a new spot for #3 in Wild Goose Chase as it has now been vandalized 2 times in 2 different locations. 3rd time is the charm as they say.
