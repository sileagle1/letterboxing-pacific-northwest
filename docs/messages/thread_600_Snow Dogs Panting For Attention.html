Snow Dogs Panting For Attention

From: Fudrick (fudrick@comcast.net) | Date: 2003-08-15 23:02:47 UTC-07:00
Was able to schedule myself in Portland this afternoon, stopped by to see if I could find any Snow Dogs. Found 6 of them, for the first time. They could use some attention, they are getting lonely.

The long trek between Sniff and Diesel was confusing, but that was because I skipped over several of the 'at the next intersection..." clues, so got even more confused than normal. Wandered around for a while till I got back to a known location, then followed the clues exactly (for a change) and had no problem.

Didn't get the last two lonely Snow Dogs, when it gets too dark to read the clues, it is time to pack it in :-(

Great Job, Ravenhaus!!! Great park, great trails, great clues, great stamps, can't wait to get back there and get the rest.

Can't wait to hunt down the Black Pearl!

Fudrick

Re: [LbPNW] Snow Dogs Panting For Attention

From: Kerjin (kerjin@myndworx.com) | Date: 2003-08-16 08:34:23 UTC-07:00


Thanks Fudrick,

It was such a wonderful feeling to know that some one was able to follow our clues and that they appreciated the stamps. Those were the first ones ever carved by RayvenMom and she was so worried that no one would like them. Planting the boxes was an interesting endeavor as we ran the boxes 3 times ourselves and then once with a 'Beta Tester' (Heart's a Flame). RayvenMom and I did not agree all the time on the definition of a "Y" in the trail, a "T" in the trail or a "Triangle" in the trail, hence the word "Intersection" (Grin).

We certainly had a blast placing and making it, learned so much along the way, and thanks to everyone who gave hints and tricks. We are already working on the Black Pearl series.

Fudrick, please contact me off list at kerjin at myndworx dot com and let me know the details if you would please, problems, etc, etc, etc.

Steve of Rayvenhaus
