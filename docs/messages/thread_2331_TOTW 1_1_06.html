I came to tell what I got for Christmas and find the topic has changed
so I'll just double post here - my daughter gave me a lovely yellow
vest that has my trail name embroidered on it - she had it made up
just for me, which was so sweet. My husband gave me a sleeveless
shirt that says "get a clue, go letterboxing" on it with a cute little
tree. I liked it so much I had to order the hooded sweatshirt to match.

After today, my resolution is to carve a stamp for my husband and I to
use together. He has been using a stamp our daughter carved or his
thumb when we go together. He seems to be coming along and/or
instigating boxing trips more and more, although he laughingly talks
about all the physical discomforts (getting soaked and stuck with
blackberries, etc.) Yesterday he said I could pick some boxes at the
coast and we would go check out the high tides and flood waters. Boy
was it wild! Don and Gwen's box at the observation tower was fun to
get, but we got totally soaked by water blowing up over the jetty and
hitting even people up in the tower. A great day to go to the coast.

Happy New Year to all.

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2006-01-05 17:36:16 UTC
Last year about this time, I got an email from Heather, shebearof3,
saying that she'd made a New Year's Resolution. Her resolution was to
go on a hike at least once a week. She asked me if I'd like to go
along. I laced on my boots and jumped at the chance. We were very
diligent, going most weeks until school got out. Then kids and family
got precedence. Somehow, we didn't get back in the routine once school
started up again.

Naturally, this week's topic made me think about Heather. Missing the
excellent company and winter walks, I resolved to re-resolve her 2005
resolution. Yesterday, we hit the trails at Powell Butte. Along the
way, we picked up Big John and Stinkpot Turtle letterboxes, saw
spectacular views of St. Helens and Hood and got to watch a kestrel
striking at prey. It was all in all a fine way to start a new year. Funhog
