Dear Funhog,
Set out yesterday to find the Ridgefield NWR letterboxes and am
happy to report that they are both alive and well. The Carty Box was
a little difficult to find because of the overgrown blackberry
brambles but it was located. It had fallen from it's perch but is
back where it belongs. I noticed that the boxes hadn't been visited
since March 2003. What a shame. On the way to the River S box Cat's
Meow and I and friends came upon a Shwann's Ice Cream truck with a
big sign that read "FREE ICE CREAM" so of course we had to stop
considering it was one of the hottest days of the year in Washington.
101 degrees! Go figure. Hey Team Rayvenhaus, is there a code for
pounds lost in sweat?! maybe PLIS? Anyway, The River S box was a
blessing since we were pretty wiped out after the Carty Box.
Proceeded onto the lookout where we stood in the corner very quietly
and watched the swallows dart in and out to feed their young. There
were three nests full of youngins. If anyone plans on visiting these
boxes soon I would suggest applying a ton of mosquito repellent. I
was pretty lubed up but was still attacked. I walked away with a welt
on my arm the size of a silver dollar!
Camp Fire Lady & Cat's Meow
