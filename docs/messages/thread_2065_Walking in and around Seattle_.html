Being the walker that I am, I've walked quite a few of the urban
trails around Seattle. =) Berke-Gilman is probably my favorite because
much of it is shaded. The Sammamish River Trail has practically no
shade at all--it's pretty miserable on a hot, sunny day! Might be a
nice place on a cool, overcast day, though.

The Alki Trail is beautiful and nice because it doesn't cross any
streets where you have to dodge cars, but it's EXTREMELY crowded on
weekends. It's a great weekday walk, though, which I do often. =)
There's not much shade, either.

The Duwamish River Trail has a couple of nice spots, but most of it is
unshaded and not especially scenic since it follows along the
industrial section of town. I like it only because I'm just fascinated
by that port stuff. Dreaming of all the exotic places those containers
may have come from and watching the machinery that moves it all around.

There's also my Seattle Walking Tour letterbox that stroller
accessible. ;o) Give you a reason to walk around downtown for awhile!
I'm kind of fond of walking along the waterfront late at night when
the streets and sidewalks aren't very crowded, though if you read in
an earlier post, there's one particular plaque that I wouldn't
recommend getting late at night when the homeless people are trying to
get some shut eye.

The Lakes to Lakes trail system has some stroller friendly areas, but
it's NOT very well marked. I left from near Lake Sammamish and tried
to walk to Lake Washington. Imagine my embarrassment when, after a
half hour of walking, I found myself in the exact spot I started at!
And that happened not once, but TWICE! *shaking head* I did eventually
make it to Lake Washington, but it took a couple of hours longer than
it should have. =) Take a map of the area with you if you try that
one. It would have helped me out a lot!

Had another idea--the Ballard Locks. Park on either the south
(Magnolia) or north (Ballard) side and then walk to the opposite side.
It's mostly flat, great flower garden, good food on the northern side
if you're into fish n' chips type food, interesting to see the fish
ladder activity and the kingfishers and great blue herons looking for
their next meals--not to mention the boat activity through the locks
themselves. It's a place where I spend many of my lunchtimes when I'm
working up that way. Shade may be a bit more difficult to find on the
locks themselves but there's great shady parts throughout the garden on
the north and the park areas on the south.
