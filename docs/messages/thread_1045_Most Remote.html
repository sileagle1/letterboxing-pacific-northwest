From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-12-31 17:12:57 UTC
> (unless you get caught in some crazy ocean current).

I *hate* when that happens! It ruins an otherwise perfectly good
day!

The longest hike I've ever had to do to nab a letterbox--not
including the AT since there are trailheads I could have used MUCH
closer to the letterbox than the one I used in Georgia--was Don and
Gwen's Paradise Lodge letterboxes in Southern Oregon. I hiked just
shy of 25 miles to get those boxes--and that was from the closest
trailhead I could find to the boxes! Which makes them pretty darned
remote if you ask me. =)

The boxes in places like Montana, Wyoming, Utah, and such are the
most remote I've found population-wise. You can drive for hours at
a time and not see anyone else on the road.

But the letterbox farthest from other letterboxes? The two I hid in
Zimbabwe, Africa, might have that award. There *might* be another
letterbox within a thousand miles of there, but not likely.

But if you don't want to get international, I'd imagine Hawaii or
Alaska would probably get some "most remost" letterbox awards.

Carpe diem!

-- Ryan
