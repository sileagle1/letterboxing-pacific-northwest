From: frykitty (frykitty@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-10-30 09:22:52 UTC-08:00
It's my policy to make sure a box is hidden better than I found it. No special techniques, just make sure it's covered, make sure that covering is *secure* (not going to blow or wash away), then do a walk-by to see if anything is visible.

It's possible I lost my first plant because of a bad re-hide, but I think I have to take a little responsibility for that because of two factors: firstly, the spot I chose didn't make for a very easy re-hide; and secondly, I included few instruction in my box. I like boxes that have clear instructions to rehide so the box cannot be seen. I think that's helpful, especially for new folks who may not be on the lists.

It's all about the leaves in the autumn time, but don't forget to use more than that as they
can blow away. Leaves will also rot away if no one finds the box for a lengthy period of time.
I prefer using moss (for those of us west of the mountains) if at all possible, it takes a while
for moss to grow so I think when people see (or don't see) it the brain goes.......hmmm ...
nothing going on here. a couple of pieces of debris from the area on top makes for a nice
touch as well.

From: Kurious Jo (KuriousJo@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-10-30 17:54:07 UTC-08:00
Hiding the Box:
We usually walk around the box and make sure it's not visible from any
direction - paying special attention to the directions where people are most
likely to be. I like to use a mix of hiding material so it's location is
not too obvious. The sprinkling of leaves, sticks or what ever on top
really makes the spot look untouched.

Newbies:
Just a side note - it would be good for newbies to link up with an
experienced boxer. It'd be someone to ask questions of. We've had some
interesting questions from boxers outside our area. One asked a neighbor by
the park if they knew where the box is hidden. Since we haven't heard back
after several emails, I assume I didn't handle it delicately enough. It's
hard to emphasize enough the importance of hiding without stepping on toes.
Perhaps others are better at this? I flunked! {shrug}

Kurious Jo

Well it is pretty close. Actually once we rehide the letterbox using
the typical rocks or other hiding stuff I take a handfull of dirt
and duff and toss it onto the hiding spot with a top to bottom move

side move making a sign of a cross. If it still doesn't look right I
give it another blessing.

If you walk away from the letterbox thinking,"Boy the next finder is
going to have some difficulty finding the box right away." then it
is truly a good rehide.
