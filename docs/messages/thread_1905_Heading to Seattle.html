From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-08 05:27:42 UTC

Run a search of the area in which you're interested on
www.AtlasQuest.com as most clues there are rated by hike length.
Also, any boxes we've planted (dvn2r ckr, rnrB & trkr) were planted
with at least an 8 month old to a 6 year old along for the ride
(although a few are longer hikes--we usually mention something about
the length of the longer hikes in the clues--unless they're mystery
boxes then it's just a grab bag).

Any idea what part of Seattle you'll be in? That'll help narrow
down the scope of your hunt quite a bit. Seattle/Tacoma/Olympia is
a great spot for 'power'boxing but it's an even better region to see
some of the most beautiful sites on Earth--although some aren't
as 'box-laden' as others. If you have a particular preference in
mind, let us know. Also, there are some incredible playgrounds in
the region--not too far from letterboxes so that's always something
that works out rather well with kids in tow (St Edwards State Park
in Kirkland comes to mind which is home to a bunch of boxes but a
little longer hikes).

If you're in downtown Seattle--I'd lean toward any 'West Seattle'
boxes as they offer great views, that 'Seattle' feel since you're in
view of the Seattle skyline and Puget Sound shipping activity and
short 'spurts' of walking in many cases. There are a bunch of great
letterboxes in the Bellevue/Redmond/Issaquah area but some regions
(suburban streets) are difficult to navigate if you don't have much
time to get to know the area.

Not sure of any boxes still 'alive' in downtown Seattle proper but
there are a couple that aren't terribly far from downtown.

Enjoy your trip!!! We'd offer to show you around but we'll most
likely be out of town (planned trip to CA) when you're in Seattle.
Perhaps next time you're in the area?

dvn2r ckr

From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-08 19:55:38 UTC

I will be out of town that weekend too! Be sure to check out the
Flagship REI letterbox in downtown. Carkeek Park also has a great
playground for kids and it has at least 4 letterboxes that are still
alive and well.

Turkey Dinner has a kids playground at the beginning of the hike and
so does Twin Ponds.

And both West Seattle parks (Schmitz and Lincoln) have playgrounds
too! The West Seattle letterboxes also let you get down to the Beach
and play around the rocks and driftwood. --Watch the ferryboats
heading out to Bainbridge or Vashon Island. Especially nice on a sunny
day like today (Tuesday 2/8).
