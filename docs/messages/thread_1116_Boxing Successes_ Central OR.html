Boxing Successes: Central OR

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-02-02 17:32:20 UTC
After braving the icy stretches of Hwy 26 over Mt Hood, we spent the weekend in
Central OR. The thick layer of snow that has been blanketing the area was finally
gone. It was cold and clear, the perfect weather for letterboxing. I finally tracked
down Cline Falls version deux and must admit that this stamp is worlds nicer than the
long, lost original. It's only a pity that the first one went missing and that it isn't
around for folks to see Carie's carving progress! I had always wanted to figure out
how to get a good view of the falls and this trek showed me.

I then went to Redmond Dry Canyon to track down the Redmond Redhawk box. I love
hiking this canyon, especially when it's cool. Unfortunately, the promised ostriches
along the way were in hiding but I did see a harrier and several flights of geese. I
wandered around for quite a while in the vicinity of this sneeky box but was finally
rewarded for my persistence with a lovely little stamp by John Fleshman in my log.

Sunday morning, I met with Birder to tour the lovely sewage ponds in Redmond, hand
over some Mastercarve and to exchange signature stamps. The local residents at the
ponds included three bald eagles. During our travels looking for other feathered
friends, we went to Phoebe Pond in search of owls. Along the way, I succeeded in
finding the elusive Phoebe Pond #3 box. Having the box's creator laugh at you while
you hunt their box always adds an extra element of fun, doesn't it? Birder has grand
plans for many more letteboxes in the region. I'm looking forward to exploring some
of the interesting places they will be hidden.

Funhog
