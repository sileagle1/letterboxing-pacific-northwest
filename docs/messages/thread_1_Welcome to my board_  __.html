Welcome to my board! =)

From: rscarpen (rscarpen@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-03-27 18:09:14 UTC
Since at least a couple of people have expressed an interest in a
Pacific Northwest letterboxing board, I've taken it upon myself to
create one. There are some changes I've made from the main
letterboxing group, which I'll outline below so you know what to
expect.

First, I've set it up so you cannot include attachments to your
posts. If you have files or photos, there is a seperate section for
them (under 'Files' and 'Photos' to the left) where up can upload
such stuff and refer to them if necessary. I've disallowed
attachments to reduce the chance of someone spreading viruses
inadvertantly. It'll also help reduce the clogging of the mailboxes
for people that preper to receive their posts that way. And with the
files and photos sections, it's just not necessary.

The 'Links' section I've set up so only moderators can change them
(which, at this point, only inclues myself). If you want to add,
change, or delete a link, e-mail me and I'll get right on it. If you
look at the links section for the LbNA group, it's choked full
of "junk links" and I don't want that happening to this one. I want
it to be a useful, organized place that people can visit to find
information about letterboxing in general as well as links geared
towards the Pacific Northwest. They don't even have to be
letterboxing links per se, but rather anything a Pacific Northwest
letterboxer might find useful from orienteering events in Oregon to
hiking trails in Vancouver.

I've already included websites I know of for local letterboxers. If
I'm missing any, please let me know! (Actually, I know I'm missing
Bethany's and Bill's websites, but I don't know the links for them!)

I've removed a couple of miscellaneous options like the chat and
database features because, well, I've never heard of anyone actually
using them. If you REALLY want to chat with someone, you can always
go to the main letterboxing board to do that, I suppose. If for some
reason you actually do find a really useful purpose for those
features, let me know and I'll put them back.

Over the next couple of weeks I'll probably be playing with the
colors before I settle down on a final color scheme. I don't know
about you, but I'm kind of board of the same color scheme for YEARS
that has persisted on the main letterboxing group, and I'd like
something at least a little different here for a change of pace. In
the meantime, expect bizarre colors to show up suddenly and
unexpectedly for brief periods of time. =)

And finally, I won't be able to keep an eye on this board myself all
the time, and I'm looking for a couple of people who might want to be
moderators on this board. Most of the time that probably won't
require any work at all, but at least someone should be around to
keep an eye on things while I'm hiking the Appalachian Trail or
otherwise indisposed. If you're interested, let me know!

And, that's about all I can think of for now. As I put in the
description, clues for new boxes or changes to old ones should be
posted to the main letterboxing website if you want them to show up
on the LbNA website since the webmasters there may not be patrolling
this board. You can post them here as well, though, if you want to
make sure they stand out from the hundreds of posts made to the other
board, though. =)

Okay, that's the last of my long, legalise-kindof-speak. Take off
your shoes and make yourselves at home. =)

Carpe diem!

-- Ryan
