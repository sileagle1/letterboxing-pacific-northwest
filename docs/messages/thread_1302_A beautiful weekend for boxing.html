A beautiful weekend for boxing

From: bookwormbox (bookwormlb@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-04-26 19:45:50 UTC
I hope all of you were able to get and box this past weekend. What a
lovely weekend to go letterboxing!

Just an update on the boxes I went for this weekend:

Sunburst (Estacada, Oregon)
The box was slightly damp inside, but the baggies kept everything
important dry. No visitors to the box for a few months, but all was
fine. There was a Civil War reenactment going on, so I had to sneak
behind enemy lines to get this one!

Clackamas County Park Series - Rub-A-Dub (West Linn - ish, Oregon)
A nice short walk. The box looked just fine, but hasn't had many
visitors.

Paint My Wagon (Canby, Oregon)
Lovely spot, great stamp. Very few visitors!

Clackamas County Park Series - Icebox (Mollala, Oregon)
Another fun hunt provided by Sleepy Whippet. Another lonely box.
Icebox was the last of this series I had to find and I'm very proud
to have the bonus stamp in my book!

MI(lo) or Bust (Estacada, Oregon)
A VERY lonely box, with no visitors since last September! Despite
its loneliness the box was in good shape. The park is lovely now
with lots of new green things growing everywhere.

Three Firs (Gresham, Oregon)
Everything looks good, except the log book is a little empty. And
there were 2 AA batteries in the box. Weird.

So it was my week to visit lonely boxes. Hope you all had a
wonderful weekend.

Bookworm

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-04-27 06:53:32 UTC
> I'm going to be putting out my first
> letterbox soon, it will be a geocache and letterbox
> put together.

Just my two cents--you certainly can make a hybrid, but I wouldn't
recommend it. Many geocachers don't know about letterboxing, and
mistakingly take the stamp or some other hienous crime. In addition-
-a lot of letterboxers aren't exactly fans of geocaching. They'll
deliberately avoid going to known hybrids. And if you don't tell
them it's a hybrid ahead of time, they'll likely be upset at not
knowing about it and "wasting their" time chasing after a "stupid
hybrid".

Nobody ever complains about a lack of hybrids, but a good number
will likely complain (even if only behind your back) if you start
hiding hybrids. You can do it, but don't expect the boxes to be
very popular.

Good luck! =)

-- Ryan

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-27 17:23:51 UTC
Speaking from personal experience, I recommend the letterbox and
geocache be separated by at least 50 feet to avoid problems. 30 feet
doesn't seem to be the magic distance anymore since the stamps &/or
letterboxes continue to disappear (not sure why but I figured increased
traffic to the area simply increases the likelihood that the letterbox is
not rehidden well when they depart the area). Most folks have no idea
what a letterbox is and what it's for--they find it, remove it from the
hiding spot, have no idea where it's supposed to be hidden since they
don't have or know what the clues are and simply leave it out in full
view or remove it as trash when they leave. That's why good
camouflage, great hiding spots and limited knowledge/exposure of the
clues is such a good tactic.

dvn2r ckr
**one who's lost over a couple dozen letterboxes (out of 160+ placed)
to accidental finders**

That's a very good point dvn2rckr. Did you know that the clues in the Database can not be crawled by a search engine
spider? I can not speak for the LbNA site, but I'm sure they are using the same or similar database engine as I am.
Anything written in a blog or LiveJournal or a forum, such as the forum you use and that I use can and is crawled by a
search engines spider. Which explains why some boxes are locatable via a search engine and most are not.
