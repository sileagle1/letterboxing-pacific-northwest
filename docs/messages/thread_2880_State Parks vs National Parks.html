Well, up until this last year, letterboxes were absolutely verboten in
all National Parks. However, a new regulation allows individual park
managers to decide on a case by case basis. This is pretty much the
same policy in Oregon State Parks. Some managers welcome boxes (Silver
Falls) and others frown on them (Tryon Creek.)

The same ranger manages both Tryon Creek and McIver and allows boxes
in the latter but not the former. Go figure. She told me that Tryon
gets too much traffic already and that boxes might end up causing user
trails.

Still somewhat confusing but at least they are kinda maybe allowed
sometimes and in some places. Funhog
