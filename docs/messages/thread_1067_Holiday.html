From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-01-09 01:48:35 UTC
Here it is Elvis' Birthday and I can't even get out my back door to get bananas and
peanut butter to make the King's favorite gourmet sandwich for lunch. Portland is a
gigantic skating rink complete with a slick layer of water. The stairs to my house are
doubling as a winter water slide.

To avoid cabin fever, I took the time to make a new type of rubber stamp in the
tradition of that secretive Zorro's signature stamp. Its a two registration print, using
two stamps. I did manage to make the only newbie error I could possibly conjure up
for this project. I carved the background stamp going the wrong direction. Duh! It
was good for a laugh at my own expense. Fortunately, I had another piece of
Mastercarve of the right size handy to make another version. I'll post a file of this
project later.

Has anyone else come up with a creative way of spending this slippery day??? Keep
warm, PNWers. Funhog

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-01-08 19:34:50 UTC-08:00
Tooth Fairy and I (and my son) spent our 3rd snow day making letterboxes,
making popcorn balls, and sliding on our front yard like otters. Both kids
discovered they couldnt' walk but they could crawl on their hands and knees
to the top of the incline then slide on their bellies down into the street.
Great fun has been had by all but I'm very ready to call it quits. School
has already been called off for tomorrow so it looks like I'm going to be
doing some more carving.

The upside of this whole thing, I've greatly improved the quality of my
boxes that I created this weekend. I've spent much more time and energy
into putting the "whole" together. My books are much better and my stamps
are better. Hopefully it will thaw out soon so I can go hide them.

I also recieved the Dead Horse Letterbox in the mail today, so Sunday is
still on. If you want to come and stamp into it (It's a wonderful stamp),
before I send it on it's way, email me and I'll fill you in on the details.

Melanie
