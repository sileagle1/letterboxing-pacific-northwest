Venue capacity

From: hoopoe4me (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2004-01-28 02:08:52 UTC
I certainly think there are venues that support multiple boxes.

Pt Defiance has plenty of space left. With the great vistas, and the
trail network there are several themes that can work. There are
still places to take the visitor and certainly some chances for
diabolical hard cluesmanship and vexing series ( hint hint).

Bridle Trails is at saturation. I feel there is just not enough
there to support going to a different area of the park. Done what is
there, seen what is there, you have pretty much done it all. My aim
was to present basic boxing skills and introduce to park to non-
horsepeople.

Rattlesnake Ridge is a real destination box. I can't see the point
of there being another box between the road and the ultimate box as
there is only one trail option. Why bother? (I have to go back up
and replace the log book and stamp.)

I did hear someone musing about using it as a start for a 10+ mile
quest for multiple boxes. westerly toward the winery? That would
certainly be a good venture. It would require 2 cars or some very
sturdy legs. i like the idea of a series that challenging.

There are many areas of the state being neglected, such as cascade
crest and eastern WA. I set one in eastern WA to compliment one
placed by Fudrick. A person could visit 2 different venues within 15
miles of each other and have 2 different experiences ( riparian vs
upland grass zone). I am pondering a third directly in the opposite
direction. This would give a third totally different experience, a
pine forest all within 30 miles.

Tiger Mountain is lonely but nearby Cougar has several and could
likely use more.

Larabee SP could support more but those that are there are not
getting visited enough ( another hint hint)

I like series that have a theme or try to teach me something, such as
compass exercise.
