Boxing the dry side: WA

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2003-09-30 06:55:19 UTC
Just made it home from a business trip to eastern WA. Instead of
flying this time, I packed up the camping gear, grabbed the
letterboxing backpack and started up the little red car. Along the
way, I found P-Orc's lovely Maryhill stamp; struck out on Bethany's
Stonhenge, my comeupance, I'm sure; whooped and hollered when I found
Hanglider's Home Away From Home. Check out this clue and GO THERE!!!

Knowing that Hanglider and the Gardener had never met another
letteboxer, I made a point of getting together with them for an
official stamp exchange and box hunt. We tried to ferret out the
elusive Spokane Ghetto box, to no avail. The fact that the clues
didn't match the place didn't make things any easier. These deprived
folks need many more boxes to look for. Leave a box if you are
anywhere near Spokane. It is your civic duty. I did leave them one to
track down before the snow flies. I also added two of the three Manito
Park stamps to my logbook. Number three may be missing.

On the return, I tracked down Eliza B's Trees of Stone, Fudrick's
Roosevelt Park and Hoopoe's Umptanum Falls. This last has an image
that I will cherish, being a particular favorite. Bethany's Merci
Train in Yakima was my last find of the trip.

Naturally, I couldn't take a trip without leaving vinyl in my tracks.
There will soon be clues for the new one in Spokane, two in the Yakima
River Canyon and a box I placed for Matt the Rat as part of his 45th
Parallel series in Oregon. Two of my three new stamps were carved in
camp by the light of a very small campfire, a votive candle. So they
are a bit on the rough side. I forgot my lantern and the batteries I
bought for my flashlight were duds.

This was all in all, a very successful business trip. Fun,
work...50/50...or is it 60/40? Funhog
