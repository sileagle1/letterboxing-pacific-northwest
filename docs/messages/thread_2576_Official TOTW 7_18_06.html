RE: [LbPNW] Official TOTW 7/18/06

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-19 10:02:50 UTC-07:00
Hello and welcome!

TOTW is the Topic of the Week. Things got slow last winter on this list, so we decided to prod some conversation into action. We're still not terribly lively, it seems, but I guess if that means we avoid the infamous "drama of the main list," that's not such a bad thing.

Again, welcome to the hobby and have a look around. There are good resources on both letterboxing.org and atlasquest.com for beginners, and other people have glossaries for you to look up stuff about which you might have questions (though I don't think TOTW is covered on any of them, so that's a valid question...). Sometimes some people get a little bored of the same ol' questions, for whatever reason, so it'd probably be best to do a lot of browsing and reading to get familiar with everything.

By the by, if you're letterboxing anywhere near where you work, and there are several there, you might want to check out some of the "Mystery Boxes" in the state, county, and city...

Good luck!

ClioMouse

From: wny2nm2wa4 (wny2nm2wa4@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-20 01:26:30 UTC
> Re: TOTW 6/27
>
> "What have you lost while letterboxing?"
>

I hope that I am allowed to do this (re-visiting a past TOTW).

I was too ashamed and depressed to admit this at the time of this
topic until today. Read on; I think it will be entertaining...

I was on my way to the Mariner game with a friend 3 weeks ago. I
decided to stop and show him Dick and Jane's Spot in Ellensburg. We
were chatting with the ever-friendly Jane and I decided to tell my
friend about letterboxing. I thought his family might enjoy it if
they found it interesting. I sat my wife's digital camera down on
the fence while I was fiddling with something and talking. Needless
to say I forgot the camera. We decided to stop on the way home being
reasonably sure it would still be there. Wrong! My heart was in my
socks.

I contacted Jane the next day via email and she nicely checked and
didn't see it nor had anyone turned it in. I couldn't bring myself
to tell my wife for several days and when I did she clasped her
heart and gasped. Then I asked (due to her reaction) how much it had
cost and her response was "you don't want to know". (maybe this
should be my answer on the Atlasquest survey for 'what is the depth
of despair'). I was mortified.

To my wife's credit she didn't really get ticked at me, but it took
me awhile to start to get over it. We even got a new (cheaper)
camera yesterday.

Here is the real entertainment... So today a co-worker of mine
(Homeport)and I decided to go find a LB with another co-worker(today
was her first LB) at lunch. Homeport told me that she had stopped in
Ellensburg with her family to find the LB at Dick and Jane's Spot. I
thought that was nice but said "Wait until you hear MY story about
Dick and Jane's Spot" and proceeded to tell her about the lost
camera. She stopped me and said "I have your camera". This,of
course, didn't register with me because I thought she meant she had
just stopped there really recently so she must be joking. She then
asked me what kind it was and ... well you get the rest of the
picture. Speaking of which, I wish I had a picture of the look on my
face when I realized the truth! She had no clue that it was 'my'
camera. I had found the letterbox a few months ago and several other
LBers had been there since. She even contacted as many of the other
LBers as she could via email!

I will get the camera back tomorrow and be able to return the new
one to the store as well.

Homeport is a saint!

Stormcrow

PS Lesson learned; Use a case with a neck/shoulder strap for these
new smaller digital cameras so that you are less likely to set them
down. Yikes!

Re: [LbPNW] Re: Official TOTW 7/18/06

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-20 07:16:47 UTC-07:00
How could you possibly know that letterboxes are out there and about letterboxing.org and NOT GO FIND EVERY BOX POSSIBLE??? Maybe it's because I found it during the summer, when I wasn't taking classes, but there were days that summer I spent the whole day out, 7AM to dark, then was still so excited my friend and I couldn't get to sleep until about 1AM, but were still out at 7AM and rushing to the car to not get locked into a park at dusk.

Ever hear of the Pacific Northwest's (or maybe it's just Washington, but I thought Oregon was in on it, too) Quilt Shop Hop? Each quilt shop participating designs a quilt square using a few fabrics and colors and a theme, and people go to all the quilt shops and get the pieces for these squares for free. Then they put all the pieces together as they like, and they have a quilt from all over the state or region. They also do a drawing, but you have to have 16 or so stamps from all the shops you visited. My mom and I went to a bunch in this area to get stamps for my aunt, and I kept getting them in my letterboxing book. DEFINITELY some looks of, "Ok, you're really weird..." from the quilt shop ladies. I even made my mom go to a particular shop because they had a mouse stamp I'd seen in somebody else's passport.

We're definitely a unique breed...

ClioMouse
