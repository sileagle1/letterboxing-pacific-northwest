I enjoy reading, scrapbooking, board games, puzzles (both word and
picture), and whippet racing. I also love to travel, but haven't had
the time or money to do much lately. Letterboxing allows me to
travel locally and sharpen my puzzle skills, so I at least get to
combine a few of my loves together.

I'm not as good as dvn2ckr on reading classics. I love mysteries and
romance & sci-fi. I always have a huge "to-read" list. My "read it"
list is a little shorter, but I do usually end up reading between 8
and 10 books a month. Anyone that wants to talk books, send me an
email!

If any of you happen to be boxing with me when I have my dogs along,
Carmen & Sophie (known in letterboxing log books as the "Wonder
Whippets") have their own special stamps to share. And, if you are
really lucky, they will be happy to walk all over your log book and
leave foot prints on it for you. Mud is their preferred medium, but
ink will work in a pinch. :-)

Oh, and if any of you ever make it to my house, I have a permanant
hitch hiker living with me. She'll stamp your book too, but you have
to come visit and bring her kitty greenie treats! Cleo is a true
hitchhiker since I found her sitting on the rock the box I was
hunting was hiding under. She doesn't move around much though!

I am amazed at all the different activities! And I haven't done as many things in my entire

Piper and I like to do contra dancing, which is a type of American folk dancing, done to
Irish-Scots fiddle music. It bears some resemblence to square dancing, but is much more
informal (no special outfits) and you dance with everyone in a long line of people. If
anyone has seen or done the Virginia Reel, it's like that. We also do English Country
dancing, which you've seen if you've ever seen a Jane Austen movie such as Pride and
Prjudice. And the mild-mannered piper plays bagpipes, of course.

I love to read, fantasy, science fiction, old-fashioned British mysteries, and history, but
my to-read pile is bigger than my done-reading pile;) Last book was Wee Free Men by
Terry Pratchett, which was charming.

The kids like video games, super-hero movies and comic books (Spider-Man is a
favourite), baseball, and generally causing mayhem.
