Good afternoon. Since I've got a (favorite) sister in Portland and
her boyfriend letterboxes, can I be an honorary member? I know: too
many Californians up there already! During this glorious fall season
I'm taking some time to ask a favor of you all. You will recall that
when we changed over to the new automated LbNA website we took a lot
of time to make sure all the old letterbox clues got transfered over,
including boxes which are "Unclaimed" by their creators.

This presents a bit of a dilemma to us. The obvious problems are with
box maintanence. If nobody takes responsibility for these boxes, lots
of wasted trips will be spent looking when they eventually go
missing. There are other "webmasterly" problems I'd be glad to
discuss in more detail if anyone is interested.

Since you have such an amazing community of letterboxers there in the
great Pacific Northwest, I thought you might be interested in trying
to adopt all the Unclaimed boxes from the lists. If you're willing to
accept responsibility for a letterbox, you might try contacting the
placer and getting them to register with LbNA. If you're not able to
find them, or if they respond with "I'm out of the 'boxing business,"
you could be a real box angel if you were to adopt the clues and
maintain the boxes.

If you could eliminate all the UnclaimedBoxes from your area, maybe
the nation as a whole could do the same thing.

To find a list of the Unclaimed boxes near you, go to
letterboxing.org and click on "search for boxes." Choose a state and
the placer named "Unclaimed" from the pull down lists, then
click "search boxes."

Please let me know if you'd like to give any of these orphans a new
home. My address is drewclan at aol dot com.

Thanks! Jay in CT
