From: ronnienwinnie (ronnienwinnie@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-10 19:30:31 UTC
Hi. We just discovered this group this weekend and joined it
today. We are Ronnie and Winnie and we live in Vancouver. We have
been involved in letterboxing for about 4 months now and thought we
should introduce ourselves since we just joined the group. A family
member of ours in Connecticut told us about the sport and we looked
it up on the internet and decided to give it a try. We were really
amazed at how many letterboxes are in our own town! Our first
letterboxing experience was at Battleground Lake since Ronnie works
near there. We even placed our first letterbox last month at Factice
Creek in Vancouver. We were thrilled yesterday when we checked on
our box and found that we had our first visitor and just a couple of
days ago! We were so excited to find that someone would actually
look for our box! :) This is really cool!!! Thank you Funhog,
whoever you are, for the kind words about the stamp. I am not very
artistic (even if Ronnie says so) and it took me four tries to make
one I felt comfortable enough to put out there for people to
actually see. I am afraid that is not as good as what most of you
other letterboxers carve.

Our work schedules dont allow us to get out very much but we try to
whenever we can. We see from the recent posts on this board that we
missed a group event down at Silver Falls. Too bad we didnt know
about it at the time, we actually could have attended. At least, we
had the weekend free. Ronnie is a bit shy about meeting large groups
of people we dont know. I am a little more adventurous and would be
interested in getting to know some of you on line so we can learn
the tricks of this game.

I have heard from my cousin in Connecticut that there are many very
interesting mystery letterboxes available in this area but I cant
seem to find clues to them. She said something about WOM clues but
I dont know what she meant. Can anyone tell us what a WOM is?

Winnie, new to this group and very excited to have found it.

Gosh! I was so please to find your very first letterbox.

hobby, Funhog
