Trial n Error up to old tricks and Cold Moon Over Puget Sound may be missing

From: Nail Family (c.nail@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-03-31 04:30:18 UTC
3 out of 7 Nails did a tour of Steilacoom, Wa today and had very
mixed success. Saltars Point was beautiful, box intact and
inconspicuously placed in its home. Chambers Creek Duck is
just ducky. However, the Nail Boys had some trouble with
dvn2rckr's Cold Moon Over Puget Sound.

We located the park, found the disc, paced our paces and think
we located the tree of many trunks. But no box. Dont know what
could have happened. This is not really in a high traffic area
being several yards off of the main trail and not really in direct
sight of passersby. Bethany has been victim to many a random
dissapearance before, perhaps her luck holds.

But worse than No Moon is being stumped by notorious Trial n
Error. Farrels Marsh is quite a beautiful place, a gem within a
very nice community. The clues were not complicated. Just walk
down the path until you see the jumble of logs. Very direct and to
the point as most of Bill's clues are. And just as usual, the clues
are at the same time just as indirect as he can contrive. The
'jumble of logs' turns out to be actually 3 jumbles of logs with no
indication as to which to search. The obvious conclusion is to
search all of them. I am afraid that every frog, newt, and garter
snake in the area has been disturbed this afternoon and no
tuperware (or more likely black vhs box) was to be found. After
30 minutes of searching and much muffled grumblings on the
part of Nailhead, the Nail boys gave up to find other boxes in the
area. We will await a response from the Bill who we consider
the king of the minimalist clues in the South Sound area as to
whether it has really walked off before we make another attempt.

2 for 4 is not too good. But we did make it worthwhile by placing
a 'carved in the field' box of our own. Look for the Nursing the Fir
Letterbox clues to be posted here and on the main letterbox_usa
site very soon. Chambers Creek Park was just too nice a venue
to have only one letterbox. After finding the Duck we walked on
and found a lovely place to hide a small box. The clues will
require finding Bethany's duck first in order to orient toward the
new box.

All in all it was still a good day. How could it not be in the South
Sound with sunshine and 2 eager boys with stamps and
compass in hand.

Nailhead
P31 F92 X24
