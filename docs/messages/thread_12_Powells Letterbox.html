Has anyone tried getting the letterbox at Powell's yet? I figured
somebody would have jumped on that one the day it came out if for no
other reason than curiosity. Especially Funhog. You falling down on
the job, Funhog, or just playing in Hawaii or some other far-flung
location? =) I for one am itching to get out to Portland and nab
the letterbox!

And, I might point out, that's a great rainy-day letterbox since you
don't actually have to be outdoors to hunt it down.

But without giving anything away, I'd love to hear about it if
someone has hunted down the box yet. I met the person that hid it
(She was inspired by the Bookstore Box I hid here in California,
although at the time she placed the Powells box, she hadn't gotten
the Bookstore Box yet.). She actually lives in California, so
probably won't be frequenting this board, but visits the Portland
area a couple of times a year and is itching to find a lot of boxes
on her next trip out after the sad showing of letterboxes in
California. =)

Speaking of which, California has broken over 200 letterboxes now.
They're spread out all over the state, though, so it doesn't have
anywhere near the concentration that the Portland area enjoys.

But I digress.... What's the beef with the Powell's letterbox?
Hasn't anyone tried to get it yet?! =)

From: The Forest People (forestpeople@zilknet.net) | Date: 2003-03-28 23:22:21 UTC-08:00
Actually, before it became MIA, The Forest People found it.

We were there on March 16. Some of the individual clues stumped us,
however there was enough detail in the final clue that we located the
box anyway. It was a plastic pencil pouch velcro'ed in place, in a more
or less concealed location. It was visible, but inconspicuous. I don't
want to say too much about the location, in case it is going to be
replaced.

Powell's is a great place for a letterbox (or two or three), and I
expect the staff would be all for it if asked. I hope we will see more
there in the future.

David
