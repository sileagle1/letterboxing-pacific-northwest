From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-03-31 07:23:00 UTC
> I have been thinking that I would do a mystery box (well, semi-
> mystery) with pictures as the clues.

If you want to see some examples of what you might do with pictures
as clues, there's the Petroglyph Series I hid in Nevada
(http://www.geocities.com/rscarpen/US/NV/Petroglyph). Those are one-
shot deals showing pictures of me hiding the letterbox, and you have
to find where on the trail it's taken. I can't vouch that hunting
down a box with such clues is a lot of fun since I've never had the
opportunity to FIND such boxes, just hide them.

Another approach is the St. George box in Utah
(http://www.aros.net/~hollym/stgeorge.html) that shows a series of
pictures which you need to follow. It starts with the "large scene"
and narrows down until you've found the actual box. It was a real
hoot to find and I'd be thrilled if more boxes were like that.

Then there's the Movie Mystery box which is a real hoot to watch,
although there are some downsides to such a clue since some people
have problems trying to watch the movie, modem connections are
interminably slow for such huge files, and there could be space
limitations for your hosting site such a file might fill up. Using
Geocities, you won't see me uploading multi-megabyte files! But it
IS a cool clue if those issues don't concern you. =)

And finally, there's what I've done with a lot of my clues: I've
added pictures that really don't contribute to the clue itself, but
they're nice pictures which I took while hiding the letterbox. I
still get to show off my pictures, even if they aren't part of the
clue. =)

Carpe diem!

-- Ryan
