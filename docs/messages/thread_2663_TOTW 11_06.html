From: Kurious Jo (KuriousJo@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-11-09 12:57:39 UTC-08:00
Hi all!
John's feedback and Funhog's question prompt me to explain further. Then
there's my opinion - for whatever that is worth. * smile *

To answer Funhog's questioning why the slug's gender is important. Well
it's not. It's just that the overall presentation of the slug is feminine -
it's carrying a purple purse and an orange and red umbrella that comes off
looking like a parasol when you combine it with the flowing train behind.
Of course the purse is supposed to be a Lb pack and the slug has a naturally
build in tail. It is cute, very cute. But should we use an image that
possibly alienates half (or whatever percentage) of the letterboxing
population?

In my humble opinion, here's the underlying reason to consider coming up
with something that everyone - men, women, and children - will be
comfortable wearing. I can't tell you how many times people have said to my
hubby & I how lucky we are that both of us are interested in Lbing. We hear
it from men and women, but mainly women. If we present letterboxing as a
women's activity, we loose more men. Those husbands (or other guys) that
aren't too sure yet if this is for them . . . but then they see this logo.
If you were in their shoes wouldn't you think, "Oh, it's a girl's thing",
then go away? Or as one husband said, "it's great for the kids". At that
point I didn't have the heart to tell him that we don't have kids! We ARE
the kids!

It's not one or two persons opinions here that are important - it's the
overall sport that is important. Dogglesnoffer's tree idea sounds good.
Surely there are more people out there with thoughts and ideas, hey?

Ok, I stuck my monkey fingers out there. Let's see if I get them bit off!
*laugh *

Kurious Jo :-)
