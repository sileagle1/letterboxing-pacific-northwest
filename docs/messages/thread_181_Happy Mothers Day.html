Happy Mothers Day

From: Fudrick (fudrick@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-05-11 20:09:28 UTC-07:00
Mummsy, my Mom, has been interested in Letterboxing since I first told her about it. Today, we went out 'boxing and had great success!

She logged in at the BrewHaus at Tumwater Historical Park, Yashiro at the Olympia Japanese Gardens, and the Turtle, Hippo, Gator and Bat at Harry Fain's Legion Park. Also got her first hitchhiker, I Love You.

She had a great time! Being unsteady on her feet, may not get too many, but enjoys our great hobby.

Hopefully didn't keep anyone from getting Harry's Gator and Bat. We got them, logged in, and three families showed up to play in the playground. We couldn't outwait them, so went to dinner and put them back later. If you were there and didn't find them, our apologies :-)

Fudrick

From: Fudrick (fudrick@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-05-12 20:03:12 UTC-07:00
It was a great day!

We were first at Yashiro. There was a guy sitting on the bench by pond,
doing some sunning, lunching, and writing (or some work-like activity). As
we wandered around, he finally asked if we wanted his bench. We said no,
sorry for interrupting. Nice quiet spot, in perfect condition.

The HH was well hidden, but I can see where the big box may be worrisome to
hide. The other item you noted was still there. Some people are strange.

Made a note that the gator had been chased off by a bat. Finally the clues
made sense, went right to it. How did you ever find that location?

Depending on the specifics of the South Sound Gathering, Mummsy may join us,
see that letterboxers are quasi-normal people.

Thanks for the great boxes!

Fudrick
