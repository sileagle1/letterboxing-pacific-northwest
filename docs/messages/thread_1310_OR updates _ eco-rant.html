OR updates & eco-rant

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-04-30 03:04:20 UTC
In the last few days, I have checked up on a number of my Portland Boxes. All of the
following are in place and ready to rock:
Oaks Bottom, Doyle Owl, Crystal Springs, What's for Dinner, Bishops Close (with new
log,) Hidden Dragon and both Oak Island boxes.
(BTW the camas and wild rose are blooming on Sauvie Island!)

While making the rounds of my babies, I ran into some destruction at the site of one
that REALLY bummed me out. One of the boxes is concealed beneath a huge fallen
limb. Someone had ripped almost all the bark off the thing for about ten feet. The
bark was scattered everywhere smothering the wildflowers and grasses. Sheesh! I'm
the one who returns every rock and stick back to its original position, fluffs up
crushed grasses and sweeps away my footprints whenever possible. This just about
killed me...

I'm hoping, hoping, hoping that this was not the work of an over-zealous letterboxer.
I'm probably preaching to the choir but let it be known that none of my letterboxes
will ever require digging, removal of bark, disassembling of walls or any other kind of
environmental destruction. Amen! Funhog
