The advantages of letterboxing

From: erfellie (hemenekawan@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-02-13 17:13:38 UTC

Ok so we all know the advantages of letterboxing and why we do it
and so many others just dont understand, the hiking 10 miles on a hot
summer day drenched in sweat and aching muscles and getting dirty,
but when you find that little box, its all worth it, right? I love
the hike, like I am sure you all do, and due to this sport I like to
think to myself I have become a fairly good hiker, (not quite Ryan or
Godlike hiker though) well this is much to my advantage. Well for the
first time, wednesday, I was forced to hike a very very very long
ways completely against my will. My boyfriend Luke and his friend
Chris drove a jeep waaaaaaay up into the mountains with no snow
chains and evidently early in the morning when the top layer of snow
was frozen, that was ok but around 11am the jeep just fell into it,
yup, they were driving along and whoops, it just dropped into several
feet of snow and of course to top it off they broke the transmission
upon trying to get it out.

Well I had just gotten home from a very strenuous, and fun, hike
and walked in the door, jumped in the shower and was interrupted by a
phone call from a certain boyfriend begging me to come save the day.
Well being the wonderful woman I am, I jumped in his oversized jeep
that I can barely drive or see over the steering wheel, and headed up
in the middle of nowhere to save the boys. At one point I could drive
no further so I stopped and waited for them to come down the mountain
and find me and after an hour or so they did.

Being quite exhausted I wanted to go home, but no, we drove into
town, got the parts to fix the busted transmission and headed back up
the mountain, this time with tire chains. So we get up there and were
able to drive to the busted jeep and lo-and-behold Chris left his
jeep keys back in town, 30 miles away!!!! (ever wanted to kill
someone?) so as we turn around in the snow and try to head back to
town, this jeep fell several feet into the snow also (oh joy) and
after several hours of shoveling well into the night, not too mention
the cougar tracks in the snow all around us, we came to the
conclusion that we must hike out. darn. I was wearing tennis shoes
and already had frozen feet and a fear of frostbite and was certainly
not dressed for cold weather.

So we start hiking out in 4 feet of loose snow where every step
was a challenge, and it was only 5 or so miles, but the hardest I
have hiked in these condiitions. Well into the night, freezing
weather, me totally unprepared, no food, water, warm clothes, and a
cougar close by, and here we had to hike down a mountain because the
men didnt use snow chains the first time up the mountain. Thats why
Luke needs a woman like me, to tell him when to stop being macho and
use the darn snow chains!! well too late for that though... I was
ready to get home and was hiking ahead of everyone else (they were to
slow and kept complaining) so I guess this is the part that I am glad
that I hike alot because I think it was easier on me than the less
adventerous people lagging behind me in the snow.

Chris kept saying "I'm tired, lets stop and here and wait for
someone to find us" well I just about kicked him in the head. No one
was coming to get us, no one knew where we were, and when Luke and
Chris first drove up the snowy mountain, they were making fresh
tracks in the snow, nobody had been up there in a long time. So I
told him in a very ladylike manner that we couldnt stop because we
would freeze to death and/or become cougar food :o) well this helped
him move along...slowly... but eventually we made it out of there
(obviously) alive. Although I dont plan on hiking with these two
complainers anymore and I think Luke will stop asking me why I like
to "torture" myself by hiking, well obviously it did some good for me
to stay in shape and healthy, otherwise I would have been a frozen
popsicle being consumed by a rather large kitty :o)
**************************************************************
Please remember guys, its not "girly" to stop and put on tire chains,
do it, cuz I ain't about to come save anyone elses butt's hahaha
