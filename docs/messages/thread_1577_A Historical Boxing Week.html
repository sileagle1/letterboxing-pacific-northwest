A Historical Boxing Week

From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-09-05 23:03:50 UTC-07:00
Okay this has hands down been one of the most memorable letterboxing weeks I
have ever had. - Monday and Tuesday Camp Fire Lady, Iron`Bear and I took on
a phenomenal Mystery Series. This left a taste for Mystery in our mouths
and we set out on some other Mystery Box adventures. Friday night we picked
up Sim Kitty and had dinner with Heather and Wild Woman, what a treat!

Saturday we decided to hit the trails with Camp Fire Lady and Cats Meow, we
hit the Brat Packs new 2 x 2 series. I have to say that the fact that it
was raining while we were doing this incredibly cute serious was quite
ironic, Iron`Bear found a big stick to carry around with him while we were
hiking and looked a lot like Moses getting ready to part the red seas! -
After completing this series, and missing the bonus box (Iron`Bear should
really be whipped with a licorice string for this) we headed out to find
Team Nashoba's Mr. Potato Head series, and then to Ridgefield for a few more
Team Nashoba boxes.

This morning we met with Camp Fire Lady, Cats Meow and Der Mad Stamp for a
day filled with what I consider historical boxing.

First we hit DMS's Pooch Place Letterbox. We knew this box was missing, but
DMS had a replacement, and we made sure to show him where we figured the
original box was to make sure we earned this one!

Next we headed off to Rock Creek where we found one of Green Hornet's boxes.
(#2 in the series I believe) We were headed for #3 when DMS told us that
the tree it was hidden in was gone and told us to wait there while he
scouted ahead for #4. He came running back telling us #4 was missing, but
he thought we could find #3 again if we kept looking, the tree was just a
little farther up than he had remembered. Iron`Bear quickly retrieved box
#3 and was helping DMS put it back when he decided to dig the hole DMS had
there just a little bit bigger to compensate for the next box. Suddenly we
hear DMS and Iron`Bear cheering and laughing. What is it? We asked them.
"Bear found the original" DMS exclaimed! DMS had replaced it when he scouted
ahead, but it ends up the original was still there! We were the only ones
signed into that box in 5 years!!!!!

We hit a few more boxes in the Beaverton/Tigard area as well. We are happy
to report that Hyland Forest is alive and well, as well as The Troll in the
Rock Creek Park Series. Everything is Nonsense still seems to be eluding
everyone, though it is alive and well. DMS and Sim Kitty were able to find
it, what a team they are! We ran into some Munchkins today and they just
all happened to be huddled around a munchkin size Christmas tree, who would
have thought! - Word of mouth is that that we found some seriously cool
boxes.

We are sad to report that Rock Creek #1 and #4 are officially missing, and
despite our efforts to yell and scream his name from the fence outside his
back yard, Green Hornet just would not come out and replace them!

We are also sad to say it seems like Lily has gone on vacation and the Hamby
Park Letterbox seems as though it is no where to be found.

What a wonderful day, being with friends, boxing with one of the Pioneers of
Letterboxing in North America and just watching our daughter smile.
Everyone went home with new stamps in their journals and new memories that
they will cherish forever! I just love letterboxing.

Pandora
