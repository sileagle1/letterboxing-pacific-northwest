Passport to Letterboxing Stamp Camp

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2004-08-26 18:25:33 UTC
The Passport to Letterboxing Stamp Camp is this weekend and I can
hardly wait! This is going to be an event that you will not want to
miss.
There will be some great workshops and activities and a few surprises
as well. Here are a few reminders for the event:
For everyone who will be camping, please check in at campsite #14
before 6:30 pm on Friday so you can participate in the Meet and
Greet. If you are in need of firewood, there is a kind, little old
man who lives a block from the park and sells firewood for $3.00 a
bundle.
If you are coming up for the day on Saturday, please check in at
campsite #14 before 9:30 am to be there in time for the first
workshop at 10:00 am. Be sure to bring your own food for the day
since this is not a potluck event. There is a $5.00 day use fee to
enter the park. Park in the parking lot to your right after the
ranger station and walk up to campsite #14. It is not that far.
Tickets for some awesome letterboxing raffle items are $1.00 each or
6 for $5.00 and letterbox starter kits with a carving tool are
available for purchase for $5.00 each. Please no checks! If you have
your own carving tools, please bring them for the stamp carving
workshop. Bring a hitch hiker if you would like to participate in the
Hitch Hiker Hostel hidden in the park.
PLEASE DO NOT BRING EXTRA LETTERBOXES AND HITCH HIKERS TO LEAVE ON
THE TABLES.
Please RSVP to tinarie at msn dot com so that we can make sure we
have enough supplies for everyone.
Whether you are coming from the north or the south on the I-5
freeway, exit at 179th Ave./exit 9. There will be red signs with red,
green and blue balloons that will direct you to the park from the I-5
freeway so please watch for them and we will you see at the Passport
to Letterboxing Stamp Camp at Battle Ground Lake in Battle Ground, WA.

Camp Fire Lady

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-08-31 14:09:31 UTC-07:00
Horseballs is a game thought of and designed by my "adopted" father in
Washington. He made my family a set so we could have as much fun as they
did. We brought it to camp with us and then Pupp came carved us a stamp to
go with it. SOOOOO.. anyone that plays with us gets the stamp. (Good
sportsmanship is a MUST).

It's actually a ton of fun. The goals are made up of PVC pipe. It has 3
horizontal bars, stacked on top of each other. You stand at the opposite end
with the "horse balls". (They are two golf balls with a 18 inch piece of
rope attached to them) You throw the horseballs any way you like but the
goal is to wrap them around one of the horizontal bars. Each one has a
different point value. That's all there is to it. Lots of fun for adults,
kids, and labradors.

Maiden
