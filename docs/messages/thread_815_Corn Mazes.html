I've probably been to four different corn mazes, and every single one
of them has a different twist on it. One of them had a dozen or so
checkpoints in the maze and at each checkpoint there was a picture of
the maze so you could try to memorize the way to the next
checkpoint. (Which did not have to be acquired in any particular
order.)

Another maze had four checkpoints in the maze, but absolutely no
hints as to where in the maze they were. So you had to run around
randomly checking every dead end, turn, and twist until you found
them all. Again, no "correct" way through the maze--whatever gets
you to the checkpoints and back out again. (There's a lot of talking
to other people in this maze where you compare notes to see if
they've found a checkpoint that you haven't or vice-versa or what
parts of the maze they've already completed searching.)

Another maze had numbers at key decision points in the maze that
corresponded with a numbered question on a sheet you got before
entering the maze with a list of multiple choice answers. If you got
the answer wrong, it would send you the wrong way into the maze. If
you got the answer right, it would send you in the correct
direction. (The only maze I've done that actually had just one start
and finish and one correct way through the maze.)

> running through the windy blustery corn in the dark. ooooooooh
> creeepy!!! :)

I did that once, and it's a hoot! I'm jealous! =) Even better,
when I went, it was misty--not rain, but as close as you can get
without it being so. It was wet and dark and a whole lot of fun.
I've also heard of some corn mazes that have special Halloween themes
and they hide people with costumes in the maze that will come out and
scare you at night. =)

It's definitely a hoot. http://www.cornmazes.com And even if you
can't hide a letterbox IN the corn maze (at least not until they cut
it down and start over again after Halloween), you could probably
find a place just outside of the maze that will be safe.
