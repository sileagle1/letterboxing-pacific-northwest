Hi Feather

I don't believe anyone has set down and described and listed
differences, but I'll give it a whirl.

LbNA
Oldest North American site, maybe first web based clue site.
Basically a clue depository with a few counting features a planter
can turn off. Pretty much just nuts and bolts. Has been called a
Plain Jane, but many older letterboxers like the lack of bells and
whistles.
Has a "Contact the Placer" feature most letterboxers like. Home page
looks very interesting, with all the old looking stuff laying on the
desk. Clue pages look like old manuscripts.

Atlas Quest

The new kid on the block. Has tons of gimicky features. On line
logs,Profiles,talk boards, tons and tons of talk boards,more up to
date looking clue pages and other pages. Has some features, Blue
Diamonds(that can be turned off), finders listings(that can't be
turned off). Premium Memberships (that give the paying member a few
more things)but you do not have to pay to use most of the site.
While it is a clue depository as well, it seemingly is becoming more
of a chat based site and many new boxers seem to prefer its features.
Some clues are restricted to those with logged (at AQ) finds of 50
or 100 finds.

These are my own perceptions of each site.

You can host clues at either site and link them to the other site or
keep separate clue sheets at both sites.

We keep our clues hosted at LbNA and link them to AQ so that our
editing and maintenance processes are easier.

There are other clue sites, but you have to find them for yourself.
Like some mystery boxes, the clues could be hidden anywhere.

Don
