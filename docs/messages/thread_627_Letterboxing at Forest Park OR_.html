Letterboxing at Forest Park OR.

From: Tina Schaffer (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2003-08-24 14:53:43 UTC
What a beautiful day Saturday was for letterboxing! It was Team Run-A-
Muck's most productive day yet with 10 letterboxes in all and found
every one that we set out to find! WOOHOO! We started off returning
to "What's for Dinner" box #1 that was missing on our outing last
week. Thanks Funhog for replacing it so promptly. We even picked up
our first HH "Here's Looking At You Kid". I let out a scream that I
am sure the whole park heard. It is tucked away nicely in my backpack
to be placed soon. Next it was off to find the Upper Macleay Park
Series. What a beautiful trail! All 4 boxes are alive and well and
wonderful stamps. On the way back down the trail Willow and I were
attempting the limbo under the tree when Willow who was in front did
one of those forward "lose your balance" things and couldn't stop.
Needless to say she hit the trail with a skid and a thump almost
knocking my daughter Cat's Meow over the side of the trail. Cat's
Meow who is 8 years old put out her arms to stop Willow from falling
but realized at the last minute that an 8 year old can't stop a 40
something, 6 foot tall Willow from toppeling. After we made sure
Willow was OK we had a huge laugh and went along our merry way. Where
was the video camera when you need it? Next it was off to find "Goofy
Pub Names Series". All 5 boxes are alive and well although the clues
for #4 & #5 were pretty vague. There were more than one each of the
holy trees, fallen logs and blue diamonds that confused us but we
prevailed. Everyone on the trails had their dogs along which gave us
a large variety of breeds to pet along the way. Cat's Meow was loving
everyone one of them as she passed.You see, her only pet is a
parakeet cuz we don't have a yard for a dog. Anyway, thank you
Paisley Orca and Canada Goose for some wonderful stamps.
