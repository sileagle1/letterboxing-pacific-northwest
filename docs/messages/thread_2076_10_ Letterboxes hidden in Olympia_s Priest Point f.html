10+ Letterboxes hidden in Olympia's Priest Point for 100 Year Anniversary

From: artspeak_nw (ArtspeakNW@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-02 03:40:18 UTC
Over ten letterboxes have been hidden throughout Priest Point Park in
Olympia, WA as part of the park's 100 year anniversary celebration.
The City of Olympia is celebrating the 100 year anniversary of Priest
Point Park on Saturday, June 4, 2005. Here is their little blurb:

Priest Point Park Centennial Celebration
June 04, 2005
11 a.m. to 5 p.m.
Olympia, WA, USA

Come celebrate Priest Point Park's 100th Birthday! Enjoy a day of
history, nature, performances, food, games and education in June.
Special features include a Living Room History Project, guided nature
hikes and bird counts, a salmon bake, fiary house parade of homes and
letterboxing for all ages. Come have fun commemorating the gem of
Olympia's park system.

More info: Priest Point Park is a 314 acre City of Olympia park with a
mixture of mature coniferous and deciduous woods and lush understory.
The park includes an extensive trail system, one mile of waterfront
with access to a rocky beach, mudflats, a number of picnic areas, and
restrooms. Some of the best birding is on the west side of the park.

Directions
To reach Priest Point Park, take exit 105B from I-5. Follow Plum
Street north. Continue past 4th Avenue. Plum Street becomes East Bay
Drive NE as it follows along the eastern shoreline of East Bay in Budd
Inlet. After about 2 miles you will reach Priest Point Park on East
Bay Drive. Note: For the event shuttles will be provided as parking
will be closed off at the park. If you need more info about where to
catch a shuttle (free) you can either drop me a note or contact
Olympia Arts, Parks.


The clues will be distributed at the celebration on Saturday, 6/4.
Hope to see some of you there!

After all the logbook making, stamp carving, trail exploring, clue
writing....the boxes are staying in the park. The clues will post on
the main website either tomorrow (Saturday) or Sunday. Once they
disappear I will either delete them or re-do them depending on the
alignment of the planets, the biorythms of my Labrador and how well
the stock market is faring...

I would be honored if people wanted to do a mini gathering there to
find the boxes. The boxes will take a decent amount of time to find;
not because of the clues (which are fairly straighforward) but because
of the size of the park. It really is a fantastic park. You can time
it for low tide and beach comb a little bit, cool off on the forest
trails and plan an outdoor picnic at the playground if you choose. It
is a beautiful sunset spot.

I hope some people can make it. The fairy house parade of homes will
also be fun and people are invited to create their own fairy houses in
the park as well. If you can't be there for the celebration and visit
later on, keep your eyes open for fairy houses throughout the park.
History has it, the park was once a thriving fairy settlement. The
fairy and gnome population dwindled as development encroached. The
City of Olympia hopes to invite the little forest guardians to return
with small fairy houses hidden throughout the park. If you build
it...they will come. ;)

Best Wishes,
Kristi
