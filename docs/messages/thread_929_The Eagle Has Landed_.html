The Eagle Has Landed!

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2003-11-13 04:03:34 UTC
Even though I flew in last Sunday (with help from an airplane),
I have not had a chance to post my activities until now, so
here goes. Sunday I had to drive to Ft. Canby because I wanted
to place my Lewis & Clark In WA box there and I intended to
letterbox along the way. I did manage to find the two Claquato
boxes and the Rainbow Falls one, but darkness defeated any other
endeavers. In fact, I had to hide my box in darkness with the
threat of being locked in the park if they closed the gates.
Luckily there were lights along the path that I hid it on so
it was not too bad, but was not able to hide it as well as I
wanted. I then drove on to Portland so I could letterbox the
Columbia River Gorge the next day and hide my Lewis & Clark In OR
box there. I just have to say that even though it rained on me
all day, the Gorge is just beautiful and the hikes and letterboxes
are great! I even found the Multnomah Letterbox! I reluctantly
left the Gorge to do some letterboxing in Portland because I
wanted to get another of Funhog's 3 Portland boxes (Sleight). I
already have one (Portland, TX), but alas, I was not able to find
this one. I did find a few others but it was soon time to start
heading back to Olympia for the Silver Eagle Oyster Exchange
Event graciously organized by DVN2RCKR. I did manage to squeeze
in the Happy Postman Series in Lewisville Park just before dark,
though it made me a little late to the event. This was my first
event and first time Exchanging with people I did not know and
I must say I was very impressed with the whole experience.
Everyone was very nice and easy to talk to and the food was great!
Thanks again to everyone who showed up, especially on such short
notice. The next day I had to be in Seattle for my conference
that would start in the afternoon, so I was up early again to get
in as much letterboxing as I could (I was up by 5:30 each day
so I could be at the letterbox destination at dawn). I planned
to place my Olympia Beer letterbox (first in my Beer Series) at
Tumwater Falls Park, but when I got there the gates were closed
and would not open until 8:00. I decided to drive down to the
Tumwater Historical Park and find the Water, Water Everywhere
box (and bonus) and ended up placing my box there. Instead of
waiting for the other park to open, I decided to go on to the
Wonderwood Park in Lacey and I'm glad I did. I really enjoyed
that park (it helped that it was the first day without rain) and
found a lot of boxes while trekking all over the place. I did
not plan on spending that much time there but with all the boxes,
beautiful scenery and cool weather (I'm tired of mid-80s), I
ended up spending almost 3 hours there. This left little time
for my next stop at Ft. Steilacoom Park, but I did manage to find
the Fledgling, SSLF and Where Are The Clowns boxes. I briefly
looked for DVN2RCKR's Insane Asylum box but could not find it,
even though I know she just replaced it. There were many others
I just did not have time to look for since it was time to head to
Seattle. Today was the first day I did no letterboxing and doesn't
it just figure that it was beautiful outside! I did sneak out
to place my Klondike Gold Rush box and walk through Pioneer Square
and Pike Market. I must say, I really enjoyed this area and I
don't normally like being downtown in big cities. In fact, I
have loved every place I have visited so far and envy all of you
for your multitude of scenic places to visit. I hope to sneak
out again tomorrow and maybe Friday, and of course I hope to see
some more of you this Saturday at Wapato Park before I have to
leave. Sorry for the long post but I just wanted to share how
much I appreciate being here. Hope to see you Saturday!

Silver Eagle
