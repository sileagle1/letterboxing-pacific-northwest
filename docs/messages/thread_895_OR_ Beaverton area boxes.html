-------- Original Message --------
From: "Comcast Mail"
Subject: letterbox updates
To:

Hello,
I work in the Natural Resources Department for Tualatin Hills Park and
Recreation District. I monitor Geocaches in District parks and recently have
begun monitoring letterboxes in our parks as well. My main concern is for the
safety of our park patrons, including those seeking Geocaches and letterboxes.
As such, if I note a Geocache or letterbox in a place that poses a hazard to
the seeker I will ask that the hider remove it, or move it to a new location
pending our Department's approval of the new location.

Currently I have found approximately 25 letterboxes listed as hidden in 19
District Parks. I spent today attempting to verify the locations of several
Geocaches and to find 5 letterboxes. Two letterboxes were not located, but
both locations were hazardous to seekers due to potential animal encounters,
dangerous stream banks, or dangerous stream crossings (Fanno Creek Greenway
Letterboxes). When I attempted to contact the letterboxer that placed the box
about these hazards, the email came back as undeliverable.

This brings up several questions:
1) How often do the old letterboxes get deleted from your system (i.e., those
that are inactive or missing)?
2) How often do you ask letterbox hiders to monitor and maintain their boxes
(there is nothing mentioned about this in the waivers section of your webpage)?
3) Especially in light of heightened security worldwide since 9/11, there is
great concern for safety of public buildings and structures. I see nothing in
your waivers section that suggest that letterbox placers do NOT place boxes in
public buildings, in or near utilities or utility structures, or where a
letterbox may be suspected as a threat to the public (e.g., on bridges). What
do you advise letterboxers when reviewing letterbox locations?
4) There is no mention in the waivers section of contacting the public property
managers about letterboxes placed on lands which they manage. What do you
advise letterboxers when their locations are on lands managed by public
agencies (e.g., parks)?

I have a very good working relationship with the Geocaching community and would
like to establish such a relationship with the letterboxing community. To that
end, I ask that all letterbox hiders, who wish to place a letterbox in one of
our parks, contact our office prior to letterbox placement so we may review the
location and the clues. Most of the letterboxes I discovered online, listed
the park name and location (e.g., Hyland Forest, Beaverton, Oregon). This is
quite helpful and allows me to quickly review the local letterboxes to see
which ones are in our parks. I trust you will ask the specific location of the
letterbox when it is entered as a 'mystery' location (e.g., The Imposter, ???,
OR) so that you will know to request that we are contacted by the letterbox
hider prior to its placement.

Thank you for your consideration of these matters. I look forward to working
with the Letterboxing community.

Kyle Spinks, Natural Resources Technician
Natural Resources Department
Tualatin Hills Park and Recreation District
5500 SW Arctic Drive, Suite 2
Beaverton, OR 97005
503-644-8469 x32
