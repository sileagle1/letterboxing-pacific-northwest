No brainer--DC Pagans, Unite! I think it was planted by Rev Aloysius
Holden (sp?). Some runners-up in the PNW area would include Funhog's
Ace of Fun, just about all of Der Mad Stamper's boxes and many of Trial

those took several months of 'backoffice' pondering (thinking about
them when working on other things/sleeping & ultimately solving them in
the middle of the night several months after first looking at the
clue). And you can never forget Legerdemaine--that character's boxes
are incredible works of genius--especially loved The Big Apple series!
Green Tortuga's got a bunch that take a while to solve, as well--
keeping those brain cells active and all.

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-09-06 10:10:05 UTC-06:00
> What letterbox has made you work mentally the hardest?
>
> Double Tree

Green Tortuga's Buffalo Warrior. I kept clueing into the wrong words. When
I finally figured it out though it was a "duh!" moment. I was so excited to
find it, but then I couldn't get out to it for various reasons--seasons,
travel time, trail closures. It took me over two years to get it. Then
when I finally got out there, I kept seeing the wrong things thinking "it
might be that." I went miles off-track before I found it right where the
clue said it should have been. Boy did I ever do a happy dance when I
finally found that one. It was a great feeling!

Cadenza

This has changed with time. When I first started letterboxing, I had
done little puzzle solving. Der Mad Stamper's Spirit of Dartmoor took
me months to solve, having no idea how a British crypto worked. My
skills have improved considerably over time but when Pungent Bob's
wicked $100 Dollar Bill showed up, I was stumped. It again took me
months and constant prodding to stick to it, finally coming up with
the solution. Now, I still have the first Legerdemaine clue I ever saw
in my possession and STILL haven't had the aha! moment... Funhog
