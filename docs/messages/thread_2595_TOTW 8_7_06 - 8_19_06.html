From: Kris Leibrand (bookwormlb@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-08 18:48:21 UTC-07:00
Have you ever told a lie to go letterboxing?



To those of you that are on this list and possibly understand the lure of
this hobby I admit it; I have lied to go letterboxing. More times than I
should care to count. You could almost say my whole letterboxing career has
been one lie after another. My DH doesn't get the "looking for Tupperware
in the woods" thing, so I occasionally have to be inventive when I want to
get a box. I went out a few weeks ago to pick up a box on my way to work,
realized there was another box "right nearby" and called in to work to tell
them I overslept and I'd be in late. I've had the odd "appointment" or two
requiring me to take whole afternoons off of work. Suspicious headaches
that are very close to turning in to migraines have happened, too. I know,
it's sad, isn't it? I do try and be creative with my.um.excuses and I
occasionally just tell the truth so that the boss doesn't get too
suspicious. It's hard to come up with a creative lie when you stumble in to
work 2 hours late, tell them you've overslept or got stuck in traffic, then
go to your desk and realize you have leaves in your hair or mud all over
your shoes.



No, I'm not a pathological liar, but I think a good 12-step program might be
needed!



And I'm not signing this email, so if any of you try to say this email was
from me I will deny I ever wrote it!!!



P.S. If you need a good excuse, let me know. I bet I have a good one!

> Have you ever told a lie in order to go letterboxing?

Nope--I'd be sent to the slammer if I ever did that WRT work but I have
altered work related travel plans rather circuitously to shun air
travel in order to be able to drive to and from the location instead
(this coming from someone who commuted by air monthly to Seattle from
Texas for 2 years before I even started boxing).

My previous office mates thought I was insane every time I begged
permission to drive to distant locales such as Monterey, Boise,
Portland or Spokane in lieu of flying. I'd only be reimbursed the cost
of the going airfare rate at the time & the travel time was totally
unpaid so most of those trips were on my own dime but it sure helped us
tour the Pacific Northwest along the way. I even twice begged to drive
to New Orleans from Seattle in lieu of flying but those two requests
met the file 13 response--DOA--as they totally knew I was insane!!! We
flew both times but ended up maxing our time on the ground in order to
be able to squeeze some boxing time in before we had to return. Yep,
we'd caught the bug, too--just had to get creative with our freetime in
order to maximize our fun time. ;)

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-08 22:34:34 UTC-07:00
Wow, and I thought some of my traveling around was a bit excessive!

I'll admit I've flown under the radar for a bit during the day to sneak in some 'boxing. I drive an average of about 500 miles per week and all over the South Puget Sound Region, so I try and make stops for boxes along the way between sites. Everybody assumes I'm at a site for somebody else or something and doesn't check up on me, and I'm certainly not going to offer up information. Nobody asks, as it is, where exactly I was all day. Even if they did, simply saying I stopped for a break for lunchtime at an outdoorsy place that's not a noisy construction site, the earthy types I work with will be able to identify readily. I might omit the whole box aspect, though...so I guess that's lying by omission?

I got some suspicious looks from a park ranger once while in my work truck, though. I think she wondered what was in the box in the back of the pickup, since it had radioactive symbols all over it...haha!
