what do you think about box updates?

From: (PNWEXPLR@aol.com) | Date: 2004-09-06 17:55:59 UTC-04:00
Hello all,

Funhog's recent post about AZ Roadie's list of found boxes (holy moley--does
that person ever SLEEP?!) leads me to ask what people think about posting
finds. Of course, I am talking about posts that don't include any spoilers
(including not lumping a mystery box in with a list of other boxes to preclude
people deducing its location). Personally, I 'd always enjoyed reading about
others' finds, but as I recall someone suggested they didn't like it and a flame
war erupted over the subject. By that time my asbestos suit was wearing
quite thin in places, so I stopped posting about my finds.
I'm hoping to launch a reasonable discussion about the pros and cons of box
updates on this list.

Here's an example of what I mean by a box update:
(Subject: Portland, OR 3rd Fate of Childhood Series)
Today Heather, her daughter Button and I finally went after Pandora's third
Childhood series. We had a chilly but lovely morning tracking down The Old
Lady Who Swallowed a Fly, Good Ship Lolipop, and Mary's Little Lamb. We even
stopped the evil Mukesh in his tracks--but when Heather turned the last page of
the clues she'd printed out, instead of reading where the hiding place of the
last box was, she said, "Don't hate me." Yes, she had left the final page
at home!!! We wandered about the area for a brief while in an attempt to
mind-merge with Pandora and find the box free-style, but Pandy musta still been
asleep or something because somehow the connection failed.
So guess where we're headed (again) tomorrow afternoon?
Thanks Pandora and Iron Bear! What a fun series!!!

--Wild Woman

I enjoy hearing about updates on boxes, and keep a Outlook folder
with updates. The only downside is that I don't have the list
memorized the way many of you seem to, and I can't tell what state or
part of a state the box is located in! It is always helpful to me
when people say "I was in the Canon Beach, Or, area and found xyz
-watch out for poison oak..." I keep an Outlook folder for notes
like that, and I can check for helpful hints before we go out.

I also like the updates from people who have planted boxes and report
on maintenance trips. It can be frustrating to look for a box that's
not there, and after e-mailing the placer, they knew it, but hadn't
updated the clue list yet. I'm sure it's hard to stay on top of these
things, so extra input is always welcome for others.
