WA: Box report and a thumbs up

From: hoopoe4me (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2003-10-19 20:55:42 UTC
After my last frustrating outing looking for a box ( in Paris) and
just missing out on the 2nd Paris box placed ( clues that pass in the
night) I was ready for some easy boxing on this blustery day.

I Visited the quietly placed Kenmore Rhododendron Park in Kenmore. A
nice basic warm-up. Placed by Painterly Lady from Connecticut, so I
guess it is my first CN box, of a sort. Small pocket park, I bet is
lovely in the Spring bloom.

Then I swung over to St Edwards to go on saffari at the Savannah
Series.

All I can say is Well Done Twilight and Sunshine!

I had been thinking, for some time, that I would like to do a LB (
single or series) that involved a fair amount of compass work so
people could practice their skill.

This series certainly fits the bill. The trails are ultra-short so
what looks like a lot of clues are not really all that far. MANY
little short trails that wind and intersect allows for focus,
concentration , attention to detail and in some cases very precise
compass work. Just what I need every once and a while. Managed to
get through the whole course without getting too rained on. DID NOT
however find the first box. I did not dig too deep owing to the
presence of Trailing Blackberry amid all the Salal.

So it might be missing ( this is a busy area and very Haunted right
now)or perhaps I just did not see it.

I like the Impala stamp the best.

This is a good series to introduce newbies to "how to hide a box" and
other skills like the compass work.

Hoopoe
