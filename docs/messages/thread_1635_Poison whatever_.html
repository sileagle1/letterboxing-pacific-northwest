There's poison oak out there, but it's not all that common. It took
me several months of hiking when I first moved out before I spotted
it. Out here, it's usually in sunny areas like meadows and other
clearings. If you're hiking through forests, it's very unlikely
you'll spot some, and most of the hiking trails around here are in
forests. =)

In California, the opposite is true. Poison oak is more often found
in the shade--especially near creeks and other water sources.

Just learn to identify it so you know what not to touch. There a
picture I took of some at
http://www.atlasquest.com/aboutlb/glossary/p.html along with a wordy
description of what you should look for.

Poison ivy is generally an east-coast thing, though it has many
similarities to poison oak.
