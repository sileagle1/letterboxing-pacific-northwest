Trail maintenance

From: doubletreepfx (greenthummm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-31 03:11:13 UTC
Not strictly a letterboxing issue, but this last weekend I ran into 3
people doing trail maintenance at Fish Lake Trail in the Olallie Lake
Scenic Area. A forest service employee (on his own time) and his son
and grandson were doing trail maintenance, an exhausting but necessary
job. They were cutting out downed trees and diverting the creek that
insisted upon muddying up a section of the switchback. The older man
had neat stories to tell and we swapped info about trails and
conditions over the last couple years, in the OLSA. Since hiking has
always been my first passion (with letterboxing an enthusiastic co-
activity), this was a great treat to be able to talk to this gentleman
who is partly responsible for making my hiking in the area more
enjoyable. And I think talking to them gave them an excuse to take a
break for a while... So I encourage anyone who runs into a work party
to thank them. They deserve it!
