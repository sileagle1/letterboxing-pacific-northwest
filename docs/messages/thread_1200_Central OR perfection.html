From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2004-03-15 02:25:33 UTC
...Ahhhhhhhh... another weekend of blue skies topped with the first wildflowers of
2004 and T-shirt temperatures. Furthermore, Letterboxing Notables from Northern
climes were visiting. Put it all together and Saturday was the perfect time to hit Smith
Rock SP, along with a gazillion rock climbers. The three boxes there are all in fine
condition but my Mariposa Lily is very lonely. We also visited the two entries in
Redmond's Sam Johnson Park in the late afternoon.

Today, another trio of letterboxes was on the program. We went to Tetherow
Crossing and found all of these boxes in good shape. The water in the river was high
and everything is turning that wonderful shade of green that is so special in the
desert.

When I got home this evening, I first checked to see if any new boxes had been
placed in the area over the weekend. That's when I discovered birder's latest
contributions. Then, I looked at the Talk List and saw that the Fleshman's had been to
these new boxes this very day! I only wish we had gone there today, too. It would
have been way fun to run into them, blood and all, on the trail. That sort of encounter
is highly unlikely in this part of the state but my fingers are crossed that it'll happen
someday. Funhog
