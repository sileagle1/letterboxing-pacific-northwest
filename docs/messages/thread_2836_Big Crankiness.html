Big Crankiness

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-08-29 17:19:25 UTC
Yesterday was a gorgeous day in Portland. My newfound letterboxing
pal, Heyokahman and I planned a morning rendezvous at Portland Kayak
Company. This place is conveniently located on Macadam Blvd, near
John's Landing. We left there rolling a canoe in tiny wheels down the
sidewalk towards my favorite Super Fund site, the Willamette River. We
rolled down to the Willamette Park boat ramp and launched out into a
section of Portland's water world.

Our destination was Ross Island, home to a large nesting colony of
Great Blue Herons. Along the way we marveled at the architectural
potpourri of houseboats at Oaks Park, the sight of mergansers and
raptors on the islands, and the sight of a gigantic cement pipe. Could
this be the headwaters of the Super Fund Willamette? On the island we
found deer prints and lots of pudgy, warm and very sweet blackberries.
Oh! there was that cool Big Cranky letterbox, too!

At a leisurely pace this whole trip out and back took about an hour
and half and cost only fifteen bucks for the canoe rental. It's a
highly recommended way to savor a beautiful morning. It reminded me
why I am so lucky to live in the Pacific Northwest, not to mention to
be alive. Thanks, KuKu for the nudge...
