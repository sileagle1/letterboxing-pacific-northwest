WA: Chinook running...

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-27 00:54:14 UTC
If folks are interested in a little salmon run action combined with
letterboxing these days, check out the chinook runs at the New
Market Falls and '7' Oars letterboxes right away. We must've seen a
couple hundred 3'+ chinooks and about a dozen seals looking for
dinner. Truly one of those neat adventures--children will really
get a kick out of these sights.

For best views, you can see the seals/chinook action best at the 4th
Ave bridge near downtown Olympia. Either park as you would for
the '7' Oars LB or you can park on the North side lot of Capitol
Lake and cross the street heading toward the bridge (this is where
the KFC used to be). There's a perfect little overlook there that
affords a great view of all the 'brackish' chinook activity. Then,
head South to "New Market" Falls Park (this will require some
research) and you can see the 'freshwater' action there. Oh,
there's also a free salmon cycle poster giveaway near the Falls Park-
-they may even have some salmon identification key chains for you,
too! Really interesting for the kids!!!

Chinook are running a bit early this year--so don't miss it!
