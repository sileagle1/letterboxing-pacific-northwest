is there a fudrick in the house?

From: Lisa Russell (fourkidsoneme@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-10 13:27:54 UTC-08:00

Hello- we set out last Saturday to find the Irene
Rinehardt park box in Ellensburg. I know it's cold
9and snowflakes were actually falling) but we didn't
care, we have cabin fever and just HAD to get out! if
it wasn't so cold we would have done the Canyon Road
boxes, too but we ran out of steam.

Anyhow- we didn't find it. I think the "Why's" on the
path must look totally different in the winter. We
couldn't tell what was new growth and what was old
growth. We THINK we found the "bumping head spot" on
our way back to the car (indicating we turned wrong at
a "why") but we were stumped to find two "triangles at
the 5-10 pace mark. One was lying on the ground, the
other was standing up. Both were covered in thorny
brush but we barreled through, dug through the thick
layers of fallen leaves and gave up after about 20
minutes (and we really had to go potty)
We'll try it again later in the year when things are
actually growing and alive and snow isn't coming down!

I chronicled the whole thing in pictures, visit
http://www.geocities.com/fourkidsoneme/ltrboxjan05.html
to see our day

We found it not terribly long after it was first hidden--Aug 03. I
remember finding it in a spot that was on the opposite side of the
trail than the clues suggested. Of course, at the point we saw
anything that remotely resembled what the clues said we were so
turned around that it was really tenacity that found it for us. We
saw some sort of landmark mentioned in the clues (going off of a
2+year old memory here--is it a metal pole or concrete post? did it
mention something about 'orange'?) and tried it from there. It was
on the opposite side of the trail as we expected it to be is all I
really remember. Maybe this will help?

Re: [LbPNW] is there a fudrick in the house?

From: Colleen (colleendurham@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-02-12 21:47:20 UTC-08:00
We got stumped too!! Literally!! I recognize almost every one of you pictures, because we were there on Thursday. I brought a non-letterboxing friend with me and after hiking for about 3 miles in the mud (kinda hard on her new Toyota Tundra interior) we finally gave up. For us the clues stopped making sense just after crossing the first bridge. We never did find the head bumping tree, but figured it was where the trees were sawed through to make the path passable. We also had trouble with another fudrick box--the Very Presidential Mystery Letterbox. I'm sure we found the correct location, but many sand trucks had been there scooping sand. I think it may be covered over. Darn! This was just about enough for my non letterboxing friend. I had to drag her into Schmitz Park (West Seattle) where we found Schmitz Squirrel and Schmitz Creek. I don't think she's recovered yet! Colls
