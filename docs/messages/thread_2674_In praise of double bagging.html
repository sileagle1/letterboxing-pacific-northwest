In praise of double bagging

From: Colleen (colleendurham@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-11-17 21:09:37 UTC
Seems like I'm getting over to the west side of the state more often
now that I've got a kiddo working there. I got out Thursday and found
one of daelphinus' little gems. My heart sank a little when I finally
pulled it out of it's hidie-hole--the plastic box was filled to the
top with water and a waterlogged worm was floating inside. Not only
that but the plastic baggie holding the stamp and logbook was wet
too. When I got to the bag holding the logbook, it was sealed tight
and the handmade book inside was bone dry! Thanks to The White Family
who had been there about 1 month ago, every bag was closed tightly and
the box survived.

I re-planted the plastic box upside down in hopes that it might stay
drier inside, but who knows? It was raining sideways just the day
before. There is nothing worse than a moldy, smelly logbook and it is
so easy to keep them dry.
colls

Thanks for the update. Another trick, too, is using industrial
grade zippered plastic bags--and double-bagging those, too. They
come at a hefty price (especially when I had to by them in
industrial business bulk lots just to find them) but they outperform
even the toughest ziplock freezer bags anyday. Yes, and definitely
closing the bag properly helps, too. ;) I tried to 'fix up' most of
our WA area boxes as well as possible for the long haul anticipating
the rains by changing out the logbooks to waterproof paper and the
industrial plastic bags but as time neared for our big 'unwelcome'
move out east it became obvious we weren't going to be able to
revisit each of the surviving boxes. Yes--many kudos to the White
Family for taking the time to ensure the bags were tightly sealed
and much thanks to Colls for the positive status report on a
letterbox (that about 6 months ago we came about 5 minutes away from
just retiring it to make life a bit easier on us in absentia). That
kind act is greatly appreciated and is a nice and very welcome
change to most of the status reports on our (quickly disappearing)
letterboxes we get the majority of the time!!!

Hope everybody in the PNW is drying out these days! All this
national coverage of all the rains is making us even more homesick
for the PNW region than before... :( We didn't get the massive
rains on this side of the country as you all did but we did have to
deal with tornadoes and fallen trees just about as far as your eyes
can see a few days ago. Have a wonderful Thanksgiving!
