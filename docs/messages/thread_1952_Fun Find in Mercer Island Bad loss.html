Sorry to hear about your missing compass. I, too, lost my compass
somewhere last week between Newport Beach and Big Sur, CA. :( The
last three days of our 18 day 'letterboxing' tour of CA, OR & WA was
compass-less. For an interesting tidbit: Sans compass, we actually
found a letterbox we would NOT have found had we had our compass on
us becuase we actually found another 'clueless' letterbox--which is
still clueless to this day (at least, we can't locate clues for it
still). We poked around trees in the general location of where the
clues said the letterbox we were ACTUALLY looking for was supposed
to be--then we find a letterbox thinking it was the one we were
after; opened it up, saw it had a totally different name and was
planted by a totally different group of letterboxers. So, we
stamped in and then set out hoping to find the ACTUAL letterbox for
which we had clues--within 5 minutes we found THAT box, too. We've
been trying to locate the clues to the 'clueless' one we found ever
since--but to no avail. Anybody know where we can find clues
for 'Shell' LB in Carmel, CA?

Anyways, the original Heart of Lake Washington Letterbox went
missing a couple of years ago. We planted a replacement in a new
spot--posting new clues to it in the same park. The replacement
went missing several months ago and we retired the box assuming that
the culprits who took the box HAD to have the clues to find it--
since it was hidden in a unique manner. So, the clues to the
replacement letterbox have indicated it is, indeed, missing and that
we retired the replacement letterbox ever since we confirmed it was
missing. I don't have any way to amend the original Heart of Lake
WA clues because Modern Gypsy is the one who entered those into the
dbase. Sorry about your unfortunate day. :( We spend a LOT of
time on MI (thank my work commitments for that), so we'll keep our
eyes open for your compass.

dvn2r ckr
