From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2005-04-18 04:19:54 UTC

> I'm a bit leery about it going 'public' though--especially when
> talking about specific boxes and unique circumstances in the region.

The main reason I kept the board private is to make it more difficult
for spiders to troll the site. Google can't google the pages to list
them. Spammers can't look for e-mail addresses like somebody@y...
(which we all know is short for somebody@yahoo.com) and such.

If someone really wants to read the messages here, though, there's
nothing to stop it. Anybody can sign up and be able to read the
boards--there are no restrictions on that. The first post or two
requires moderator approval, but no approval is necessary to actually
read posts, so if you're inclined to say something that you'd rather
not have some people hear, it's probably not a good idea to be posting
it here in the first place.

So, so far, two votes for not making the board public and one vote for
allowing the RSS feeds (by making the board readable by non-members).

Anyone else out there with an opinion?

-- Ryan

> So, please, by all means, open this list up to the general public.

It sounds like a couple of you might have mis-understand what I said
about the RSS feed. The first post people make would still be
moderated to elimite spam from cluttering up the lists--the only
difference is that people could now read posts without actually having
to sign up for the list. They still wouldn't be able to post without
signing up and making it through the moderating process.

E-mail addresses are "hidden" in either case, though it's often easy
to figure it out.

I'm kind of inclined to allow the RSS feeds myself because, like you,
I like to make things easier for people and realistically, it's
unlikely to cause spam and other horrible things to happen. This talk
list IS public and open to everyone for reading as it is--there's just
an extra step people have to go through (i.e. officially signing
up)--to get at it.

But it sounds like the overwhelming majority would prefer to keep
things as is, and that's okay with me too. =)

Happy trails!
