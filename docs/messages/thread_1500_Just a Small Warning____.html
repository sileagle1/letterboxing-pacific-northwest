Just a Small Warning....

From: Wood Fmily (letterboxing2004@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-06 13:03:34 UTC-07:00
Yesterday I headed out solo (without kids) to find the
Hamburger, Hamburger, Cheeseburger box in Vancouver.
This was my second attempt because the instructions
are just not clear for me. Anyway, while there I
happened upon two men...one who appeared as if he was
waiting for someone and began to approach me but then
decided against it...the other was waiting on the back
side of a fence and he was just standing there
staring. I do not know if these men were searching
for what I was searching for, but my conscience tells
me no. I've contacted the placer for verification
that it was not them.

Just a small warning....when letterboxing....go in a
group if possible, or with at least more than one
person. These two men in the vacinity of the
letterbox were enough to creep me out. I was alone
and they could have been there for ill reasons. I may
have unintentionally put myself in a situation that
could have turned out dangerous. They were there
waiting for someone and if you've found this
box....then you know why this is even creepier than
usual.

Thanks.
ISignASLily

RE: [LbPNW] Just a Small Warning....

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-08-06 13:46:40 UTC-07:00
This particular box requires thinking outside of the box, no doubt about it.
I got it, but it's a tough one. I can think of several others in Portland
that have stymied people! Of course I would never name names.. but the
placers know who they are :)

But you're absolutely right, I try to never letterbox alone, and to assess
every situation for safety before I head out. As a matter, because I often
will go down the trail and pick up a box and bring it back to a central spot
for the kids to stamp in, my husband bought us a set of walkie talkies so I
can stay in contact with someone at all times.

I can tell you that the two men you saw are NOT the placers of that box.

Stay safe and always take a friend. Yet another good reason to make
connections with other boxers, to have someone to call and say, HEY, I need
a spotter.

Maiden

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-07 16:46:14 UTC
Just a warning atop a warning, here. I work in the security
industry and I don't recommend announcing your individual
plans/whereabouts on the internet--especially if you only plan to
meet one on one with another individual you've only met via the
internet. I'd recommend making your coordinations via email with
plans to link up publicly in a very popular location like a
McDonalds, busy venue or some other place where you're guaranteed to
find other folks whom you can seek help from in the event the person
you're trying to link up with turns out to be a 'creep'. You can
just never be too cautious these days,

dvn2r ckr
