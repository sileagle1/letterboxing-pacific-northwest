North Head and Cape Dissapointment

From: wingnut_lb (wingnut_lb@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-08-24 15:24:03 UTC
I just have to say, this has become my all time favorite letterbox to
date. We had the most incredible day. My dh was on a fishing
charter out of Ilwaco, so dd and I came along and boxed that day.
(Sat)

Well, we headed off to do the lighthouses, we got to the top where
North Head lies and ran into two doe and 4 fawns, 2 were yearlings
and 2 were just beginning to loose their spots. They stayed frozen
in the grass as we approached, and the littlest were bedded right in
the spot the box was, so we went a bit further and allowed them to
slip away before returning. Well, dd and I sat down to log in and I
feel somthing right off to my right shoulder. Here's a fawn, just
peeking in to check us out! We sat real still and soon the whole
herd was grazing amoung us. It was the coolest thing! I was very
aware that these are WILD animals.... and kept my eyes peeled and dd
close, but they seemed to do their thing and we did ours. After
about an hour, I finally stood up and headed down the hill, the deer
just stayed there and watched us go! It was so cool!

The veiws were spectacular, and the day was lovely, and the stamps
are great! The boxes are in great shape with relatively few finds
considering they are from 2001!

Thanks for the opportunity for this experience Paisley Orca! I got
fantastic photos! And memories to last a lifetime! Wingnut
