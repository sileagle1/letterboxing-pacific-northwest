Priest Point Park "Revegetation"

From: Clio Mouse (cliomouse@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-10-08 10:39:16 UTC-07:00
I have been told by a fellow letterboxer that the east side of Priest Point Park is undergoing "revegetation." I'm not sure what all this entails, but the group that went letterboxing this weekend has explained that only two series on that side are available at the moment. Mostly at risk are by ginormous Kirby series, but also some of the Centennial series and part of the Nail family's. Perhaps some of the Flowers are in the "revegetation" area, but I don't know. Unfortunately, I can't find specific details online.

Just thought I'd give people a heads up, in case your boxes are in the area and/or if you'd planned to go find some there.

Happy 'boxing!

Re: Priest Point Park "Revegetation"

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-10-11 21:36:49 UTC
Yes, there were signs that they were already removing ivy from the area
back when we visited the park several months ago. In fact, that seems
to be the case for most of the Pacific Northwest--removing ivy is a hot
activity these days as they're desperately trying to save the trees--
the ivy strangles the trees. Along the same lines, I'd highly
discourage folks from ever using ivy as a natural camouflage for your
letterboxes out there as it's likely not going to stay there for long.
If you're planting in an ivy-infested area, perhaps take the time to
remove the ivy from around your hidey-hole--it may increase the
lifespan of your letterbox. We've run into ivy-removing teams in parks
ranging from Tryon Creek SP near Portland (not that there had ever been
any letterboxes there) to Tumwater Falls near Olympia, to Seward Park
near Seattle, Discovery Park, ALL of Mercer Island is scheduled for ivy
removal over the next few years (it was up for a park levy vote--the
debate over whether volunteers would do it or if they'd just hire
companies to do it) and many other regional parks. Good thing to keep
in the back of your mind when you're planting new boxes in the region...

daelphinus
