P.S. Oh, one more thing... Squeamish types, stop reading now.
Along the same lines, for anybody actually reading our blog from
these past few months ( www.dvn2rckr.blogspot.com ) trkr actually
pushed the 'etiquette' button on the little grey box mounted on the
toilet stall walls adjacent the TP (if they even had TP mounts)
earlier today. We weren't quite sure what the 'etiquette' box would
do--it had a red light on it so we wondered if it was an alert for
EMS assistance and we weren't too willing to test it out for fear
the 'authorities' might really show up! Well, he pushed it earlier
today in Seoul and it played a recording of flushing toilets. We
weren't sure if it was to help 'encourage' folks to go (nothing like
the sound of rushing water to 'start' the process) or if its purpose
was to drown out other sounds that likely would occur in water
closets...

Traveling through foreign countries always seems to introduce you to
some interesting things along the way...

The oddest thing was, though, I lived in Korea for 16 months several
years ago and NEVER saw an 'etiquette box' before. This is
something new--even though the boxes look like 1955 technology!
Maybe we'll see them in the US someday soon... ;) Now, I'm back to
aVOIDing the subject of letterboxing.
