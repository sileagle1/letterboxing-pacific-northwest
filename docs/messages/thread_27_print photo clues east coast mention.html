Believe it or not, those stamps are better than some that were going on in
a certain unspeakable east coast former English colony at the time. There
was a school of thought in those days that the place and the clues are all
that mattered, and the stamp was a necessary evil. With the advent of
geocaching shortly thereafter, that school was annihilated and the stamp
necessarily came to prominence in letterboxing ...

... leaving those of us remaining from that school to have to wade thru a
lot of butt ugly clues :-)

(of course, those clues are butt ugly also, but that is a different kettle

upgrading all the old ones ... so you have a piece of letterboxing history ...)
