Shakespeare--found on his birthday!

From: Jess (jmwestin@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-04-26 21:42:30 UTC
On Wednesday, the drama teacher at the high school where I am
student teaching and I took 12 kids down to Ashland to see three
plays (all of which were excellent!!!) Before leaving for the trip,
I made sure to print up the Shakespeare LB hints, however, I debated
printing up any other hints, thinking, "I have 12 kids to watch,
I'll never have time to hunt for boxes." The box that I especially
wanted to find was the Applegate Rest Area box. Of course, I was
thinking, "There's no way the bus will be pulled over just so that I
can find a box." So then you can imagine how INCREDIBLY
PSYCHOTICALLY ANGRY I was with myself when everyone on the bus
needed to go to the bathroom when we got to the Applegate Rest
Area!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

I walked around, looking for any places that looked "letterbox-
friendly," however, without the clues, I was S.O.L!

Anyway, that night at dinner, I explained to the drama teacher, her
friend and 2 of the kids why I had been prowling around random trees
at the rest stop and told them all about letterboxing.
Enthusiastic, they all decided that they wanted to go find the
Shakespeare box with me, so we set out for adventure. It took a bit
longer to find than we had expected, so by the time we got back to
the bus to meet the other kids, we were 10 minutes late. :) The
chaperones were the late ones. Nice. :)

Although I never found the Applegate LB, one good thing sprouted
from this trip (aside from finding the Shakespeare box ON
SHAKESPEARE'S SUPPOSED BIRTHDAY)--once we found the box, one of the
other chaperones was so excited about letterboxing that she went and
bought a notebook and an eraser and carved her own stamp that very
night so that she could go back to next day to put her own mark in
the book! Yay!!! A newbie!

--Jess
