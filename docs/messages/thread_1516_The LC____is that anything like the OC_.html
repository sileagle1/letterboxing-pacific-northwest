From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-08-11 17:07:24 UTC-07:00
I dunno I personally think the whole thing got blown into dynamic
proportions as is very common in our hobby and is a great example that we
all need to practice some basic forms of communication.

Having taken several psychology courses I can guarantee that one of the
biggest errors we all make when reading emails is that we assume we know
what the other person meant half the time. Something we might all want to
think about doing the next time we read something that gets our feathers
ruffled is to take the time to ask the writer if our interpretation of their
words is correct. One of the tremendous downfalls of communicating in an
internet medium is the lack of tone, gesture and body language which all add
to our ability to comprehend the context in which something is said.

As for last wills and testaments, I apologize to those I may offend with
this -- but I think it is going quite a bit overboard. I can honestly say
that while I would want my boxes retired as well, should I ever leave the
world of letterboxing be that of my own choice or my untimely departure, the
last thing I'm concerned with is what the rest of the world does with my
letterboxes. I think when we start writing last wills and testaments and
leaving do not resuscitate orders on our boxes we need to ask ourselves if
this has gone beyond a fun hobby, a enjoyable sport and became too much of a
life altering event.

Just my two cents -- which only amounts to two pennies,
Pandora
