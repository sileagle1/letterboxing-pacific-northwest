From: IronBear (ironbear@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-10-25 15:36:26 UTC-07:00

"To laugh often and love much; to win the respect of intelligent persons and
the affection of children, to earn the approbation of honest critics; to
appreciate beauty; to give of one's self, to leave the world a bit better,
whether by a healthy child, a garden patch or a redeemed social condition;
to have played and laughed with enthusiasm and sung with exultation; to know
even one life has breathed easier because you have lived--that is to have
succeeded." - Ralph Waldo Emerson

Ralph Waldo Emerson was a man that truly understood some of the
infinitesimal and yet paramount things in life - to bad he didn't know about
Letterboxing because I believe that his words that you just read would have
been a little different. Nevertheless - this weekend proved to be a time
that Ralph Waldo Emerson was talking about, without question.

Many thanks have already gone out to the Letterboxing community for the
Family Fun 2 gathering - and my expressions are not going to be any
different from everyone else that has commented. I would like to thank each
and every one of you for coming to this tremendous gathering. I will not
mention people by name - because the reality is that each of you stood out
to me in your own unique and individual way. So I thank you for making my
experience a memorable one. You are the living and breathing essence of what
Letterboxing is - I had the honor of meeting the vast majority of you out on
the trail somewhere during the day. Because for a good portion of the event
Der Mad Stamper and I were the flashing blur that you would see dashing
through the forest. But I think that is one of the most prolific ways to
meet a fellow Letterboxer - to see their eyes light up - or just stepping
out of the shadows after re-hiding a box - despite the cold rain and hale.

Furthermore, it was as Mr. Emerson said - "to have played and laughed with
enthusiasm and sung with exultation; to know even one life has breathed
easier because you have lived--that is to have succeeded." The smiling faces
- the enthusiasm that I saw despite the cold rain and hale - gave me pause
and made my being at this event all worth it. As the quote states you made
my life breath and bit easier. I stood back with ardent pride as I saw all
these wonderful people talk and exchange stamps - stay warm by the fire -
laugh with old friends and shake hands to greet new ones. I was exuberant
when I saw the reaction to the cooties by the kids and the adults - who I
think couldn't help but be a kid as well from time to time. The food - what
can I say - it was the best - and it was the little additions of things like
the familiar welcome sign - gifts bags - and event stamp that made it even
more special.

The park is an amazingly beautiful one - but this weekend it became even
more so with the addition of some of the most wonderful Letterboxes I have
been able to hunt down. I could see that the people in our community had
given of one's self - and left this world a little better with a few tokens
of themselves - a box - logbook - stamp- and clues. Der Mad Stamper and I
weren't able to get all of the boxes that were planted for the event. We
missed one - sorry Heather just ran out of daylight. In addition to that we
got almost everything that was already there but ended up not hunting for a
few I think four actually. All of them were treasures to behold - and I am
honored to of had the chance to get them. I will certainly be returning to
get the rest soon.

Last but not least - I have a heartfelt apology to make. I had gone out
before the event and planted four boxes for you all to enjoy several days
before. When pandora and I returned home - pandora proceeded to sit down and
write the clues from my terrible writing skills for you to enjoy. When she
read it back to me - what she read was not what I heard obviously because
there were several places that the clues were wrong. So terribly wrong that
there was no way anyone would be able to continue to hunt for this series
and complete it. Unless of course you had just happened to see Der Mad
Stamper and I take off in the right direction and go along with us for the
series - such as Heather did. So with that said you all have my most sincere
apologies - it has taught me a valuable lesson and will hopefully not happen
again. I have corrected the errors in the clues - and if anyone would like a
copy right away please just let me know in a private e-mail and I will send
them out to you as soon as I can. Otherwise the new clues will be posted to
the respective websites later on.

So thank you all once again for showing up - laughing - playing - loving -
respecting - appreciating beauty - giving of yourself - and making this
world a better place.

I bid you all well.

Iron`Bear
