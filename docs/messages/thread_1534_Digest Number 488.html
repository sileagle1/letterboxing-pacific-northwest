Hi there,
I live in Corvallis, which is right next( about 20 mi) to Lebannon. If
you're coming from Salem(I5) you will want to take the last Albany exit
(past the airport) and take a left that is US-20. Anyway, there aren't many
LB's in the corvallis Albany Area, I hope to remedy that... :-) they are
mainly in the Corvallis area. The LB's at EE. Wilson are fun an easy to
get to. There's only one now at Walnut park but you have to wander for a
while. the one at Adair Village is also easy to find, but watch out for
Wasp nests. If you have any questions let me know... I'm pretty familliar
with the area.
Good Luck!
Scottish Amy

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-08-17 21:17:08 UTC-07:00
There are numerous boxes in the Corvallis/Philomath area. Mostly hidden by
just two people. Myself and Rat Pac. We have done our best to improve the
boxing in the area. The Peavy Arboretum boxes are awesome. I just picked
those up this weekend. There are actually 7 at Adair Village. The single box
I haven't gotten yet but the series is wonderful and the walk very nice.
(Read long) There used to be two at Walnut Park, but mine there came up
missing. I have yet to replace it. There are also three in the Philomath
City Park.

And of course we'd love to invite everyone from the South Valley to come up
for the Passport to Letterboxing stamp camp August 28th. Saturday is going
to be a full day of fun classes, exchanges, boxes, and raffles! Come and
join the fun.

Maiden
