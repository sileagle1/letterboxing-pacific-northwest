From: Mark (nomad_pnw@hotmail.com) | Date: 2003-12-08 22:51:48 UTC
Melanie,

I use PZKut and prefer it over most other carving materials. It's
especially good to hold fine cuts in detailed carvings, like scrolls,
etc...

You are right that it is pretty thin and can be hard to stamp with,
so I back out stamps with wood. Scrap wood can be found almost
anywhere, and you can even use wood from throw away pallets found
trashed behind many stores.

Mark

Re: PZ Kut

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-12-09 00:32:57 UTC
For choice of medium suggestions, I suggest trying everything and
then stick to what you like best. =) I haven't used any PZKut
myself, although I think I should give it a try. I've seen some
pretty fantastic stamps carved with the stuff.

I like the pink stuff over the Mastercarve just because it tends to
be cheaper. (That extra thickness adds cost!) I'm--well--frugal.
If money were no object, though, I'd probably use the Mastercarve
more often. I love how it smells, and I like the extra heft where I
can carve both sides and it's easy to stamp even without a backing.
But that's just my take. Of course, some people--unlike myself--
actually earn a living and money may not be as an important factor as
for myself. ;o)

But I digress.... try them all, then stick to with what you like
best. =)

-- Ryan
