Re: New boxes in Spokane

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-09 00:14:08 UTC

Stage 3 = that anxiety attack that comes with your first "I think it
may be missing report"

Stage 4 = that panic that propels you to create a replacement
letterbox before you leave your home so when you check on it and it
is indeed missing you'll be all ready to replant it without more
than a day's gap in 'coverage'--then, you show up to the hiding
spot, probe for your box and find it there alive & well, whew! just
another false missing report. After this happens a good 3 or 4
times, then you start doubting every SINGLE missing report you get.
Eventually, you get real prepared for the subsequent follow-up
email "Please don't hate me but upon reattempting this box armed
with a compass/brain/explanation of the difference between right &
left, fir & pine, mushroom and toadstool--I actually found the box,
alive & well, right where the clues said it should be!!!" ;)

Then, you transition to Stage 5...assembling that ever available
letterbox maintenance/first aid kit for your car and the additional
mini-kit you carry with you along the trail (complete with extra
ziploc freezer baggies/jewelry sized ziploc baggies, pens,
hitchhiker letterbox info label and additional watertight container
just in case the one you visit is damaged). This ultimately evolves
into your 'create a letterbox on a whim' kit for your car--so
whenever the muse is right and creativity abounds, you quickly
assemble a letterbox and plant it whereever you are at THAT VERY
MOMENT--even knowing you're sure to NEVER return to that spot on the
Earth ever again!!!

Then, Stage 6.5 manifests itself... ;)
