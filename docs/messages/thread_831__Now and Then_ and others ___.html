"Now and Then" and others ...

From: womanonsafari (laurajw@concentric.net) | Date: 2003-10-20 00:23:48 UTC
We went out yesterday for a gorgeous hike in Seward Park. Brought my
letterboxing stuff with me and went a-lookin' for Now & Then. What an
awesome series - I've never really walked throughout the park before.
Such gorgeous trees. Both boxes were happy and dry. "Now" even had a
suspicious little salamander on it, wondering WHAT we were doing
there - it had been over 2 months since a visitor had been by.

I'm SO glad I brought markers with me. I'm getting into using
markers, especially after my stamping friend told me about the
breathing on the stamp thing. I was always hurrying to try and color
it in before all the ink dried and she (so wise!) recommended
breathing on it to moisten it before stamping in! sure enough! soo
cool! makes so much sense!

I forgot to report that on October 3rd the "Frog Rock" box was all
happy. Again a great stamp for markers. Such a cute stamp.

The Ballard Locks box placed by Wives and Daughters was a challenging
one to get to with all of the tourists running around on a sunny
Saturday (sept 27). Fortunately, there was a guy who had his whole
bottle-top collection on display near us and was taking photos of
them with the different things so no one really wanted to look in our
direction. :)

We also logged in at "After the Story's End" by Team Garlic. Nory and
I have been looking at that one for months! Maybe ever since we first
learned about letterboxing. Go do it! Such fun!

We tried to go after another one of the Tracy Outlaw series but he
got away again. WAY too many people and dogs at Golden Gardens to get
near that one. We'll have to try again when the weather's not so nice!

Woohoo! Can't wait for a winter full of letterboxing distractions!

- starfish
