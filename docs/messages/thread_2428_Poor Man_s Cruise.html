Watch out for the commercial cruises. Some are OK, but nearly all charge
hidden port taxes, and lots of them charge you for the "excursions" every
time you set foot off the boat. I worked for an up-scale eco-cruise line,
and we heard stories about folks who went with such and such a cruise line
because it was so much cheaper. But by the time they paid for everything
once they got there, it was very close to the same price as our
all-inclusive price.

Also, and I know I could be biased here, but if you were to take a
commercial cruise to Alaska, of all places, a large cruise ship, IMHO, is
not the way to do it. I always thought the reason you go to Alaska is to,
well, see Alaska. But I can remember traveling in Glacier Bay or Tracy Arm.
We would stop the boat anytime we saw bears, whales, otters, moose on shore,
anything. We would send passengers out on zodiac boats to hike on the
bear-free islands. We would pass these huge ocean liners and see absolutely
nobody on deck because they were all inside gambling, eating, and playing
shuffleboard. The rangers in Glacier Bay used to be amazed that folks would
come to Glacier Bay, only to watch the world go by on a closed circuit
television while they walked on the treadmill.

So, if you don't take the ferry, and you consider a cruise line, consider
one of the smaller ones with smaller boats, say 50 - 70 guests instead of
2000. They may appear more expensive on the surface, but there's the hidden
charges on the large lines. And the small ones are, well, smaller. They
can fit into delicate little places the behemoths simply cannot go. They
carry a full staff of naturalists on board to help interpret the experience.
You will learn and experience amazing things.

My two cents.
