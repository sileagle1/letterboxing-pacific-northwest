Re: Trip to Seattle

From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-27 22:57:13 UTC
Just a reminder that the Kubota box is missing. It was a really great
one, but the Japanese Gardens are very well manicured and I do not
plan to replant that letterbox.

Depending on what part of town you are staying in...on the Eastside,
there is the whole Issaquah/Cougar Mountain area -- they are all great
stamps and it is a neat place to hike. In Bellevue, anything planted
by Green Guillemot. In the Northeast, the Redmond Elementals and WSP
are terrific (anything by Mischief is terrific.) West Seattle has
several great boxes along the Alki waterfront which is a lovely walk
(Constellation, Cormorant Cove, Weatherwatch, Birthplace of Seattle
and the Lincoln Park and Schmitz Park boxes etc.) Around SeaTac are
Turkey Dinner, the Flight Path series, and Halitaeetus leucocephalus...

--Amanda
