Just a quick note about a recent LB maintenance gig we undertook.

This past weekend we were able to check on a handful of LBs that we
pretty much thought were non-visited since we hadn't heard a peep
from folks who'd visited them since we'd placed most of them (minus
two emails from one dear soul who grew up in the region where a
couple of the boxes were hidden).

The following boxes were alive & well with many visitors:

Anchor's Away
Broken Dreams
Sea Otter
Tluaniuq l*ake
Whale of a tree

We also visited a few other new plants in the area:

Riches at Rubeeeeeeeee Bch
Elma Slug

The rich letterbox was in dire shape as the last visitors failed to
close it properly and it got completely waterlogged. We tried to
revive it as best we could but didn't have the opportunity to stay
there long enough to let the logbook dry completely. Next visitors
please let it air dry some more. It should do alright as long as it
gets a chance to recover properly. The stamp was awesome and the
venue was one of the most beautiful we'd ever been to. Definitely
worth the trip!!!

Elma Slug was doing well and a great box sanctioned by the venue!

On a side note, several of the boxes we placed were left with
plastic bags that were completely open! Even double bagged but both
bags weren't closed at all!!! The logbooks have no chance in that
environment without protection since it's the rainiest part of the
entire country. Frustrating... Somebody even took a pen from one of
them? Really frustrating because these boxes are almost orphans so
maintenance occurs in very long cycles.

Another odd thing is that we really visited one of the boxes simply
because we hadn't heard from it and we knew we left a popular HH at
it this time last year. We were stunned to visit the box, noticed
at least 15 folks had visited, yet the HH was still sitting right on
top of the LB. None of the visitors had even stamped in to the
HH!!! Odd. Needless to say, we moved the HH on since it seemed to
be caught in a maelstrom of immobility. Now, it should see the
light of day again and will perhaps float to a sunnier place in the
country.

For folks who yearn for a neat vacation to beautiful sites away from
traffic & people, this trek should definitely be on your list. We
essentially had the region to ourselves this trip. Could it have
been due to outrageous gas prices??? The peacefulness of the trip
was definitely worth the extra cost of the gas. ;)

See you on the trails and possibly in Des Moines this weekend,
