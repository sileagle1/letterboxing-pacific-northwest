Savannah Series at Bastyr (CHANGES)

From: Twilight (twilight2001@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-08-26 07:45:27 UTC-07:00
This set was just posted on August 12th -- and we've moved some of the
boxes to better spots already (we wanted to avoid troubling the "team
building" folks, so moved many out of their groves to just outside so
you could find them even if "team building" sessions were still on) --
and NEW directions were updated YESTERDAY on August 25th.

Also: The first several pages of each logbook have a lot of kids in
them (thumbprints and signatures) -- but that's by design. These were
placed initially for the YCamp class on Letterboxing we held last week
for the tweeners (10-12's) -- so the kids were there at our behest and
with our assistance.

NOTE: The Impala (box #3) box is missing right now -- not the kids, it
was there after the last one got their stamp -- not sure who or when --
but that will be replaced this week sometime. If you hike it before we
get there with the replacement stamp, you'll still need to "walk the
route" to #3 as all the boxes are contiguous. But that's a short detour
-- and if we're fast enough, the Impala will be back back munching on
her Salal before you get there anyway :)

Thanks to Ravenmom who alerted usthat something was wrong (like the old
directions were still up making it look like over 1/2 the boxes were
missing! ACK!!!)

Enjoy!

Twilight & Sunshine

From: ccrwalsh (cwalsh@nwlink.com) | Date: 2003-10-02 23:52:17 UTC
Hi Twilight-

Feeling motivated today, so I packed up Ducky and Acorn to have a
look-see for these boxes. And the day just got stranger:

Box #1 Didn't seem to be there (yes, we're working with the updated
directions). We figured we'd double back and look harder once we knew
better what we were looking for.

Box #2 Found the Mammoth right where he wanted to be, stamped in and
tucked him away, covering him a bit better (the lid was quite
visible).

Box #3 On the way to the tree, we saw a LB lying out in the open by
the path, a meter before the tree. Hmm, yup, the Impala was in it.
And no stamp from Bethany--we figured we had found the lost box. But
wait, the log book says it's a lion. Curious.

Acorn has a look around the base of the tree itself, and finds a
cleaner box with--an Impala in it. A replacement? Curiouser.

Now, this is where we scratch our heads: We can't put the old box out
in the open again, we debate about taking one home and sending it to
you, but we can't decide. So we put *both* Imapalas in the well-
hidden spot. We figure we'd let you know, in case you wanted to round
one up again. (Not sure what to make of the Lion stamp in one log
book...)

(BTW, a fellow out walking his dog wanted to know if we were
Geocaching--hard to hide the compass when you need it every few
steps...)

#4 Could not find this one. Smiled at the smiling log, looked high
and low, nothing. Except piles of leaves, etc.--looks like things had
been cleaned up recently (or since late August, when the last person
stamped in) and the debris dumped behind this log.

#5 Right where it should be, well-hidden, and we replaced it that way.

On our way to #6, Ducky went splat, and the ensuing meltdown ended
our trek for the day. We'll continue another day!

So we found two of the supposedly missing one, and didn't find the
ones that were there. Curiouser and curiouser...

Thank you for the lovely series! Can't wait to complete it.

Celt, Acorn & Ducky (Ravensburger had to work...)

From: womanonsafari (laurajw@concentric.net) | Date: 2003-10-12 17:35:53 UTC
I went out with a friend and his 2 girls and we had a blast! it was
perfect as there was no one out (at least initially) so the kids
could be VERY obvious about what we were doing. Later there was
another family that was letterboxing as well and they were right on
our tails! First time that's happened to me. Anyway!...

We found all of the boxes were safe and sound. The impala box (#3)
sure enough had 2 boxes hidden. VERY confusing. (i didn't read any of
these posts until now. i wish i had!) One had a blue impala stamp
with the yellow lion logbook ( the original logbook). The other had a
black impala with the blue impala logbook. So since we weren't sure
if the lion stamp was going to be missing a logbook, we took it with
us. We swapped the blue impala stamp and put it back with the logbook
and kept the black impala with the original lion log. Phew! When we
got to the lion, we decided to leave that box be and take the poor
leftovers home with us. I was afraid they might go missing otherwise
or confuse folks. Please send your address my way and I'll send them
to you. We didn't know what else to do but that's what we did.

Otherwise, we found that the clues for #4 and #5 were confusing. It
seems that they both have a few sentences left over from when they
were inside the groves vs. now outside the groves. Still found 'em
though!!! :)

Such a terrific series and wow! what fun to do with the little ones!
The stamps are gorgeous and fun especially for me, the animal lover!
Woohoo! Thanks!

- laura/starfish
