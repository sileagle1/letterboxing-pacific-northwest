> I have never heard about southern hemisphere compasses. Sounds like
> an urban legend, but a cool one none the less!

Actually, there is, alledgedly, a certain degree of truth to this. The
magnetic flux lines around the earth aren't parallel to the ground
anywhere except the equator. Around the rest of the world, they're
angled to the ground and therefore it pulls the needle point into the
ground.

In the northern hemisphere, the lines of flux pull the north needle
downward while in the southern hemisphere, the lines of flux pull the
south needle downward. So compasses in the northern hemishere have a
counterweight on the south needle to keep the needle level and vice
versa for the southern hemisphere.

It's not really the location of where the compass was made that
matters, but what side of the compass the counterweight is on. You'd
be hard-pressed to find a southern-hemisphere compass in the northern
hemisphere and vice-versa but, allegedly, they really do exist!

I've never taken my compass to the southern hemisphere to see what
happens, but perhaps the next time I visit, I will....
