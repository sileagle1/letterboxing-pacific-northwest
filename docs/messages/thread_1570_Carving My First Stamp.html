Carving My First Stamp

From: Suzanne (quillow@inkandfeather.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 17:35:33 UTC
I have finally decided on a series of stamps and using the
instructions on Brett & Penny's Letterboxing website for using Adobe
Photoshop to create the image (awesome details!) I have it printed
out and ready to transfer onto Speedball SpeedyStamp. How do I do
that? I'm using an inkjet - will the acetone (nail polish
remover) deal work or do you all use something else as a transfer
medium?

Thanks for your help!

Suz


Re: Carving My First Stamp

From: bcostley (bobbyeubanks@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 17:45:46 UTC
Acetone will most likely not work with inkjet. With ink jet you are
pretty much limited to tracing with graphite and transfering it that
way. I suggest printing on a laser printer or copying with a copier,
then using acetone or iron. I now prefer using the iron instead of
acetone.

From: pandora{I`B} (hispandora@phlsystems.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 10:52:32 UTC-07:00
I'd agree with Brett on this one -- I have always found the heat transfer is
more likely to stay on the material while carving, like when your hand rubs
over it or something, and is much more user friendly than other methods.
Personally I almost always transfer images by having them drawn or tracing
them with a #2 pencil and then turning the paper over so the side drawn on
is facing down and rubbing the impression of the image onto the material. I
like this method for no other reason than if I want to change something on
the stamp I can get rid of -- erase per say, the original image and re-draw
it, doesn't work for everyone, that is just my selected method.

Pandora
