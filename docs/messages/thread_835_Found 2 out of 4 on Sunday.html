Found 2 out of 4 on Sunday

From: moores_olympia (moores_olympia@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-10-20 16:13:05 UTC
We went out on Sunday looking for 4 different letterboxes around the
Olympia area. Our oldest son who is almost 16 decided he would join
in to see what this was about.
The first one we went to was Yashiro Pagoda Letterbox but was not
able to locate it. Not sure if we just were not looking in the right
place or it was gone.
The second one was Hedwig's Journal. This was a nice journey around
the capitol campus a little. Clues were easy to follow and found the
letterbox in good shape.
The third one was World's Most Boring Letterbox in Lacey. At first
we had a little trouble finding anything. The kids just kept saying
the only thing they could find was trash. They didn't think of that
the "trash" was a container for the letterbox. It was a fun surprise
for them.
Then the last one was out at Lake St. Clair. I can't find where I
printed out the clues for the exact name of the letterbox. But we
were not able to find this one. We found where it should have been
but nothing was under the flat rock. We thought we might go out to
this one again when the sun isn't going down and maybe we just
couldn't see it.
Hope everyone had a great weekend.

The Moore Family

I am so sorry to say that the Chuck's Tacklebox boxes at Lk St Clair
have been pulled. I intend to add them and 3 others that are still
at a couple of other lakes in Pierce County to the 7 that currently
reside at Miller's Sylvania, a park run by the State (notice the
creative and indirect reference to the park so as not to draw a
ranger's attention on a random web search). This series will have 12
total boxes within that park. Since no one had even attempted to
find the St Clair boxes to my knowledge, we decided to pull them and
bring them together with the rest of the series.

I am glad that you are enjoying the local boxes though. I would
encourage you to seek out the boxes at another state run park called
Tol mie. These are especially fun this time of year as the trees are
turning and the rains return. Also, check out the boxes on the
Chehalis Western Trail and Woodard Bay Nature Trail. Olympia and
surrounding area has lots of great boxing opportunities. I hope that
you and your family will continue in the fun.

Nailhead of the Nail Family
