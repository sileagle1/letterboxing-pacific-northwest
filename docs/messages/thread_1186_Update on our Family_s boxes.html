Update on our Family's boxes

From: Nail Family (the7nails@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-08 04:02:03 UTC
The Nail Family recently forked over $50 for a parking pass for this
states wonderful nature places for the year. This gave opportunity
to visit a couple of favorite spots near Olympia.

Nailhead and Nailmom did some box checking today and we are glad to
report that the Huckleberry Box at Tol * Mie park is indeed, intact
and awaiting visitors. While on the path we checked on Fudrick's Mie
box, in fine shape and still well hidden. Side note to dvn2rckr-
there has been much trail maintenance/boardwalk construction at the
park and you may want to check on your boxes hidden there to make
sure the clues are correct. We went on the spur of moment and
didn't take a clue sheet along with us and didnt remember the
location of your boxes either due to the changes on the trail.

Yesterday, Nailhead did some checking on boxes at the Millers'
Sylvan*a. The water in the lake is high and flooding into the rest
of the park in places making the trails especially muddy right now.
Chuck's Tacklebox Series is in pretty good shape with a good number
of visitors but one box is confirmed missing. This particular box
is in a flooded area and our guess is that it floated away with the
high water. Not sure if we will recarve it or just replace it with
one of the 5 other boxes in the series yet to be located there. The
latest visitor to the Chuck's Tacklebox Series, last fall, was a
33year letterboxing veteran from West Sussex, England! What an
honor to have such a veteran boxer visit some of our boxes. Wow,
what stories she could tell us about bonus boxes, coockoo clues,
mystery clues and the like! Can you imagine letterboxing for 33
years!

Hope everyone gets to get out to see these fine boxes soon.

Nailhead of the Nail Family
