PLB - Stamping In

From: sandymess (sandymess@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-02 21:56:06 UTC

My 2 cents. I think that if you don't hold up the box what difference
does it make how many sign in??? If you start a box and don't extras
signing in then state that in the log book or somewhere.
I think that one of the purposes of the PLB is to be able to see
other signature stamps from all over. So the more the merrier, as
long as your are not holding the box for a week so someone can stamp
in. Purple Iris

I personally feel it's up to the person sending out the box. I've been known
to contact people that mailed out the box and ask them if it was OK.. but I
feel it's common courtesy to ask. The person may not have accounted for
extra stamps when putting their log book together.

Maiden

Re: PLB - Stamping In

From: Smiley (dwharris@ipns.com) | Date: 2004-07-02 22:15:36 UTC
I agree that it's polite to ask, as I did with Pandora when I
recieved her Pandoormat box. It was the first PLB I had recieved and
I wasn't sure of the protocol. She was fine with it as long as I
wasn't holding up sending the box out. I let the kids stamp in
the night I got it and got it to two friends the next day and then
sent it off. If I sent one out I would love for as many people to
sign it as wanted to. Like Purple Iris said, it's nice to see as many
stamps as are out there...so unless the sender only had enough pages
for the number of people signed up then I would think that it's OK,
unless otherwise stated by the sender. If in doubt then contact them.
It's also nice if you have a close group, that a number of people can
sing in without spending so much on postage. Especially those that
are single mothers of 3. :)

Thanks for listening....

"Smiley"
