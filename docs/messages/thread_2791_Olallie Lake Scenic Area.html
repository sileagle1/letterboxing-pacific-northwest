Olallie Lake Scenic Area

From: doubletreepfx (greenthummm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-31 03:30:45 UTC
For those who might be interested, there is still some snow on the
road to Olallie Lake, but with this week's temperatures, the road
should be clear soon. The road is open to Lower Lake, (which is after
Fish Lake but before Olallie Lake/Monon Lake/Horseshoe Lake) but the
trails in the area are quite snowy still. Snowy but hikable if you
are willing to brave them. Another week or 2 and the trails should be
completely clear and mosquito-ridden everywhere.....

The trilliums, mountain violets, skunk cabbage and numerous other
flowers are in bloom at Fish Lake right now, if you enjoy seeing
them. The fishing is good, despite those darn sinking bobbers, they
just wouldn't stay afloat for very long...... And lots of letterboxes
in the area, many waiting for first-finders still. I've lost count
how many boxes there are in the area, many besides mine. Take a few
days vacation this summer and camp in the area, and I promise you'll
never find them them all!

Double Tree

Oh, this all takes me down memory lane. My hubby and I, along with
another couple and their Irish wolfhound would camp at the cabins at
Olallie Lake over Memorial Day weekend.

This was always a backpacking trip on snowshoes. And it was always a
bit of a crap-shoot because we never knew just how far we would need
to hike. It all depended on where the snow made the roads impassable.
When we hit the snow we would just pull over, gear up and start
hiking. Sometimes it was three miles and sometimes it was ten. And
the dog would always step on my hubby's snowshoe, and down Tom would
go with his full pack. It happened so often that we couldn't help but
laugh.

The area was closed to the public, we had the keys to the cabin and
the entire place to ourselves. Beauty!
Marmalade

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-09-12 16:14:15 UTC
When I was up there I asked the same question. It seems they had water
quality problems at a couple of other Cascade Lakes and the county
decided to shut down the water at Olallie too even though their weekly
water quality tests showed nothing wrong. The folks who run the place
supply water safe for drinking and another container for washing. You
get a fresh supply everyday. The wash water is the same water that
everyone there has been drinking for years.

The lack of shower is annoying, especially for those PCT thru-hikers
who have been looking forward to a warm bath. We have a solar shower
that works great for camping. As long as the sun is out and you shower
in the evening when the water is still warm, it's a great thing to
have. Don't let the water situation stop you from visiting one of our
most wonderful spots. Funhog
