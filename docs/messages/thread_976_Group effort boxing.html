From: hoopoe4me (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2003-12-01 00:33:31 UTC
We waited but not overlong. I suspect the average LB'r is a punctual
type person 8) Sorry the kids are in cranky mode. Right now with so
many kids coming down with the flu it is a bit scary.

The RayvenClan and Rayvendingledogs joined me. The hike was OK for
me, but RayvenMom, still recovering from surgery had a bit of a tough
go at the first stiff climb as the adrenaline kicked in and kicked
her but good. She was the lead spotter and spotted the clue catchers.

The clues were well written but the venue holds many challenges. In
a forest filled with moss covered trees and olde stumps finding the
right one is problematic until you come upon The Right One! There
are always lessons to be learned.

We found two of three, totally missing any referenced stump log or
stream for box three.

I am a bit perplexed by the presence of Euros in these boxes.???
Anyone know how this is significant?

The boxes could use some new baggies, none of use were carrying any.

I left the RayvenClan with well worn out RayvenDogs to return to the
barn to shave Jay the Wonder Pony for the second time this Fall.
They were planning to tackle some of the Tiger Mountain boxes. I
suggested the coal mine associated boxes as the elevation gain and
distance should be doable for RayvenMom.

I got a sneak peak at the Black Pearl Treasure that will soon be
winging its way around the country. I am eager for this series to
start here. It should be mighty fun.

Cheers to all, rumor has it the sun was out today. We were fairly
fog bound.

Hoopoe
Redmond WA
