Re: letterboxes at the Oregon Country Fair?

From: heronafoot (heronafoot@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-18 19:35:44 UTC
Becca - It is funny that you thought of this. I am in the process of
carving a stamp of the Oregon Country Fair logo and was going to plant
it as a limit time letterbox for the month of July. I know several
people who work the fair every year and will ask them their thoughts
on putting something inside but for mine I think I will find a place
outside so it can be left for the entire month. I live on the coast
and will be going there next week (monthly shopping at Costco ect.)
and plan to scout out the area for an appropriate spot. I did attend
the fair a couple of times in the past but it is so hot, dusty &
crowded that the only time I would consider going in would be Friday
evening.

Also I am still deciding about attending the Silver Falls gathering
and could be interested in sharing transportation. Not sure how it
can be worked out but if I go it will be either Fri evening and I
would probably stay in a motel that nite and go back the next morning
or only go for the day on Sat. That leaves our paths crossing only on
Sat morning from Eugene to Silver Falls. Something to think about.

Jeanne

That sounds good!

I am one of the people that work there! We are headed up there
tommorow. It is a very good idea to have one up there outside of the
fair. That way everyone will be able to find it.

I will not carve a stamp of the logo; I'll think of something else. I
think I may still have it inside the gates, and ask around about where
I might be able to put something like that. I was thinking of leaving
it someplace right inside one of the staff gates, and making sure that
the person guarding the gate knows it is there. People will still have
to ask for it, but at least it will be safe.

I will of course carry my stamp with me and let the board know what I
will be wearing. Maybe I will run into someone and get to do an
exchange! Friday is the only day that we will be able to get up there
this year as well.

Maybe see you there!
