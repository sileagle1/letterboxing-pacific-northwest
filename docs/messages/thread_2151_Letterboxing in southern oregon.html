Letterboxing in southern oregon

From: erfellie (hemenekawan@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-18 18:39:04 UTC
Where has everyone been..all up north I guess. It seems there has
been very little letterboxing activity in the southern parts of the
state. We have some really great letterboxes here, and not just mine,
Sandi has a few planted (under my name, but the clue directions will
say if its hers) and hers are out in Galice and she has some really
great boxes, particularly "Mystical" and "passion on the rocks" (its
about her/her husband/me/anyone elses love of rock-climbing). They
have been sitting there for months and no one has yet to hunt them
out. I must say she is getting a little disheartened since she put
alot of time and effort into carving these great stamps, I keep
telling her to not be so dissapointed...If you carve, they will
come...it just might take them awhile hehe :oP
For anyone who is up for a cute love story read on, all others stop
here! Sandi made the passion on the rocks letterbox to commemorate
her engagement to her (now) husband. They were out rock-climbing at
Broughtons Bluff 2 years ago and once they had reached teh top of
this enormous rock wall, he slyly whipped out a beautiful emerald
ring and proposed to her on top of the rocks. Of course she was so
thrilled and excited she had some difficulty rapelling back down to
the ground haha and this is actually how we found out about
letterboxing. Once they had come down and packed up their gear, they
saw a tupperware container that they thought was someones trash (the
Broughts Bluff Letterbox) so without looking at the contents they
picked it up and took it with them to throw away, so they threw it in
the truck and headed back home to southern oregon. When they got home
they opened it up before throwing it away (luckily) and saw what it
was and emailed the owner and mailed it back to him (and from there
we learned about LBing). Ironically there was a hitch-hiker in it,
the LOVE hitch hiker (it was only the V letter though, we still have
yet to find the other letters) and so it was perfect timeing for that
particular HH to be in the letterbox, Sandi was so sentimental about
this HH that she kept it for a year (sorry to whoever was looking for
the V) and refused to place it in another box until I finally
convinced her that it was time to let it go, so we put it in a Funhog
box :)So anyways, the Passion on the rocks LB is to commemorate that
day up there on broughtons bluff where her whole life changed (for
the better)and now she is very happily married to a good solid, but
sometimes dorky, man :oP

Don't despair, there will be visitors but they may
just take their own sweet time. My very first letterbox
was placed in Central Oregon and there was not a
single other box (or letterboxer) within fifty miles at
the time. It took over a year for the first stamp to
show up in that logbook but I cannot tell you how
exciting it was the day I saw that first entry! That box
has been in place for nearly four years and in that time,
it has see the light of day only about a half dozen times.
I figure anyone who has made it all the way to this out
of the way place deserves extra credit.

There are now four actual letterboxers in the area and
many boxes besides mine. We still hide our letterboxes
for each other but Central Oregon now is home to nearly
a hundred letterboxes. A while ago, someone left a box with
a commercial stamp in it but virtually all the rest are home
to handcarved stamps. The letterboxes in less populous
areas will always have fewer visitors, as will the boxes on
longer hikes. However, I just figure that makes these more
special, collectors' items of sorts.

Keep the faith! Funhog

Hey there,

Don't worry. You'll have visitors to your boxes in time. I'm
actually part of that 'odd' faction of letterboxers that prefers the
far flung planted letterboxes to the urban, overpopulated areas. My
business trip/letterboxing reprieve from purgatory last weekend is
perfectly indicative of that--we drove 280 miles OUT OF THE WAY to
look for a handful of letterboxes in Wallowa County, Oregon instead
of following the highly letterbox-populated route along the
interstate on our way to Boise.

As for your neck of the woods, we've found several boxes in
Merlin/Galice already--unfortunately, that was a long time ago,
different LB planters and under different 'life' circumstances.
We'll likely pass through that region again next January, so we hope
to attempt some of your boxes then. I'LL DEFINITELY NEED THE
VACATION!!!

As for some boxes I've planted, I've had two, in exquisite
locations, that went unvisited for over 2 years. Only recently I
received a message from somebody saying they found one of those
boxes that we planted in April 2003. Folks will eventually find
them!!!

Meanwhile enjoy the adventure and think about how much less LB
maintenance you'll have to do on your boxes over time. ;)

Hi! I hope nobody thought I was complaining, I was just kind randomly
writing, its been so long since I have conversed with anyone on this
list. Nice to here from you Dv2rckr (did I spell that right?). I must
agree I love hiking for boxes more so than urban drive-bys, even
though I do have a couple drivebys, I try to make the clues a bit
more creative, or put it on a short hiking trail because I know this
is a great family sport and those with little ones sometimes cant
make the long hikes. I love the long hikes when seeking an LB though
because it creates memories, rather than just a 5 minute driveby LB
which can be forgotten(not much happened) but when your out hiking
for a few hours lots can happy, great conversations, scenery, animal
sighting, sprained ankles, being stabbed in the butt-cheek by a tree
branch and ripping a huge hole in your pants and your cheek and
bleeding all over the place while your best friend laughs
hysterically...all of which has happened to me...and climbing up a
lighthouse cliffside holding onto a pompas grass and laughing so hard
you think you might pee when you see your best friend fall and
dissapear into the pompas grass below...being attacked by a vicious
rabid sheep with big horns and a mean glare...being surrounded by
blackbears and yelling as loud as I can and waving my arms till they
ran away as my golden retriever stares stupidly at me wondering what
i am doing...ohh there is so much fun to be had in those long hikes!!

Oh and as far as the remote letterboxes go, that never get visitors,
one of them is Paradise Lodge in Agness, OR. When we found it I think
it had been there for 2 or 3 years and I think we might have been the
3rd people to visit it, that was one of the best hiking memories and
LB hunt we have ever had! Anyone who has the chance to do it, DO IT,
dont be deterred by the length of hoke, its well worth it, its like
heaven out there!
