Becca, we were camping at Cape Lookout over the 4th of July and I
enjoyed maiden's box "maiden heart" - also found, adopted, and moved
birder's "Cape Lookout" box, writing new clues. My 83-year-old mother
and I hiked up and found "Bessie" at Munson Falls - a very short hike
considering the fantastic waterfall reward at the top. There is a
picnic table at the falls for looking at the letterbox, and although
there are a lot of people coming and going, it is easy to grab it and
re-place it without being seen. I'm sure you would enjoy all three,
and none involve walking more than a mile or so. The Munson Falls
hike is the longest, and it's only one mile. You might be the first
to sign into the newly placed "Cape Lookout," and birder's stamp is
really cool. Have a good camping trip. We love Cape Lookout
campground because it is right on the beach, but I am not sure you
could get a spot there this late - they often fill up. The day park
area is very big and has LOTS of nice spots, again right on the beach.

From: (Doublesaj@aol.com) | Date: 2005-07-15 19:51:40 UTC-04:00
Having just been there:

Ecola box in Cannon Beach is not there--a giant geocasche is though
Chris the Crab atCape Foulweather is a gem of a site even though I think the
placer's direction is wrong--the view from the gift shop is terrific!
Funhog's Beneath the Sea #2 and #3 are there and nice short hikes
Alsea Bay in Wadport-#2 and #3 are missing
Ona at Bever Creek in Newport is gone (we have it! Found the stamp under a
rock with no container or log. We know the placers live near us in CA so are
returning it to them
Tank's First Campout by Pupp in Newport is a drive-by
Cape Lookout by Birder and Calli-K at Cape Meares is short and a nice stamp
Bessy by The Mudstumpers in Tillamook's Munson Crk. Falls is easy and cute
and if you get as far as Bayview Jetty by Birder in Barview, OR have a
meal at the 42nd Street Cafe right on the main drag. We lucked into it for
breakfast and had, honestly, the best food we've ever tasted! Smoked salmon omelet
(they smoke their own) and their homemade jam is to die for! Great coffee
too. I hear their wine list is the best in the area too.

Have fun!

Both the original Ecola and its replacement letterboxes are still
there---somewhere. The challenge is figuring out where the 16+
geocachers who mentioned finding one or the other (those are the
ones who put it in their geocache log entry--how many others found
it but didn't mention it?) over the past 12 months
actually 'replaced' the letterbox when they were finished perusing
it. I last personally set my own eyes on it in Jan 05. The last
few geocachers reported finding the letterbox on:

29 & 26 June,
21 & 7, 7 May
27 Apr
10 Mar
27 Feb
30, 23, 22 Jan

Who knows where the letterbox is. It's funny--one guy even
mentioned finding TWO ecola letterboxes before he found the
geocache. So, they're both there (the original and version 2.0
because the first one wandered off)--somewhere.

I may just kill this letterbox, though. Too much of a pain to
maintain it (while living 500 miles away from it roundtrip) with so
many geocachers finding it stat letterboxers. The box itself is
labelled all over it that it is a letterbox, not a geocache and to
rehide it exactly where they find it. Each time I've checked on it,
I've found it several meters away from it's original hiding spot.
I'm chalking it up to a lost cause... :( Sorry--it was my attempt
to introduce letterboxers to a little known fact of Lewis & Clark
history.
