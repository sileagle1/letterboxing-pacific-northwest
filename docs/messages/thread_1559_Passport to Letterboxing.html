From: Mitch Klink (letterboxr@aol.com) | Date: 2004-08-30 16:42:20 UTC
For the record, Passport to Letterboxing was just about the most fun
I've had since I started calling myself a letterboxer! The whole
event was a dream come true for me. It was exactly the sort of thing
that Erik the Vermont Viking, Daniel, and I were envisioning when we
started this whole thing six years ago! It was a community of
people... of all ages and from all walks of life... sharing an
intriguing, multi-faceted experience together... camping, hiking,
carving, stamping, laughing, caring... and communicating! Wow! I
get goosebumps when I think about it! The Spirit of Letterboxing is
alive and well in the Pacific Northwest!

It occurs to me that letterboxing has grown to become a genuine means
of communicating with people from all over the country! Sure, it's a
primitive, slow-paced form of communication... but what a romantic
and magical way to communicate! As I sat there on the trail looking
at all the messages that had been left in my Marshall Lookout logbook
over the past four years, I was absolutely amazed at how many lives
had been touched by my simple act of hiding a little box. There were
greetings in there from all over the place! No flames or sarcasm or
messages of ill-will... just simple, friendly words from a multitude
of interesting people. Even if they left nothing more than a stamp
imprint, I felt like I could tell a little bit about them by
examining the image. (As a bonus, I even found a fascinating clue in
there!)

I want to thank every single person who attended this event for
making me feel welcome. You all treated me like a celebrity, but I
want to say that, in reality, you are all MY heroes! You have all
been working (and playing) so hard to keep the Spirit of Letterboxing
alive in my absence! Without all of you, I could not have
experienced the joy of seeing how wonderful letterboxing can really
be! And when I see the love for the hobby growing in the hearts of
young, enthusiastic letterboxers such as Cat's Meow, Toothfairy,
Lucky Charm, and others, I realize that we've only just begun! You
are truly an inspiration to me. I am realizing that this is
something bigger than any one of us, or any group of us, can
control. Letterboxing is alive! It has a mind of its own, and it
will grow up to be whatever it wants to be. I have a renewed faith
that it will continue to be something wonderful!

Thank you! I love you all!

Sincerely,
Der Mad Stamper
