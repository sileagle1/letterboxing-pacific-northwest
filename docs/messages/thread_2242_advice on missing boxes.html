From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2005-09-19 16:56:45 UTC
> so, what shall we do?

Take two geocaches hostage for each letterbox that goes missing and
say you won't return them until the letterboxes are replaced in their
original locations without damage.

Just kidding. ;o)

But seriously.... I'm not sure there's a whole lot you can do.
Sometimes boxes go missing because some hoodlum just thinks it's
funny. Some people make their clues "tricky" to discourage that. (At
least then if they swipe a box, at least they'll have to WORK for it!)
Others turn clues into WOM boxes help discourage theft or vandelism.
And a few boxes on Atlas Quest are listed as being for premium members
only for that reason (under the assumption that anyone willing to pay
for premium membership probably isn't the kind of person to start
stealing letterboxes). Speaking of which, you don't have to be a
premium member to list premium member only boxes. A misconception some
people have. ;o) (As another aside, I'm donating all money from
premium memberships this month towards helping the victims of
Hurricane Katrina, so signing up this month would even be for a good
cause!)

As for what to do about the boxes that are already missing--it's up to
you. Perhaps the vandals have had their fun and won't check back
looking for replacements if you replace the letterbox. Or maybe they
will check back and keep an eye on the location to vandalize any
replacements that come along. There's not enough information to know.
Maybe waiting a month or two and then replacing the boxes will thwart
a vandal who gets bored of checking the location to find nobody has
done anything about the missing box and stops checking the location.

One person I know who's suffered from missing boxes in the past has
put hexes and curses on some of his boxes, though I'm not sure how
effective that practice has been. ;o) I do like the idea, though. At
least then if a box goes missing, you can shake your head sadly and
think, "Poor guy, running around with the nose of an elephant and the
tail of a pig. Well, I guess he deserved it." =)

We just chalk it up to the fact that no matter how good we hide them
someone (person or critter) is gonna stumble across'em from time to time.
Some folk'll look at them, become interested and join the hobby, some
folk'll put'em back and forget about'em and some folk'll toss'em away
thinking they're trash.

If we see signs of actual destruction and/or vandalism then we'll move the
replacement to another location. Otherwise, we recarve and replace.

It's like having children..... At some point in time you have to cut the
apron strings and let they face the world alone. (smiles)
