Hah! I wish I could've been a fly on the juniper when you
found that box... I drove by your house after I stamped
into Creepy Crawler but decided not to stop, chortling
as I thought of you finding my stamp before yours in
that letterbox. Sounds like I'd better get one of my boxes
down into the Redmond Dry Canyon before all the good
spots are scooped up!

I did place the latest edition of the Crooked River Bridge
box this weekend, new stamp, new location... If you have
that old, old, old version in your logbook, it's time to look
again.

Yesterday, the non-letterboxer in our household decided
he would like to go with me to find birder's Cyrus Springs
box. The lure of the dirt roads and remote location was the
clincher. Those old Oregon homesteads, with their stands of
Lombardy Poplars always remind me of my favorite book,
Wallace Stegner's Angle of Repose. This is my very favorite
birder stamp, to date and the area is wonderful, one I'd like
to explore further. Funhog
