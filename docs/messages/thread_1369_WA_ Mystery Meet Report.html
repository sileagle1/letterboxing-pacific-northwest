I know I'm omitting an entire family but, unfortunately, I never got
their signature stamps. I think the final count was mid-low 40s.

Meeting all the area letterboxers from near & far was awesome! It's
so cool to finally get to put faces with signature stamps and email
exchanges. The food was great and the weather held up perfectly.
Just threatening enough to discourage all the locals away from the
park but dry enough to be able to enjoy fellowship, stamp exchanges
and to seek out a few boxes in the area. Also, one of the traveling
letterboxes that Team Rayvenhaus brought was ABSOLUTELY AWESOME!!!
Not sure if they want me to reveal the details of the box on the
board but, hands down, this letterbox was one of the best I've ever
seen! The message, concept, stamp, container, craftsmanship,
artistic effort--you name it, was simply impressive and the final
product was stunning! Definitely above & beyond the call of duty!!!
A great tribute to some true heroes in our lives!!!

Thanks, folks, for joining us to heartily welcome Pete & Wanda to
the region. We look forward to seeing you at future gatherings and
for getting an opportunity to possibly look for a letterbox or two
you might place in the future!

dvn2r ckr


Re: WA: Mystery Meet Report

From: Danny McMillin (macdanny@mindspring.com) | Date: 2004-05-30 22:29:54 UTC-07:00
Whew!

I don't know where to start. Piper (the artist formerly known as Scout the
Barncat) and I had one super-duper-blowout good time at the Mystery Meet in
Des Moines, Washington yesterday.

Three cheers for dvn2r ckr, for organizing the Mystery Meet. She also
planted great letterboxes, carved an awesome event stamp and coordinated
the arrival of Wanda and Pete, our mystery guests from the great state of
Rhode Island. dvn2r ckr has more energy than any three of us put together,
I'll wager.

Since this was our first gathering, we had no idea what to expect. Folks
just started coming up and the stamp swapping frenzy began. It was so nice
to place a face with some of the familiar stamps we've seen in the journals
in the Northwest. I chatted with the Pirate family and we were soon
comparing our journals. I had hit several letterboxes right after them. It
felt as though I've been following them around for weeks!

I second dvn2r ckr's words describing Rayvenhous' event stamp. Every aspect
of it and its box were very special for the occasion. Well done
Rayvenfamily, well done.

And meeting Rhode Island's two famous letterboxers was so much fun. Wanda
and Pete are the tops. Piper and I were lucky enough to go letterboxing
with them for dvn2r ckr's two boxes planted for the Mystery Meet. It made
our day.

So all in all, a most rewarding day. Thanks to all we met. You made us feel
a part of this fine letterboxing community.

See you on the trails.
Keegan the Airedale Terrier and Piper
