From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2008-02-26 16:33:36 UTC
If I remember correctly, storm damage from the massive flooding during
the winter of 2006-2007 hampered the use of the trails (and roads) in
that area for much of the hiking season. Getting to the Mountain
Tourists may have been nigh impossible from the recommended trailhead.

The road to Ramona Falls was closed after the Sandy River rerouted
itself through the road, avoiding the old bridge altogether. At the
Lolo Pass PCT trailhead, massive numbers of blowdowns made the trail
impassible for weeks and weeks. I would LOVE to go for that one this
summer if conditions allow. Backpack anyone?

Speaking of backpacking, there is talk of a backpacking gathering this
summer. Are there any letterboxers out there who are hankerin' to lug
their home on their backs, sleep on the ground and letterbox under a
full moon at an alpine lake mid-July?

Sounds like some exciting times out there in the past 2 years! My box might
not have survived such trials & tribulations. However it did seem like a
fairly secure spot, so it is probably OK. If anyone does head that way,
don't forget that you can also look for Green Tortuga's McNeil Point box
when you're in the area. (There currently seems to be a problem with the
link to the photo in his clue, but just delete the last 3 characters in the
URL and you can get to it.)

We're not up for backpacking (prefer to travel light), but are still
considering a trip to Olalie Lake. We've heard good things about the hiking
& boxing there from a few of you.
