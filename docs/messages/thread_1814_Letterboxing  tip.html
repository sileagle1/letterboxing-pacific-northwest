Letterboxing tip

From: erfellie (hemenekawan@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-12-21 00:09:43 UTC

Hi! Mighty quiet 'round here... must be the holidays :o) I thought I
might just throw out a tip ;o) today is Sandi's birthday (shes 25,
but you didn't hear that from me) so for her birthday present I was
thinking, what do I get for a letterboxer? which turned into, what do
I want for letterboxing? what every one wants! A FLAT DRY SURFACE TO
STAMP ON!!!!! So how did I fix this problemo? I bought 2 school
pencil pounches with the 3-ring binder holes on them, and a square of
particle board which I drilled holes into on the left and right sides
and attached the pencil pouches with strong ribbon knotted several
times. The pencil pouches can hold the logbook, stamps, pens,
inkpads, napkins, car keys, and all the other stuff we tote around
with us and it is small enough to fit comfortably in a backpack or to
be carried, because it folds up. And the wood makes for a flat, dry,
even surface to stamp on :o) Hope this might help some of you people
for holiday gift ideas for other LBers or for yourself on the trail.
As for me, my days of toting around the gallon sized ziploc freezer
bag are over! Happy holidays to you all!
Erfellie
