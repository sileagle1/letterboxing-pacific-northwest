From: Nail Family (c.nail@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-04-20 01:47:55 UTC
Yes, Froggy left his mark in my book. Nice stamp, one of the best I
have seen of yours so far :0) Found some Sunshine 'faring well'
too. It was great to get out there again.

Incidently, I met a woman in Arizona back in early March, (a
salesperson in one of those souvenier stores in Tombstone of all
places) who is from Bothell. As a young girl her father owned a
demolition company that tore down the ghost town of Bordeaux not far
from McLane Creek. Funny that I would meet this woman in Tombstone
but that is life I suppose. She told me the story of how when her
father was doing the demo work on the buildings in that small town
that she would go around behind the men and pick out nails from the
lumber. WWII was going on and there were lots of people recycling
metals for the war effort. I had heard of the town but didnt really
know anything about it until I returned to Wa. I did some research
and found that there was a logging railroad that ran from Tenino thru
the Mima Prarie area and on thru the town of Bordeaux which was the
home of a logging company and on to Mud Bay. The boardwalk trail
that serves as the 'short cut' around the beaver ponds at McLane
creek are actually part of that old railroad bed. I think that with
all the history in that area and the chance meeting of the woman in
AZ and the incidental fact that my name is Nail, there is a good
chance that there will be a new letterbox series coming out soon.
Keep your eyes peeled, boxes may begin hiding in that area soon.

Nailhead
