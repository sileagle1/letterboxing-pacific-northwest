From: steagallmom (esteagall@aol.com) | Date: 2007-02-24 02:27:46 UTC
Here are some great places within walking distance of the
staion.

The Smith Tower- once the tallest building in the west

The Klondike Gold Rush Museum- actually a national park

Uwajimaya-, Asian grocery and book store



Google will get you good info on all.


The Smith Tower is still a working office building and I'm not
sure if they'll let you up to the top or not, but the Lobby is beautiful
and really takes you back in time. They have operators that run
the elevators.

The Klondike museum has moves since I was last there but the
web should get you updated info. It is free and very informative. If
it's really rainy you can sit down and watch a movie to warm up.

Uwajimaya is THE store in the International District. Lots of
weird and differant food. There is a food court in the store with 5
or six differant resturants, very casual. My son always likes the
Dragon dangling from the ceiling.

Know you'll have fun no matter what you do.

the top of Columbia Tower (a.k.a. Bank of America Tower)--the tallest
building in the northwest--IF you're willing to walk 69 stories (1,311
steps) and pay the now $50 entrance fee for the Big Climb for Leukemia
on March 18th. Tons of fun. =)
http://www.leukemia-lymphoma.org/all_page.adp?item_id=91560

I can't do it this year, but I have before and you really do get some
AMAZING views from the top. *nodding*
