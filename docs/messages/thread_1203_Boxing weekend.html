Boxing weekend

From: kleibrand2002 (bookwormlb@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-03-15 19:15:32 UTC
Oh what a wonderful weekend it was! I managed to combine my love of
letterboxing and my love of whippets into two fun-filled days.
Saturday morning on the way to the first whippet race I stopped and
picked up Turtle Crossing. (Got to use my new Dauber Duos for the
first time on this box, and I'm hooked!) Then I made a quick stop in
Carver to pick up the Clackamas County Park Series - Sit-A-Spell.
Rounded out my stamping morning with a quick stop in Barton for
another Clackamas County Park Series- Sunrise.

After a day of racing (my Sophie took 9th and Sleepy Whippet's Raz
took 8th)I invoked the "just one more box" plea to my dogs and
stopped at Metzger park for Bad Baby Sitter. Just a note to anyone
that's interested in finding this box...the trail is still a mess
from the ice storm. Lots of big limbs and even a few trees down on
the path. Be prepared to do a little creative hiking and don't take
a stroller!

Sunday morning found us in Eagle Fern Park before the oval track
meet. I took "the girls" out letterboxing with me and decided that
whippets and letterboxing don't mix. They really hate the walking
slowly and standing around parts. The first box we found (Morning
Ritual)involved a lot of yanking on the leashes, cursing, tripping,
and a couple of stomped on toes. On the way to the second box
(Triple Trouble series) I got smart and left the leashes on, but
dropped them and let the dogs roam a little (I was following the
letter of the law if not the whole intent, but we were the only ones
there). This was just as bad as tripping over them since I had to
constantly keep track of where they were and what they were doing
(eating nasty things of dubious orgins off the ground) and trying to
keep them out of the water. After the first box of the series they
went back to the car and I finished the series in relative peace.
Very cute stamps in this series and a nice easy trail (for anyone not
encumbered by 2 whippets). With 15 minutes to go before racing
started, I hunted down Frank in the Cotton-Tail Rabbit hunt. He was
a little damp inside but he had a little friend to keep him company.
I imagine he's pretty lonely now!

This is a lovely park and well worth the drive. If you are planning
on going out that way in April oh, say about April 24th & 25, come
watch a race meet! It's a lot of fun.

Bookworm
