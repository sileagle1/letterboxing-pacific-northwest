Baseball Goddess Letterbox

This box is located in southern Oregon off I-5 going northbound.

After the #80 (near Glendale, not Cow Creek) exit is the Cow Creek
Rest Area. Park in the southern most
parking spot in the passenger car area. Take the path in front of
you. Walk
past the second picnic table and take the path to the right. After
stepping
over the log, take 60 steps down the path then go left to where the
path
T-intersections with another path that runs along the fence line.
There is
a big tree there. To the right is a bending tree. The box is under
the bend.
***************************************************************
Dig dog LB

This box is located on I-5 in Oregon, near Metz Hill. It was placed
12/4/04

Going northbound on I-5, go to the Cabin Creek Rest Stop. This is the
rest
stop after the 142 Metz Hill exit. From the southern bathroom, take
the
path behind the restrooms downhill. To the right of the trail is a
multitrunked tree family in a cicle. To the left is an out building.
Stand in the path behind the out building with your back to the
restrooms.
Take eight steps to a large tree that will now be to the left of the
multitrunked tree family. The box is under the forest debris behind
the tree.
**********************************************************************
**
Bird Dog Letterbox


This box is located in Southern Oregon, near Rogue River. (about 20
miles north of Medford, OR)

At the Rogue River rest stop lives a whiteish stump, near the river,
in
the picnic area, kinda behind the restrooms. The Bird Dog letterbox
lives in this stump.

Jett and Pupp hid this box. Jett was very interested in this stump
and
actually put her nose where this box now lives. Jett would have liked
to
have played in the river but it is too rapid and it was too cold!

Please be discrete and hide this box as you found it or better. There
are
tables near by to stamp in at your leisure.


Happy Boxing,
Team Nashoba

Moon Goddess Letterbox


This box is located in Oregon, along I-5. (after exit 61)It is near
the town of Hugo in Southern Oregon.

Going northbound, go to the rest area between Rogue River and Hugo.
Go to
the pet area that is between the parking lot and the freeway. At the
southern most trash cans go south for 21 steps to three trees
together
- one forks close to the ground. The Moon Goddess lives there.

Please be discrete and hide the moon goddess well when you are done.

Happy Boxing,
Team Nashoba

Re: Clues for rest area boxes on I-5

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2005-02-20 17:39:26 UTC


Just to elaborate a little about not posting clues in obvious
places...
Not only to make the clues a mystery but also to protect the
letterboxes from repeated vandalism. If the clues are harder to find,
then the repeat offenders are not so apt to take the time to search
out the clues to do their dirty deeds.
Don't beat yourself up about posting the clues. We all make errors
and we live and learn. I'm sure there are no hard feelings. I know
Pupp and I know for a fact that she would not feel badly about this.
Chalk it up to another lesson learned and get out there and box!
Camp Fire Lady
