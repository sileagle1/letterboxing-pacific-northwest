From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-25 03:16:29 UTC
Try Wally's Chowder House in Des Moines--it isn't terribly far from the
airport & it's sort of the local secret. Great seafood (from fish &
chips all the way to Copper River Salmon when it's in season) and if
you're lucky and they have blackberry pie, it's incredible! We've been
going there for at least a decade now--it's usually our first stop
after landing at SeaTac! There are a handful of letterboxes in/around
the area, too.

As for the Bellevue area, we usually would just hit the Fatburger there
near Bellevue Square. Also, there are some great Thai restaurants on
Mercer Island--77th St Exit off of I-90--the one with which I'm most
familiar is Thai on Mercer and it's adjacent the Starbucks and Subway
in a small retail strip--across from Walgreens (if I recall correctly--
or it might be a QFC?) and across from another street from the Tullys.
The chef there is incredible and focuses on healthy but incredibly
scrumptious Thai cuisine! Our kids scarfed it down almost as quickly
as we did! It's a bit pricey though but well worth the money.

From: thatstatguy (2dragons@peak.org) | Date: 2006-08-25 16:59:03 UTC

I grew up in Burien, about 5 minutes from SeaTac (before the "port
package" you couldn't talk on the phone or hear the TV when the planes
went by right over our house), so I can recommend a few good
restaurants in Burien, which'll be pretty close to the airport! ;D

A map: http://maps.citysearch.com/location/10808388?
You can page through the "nearby businesses" (I started at Azteca) and
see what else is there. The map shows how close you are to the airport.

Huckleberry Square:
14423 Ambaum Blvd. S.W.
Burien, WA 98166-1423
206-246-7006
6am-10pm/7 days
American style food with some Pacific Northwest flavors. We've been
going there, particularly for the potato soup for about oh... 15
years? 20?

Azteca:
153 S.W. 157th Street
Burien, WA 98166
206-243-2241
11am-10pm/Sun-Thur, 11am-11pm/Fri-Sat
Brilliant (though not *completely* authentic) Mexican food. Don't
stuff yourself on the free chips and salsa, the meals are ENORMOUS. My
sister and I used to always half-starve ourselves during the day so we
could do justice to the dinners at Azteca. You've got to try the
deep-fried ice cream. We were last there for my Dad's 60th birthday in
February, they've done some serious remodeling! The restaurant's a
little tricky to spot, it's sort of behind the Value Village
(secondhand) store.

Toshi's:
14200 1st Avenue S #F
Burien, WA 98168
206-242-6551
11am-9pm/Mon-Sat
Teriyaki. Tiny little hole-in-the-wall place in a strip of businesses
next to the Fred Meyer and behind the Jack-in-the-Box. Not exactly a
dining-in place, though they do have a few tables, but oh, I love
their teriyaki chicken and the vegetables. Mmmmmm. One of my parents'
favorite places. Similar advantage to the Ivar's- you'd just go down
1st Ave to 148th to get on the 518.

Ivar's:
125 SW 148TH St Ste D
Burien, WA 98166-1940
206-242-3281
11am-11pm/7 days
Seafood! I adore the salmon chowder in a bread bowl, but it's all
great stuff.
Yes yes, I know, there's Ivar's restaurants all over Seattle and if
you *really* want the Ivar's experience, you have to visit the one on
the waterfront, this one's really a quick-stop kind of Ivar's.
However, this one has the advantage that it's right by the entrance to
the 518 freeway which goes right past the airport. It's just a little
west (like less than a block) of 1st Ave, on the south side of 148th.
