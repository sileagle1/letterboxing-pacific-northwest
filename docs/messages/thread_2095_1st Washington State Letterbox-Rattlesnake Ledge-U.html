1st Washington State Letterbox-Rattlesnake Ledge-UPDATE

From: samanark (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-16 15:32:51 UTC
Ryan and I spent the day yesterday at Rattlesnake Ledge in North
Bend, WA. Rattlesnake Ledge was the first letterbox in the state of
Washington and I had not been there since I found it in April of
2001! It was my very first letterbox find.

In 2003, "the powers that be" rerouted the Rattlesnake Ledge trail
and it is no longer just one and a half miles! It is now 2 miles to
the Ledge. However, it is much more of a gradual incline and a
definite improvement over when I went up in 2001!! I only had to
rest twice on the climb up and when we got there I actually said to
Ryan "We're Already Here! Wow!"

I had carved a "replacement" stamp for the Rattlesnake Ledge box
because I had heard talk from other letterboxers that the stamp had
cracked into pieces. I was going to add it to the original box and
leave the pieces in there as well! (For historical interest). When
Ryan and I got to the top and started hunting for the box, we found
that the snag that had held the letterbox was now a stump and the
upper half had fallen over. The rotten wood was broken into many
pieces. We sifted through some of the wreckage thinking that we might
come across the box, but no luck. Since we had also carved some
additional rattlesnakes to hide on our own (which would have put 3
letterboxes on this trail!) we had an extra letterbox with us. We
also had my "replacement" stamp with us as well! So we hid a
letterbox in the original location. The snag is now a stump and the
letterbox is hidden underneath rocks and debris at the base of a pine
behind the stump. It contains the "replacement" stamp and Ryan's
rattlesnake stamp.

Ryan continued to hike 9 and 1/2 miles to Snoqualmie along the
Rattlesnake Mountain Trail. I returned down the Rattlesnake Ledge
Trail to the car (and to hide my Lazy Rattlesnake Box). I then had to
drive over to Snoquamie (exit 27 off I-90) and pick him up where the
Rattlesnake Mountain trail comes out! Be glad he didn't hide his
Rattlesnake, or you all could have been subjected to a possible 9
mile hike!! :-)

-Amanda from Seattle

FUN! Now I have something new to attack on Rattlesnake :>

I ended up on the top of Little Si (missing the "Ah, Little Si" box
entirely, not because it was hard to find, but because I didn't realize
it was up there -- yea, my bad) planting a new box from Rhode Island up
there. Clues to come, but it's on the lower lookout -- the original box
is on the upper look out.

This one is a 5 mile round trip, some rock and root scrambling, and some
very nice rock climbing off to the side, should one be so inclined.

"Little Si among the ferns" will have clues posted soon (maybe even
today if I get my act together) -- so you'll have 2 to hunt at the top
of that lovely climb :>.

It's definitely a 2-water bottle hike though...
