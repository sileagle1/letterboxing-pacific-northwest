Mudstumpers camped at Triple Falls last weekend and someone turned our cache into a garbage dump, amd several rocks are littered with grafiti. The fire pit was understandably destroyed but they scattered the ashes over the whole area, which made for a very dirty campsite. What a shame that such a beautiful and peaceful place has become an eyesore. We hope to get back there soon to clean up the trash, but alas, when we got back to our car, it had been vandalized, window smashed and items stolen. Puts a real damper on faith in humanity.

find time to hide a letterbox in the area. The stamp image was drawn

campfire. The clue will follow soon.

On the return journey, we stopped at Deadman Pass and stamped into
Heather's box. The carving is way cute. Being subtle in that rest area
is far from easy, especially when the park guy is watering the lawn,
trucks are coming and going and a Winnebago pulls in every thirty seconds.

Before we left on our trek, we spent a couple nights in Central OR. I
excitedly hit the trail looking for the boxes hidden along the Redmond
Dry Canyon Trail by the mysterious new boxer, "Birder." Box 1 was an
easy find but the second eluded me. The "dead wood prevails' but I
didn't. All I managed to find was some really rank porn hidden in a
crevice and a bunch of trash. Instead of leaving with a new stamp in
