An OLD Der Mad Box - The Raptor?

From: Krista (kwillmorth@rmci.net) | Date: 2004-10-16 15:14:56 UTC


Halloo to All,

I'm hoping to get a little help regarding a box DMS placed somewhat
near me back in 2000. (And thanks, Mitch, BTW, for doing that recent
interview with Mark. I really enjoyed learning more about you and
about the hobby. Especially here in the PNW.)

Anyway, about a month ago I sent out a request on the main talk list
to ask if this box was still active. (I didn't realize DMS wasn't
active at the time. =) I did receive a reply from someone who said
they had found the box in 2003, and it was safe and sound. I figured
it was worth a shot and headed out to find a couple boxes yesterday
afternoon.

I was completely skunked by the clues to "The Raptor" letterbox - I
walked them through completely twice(just paces and compass
bearings), and my ending points were at least 50 feet apart. Neither
place looked to have a prime spot for placing a letterbox. So, I
have no doubt it was my interpretation of the clues giving me fits.
I WAS carrying my 15-month-old and trying to watch for snakes and
gopher holes (both of which I found!) This no doubt made my paces
uneven and I had trouble finding enough landmarks to make sure I was
keeping the correct bearing in the first place. I think this is one
I need to do with a partner.

After all this preamble, I'll get to my question...=) Could someone
who knows about this box please email me offlist and cofirm for me
whether or not I was in the correct area? I don't want any
additional clues, but I do need to know if the box is beyond the
barbed-wire fence as that area is off-limits to foot traffic at this
time. You CAN get back there through gates, but they are trying to
preserve that area for native plants and I don't feel right tromping
out there just because *I* have a good reason. =) I did find one set
of rather distinctive rocks I felt could be near the hiding place.
If I'm warm, I'd appreciate some confirmation. I'll just put it on
my list to check in another 6 months or so. If that doesn't sound
right, I'll just keep looking! But, I'll take a baby tender next
time. =)

Thanks,

Grifos

Re: [LbPNW] Re: An OLD Der Mad Box - The Raptor?

From: Krista Willmorth (kwillmorth@rmci.net) | Date: 2004-11-30 15:13:44 UTC-07:00
Resurrecting an old thread I meant to update a month or so ago...

Set out looking for this box again about a week after this reply from Der Mad Stamper. (Thanks BTW!) I went with my mom and baby this time, and proceeded to pace around from the same starting point as before. Predictably, we ended up at slightly different end points, but still didn't have any luck finding the box, or really even a spot that looked appropriate to look "deeper."

So, we decided to take a walk further down the way and take some pictures of the canyon that I had skipped before b/c I ran out of time. Ummm, words cannot express just how dumb I felt when I put 2 and 2 together and realized I was starting from ENTIRELY the wrong spot. Erg! I don't think I can fully communicate my moment of duh-ness without possibly giving away a clue, but suffice to say, when you're SURE you've found the correct starting point, you might want to think again. =)

Anyway, starting from what I now THINK was the correct place, we came to "a crossing" precisely where indicated, as well as an "animal lair" at the stated number of paces. We must have the same pace size DMS, because those bearings and paces worked out perfectly. However, we STILL did not find the box. =) We were up to our armpits in the location we felt was correct (after checking well for friendlies and not-so-friendlies), but no luck. We also covered about a 30 foot circle around the location with similar results. We never did find the geocache that many had mentioned either, so perhaps we were still not in the correct location. (Though we did find some scattered poker chips...McToys?) But, I'm getting a GPSr for Christmas, so perhaps I can try finding the cache, THEN the LB. =) It will probably be spring before we get out there again though. Unless I just can't stand the failure all winter...this is one of only two boxes that have skunked me so far! =)

So thanks DMS, for a lot of fun, a needed lesson, and some good views. We went on up the way and found Swan Falls Dam, Boulder Graveyard, and Water the Eagle, so we did come away with some stamps. We only found out the REALLY good news a few days later when we discovered that taking so long to look for the Raptor meant we didn't end up being the ones who found the human remains up at the top of Initial Point - which, but for the darkness, would have been our last stop for a LB on the way home. Some "photographers" found skeletal remains of two bodies up there on Halloween. They were described as hidden in a "cache" and there for a few months, so I think the finders just may have been geocachers looking into crevices...just like we would have been. Ewww! SO glad my toddler wasn't the one to come up on those!

Well congratulations if you made it to the end of my novel! I don't post much, so I guess it all comes out at once! =)

Grifos

Re: An OLD Der Mad Box - The Raptor?

From: Mark Bendickson (nomad_pnw@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-07-05 05:18:43 UTC
Ahh yes, this one brings back fond memories for me.

We arrived at the location just before dusk, and scrambled to follow
the clues to the supposed spot where the box could be found. I
poked and prodded in all the obvious holes and such, to no luck.

After the sun set my wife rewalked the clues with flashlight in hand
and standing above what just had to be the spot, asked me again if
I'd checked out all the obvious hiding spots visible to the eye. I
swore to her I had, but donned my glove again and gave it another
go. Well, wouldn't you know it, the back wall I had previously felt
with my fingers on my first prod suddenly moved when pushed on from
the side, and behind it lay the treasure we sought.

Another very clever hiding spot from the master letterboxer "Der Mad
Stamper" himself.

Anyway, it was there back in September of 2001, but who knows if it
is still there today.

If you have specific questions about the right location of the box,
you can ask me, although, I won't give the exact location away but
will tell you if you are in the correct vacinity or not. :o) Use
my email addy to contact me.

Happy Letterboxing!

Mark and Beth
