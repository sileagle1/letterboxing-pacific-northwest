Hello Funhog,

This message is specific to your;
Beneath the Blue #2 #3 and #4 LB's

I'll start with the potential troubling news...
Beneath#2 at Beverly Beach State Park / Camping may be (reported)
missing. I spent the last day+ at the Park with no luck. It appears
there has been considerable erosion along the creek that runs through
this 'plant' area. Also some trees that were marked for cut have been
taken down as well as new growth areas added. I will hope that out
of coincidence the area I was first drawn to for the find produced a
buried in dirt ziploc bag that was torn. No other 'evidence' of your
LB though so I'm hopeful that I simple missed, and you, or someone
else nearby can verify if it is still in place. I'll keep my fingers
crossed.

Now for Beneath#3 at/near the Lighthouse - Cape Mears outside of
Tillamook. I didn't go after this one specifically. A friend took my
daughter, followed the clues and came upon a cache, with a camera and
trinkets.... they didn't take any pictures or trinkets and there
wasn't a note if the person who placed the cache had taken Beneath#3
for repairs, or otherwise.

And - Beneath #4. I recall you had mentioned to me in another
communication that you hadn't been contacted or updated on the
status/condition. I am pleased to report that this box is alive and
well as of 5/25. Also, I was not the first - Hedgehog and ??Biscuit??
had discovered Beneath#4 in March03. This one proved to be a
challenge for me, for some reason. Once found it all seemed so easy
(an hour later). Ah sweet, sweet victory. The Beneath#4 may be the
best carve I've acquired to date

DID EVERYONE GET THAT??? If you're into a really nice carve Funhog
has outdone herself on Beneath#4, classy - very classy and fun. Nice
up-n-down trail, beautiful views - great day.

Thanks for a busy weekend...
soggy2pair
.cj.cristy.tuscany.kyra

From: (PNWEXPLR@aol.com) | Date: 2003-05-26 16:22:30 UTC-04:00
I'm here to say that I found Funhog's Beverly Beach/Beneath the Blue #2 on
3-22--and I'll wager it's still there. I had to work hard for that one-- the
trail was completely covered with water in places, but onward I slogged!!!
<>> (And keep in mind that Funhog often makes you
work for your prize...the first place you might think matches the clues...well,
maybe so, maybe not.) Even once I was in the correct vicinity, I was nearly
convinced it was missing, or that the compass reading was off, but I read
those darn clues again & had another try--success!

I found a pretty little western red-backed salamander guarding this box,
which was as delightful as discovering a hitchhiker!

Have another go when you get the opportunity. Like all Funhog's boxes, it's
worth the trek.

--Wild Woman
