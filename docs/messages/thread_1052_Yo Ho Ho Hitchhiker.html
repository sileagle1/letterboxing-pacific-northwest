Yo Ho Ho Hitchhiker

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-01-03 15:14:47 UTC-08:00
Hi all!

I picked up a VERY interesting hitchhiker today, it was started by a kindergartner in Olympia, WA. He wanted to combine his love of geocaching and letterboxing so he dropped this hitchhiker into his geocache. Unfortunately, the original stamp is no longer with the log book. There is a stamp in it, but not the original. The first few people that moved it were geocachers so I think the original stamp may have gotten switched with the box stamp they left it in.. not quite understanding what to do. Has anyone in the Olympia area found a stamp that doesn't match the box its in? I'm going to plant the hitchhiker tomorrow unless someone happens to know where the original stamp is.. if so I'll hang on to it and maybe we can get both stamps back to their rightful homes.

I also released a hitchhiker of my own today.. the Back to School Hitchhiker. I released it in memory of my now dearly departed free time ;) I start back to school on Monday to get my degree. I'm going to let it travel until I get my degree, about 15 months from now and then recall it.

While doing all of this I ALSO made a slight adjustment to the placing of the third box in my circus memories box. It's location wasn't the greatest. I'll adjust the clues on the web site tonight, but if you're carrying around the old ones they wont' get you there anymore!

Melanie

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-04 00:07:17 UTC
I have two ideas--there's a letterbox called Bruin and another that
was called Up Indian Creek without a Paddle--both carved by young
students in the region. I haven't been able to find either (never
even attempted Bruin). Any way, you think it could be either of
these two? The fact that it passed through a g*e*o*c*ache somewhere
along the way means that it's original stamp is probably long gone,
though--at least that's been our experience to date. I once had an
entire letterbox get swallowed up by a g*e*oc*ache somewhere near
the LB's hiding spot--even though it was well labeled, identifying
it clearly as a letterbox. It didn't even have the same name as the
c*a*che--comments on the online c*a*che log even asked why there was
a stamp in it that had a different name than the name of the
c*a*che? To date, I never have been able to recover that letterbox--
or whatever's left of it. The only other LB I haven't found in the
Olympia area that may also fit that stamp description is Lost One
Wand with Magical Powers or something like that.

I just did some checking on the Olympia West Travel Bus geocache and it seems it's basically a "hitchhiker" cache. I wonder how many hitchhikers have inadvertently made their way into that particular cache.. especially since it seems to be in the same park, if not at the same location as the Lying Man letterbox.

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-04 02:41:27 UTC
The Lying Man Letterbox and a neighboring c*ache are under the same
log on opposite sides of it (about 2' apart). Yes, most c*achers
find the letterbox first then find their 'prize' when they realize
the letterbox isn't their 'goal'. However, there aren't that many
folks active in letterboxing in this region so I can't imagine that
it's happened to many HHs (they're few & far between in these
parts). I'd guess this is probably the only instance. You know--it
may, indeed, be the correct stamp for the HH anyway--as it looks
quite similar to the placer's signature stamp. I even think I
recall them mentioning the HH being a LB instead of a 'prize' when
they posted it to the TB c*ache.

But then again, who knows?

dvn2r ckr
