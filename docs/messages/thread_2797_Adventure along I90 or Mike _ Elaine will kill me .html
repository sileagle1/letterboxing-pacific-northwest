Adventure along I90 or Mike & Elaine will kill me yet!

From: Colleen (colleendurham@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-06-20 02:20:10 UTC
When a new nearby letterbox listing goes up, I'll admit, I do get a
little crazy. When the planters are a couple of my favs, well,
there's not much that will hold me back, including the good sense
that God gave me, apparently.

Sunday night found us eastbound heading across the Columbia River
near Vantage. The Wild Horse Monument (with a new letterbox) is at
the top of the hill and even though the light was fading, we decided
to make a quick stop and nab the box. It is a short but steep climb
up to the horses and once you get up there, the view is stunning.
Climbing up wasn't too bad. It was the getting down that was going
to be a problem, my particular assortment of fused and titanium
joints plus an healthy fear of heights means I should stay on flat
terrain. But, I didn't.

While I was happily stamping away, Doug was trying to figure out how
best to get us down in one piece. There was a faint path of tire
tracks leading away from the parking lot below and another set of
tracks on top not far from the horses. Who knew if it was the
same 'road?' So against the collective advise of the other trekkers
on top, Doug clambered down and jumped into the Chevy truck. He
crawled around the hills and through the gullies until we could only
see the brake lights. Then he disappeared. About that time the
mildly interested tourists decided it was getting dark and time to
get back on the road. I waited up there alone another half hour or
so while it got darker and windy. Suddenly I could see headlights!
He was a long way off, but he was getting closer! When he drove up
I don't know who was happier, Doug, the rescuer or colls, the
rescued. And there we were, a 50ish+ couple on an adventure, bumping
along a 4x4 track under the moonlight, escaping fate.

I'm betting we've all got 'em, letterboxing stories probably better
left untold where the thrill, challenge, or maybe just plain
stubbornness trumps common sense.

PP & FL, wanna a ride? DB wants to go again. And, thanks Mike &
Elaine, another excellent box as always.
