it yesterday, and wanted to get the word out about it. Also posted on
AQ and LBNA. Known as Hot Treats for the series of 3.

Get on I-5 toward Seattle. Take Exit 118 Dupont/Steilacoom. Follow the
road to the first red light, McNeil St, make a left. Make a left and
follow McNeil through two traffic circles. Where Ridgeview meets
McNeil there is a small park with a small side parking area.

Head 200 degrees on the sidewalk and walk up Ridgeview away from the
park. Walk till you come to a gravel path and a pedestrian crossing
sign. Turn right and head up the path.

This is a Nature Growth Protection Easement. Enjoy the view and breeze
as the path takes a turn. After the turn there is a NCPE sign, once
you pass the second sign stop and look around. On the left is a tall
percolating coffee pot, here on the SW side, you will find a shot of
energy to keep you on your way.

Back on the trail you will walk up down and up again. You will see an
old fellow ready to share a cup of tea anytime of day. His table is to
the left of the path, set and ready for Tea time.

Continue to the end of the path and enjoy the chilly, breezy view.
It's now time for Coco, so here is what you do. Turn around and head
back down the path. At the fork in the path (one way you can not go)
count 27 steps. This takes you to a tree standing tall on the left. In
this tree is a Chocolate lover's delight. Look high not low for a warm
winter's drink
