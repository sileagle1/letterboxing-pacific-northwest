The best way to start a gathering is to simply put out a 'feeler'
note and see if anybody else in the region is interested. Then it
sort of develops after that as you try to come up with a date that
works for the most folks.

We've been to an assortment of gatherings around the country and
even one in BC that ranged from multi-day camping events, to one
LONG day of non-stop letterboxing, several 'couple hour' meets at
local parks to welcome non-local letterboxers visiting the region,
to a gathering in a mall food court concluding with a mystery dinner
at an interesting restaurant elsewhere in that same city to a simple
meet at this restaurant at this time--we'll have a table for 15-20
reserved, etc. We've even attended a gathering at somebody's own
home--sort of an appetizer/potluck type affair that ended with
looking for a couple local boxes as a group before the sun set.

You're really only limited to your imagination. I've hosted a
handful of these events and along the way have attempted to each
time vary the park/meeting location, the day/time the group meets
(to work with folks' varying work schedules--weekdays vs weekends,
church schedules, kids/no kids, etc) and the theme for the event
itself. Focus on something rather unique for an event (for fear of
violating corporate copyrights/trademarks)--my themes are usually
quite bizarre which hopefully evoke interesting creative responses.
Somewhere along the way pick somebody to carve an event stamp for
the gathering. One gathering we went to, they made the event stamp
a 'free for all' instead of assigning one individual and the six
resulting stamps were incredible (all different ideas on a similar
theme--it was really cool)! I really treasure those stamps--they're
along the same lines as Monet's paintings--same location, differing
light conditions/times of year. Just cool!

Anyways the modified movie adage 'if you plant them, they will come'
usually works to encourage more letterbox planting in your area.
The same thing seems to ring true for coordinating letterboxing
events.
