From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-03-09 06:50:50 UTC
Hmmm... I'd always thought the springs were piped into the pool and
didn't realize there were any there "in the wild." I'd love to know
more myself. If you learn anything please pass it on.

However, the land around the resort is all reservation land and I
suspect that the tribal police would frown upon any outsiders
traipsing around in the sagebrush. I have spoken to someone who owns
land on the reservation side of the river around the confluence of the
Warm Springs and Deschutes Rivers. It's an old family inholding from
the 1800s. They are unable to use their land even though they own it!
Gun toting tribal police have actually ordered them off in no
uncertain terms. The good part of this story is that the land is in
effect protected from development by the tribes since they don't own
it and the current owners have no desires in that direction.

There are several letterboxes along your way, though. There's the
Crooked River Bridge II, High Desert Tribute/Grasslands, Waterfowl
Highway and the Warm Springs Teepee. Hopefully, you'll have some time
to visit some of these goodies. Let us know about your trip. Funhog
