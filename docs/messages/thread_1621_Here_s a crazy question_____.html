Here's a crazy question.....

From: Wood Fmily (letterboxing2004@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-18 13:00:11 UTC-07:00
We were blessed to have been the first at a recent
letterbox which surprised us with a stamp that was
quite opposite of what we expected. My transfer
wasn't what I had hoped...bummer...but the stamp
itself was fun.

Here's the crazy question...

That particular stamp will envoke more "memorable
moments" for some - not all - if stamped in the colors
the carver and placer intended it to be stamped in -
or at least by the stamp the placer put into the log
as a guide.

What happens when the boxer comes across a box where
that stamp has been hit with one of those intense
black stamp pads and the ink embeds itself into the
stamp? We came across an entire series where we
fought with cleaning each and every stamp only to have
the black filter through every color ink we used. It
was moderately frustrating but part of the hobby I
presume.

Charlie TrailDuster shared on the trails with us that
he takes the time to clean off the stamp and use
different colors to really enhance what is often an
already remarkable carving. This is a suggestion
we've taken to heart....but it wasn't possible on this
one particular series.

What do you do when this happens? What is being used
to clean the stamps while on the trail? and do you
test stamp on scrap before stamping in your permanent
log book?

THanks in advance for the answers.
LILY of TeamWARE

From: devonpeter (devonpeter@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-19 01:33:27 UTC
I have come across this. I pour water over the stamp (from my water
bottle I carry) & clean it with a dry paper towel. Then I use the
back of the clue sheet to try it. If still not good, I wash again.

Devonpeter

From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-09-18 18:37:23 UTC-07:00
I at one point carried non scented non aloe baby wipes to clean stamps and
hands with.. but found it was more trouble than it was worth. I usually just
blot it off again and again and again on the back of the clue sheet then
color away. Sometimes it still doesn't come out like I envisioned.. but
sometimes it comes out WAY cooler than I first thought.. due to the two
colors blending.

Maiden

We tried the blotting...we tend to do that anyway as
we've found numerous boxes where the stamp appears
it's just been inked (like we are on the tail of
another boxer) when in fact it's been a week. Not
sure what to attribute that to other than the stamp
has signficant ink left on it after the stamp in takes
place OR there is condensation getting into the bag
and holding the color onto it.

We tried the baby wipes deal (as devonpeter) suggested
and that just wasn't working - and prompted this
question.

I was hoping to find a chemical or solvent that would
cure the inking issue but ensure the integrity of the
stamp remains. Like Q-tips soaked in alcohol or
something. I don't have one of the treacherous blank
ink pads here....everything I've got wipes off like a
dream....so I'm hoping to pull from the expertise of
fellow boxers.

Thanks for the advice....keep it coming.
