Mee-Kwa-Mooks Letterbox Update

From: Danny McMillin (macdanny@mindspring.com) | Date: 2005-01-22 15:12:49 UTC-08:00
I just received a message from a non-letterboxer who found the contents of
the Mee-Kwa-Mooks letterbox in West Seattle. It seems someone had found the
letterbox and trashed it. The kind person who found the remains, hid them.

I was able to fetch the handmade logbook and more importantly the stamp.
The logbook was soaked to the bone. But since it was hand-made from
watercolor paper, it held up quite well. Folks who used waterproof ink
should know that their stamped images are just fine. However some inks used
ran and smeared with the wet conditions.

I am drying out the logbook and hope to return it to the park if it passes
muster. I will be creating a new hiding place for it as the last place
seems to have been found out by the local knuckleheads.

I adapted this letterbox last year and feel poorly about what happened.
This is the first LB under my care to be vandalized. Dang...

Until further notice, Mee-Kwa-Mooks is on holiday.
