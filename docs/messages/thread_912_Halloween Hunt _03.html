Halloween Hunt '03

From: Rinda (sleepywhippet@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-11-10 04:44:39 UTC
It started out as I didn't get Campfire Lady to stap my book at
PNWLB Fright Fest '03. We planned to go letterboxing but the
weather didn't cooperate. So we decided to go today. We met at
Pittock Mansion and discovered we had a on the spot gathering going
on. Pandora, Iron Bear, Wingnut, Emily, Transplanted Cowboys,
Bookworm and See Shore all showed up. Pandora even carved an event
stamp for this mini gathering. I also got Transplanted Cowboy's
Traveler - just ask them if you can see their brand (as in livestock
brand)

After everybody stampd in we all headed out to gather boxes. We
gave up on Sleight and we figured we had the clues Sleight'ly wrong
and weren't getting very far.

Seeing as how I had a meeting at church I had to get to I knew I
needed to head back to Estacada at a decent time. Bookworm, See
Shore and I decided to get some of the Hoyt area stamps. Yeah right
with Veterans day upon us the place was jammed and driving a big 4wd
f250 isn't conducive to parking in tight places.

We gave up and decided to head to Clackamas Community College and
see what we could find.

First was to take See Shore to find the Oregon Trail box - the box
has been smashed and has many holes in it. Not wanting to leave it
vulnerable in the wet weather and with people stamping in from so
long ago that log book is a piece of history itself. Lots of people
stamped that it was their first box to be found. We triple baged it
and hid it back carefully - can't see it even without foilage around
it.

Second was the 'department of redundancy department' in fine form if
I do say so, fine form indeed.

On to CCC where we found the 'duck pond' series. Wow were those
ducks (and one chicken) friendly - they were following us down the
trails begging for food. We were forced to surrender a few precious
cinnamon chip cookies to them. Mallards taking cookie pieces from
my hand - scarey!

We then found Perseus in great shape - a little wet inside but we
dried it out and nothing inside the sealed baggie was damp! The tree
has been cut down that is in the clues but the clues still tel you
where the box is. We could see the box plainly when we got to the
area and hid it better when we left.

onward to the Boxed Set. We had fun with the challenging clues and
finaly decided that this LB'ing from the car was great fun - did
that series in record time! Had to the sun was going down and I had
a meeting to get to.

I made it to my meeting with 7 minutes to spare - wow!

So glad we all got together today! What a great time.

When can we do it again?? I haven't hunted in Vancouver area yet -
when and where do we meet ?? :)

Thanks all for the great time - great to see people I recognize from
the Fright Fest 03!

a very sleepy, Sleepy Whippet!
