So many awesome ideas for a patch, so far!!! It's exciting to see
these. Akin to having multiple folks bring event stamps to a
gathering. I attended a gathering 2 years ago and 6 folks brought
event stamps--they were all modifications on a theme & they were all
awesome & unique in their own ways! I'll treasure them forever!!!

I just posted my proposal but it needs modifications that I'm unable to
apply given my software on hand here at home. I couldn't figure the
exact font to use for the "N". I also don't have a powerful enough
graphic program to insert the mountain between the orca's flippers
instead of on top of them. I'd also eliminate the black, rectangular
border around the orca and confine the outline to an oval that snugly
hugs the orca--possibly inserting the orange 'fill' in that area like
the LBNA patch. I really like the concept that ties the regional
patches to the main organization yet still allows enough modification
to add a regional touch to it. I was also trying to figure out a way
to insert an evergreen tree--since it's so representative of this
region. Couldn't find an image that was strong & bold enough for it
yet wouldn't add distraction or busy-ness to it. Prolly the simpler
the better when it comes to a patch.

I think it's very cool that folks are sharing ideas. Just like you, I
hope Maiden doesn't mind my interpretation of her concept. Since the
Northwest is also known for it's contributions to the art of
calligraphy, I wanted to use a lettering style that would reflect
that. On top of all that, I am beyond excited by the new skills I've
gained taking an online Photoshop class at PCC and this was a perfect
way to show some of them off.

So far, the additions to the album are all wonderful. I know we'll end
up with something really nice as a result. I've already had three out
of state people tell me they want one when we get them!

One more detail about production. I've learned that you can use up to
seven colors without an increase in price. Funhog
