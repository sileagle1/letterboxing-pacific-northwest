On another note, I had a grand weekend in sunny,
warm (!) Central OR. Saturday was big letterboxing fun
traipsing around with 4DToes and snagging two elusive
birder boxes. I'm sure Chessy's camouflage face paint and
fatigues made us virtually invisible to passing dog walkers,
all the better for letterboxing. Birder's stamps are a coloring
extravaganza. You always have to remember to bring
a full array of colored markers.

On the way home, I checked on two of my boxes, Waterfowl
Highway (fine) and Crooked River Bridge (missing.) As sad
as it is to lose a letterbox, I'm glad to have the opportunity
to make a new and improved stamp. It will be hidden in a
different location in the same park so it will be an official new
letterbox when it finds its way out there.

Before I headed back to the Willamette Valley, I made a copy of
TeamWARE's Timberline clue. My curiosity was piqued by the
idea that this box was meant to be accessible year round. I
couldn't imagine anything outdoors up there to be exposed
half the year much less twelve months. Sure enough, put this
one on your summer/early fall list. It's already under a foot of
snow and won't be seeing the light of day any time soon. The
sunset view was glorious but I will have to just wait until next
June to see this stamp. Funhog
