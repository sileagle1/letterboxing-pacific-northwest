P.S. And for those who don't know, the dreidel reads "Nes Gadool
Haya Shem" which means "A great miracle happened there" (referring to
the biblical Maacbees). If anyone wants to learn to play: its a
gambling game sort of. You can bet buttons, pennies, nuts, or
anything really. After spinning it depending on the letter facing
upwards upon dropping you take that action. Everyone puts in lets say
1 penny into the "pot" doesnt matter the number of players. If it
lands on "Nes" (looks like a backwards C) the spinner wins nothing.
If it lands on Gadool (kind of looks like C with a tail on the right
side) the spinner Gets everything penny in the pot. If it lands on
Shin (wierd looking W) the spinner must Share, and put one penny in
the pot. If it lands on Haya (kinda looks like alowercase "n") the
spinner gets Nothing and its the next players turn. Winner is the one
who wins all (or most) pennies at the end. Happy hannukah, by the
way :o)
