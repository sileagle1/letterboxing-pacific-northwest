Welcome to the wacky world of letterboxing! My only words of "wisdom"
are to take your time with your plants. I was so enthusiastic when I
first discovered letterboxing that I planted lots of boxes right away.
Over time my carving skills have improved greatly and I wish that I
had fewer of my early stamps out there. Also, the more boxes you find,
the better you will become at hiding well and writing interesting clues.

Enjoy your newfound hobby, Funhog

From: (KuriousJo@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-07-03 04:28:49 UTC
Hi HappyCampers4!!
Not sure how much wisdom this is but . . .
- My favorite placements are placed somewhere special to me or the theme has special meaning.
- Place in locations that you think people would enjoy being. . . maybe spending some time.
- On the other hand drive-by boxes are fun for finders who are passing through the area or locals that just don't have much time on a given day.
- We have a skunk stamp for use when we come home without a box. This helps ease the disappointment. And you have a stamp image to log the "attempt" by if you wish to record it.
- Start with a good quality log book. The stamps, boxes and outings recorded there can become a keepsake of wonderful memories.
- Respect the placers perogative - how they write clues, where they place, the carving, etc. Each box is a gift.

I think it's great that you're already placing boxes! When you place your own you realize how much can go into each box. Kudos to you!

Kurious Jo
