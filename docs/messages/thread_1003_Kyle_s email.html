Hi all,
Last Saturday, Elisa and I met with about 20-25 Letterboxing
enthusiasts to discuss letterboxing in District parks. Our focus
was
primarily to introduce ourselves to the Letterboxing community
and to
encourage the activity, especially since it is a great
family-oriented
activity that we promote at the Nature Park. We asked that they
monitor
and maintain their boxes and that they consider the safety aspect
of the
letterbox locations (steep streambanks, poison-oak, etc.). We
also
asked that letterboxes be adopted by another person if the
original
letterbox owner is unable to maintain them.

There are currently about 30 letterboxes in 16 parks, though
some are
not being maintained and may be missing or in disrepair. I have
located
a small handful just to familiarize myself with the activity and only
found a couple that I felt posed a minor hazard to either the
hiders or
the seekers. I've contacted the owners and apprized them of my
concerns
and they have been cooperative.

Elisa and I suggested a Letterboxing event (such as the
Geocaching
Ridgewood View Park clean-up last May) that would encourage a
sense of
stewardship of a park or parks and which would offer an
opportunity for
the Letterboxers to promote the activity to people that may not
know
about it. I also suggested a Letterboxing class at the NP (like the
Geocaching class) to teach families about the fun activity and
maybe to
give them the chance to carve their own Letterboxing stamp.
They could
then seek the letterboxes in the NP as a starting point for finding
other letterboxes in the District and the region.

Kyle
