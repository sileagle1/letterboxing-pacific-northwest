Maricela's letterbox

From: eric jaroch (e_jaroch@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-26 14:10:31 UTC-07:00
My daughters and I were enjoying the nice weather on
Monday and decided to take a hike and check on our
letterboxes here in town. Angelica's box was fine,
but when we got to Maricela's, someone had swapped the
stamp with a plastic ring! My daughter was devastated
for a few minutes, until she realized that we were
taking the whole box home so I could make her a new
stamp. Then she got excited about the prospect of
finding a new home for it, plus the bonus of getting a
ring out of the deal. Oh, to be six again!

Karina's and Sierra's boxes were fine, although park
maintenance is going to require a tweaking of the
clues.

We will work on getting Maricela's twin out there as
quickly as possible.

Eric of the J-rock family

One thing I've started doing to my stamps to hopefully avoid
this 'tradefully' bad problem is to write the name of the letterbox
on the back of the stamp. Since I recently had a mishap involving a
letterbox stamp that ended up in a geocache about 100 miles away
from its 'resident' letterbox carried off by an unknowing soul, I've
started writing the word letterbox after the name of the box on the
stamp. Hopefully, this may preclude these problems from occuring in
the future.
