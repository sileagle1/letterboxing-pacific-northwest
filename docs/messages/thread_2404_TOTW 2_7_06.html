An interesting question. The designs are essentially the same, and
could be easily spliting the vote which would indicate that they
should be combined so clearly we should combine. But which would make
the runoff, the one with more votes? But perhaps someone is really set

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-07 20:56:35 UTC
I, too, prefer that the words NOT be spelled out. This is quite
similar to why I have yet to put a letterboxing sticker on my car. The
last thing I want park officials noticing is that a 'dreaded'
letterboxer is in their park. If the patch is 'catchy' it will
generate interest and might prompt folks to ask about it--if it's
spelled out then it forgoes that bit of 'mystique' that is so crucial
to letterboxing.

Any thought about going with two different patches? Thunderbird's for
WA and Funhog's for Oregon? I say the more the merrier. The two regions
are so distinct--why not?

dlphinus

Actually, I kind of like that idea, but as a long-term project. For
now, I'd stick with one patch since most places require a minimum
order of either 50 or 100 patches, and either way, someone's likely to
be left holding a lot of excess patches. I know the folks on the
Southeast board purchased 200 of them and still have over 150 of them
laying around collecting dust.

So from a financial standpoint, one patch would be much perferable.
Even with the minimum order, they'll likely still be some left over.
With two different patches, you'll wind up with even more extras.
