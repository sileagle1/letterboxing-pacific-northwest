Be the First on Your Block

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-09-09 03:48:23 UTC
...to have a fantastic collection of Double Tree stamp impressions! I
just returned home from three days at Olallie Lake where I
rendezvoused with the prolific stamp carver/enthusiastic hiker Double
Tree. Since she first joined our wacky hobby, she has been seeding the
Olallie Lake Scenic area with letterboxes, many of which still await a
finder! Every one is a treasure and they show her progression as a
carver from her earliest efforts to those she created just this year.

This part of the Mount Hood National Forest is gorgeous, with many
trails to discover. Most are easy to moderate in difficulty and would
be just great to enjoy with kids. The views of Mount Jefferson are
everywhere with peekaboos at Mount Hood here and there. Lakes both
small and large sparkle through the trees on every trail.

There are yurts and cabins for rent, both free and paid forest service
campgrounds and plenty of great backpacking sites. The drive is about
two and half hours from Portland but well worth every minute. If you
make the journey you will return home with lots of wonderful stamps
by Double Tree, Logos, Mudstumpers and Pandora. I certainly did. Funhog
