From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-12 06:57:21 UTC

If you have military privileges--there's a military rec site with
cabins near Sedona--actually in Flagstaff--it's Ft Tuthill-Luke AFB
Rec Area. We used to camp there all the time when we lived in
Southeastern AZ off and on over the years. They have A-frame cabins
and even RVs you can stay in--but we only camped (my family, minus
me, stayed in the A-frames on other occasions). I think you can
even rent skis there for nearby Flagstaff Ski Slopes. We used this
spot as our 'launch' point for hiking trips up to the Grand Canyon
and mountain biking treks in Sedona. One long day trip involved
camping there and a 16 mile rim-river-back to rim canyon hike with a
return back to the campsite before sunset--so it's a doable option.

Not so sure about the other CA locales. Lemoore NAS is 'relatively'
near Modesto (closer to Bakersfield) but one room there ran
$56/night! Ouch!!! Super 8 or Motel 6 on the interstate was about
$45/night and didn't require a 60 mile 'out of the way' drive
for 'supposedly' cheap lodging.

Check out: http://www.hostelsweb.com/index.html#northamerica for
hostel info. One thing to note, though, is sometimes hostels can be
MORE expensive for a family than an inexpensively priced hotel--as
you often pay per person at a hostel--unless they have
private/family rooms available. We're headed to New Orleans in a few
days and a 3* hotel, through travelocity, was less expensive than 4
beds in a hostel. So, I twisted my own arm and opted for the hotel
WITH POOL & FREE WI-FI & WALKING DISTANCE TO THE FRENCH QUARTER!
Rarely see that stuff at hostels... ;)

Enjoy your trip,
