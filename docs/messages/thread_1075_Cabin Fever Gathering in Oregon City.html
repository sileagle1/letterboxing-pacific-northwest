Cabin Fever Gathering in Oregon City

From: Tina (tinarie@msn.com) | Date: 2004-01-12 16:57:39 UTC
Thank you Maiden for putting together the Cabin Fever Gathering.
Cat's Meow and I had a wonderful time and met some great new
letterboxers in the area. Your daughter Tooth Fairy is a doll! After
exchanges, stamping into the Beat a Dead Horse Postal Box and
catching up with old friends and making a few new ones we were off to
box at CCC. Since the recent snow fall the area has had few changes.
A lot of trees are down and we believe the tree that houses Perseus
has fallen and has been cut up. We were unsuccessful in finding this
box. The first box in the Duck Pond Friends series was found but we
were unable to locate the second box. I am not saying it is missing,
we just couldn't find it. If anyone has found this box please email
me off site and let me know if we were in the right place. All of the
Boxed Set Series is alive and well but somewhat soggy inside the
boxes. We replaced baggies as much as we could but the log books were
slightly damp. Thats to be expected because of the snow. We ran out
of time and dark was upon us so we were unable to go after Nutria but
that is left for another day. Before the event, Cat's Meow and I and
Willow and kids went after a couple of boxes. Finally we have gone to
find The Department of Redundancy Dept. letterbox. It is alive and
well and was dug out of the snow. The Oregon Trail box is alive and
well but the box is really thrashed. It needs to be replaced soon
before the snow melt get it soaked. The parking lot of the Museum was
ice covered and slippery so it made for a really challenging morning.
After being cooped up inside for the past week it felt wonderful to
finally get out and letterbox. The storm had given me some time to
carve a few stamps though. Look for my Krispy Kreme Letterbox to be
placed soon in honor of the Grand Opening of the new Krispy Kreme
store in Vancouver and a new series of 4 boxes that were introduced
at the gathering. Happy Trails!
Camp Fire Lady
