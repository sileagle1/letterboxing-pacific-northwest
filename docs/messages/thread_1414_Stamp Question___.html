Subject: (OR) wow! talented new boxers!

In the past two days, I've had the delight of tracking down some newly
planted boxes. Many thanks to Pupp and Smiley for the fun! Yowza--how can each
of them carve so much better than I do with so much less practice??? Fun
clues, great stamps--check 'em out, folks!

I had the added pleasure of MEETING Smiley and two of her kids, as they were
surreptiously seeking one of Pupp's Lbs. I'd already found it, unaware that
these lbers were hot in pursuit behind me! I'd taken a little walk, and in
wandering back where the box was hidden, I detected in these strangers the
tell-tale signs of fellow boxers (a certain nonchalance, a clutching of certain
downloaded pages...) and couldn't help but greet them with, "I got firsties."
What a fun visit!

Today Heather and I were recalling our concern (when we first started boxing)
that at the voracious, obsessive rate we were seeking the local boxes, we
would soon run out of ones to seek! Man, look at that Portland list now! Who
can keep up? (Besides Funhog, of course...) ;o)

Welcome new boxers, and THANKS for planting!

Wild Woman

Hi all,

I went to check up on my Egyptian Series in Orchards Park this
morning. I'm sad to say that box #1 & #2 have gone missing. #3 &
#5 are both safe and sound. When I checked up on box #4 I found a
little baggie on the trail in front of the hiding place, and was all
set to find it gone as well. It was there, but sitting out in the
open and was all moldy and gross inside, so it has been pulled for
maintenance. I will re-carve and re-hide them all soon. For now,
I've changed the clues on the letterboxing.org site, so toss out any
old ones you may have hanging around.

For those of you who have box #1 & #2 you may want to go back again
when I replant them. These were my very first stamps I ever carved,
and I've gotten a little better since then. I even have a few new
ones to hide...with some special treats inside. Coming soon to a
stump or rock near you soon!

Hope you are all out there having fun finding boxes!

Bookworm

Message: 5
Date: Tue, 29 Jun 2004 03:33:51 -0000
From: "nikanderfamily"
Subject: Our finds so far

Hello all,
Well we have been officially bit by the letterboxing bug this year.
We have been successful at finding the Springwater trail #1 and # 2.
These were fun and very kid friendly. We just had to watch for all
the people going back and forth.
Next we figured out the Bugle Mystery Letterbox. This one was fun
because it was our first harder one that we have done.
Then one a weekend adventure we headed to Seaside where we found the
Seaside Carousel Letterbox, This one definetly took patience. We then
headed out to Cannon Beach and found the Ecola Letterbox. We also
found a geocache so that was intresting to see what that was about.
The geocache was so plain in site that we hid it a little better.
Then we headed home but even though it was getting dark we had to
stop and get the Picea Sitchensis Letterbox. We tried to get this one
last year but it was when it was missing. We had to use a flashlight
but we just couldn't live with ourselves if we didn't get it this
trip and we were rewarded with a great stamp. Right now we are in
pursuit of the Mother May I series which is very hard. We have been
successful with the first stamp so far but we are determined to get
them all. Thankfully we live just a 15 minute walk away. We are
excited about all the new letterboxes planted out in our area now.
There is so much to keep us busy. We are getting closer to wanting to
plant some of our own but just ahven't had the time to do that yet
with our 2 little babies in tow. Maybe soon we will plant some. I am
really liking carving stamps. Well happy hunting all.
The Nikander Family

Subject: Stamp question?

When we started this hobby last year we started with a stamp we
bought. Then we learned what it took to carve one of our own then we
designed and carved a family stamp. We also carves a stamp for when
my husband and I just go out letterboxing without the kids. Then this
year the kids wanted their own stamps so I carved each of the kids a
stamp but we found that it was too time consuming to stamp in that
many stamps especially in public places.So we told them to keep those
and use then when they go letterboxing with someone else than use
(like their grandparents, who we have hooked into this hobby). So now
we are improving on our first stamp(because the baby took a bite out
it). Did others of you take some time to come up with the stamp you
wanted to use? Do you change stamps periodically? Or have you had the
same one from the start? Thanks for the input.Happy hunting.
The Nikander Family
