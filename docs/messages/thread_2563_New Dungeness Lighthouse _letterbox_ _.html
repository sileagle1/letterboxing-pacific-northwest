Howdy Y'all,

Had a great weekend out on the northern Olympic Peninsula
(Sequim/Port Angeles area). There are a ton of cool boxes out there,
some older, some pretty new.

One of the highlights was hiking out to the end of Dungeness Spit.
It's about 10-11 miles roundtrip, and took us about 5.5 hours with
stops for rests, food, and bird watching. It's flat, and the sand is
good walking if it's low tide, or at least below about 4 ft (check
www.saltwatertides.com).

There are free lighthouse tours at the tip between 9am-4pm, which was
a real treat. Also, next to the visitor's log, there are two very
nice custom-made (but not hard carved) stamps featuring the
lighthouse. I didn't bring my own logbook, but there was paper there
for stamping. As I understand it, a letterbox is anything with a
logbook, a stamp, and a clue -- and it often involves some mental
and/or physical challenge. The New Dungeness Lighthouse has ALL of
those things - well, except the clue, which I am providing right here
in the form of this message. So I logged it as a find! =)

Anyhow, check out Sequim/Port Angeles area if you get the chance. And
if you decide to hike the Spit, check the tides, pack food and water,
and bring your logbook and ink pad!
