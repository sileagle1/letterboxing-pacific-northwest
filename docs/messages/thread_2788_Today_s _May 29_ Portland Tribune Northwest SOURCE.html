From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2007-05-30 01:37:19 UTC
I just wrote Ms. Snyder the following email:

I recently read the Portland Tribune article in which you were quoted.
I love having Forest Park as an urban resource and have hiked the
entire length of the Wildwood Trail as well as many of the other trail
found here. Sadly, I was dismayed by part of the current article.

As an active user of Forest Park, a volunteer educator with the Oregon
Zoo, a birdwatcher as well as an avid letterboxer I found myself
taking offense when you portrayed this hobby as an "illegal activity"
because it takes users off trail thus introducing alien species. All
but one letterbox I have ever found in Forest Park has been adjacent
to an existing trail and that one has probably had fewer than three
dozen visits in eight years. You said "bird-watchers, Boy Scouts,
school groups, dog walkers (hopefully with dogs on leash, as required
by law) and spiritual groups" use the park. I would venture to guess
that members of every one of these groups would be just as likely to
track in an alien species and have been known to go off trail some
time or another. In fact, the worst alien invader in the park is
probably English Ivy and most likely made its way in via local
landscaping rather than on someone's shoes.

I cannot speak for geocachers since I don't participate in that hobby
but most letterboxers make a great effort to respect the environment.
If you read the education sections at either of the primary
letterboxing sites, Letterboxing North America or Atlas Quest, you
will see that Leave No Trace concepts are highly encouraged. I am sad
to see letterboxing portrayed in a negative light since I know it as a
wonderful way to get folks out of their houses and out on the trails.
Many participants have discovered the bounty of the natural world
around us here in Portland by being introduced to letterboxing.
