From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-04-01 19:09:33 UTC
> Anyone have any suggestions for someone who has never carved
> a stamp in her life? Help!

Just go at it! Nobody I know has ever made a great stamp right out
of the starting gate, and you don't have to show anybody any stamps
you don't want them to see! =)

Der Mad Stamper has an excellent tutorial for carving stamps at
http://members.aol.com/Letterboxr/carving.html which is the primary
place I learned how to carve. After that, it was mostly just lots of
practice!

I created my own version of Carving 101 (minus this cute
illistrations and used photos instead) which also shows some of the
tools you might find useful. That webpage is at
http://www.geocities.com/rscarpen/Lb/Docs/Carving

The bottom of that webpage has links to other nice webpages related
to carving. Read, practice, and learn! =)

-- Ryan

From: Fudrick (fudrick@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-04-01 18:18:10 UTC-08:00
Just go for it!!!!

When I decided to try my (shaky) hand on my first stamp, I borrowed my wife's tools and pink stuff, sketched out a picture and words, transferred it, and started carving. Making the first test stamp, I thought "Most certainly I can do better than this". The following night, I made another sketch (same topic) and tried again. When I made the first test stamp, I thought "I guess I can't do any better!" Not that it was perfect, but that I couldn't do better.

The encouraging news is that I am getting better. With time and practice, my technique is improving and am trying more intricate designs. Still nowhere close to the ultimate skill of His Eminence, but improving.

Fudrick, the humble
