From: Samantha Stephens (samantha.stephens@ptk.org) | Date: 2005-07-19 04:27:13 UTC
On my way home from visiting family in Montana, I stopped off to find
the Higgin's Point Letterbox in Coeur d'Alene. If you're ever in the
area, you absolutely *have* to take the time to get this box!

It's in a perfectly lovely location, with great views of the lake.
(No hint there, it's all lake! ;) ) A very nice walk as well. But
most of all, you have got to see this stamp! It's hand carved, and
gorgeous! Everyone I've shown it to so far thinks it must be a store
bought stamp. Take some extra paper, though. The log book (which is
also super nice) is full. The placer is aware and will be replacing
it, so no worries.

The same placer has one more box in the area that I didn't have time
to go find, sadly. I will definitely hit it on my next trip. If I
had realized it was the same placer, I probably would have gone for it
anyway, and just planned on driving home in the dark! ;-P

I also found the Sunshine box out farther east in Idaho. It's a very
easy find, but a beautiful drive, very nice stamp, and so worth it!
Check it out too. :)

Next time, I'm planning way more time in my visiting schedule for
'boxing. And planting. Much of Montana is awfully bare of boxes.

Hope everyone's enjoying this beautiful weather!
