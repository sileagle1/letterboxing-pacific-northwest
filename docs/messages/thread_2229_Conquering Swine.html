Conquering Swine

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-09-08 18:18:28 UTC
Ever since I started letterboxing, Ryan's letterbox at
Nesmith Point has been one of my personal goals. I
like long hikes but I'm not the fittest pig in the pen. Dog
Mountain has always been my standard for a serious hike.
Tales of the difficulty of this other trail have kept me
back. However, with the appearance of Double Tree
this year, I have been on the DT Fitness Program all
summer, finding her letterboxes right and left.

For any of you who have found many of her boxes, you
know that some of them are looooong hikes, some with
a considerable elevation gain. In the last few weeks, I
have been on her Ruckel Creek hike (twice... now, that's
another story) been to Tunnel Falls and up the hill to
Wauna Viewpoint. For good measure, I've thrown in a
visit to Ryan's Angel's Rest box and a few other short,
easy jaunts.

Yesterday, I decided that I was probably closer to being
able to complete the hike to Nesmith Point than I've ever
been. I packed up my letterboxing gear, found some
lunch stuff, filled bottle after bottle of water (I took about
a gallon) and headed out. I am VERY pleased to report that
the letterbox at the highest point in the Columbia River Gorge
is alive and well. I rested a lot, took a long time but I made
it with no mishaps and, unbelieveably, no sore muscles today.
I'm so danged proud, kinda like a pig in mud! Funhog
