My new idean for an LB

From: erfellie (hemenekawan@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-04-13 02:32:27 UTC

Hi everyone!
Sandi and I have been throwing around an idea for a new letterbox
for about a week now. We are very fortunate to live right next to an
utterley amazing, wonderful, meaningful, fantastic wildlife
rehabilitation and education center. For example, when someone hits a
wild animal driving down the road, you can scoop the injured animal
and take it to this place for help in recovering, and/or you can call
this place and have them pick up the animal that is injured. After
the injured animal is healed, they are relocated and released into
the wild. Unfortunately there are some animals that have come to this
place, such as a grizzly bear, a kodiak, several black bears,
cougars, lynx, foxes, and other animals that will never leave, this
is their permanent home because they were domesticated as cubs (made
to be pets that grew to big) or were injured so severely that they
can never be released back into the wild, like an eagle whose beak
was ripped off and a wing in a car accident. These animals are not
like zoo animals, they actually try to give these animals as little
human influence as possible, to increase their survival in the wild.

The truly amazing part of all this is the fact that this entire
rehabilitation center is run 100% on donations of kind and caring
people, but as you can imagine, they never have enough funding and
are constantly struggling to make ends meet. Every single person
(with the exception of I think 3 people who are paid employees) is a
volunteer, and they can never have enough volunteers. Anyways, this
place gets a huge portion of its donations through free tours held
for the public. You can call them and make an appointment for you
free tour which consists of about an hour and a half and is easy
walking for kids and the elderly where you will meet a huge variety
of wonderful creatures, some you have never even seen or heard of :)
They also travel with some of the animals (the permanent residents)
and do educational tours at fairs and other events. Like I said, its
not anything like a zoo, you are not charged for entry fee, not
forced to donate, and their are no vendors other than a small gift
shop. If you only came here you would see what a wonderful place this
is.

Anyways...the letterbox idea...Sandi and I want to create a very
artistic and wonderful letterbox in celebration of this rehab and
education center. We will post directions on how to find this place,
only 10 miles off of I-5, and instead of finding the hidden letterbox
you would go to the counter at the gift shop and ask for it. The only
catch is that you must donate $1 (or more if your feeling generous)
to be given the letterbox for stamping, afterwards it would be given
back to the employee to replace behind the counter. I very highly
reccomend taking a free tour while you are there also, it is best to
call in advance to schedule it. remember, the donation even if its
only $1 does make a difference to these animals. It is food money,
habitat money, veterinary/medical money.

So my question is I guess, if Sandi and I were to go through with
this idea, would any of you actually participate in it? would this
attract any letterboxers (since its not hidden and therefore not much
of a treasurehunt)? Or are we waisting our time with this idea? We
certainly would not do this if we didnt think it was a worthwhile
cause, and I can guarantee this rehab center more than qualifies
as "worthwhile". So Please, if you can, send me an email or a posting
with your opinion on this idea. I really hope that you guys think its
a good idea.

Thanks for your time in reading this long posting.
Happy trails to all!
Erfellie
