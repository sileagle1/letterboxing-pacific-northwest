From: Melanie (maiden1974@verizon.net) | Date: 2004-11-14 10:21:23 UTC-08:00
I to had noticed how quiet it has been around here. Must be the beginning of
Winter and the holidays is effecting everyone.

Personally, the Brat Pack spent Veterans Day out letterboxing. We left 3 new
boxes in the Portland area for others to search for and found Adele and
Sophia. Both of those boxes are wonderful. They are from a new carver and
have only had a couple of visitors each. I highly encourage everyone to go
and give these some attention.

I think our best story was up in Olympia on the 6th. The Brat Pack had gone
to say goodbye to Team Ravyenhaus and decided to pick up a series in
Millersylvania Park. It was a series planted for the Boooogie in the Park
gathering that we just hadn't had time to get to. We hit the trail at 3:30,
in search of a man that had been bitten by a creature of some sort. It was a
wonderful walk through dark forests and swamp lands. We had no problem
finding boxes one and two and were completely entranced with the story line.
The sun had started to fade but we continued on dauntlessly, hoping to find
those last two boxes before calling it a night. By the time we got to the
correct area for box 3, we needed the flashlight to read the clues. I broke
out my little LED flashlight and we read the clues and searched the area.
Didn't find the box, but decided to head on. The light continued to fade and
the sounds of the forest were coming alive. We heard an Eagle screech as it
passed overhead and all kinds of things scampering (lurking) in the bushes.
We kept the flashlight off, trying to save the batteries in case we needed
them later. The moonlight was reflecting off the fallen maple leaves giving
the trail and unearthly glow. We found the correct intersection but by now
all light had left the forest. The last clue required us to triangulate off
mossy trees and without feeling up every tree, there was no way to tell
which ones were mossy. We had to call it quits and concede that the monster
had alluded us this time and we would have to search again for it. Just
about then we figured out that we had no idea if this trail looped back to
the parking lot or if we needed to go back the way we came. My flashlight
happened to swing across a sign that told us a building near where we had
parked was .3 of a mile a different direction, so we headed that way. We
finally stumbled upon the Environmental Learning Center where the SCA was
camping out. Being the kind souls that they are, they offered us hot food
and drink but we kept on the other half mile or so to the car. Just as we
broke into the last clearing an owl called to us. We thanked him for being
our guiding spirit and happily collapsed into the car. It was one of the
most memorable letterboxing trips I've been on so far. And of course we
learned one lesson, when letterboxing in November, BRING A BIGGER
FLASHLIGHT!

Maiden

I know that of several Boxers that have ventured this way - pandora and I
included - and all of them have given nothing but praise and amazement in
regards to this mysterious quest. If any of you have not been able to being
the journey as well - I would certainly encourage you to do so. So far this
woman's boxes are by far the best I have ever personally seen - on many
levels. And I will venture to say that the boxes she has out right now when
tracked down are not to overly difficult given the weather conditions this
time of year - so don't miss out and go for them!

It is my understanding that those of us graced with her note - can pass the
torch so to speak to other fellow Boxers -PRIVATELY- so if you are wanting
to be a part of this experience I would encourage you to get a hold of
someone who has it in their possession. Plus be watchful while you are out
boxing - you never know what you might find. Also from what I have been able
to gather I think that the ones that have already begun the quest with her
are in store for yet another saga to her tale so keep your eyes peeled - and
ears open - it feels like there is a chilling atramentous mystery on the
wind. To add to all of this - it looks to me like the wonderfully mysterious
woman has hired a certain stealth-detective so I am sure we are in store for
some wonderful findings from him as well.

All of this is just awesome whoever you are you have my sincere thanks - and
this time of year it really gives us Boxers a way to stay ingrained in this
hobby - even if you work all week until the late night hours.

I bid you all well.
