Hi--

I think the changes are awesome, but I noticed a couple of things in Eastern Washington that probably should be changed.

The Southeast Washington section includes the Quick Draw: Rock Climbing Series #4 Box located near Vantage, WA. Vantage is actually in Kittitas County, and this box belongs in the Cascade region.

The Northeastern WA section contains the Canterbury Box, nearby city: Olympia, WA. Olympia is in Thurston County, and this box should show up in the Thurston-Pierce section.

Minor details, but I'd hate especially to have someone miss Quick Draw because they were looking at the wrong map.

Woo Hoo, Choi!!! Thanks for taking the map to the next level. :) It
looks awesome and it functions really well. The regional lists are
much more manageable now. It will definitely come in handy when/if
this hobby gains popularity in the state (just imagine--Connecticut
has 2,500 'listed' letterboxes in an area that's about 1/20th the
size of WA--hard for me to fathom!!!--imagine if this region every
came anywhere near to that sort of LB population). Just noticed a
couple of boxes that 'fell' into incorrect regions but it's because
their designated counties are obviously incorrect. Not sure if the
planters did this intentionally to make 'finding' boxes a bit more
challenging or by accident. In a cursory look, I think I only saw
three that fall into this category.

I haven't yet checked the regional names to see what we
ultimately 'went' with. I'll run through those and compare them
with the feedback/recommendations we received from the LbPNW
community.

trkee--thanks for your tireless effort in manipulating the 'magic'
behind updating the map. Without your 'remote' assistance this
wouldn't have happened any time in the 'remotely' near future.
The "Paint" editing program just doesn't cut it... ;)
