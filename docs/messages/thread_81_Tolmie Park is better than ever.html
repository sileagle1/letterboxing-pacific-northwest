Tolmie Park is better than ever

From: Nail Family (c.nail@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-04-20 01:36:25 UTC
I got to sneak away for a couple hours today between work segments
and took a needed walk through Tolmie State Park in North Lacey.
Checked on the 3 boxes hidden there. Both of dnv2rckr's boxes are
still in place, contents dry and having had several visitors. My own
Huckleberry box is doing quite well too. However, there is some
trail work going on right now and the box was actually exposed to
view from the trail. Workers have dropped off lumber and concrete
supports to rebuild one of the bridges nearby and a large board was
placed right next to the exposed box. I rehid it and did my best to
conceal it with some loose bark found nearby. So it is ready for
visitors again and hopefully not the unasked for kind.

Funny thing, all threeof the log books in the 3 boxes have had
visitors log in on both ends of the log book!?! I thought that only
Trial n Error did such things but he actually had logged in all 3
boxes correctly. Go figure. Whatever the manner of logging in, the
boxes are all in great shape and ready for more visitors. I plan to
place a new box out there soon. It is so close to my work that I plan
to use it as a getaway frequently. I would very much like to make a
quality stamp for this new box so it may take me some time to get it
together. I have already found a very nice hidey hole for it, just
have to get my creative juices flowing and get to carving. Look for
the clues on this list soon.

The park is wonderfully peaceful right now with lots of new foliage
coming out, birds everywhere, and lots of evidence of small animals
around. The park service is doing alot of maintenance and
enhancement of the trails now and the lower parking lot is still
closed due to the earthquake damage but I think that only keeps the
bulk of the crowds away (not to mention the $5/day or $50/yr parking
fee). All in all the park is lovely and quite void of humans right
now. A dedicated letterboxer can justify the parking fee and the trip
out is well worth it. There are lots of benches placed along the
trail for hikers to take a rest among the woods. Make sure if you
visit to take the time to sit and be quiet for a while and take in
all the sights, sounds, and smells. It is great for relieving stress.

Hope to see you on the trails soon,
Nailhead of the Nail Family
