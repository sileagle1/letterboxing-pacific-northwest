If you're talking about onboard ship, you didn't need a tent when I went. It was so long
ago, though (more than 20 years!) that I'd check with the ferry staff to be sure. Would
hate for you to find that you needed one only after you got there. I'd probably also check
with them to see what they recommend for the sleeping bags--it can be downright chilly
when the wind hits you from the water! Though I do seem to recall that if you wanted to
there was a place indoors (with windows all the way around) you could retreat to if you got
too cold.

If you ever end up getting farther north, you should also check out Mt. Denali National
Park. (Denali is the Alaska native name for Mt. McKinley, and is the official name used in
the park.) The Alaska Railroad has a train that goes to the park from Anchorage. The
train itself is fun because there is an observation car and the railroad goes through places
you can't get to by car. If you do manage to get to the park, make sure to find out about
the bus tour. It goes through sections of the park that are off-limits to tourists, so you
get to see the animals who don't have any contact with humans in their natural habitat.
(You aren't allowed to leave the bus, but they stop and let you watch animals that they
see.) A friend of mine who went saw all kinds of bear, moose, caribou... and the park
itself is absolutely lovely.
