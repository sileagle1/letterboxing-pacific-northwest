Re: RE:It's good to have friends

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-07-03 01:43:49 UTC
> What do you all think about this one? Is it a no no to give a Postal
> Letterbox to a friend that wasn't on the list for it?

Hmm..... This is essentially a two-part question as I see it, since
it has the *potential* to upset two parties: (1) The creator of the
postal box if they didn't want the box used in this manner and (2)
the person receiving the box in the mail may not want to deal with
it! Not everyone is a big fan of postal boxes.

Anyhow.... As for passing it on to people not on the official
mailing list--I don't see any harm in that as long as the person who
created the box doesn't mind. If they don't want it happening, they
ought to write so directly in the logbook for the box. Presumeably,
if they haven't written such a comment, I'd assume they'd be okay
with others stamping in as long as it didn't delay the box's travel
too much.

If you're mailing it off to a friend not on the list, though, I'd
double check first that they were actually interested in the box.
I'm not really interested in postal boxes myself, so I'd be rather
put out if I got one unexpectedly in the mail!

But as long as *everyone* involved doesn't mind, go for it. =)

-- Ryan
