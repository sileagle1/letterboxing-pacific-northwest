OR Zoo News

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-11-30 23:25:06 UTC
Funniest thing: yesterday I got an email from Security at the Oregon
Zoo saying they had a "box with a stamp book in it" of mine. The found
my email addy taped inside. So, today I went up and fetched it only to
find that this was the original Packy's Place box that went missing
over a year ago! So, I trundled on down to it's home to check up on
the replacement box I had made. Lo and behold, that one was missing...
so, the original is back in its hiding spot awaiting the next visitor.

While at the Zoo, I checked on all the other boxes that I know of
livimg there and found every one of them safe and sound. Both the
Wapiti and Winking Eagle boxes have moved slightly from their original
homes but the clues will still get you there. I think both have
groundskeepers who have taken them under their wings since their
camouflage materials seem to move along with the boxes. Take extra
paper if you go for the Lorikeet box as the log is full, once again.

I added the wonderful Mystic Tiger to my logbook today. If this is any
example of their typical carving, I look forward to finding more
stamps hidden by Forest Elf! Thanks so much for this little (?)
treasure. Funhog

I ascribed to the "it's easier to ask forgiveness than ask permission"
school of thought when placing these. I have volunteered there for
years and was especially careful about choosing my hiding places. All
but one of the original boxes there have gone missing at one time or
another. So much traffic makes for a short lifespans. Funhog

From: Jess (jmwestin@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-01 01:00:29 UTC
In October, I taught an enrichment course on letterboxing at the
middle school where I work. A couple of weeks later, the science
teacher and I took all of our 6th and 7th graders on a field trip to
the zoo. Most of the kids from my letterboxing class had pre-printed
the clues to the zoo boxes and brought all of their supplies to
letterbox while they were there. They loved all of the boxes they
found there--especially the Packy box and the Mystic Tiger. We even
turned some of the parent volunteers on to letterboxing and now they
take their kids out on the weekends! Thanks for your great boxes! A
day away from school/work and the chance to letterbox--what could make
a better day!

Jess
