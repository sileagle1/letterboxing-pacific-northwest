It's almost time for the Spring Flinger on April 9th, at Priest Point
Park in Olympia!

As of right now, 54 people have RSVP'd.woweeeee. I'm sorry I
haven't been able to contact each one that is planning on attending,
but I am very much looking forward to meeting some of you and
catching up with those I already know. Unfortunately, it looks like
Bethany won't be able to make it, but she has a REALLY good excuse!

The park is gorgeous, and there is something for everyone. Bring
your hiking boots as there are trails everywhere. The park is
divided by East Bay Drive (with a bridge for foot and auto traffic
within the park). On the north side of the park is where the rose
garden is, as well as multiple picnic tables. There is also a drive
that goes up the hill and down past the maintenance facility and then
leads to the bridge. On this drive are several small areas to park
in case you want to drive closer to some of the boxes in the park.
AS ALWAYS: Be careful when you are on the trailseven though this
park is in an suburban setting, some of the trails are very
secluded. Bring a buddy! On the south side of the park is where
the beach access is, as well as the PICNIC/KITCHEN #2. Parking is
limited right at the shelter, so see if you can carpool and have a
designated `hoofer' to park the car. The south side also has the
play equipment and the Ellis Cove Trail. The trails on this side do
have some steep drop offs, so be mindful of the kids!

The potluck is going to be "BUILD YOU OWN BAKED POTATO!!" Please
bring your own dishes and utensils. But we need people to bring
Baked Potatoes plus all the fixings. Also we need some people to
bring canned pop or bottled water. If you know what you are
bringing, please let me knowI will be keeping a running list on the
AQ event page. The "kitchen" has only 1 outlet, but I'll try to
bring a outlet strip. It also has a small sink, but a antiquated
wood burning stove. But lots of space to sit and stamp!

Bring your camera, bring your gear, bring your potato and a bring
your rear!

Hope the rest of your winter is wonderful!
