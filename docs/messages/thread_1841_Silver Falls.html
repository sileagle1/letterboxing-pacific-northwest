From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2005-05-04 15:28:01 UTC
Greetings all! I have been thinking about hiding new boxes for the campout at Silver
Falls, the first weekend in July. After digging through the heap of mail on my desk
yesterday, I unearthed the trail map that Ranger Jim Bader sent me. On it he has
highlighted the trails that he recommends for us to use for hiding letterboxes. You
can see a copy of this map in the Files section of this page.

As attractive as the Trail of Ten Falls is, it already has a series of boxes hidden along
it. The traffic on this path is intense during the summer and it would be very difficult
to go undetected hunting for boxes. The staff at the park would love to see some of
the other trails get more attention. Perhaps we can help! I'm headed out there soon to
do my part. Perhaps some of you will want to do the same. Funhog

I agree. KK's that don't add to the poundage. Who knew?
Then, there was the brilliant clue offering from the
mysterious duo, Miller and Hutch, that appeared magically
in the wee hours of the morning. Hats off and keep it up.
Your oevres are a very special treat. I also can't wait to see
what comes of those intriguing fortune cookies that
appeared at the potluck.

I feel so lucky that I live in the PNW. We have such a great
variety of interesting, creative letterboxers of all ages. It
was wonderful to see so many kids (young and old) engrossed
in carving, resulting in a rash of wonderful new personal
travelers appearing over the weekend. Watching the event
stamp come to life in the hands of Der Mad Stamper under
the stars was another highlight. Green Tortuga's campfire
recitation of the works by Robert Service were another
held everyone's attention. The hogs wallowing in the
dust enacted by Thunderbird and Thundereagle will make
me laugh forevermore.

One of the things I enjoy most is that every box you find
in this part of the country is almost guaranteed to have a
hand-carved stamp in it and the boxes hidden for this event
were no exception. The gift of someone's artwork left in the
wild is a very special kind of communication. Yes, I'm one of
those curmudgeons who feels pangs of disappointment when
I open a letterbox and find a store-bought stamp inside. I'd
always prefer to find a crudely crafted first attempt at carving
than something made by a machine. Many thanks to those who
created a letterbox for this weekend and every other
letterboxing trip I have taken in the past, not to mention the
ones looming in my future.

It was a joy to see friends old and new, some traveling from
hundreds of miles away. I do hope that everyone had at least
half the fun that I did. That way I know that everyone had a
total blast. Funhog

From: Lisa Harkins (lisah@whidbey.com) | Date: 2005-07-05 08:45:32 UTC-07:00
Well, we couldn't say much more than what everyone else has said. This was
our first gathering, we thoroughly enjoyed it, and we couldn't stop talking
about it, even as we rolled into our driveway on Whidbey Island at 9pm.
Here are some of our highlights.

Being so utterly nervous approaching the potlucking crowd - it's scary
meeting new people in groups. Folks were quick to speak to us and welcome
us, and we were excited to meet the personalities behind the names we've
been seeing around since we began doing this in March.

Silver Falls is such a beautiful park that we will have to return, I feel in
several trips. There was so much to see that we didn't have time to, even
without the letterboxes we didn't have time to locate. Thundereagle and I
secretly plotted to be on the trail past campground check-out time so that
we would have to pay for another night, but Scot the chauffuer reminded us
he has to work today. I would come back any day of the week to sit at the
waterfall Funhog so magically showed us.

It's so nice to see the families doing this hobby together. So many things
these days appeal to a narrow range of people, and it was lovely to see both
parents and children enjoying the hobby. The kids were such a joy, so
polite, so friendly, and so much fun. We particularly enjoyed Button,
Downhillsk8ter, and the Team Garlic kids. Laura was having so much fun
playing, she didn't care about the stinkin' Krispy Kremes. Scot the
chauffuer, though he doesn't letterbox, really loves the scenic and quiet
beauty of the places the hikes take us, and he thoroughly enjoyed the
conversation in Group Camp. I hear Green Tortuga tells a mean story, but I
didn't hear it because I was too busy enjoying conversation with Pungent
Bob, Funhog, Woodaug, Slo-Mo, and Maiden.

We learned so much about the finer layers of this wonderful hobby, and were
glad to learn of the legendary Miller and Hutch. What a great experience!
Thanks so much for your kindness. We look forward to the next opportunity
at Liberty Lake.

Thunderbird and Thundereagle
